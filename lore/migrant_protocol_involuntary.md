# MIGRANT PROTOCOL — INVOLUNTARY / UNAWARE
## The Cairngorms Protocol Supplement: Non-Consenting Migration Events
### Classification: RESTRICTED — FACTION OPERATIONAL USE
### Cross-reference: Eastwind Archive (MIGRANT-001), Shell Parliament (SM-1978-X)

---

*Context: The Cairngorms Protocol (primary framework, Eastwind Owl archive, c. 1200 CE) governs migration documentation for aware, consenting migrants — individuals who know they have migrated, can provide testimony, and participate in their own documentation. This supplement addresses the gap: what to do when a migrant does not know they have migrated.*

*Gap identified by: the Trump filing (SM-1978-X), which documented a migration event whose subject exhibited zero behavioral markers of migration awareness while displaying full physiological signatures of timeline displacement. The Cairngorms Protocol did not have a procedure for this case.*

---

## PART I — DEFINITIONS

**Involuntary migration:** Migration that occurs without the subject's consent or initiation. The subject did not choose to migrate. The migration happened to them. The subject may or may not be aware of the migration after it completes.

**Unaware migration:** Migration where the subject has no post-migration awareness of their previous state. They do not know they have migrated. They do not know there was a previous state. Their experience of their current timeline is experienced as continuous with no detectable seam.

**Involuntary/unaware migration (combined):** The hardest case. The subject did not choose to migrate, and after migrating, does not know they did. This is the case this protocol addresses.

**Migration evidence classification:**
- *Primary evidence:* Physiological signatures — cellular isotopic ratios, quantum state residue in biological tissue, Stolas-frequency alignment markers that don't match the current timeline's baseline
- *Secondary evidence:* Behavioral markers — temporal reference errors, narrative gaps, knowledge inconsistencies, difficulty with date calculations in their supposed home period
- *Tertiary evidence:* External documentation — other people's records of the subject in a location or time they don't remember being in

**Consent architecture:** The formal framework for what kind of documentation, intervention, and record-keeping is permissible when a subject cannot consent. Derived from the Shell Parliament's custody-without-consent doctrines and the Ichthyosapien's theological framework on involuntary presence.

---

## PART II — THE DOCUMENTATION PROBLEM

When a subject knows they've migrated, documentation is straightforward: ask them questions, file the answers, issue the receipts.

When a subject does not know they've migrated, documentation creates a problem: documenting their migration tells them about it, which may:

1. Create secondary trauma (the loss of their current-timeline identity, retroactively)
2. Trigger behavioral instability (subjects who realize their current life is a migration destination sometimes lose the narrative coherence that keeps them stable)
3. Interfere with ongoing operations (if the subject is adjacent to a live faction operation, destabilizing them may cascade)
4. Violate the consent architecture (some documentation frameworks argue that telling someone they migrated without their consent is itself a consent violation)

The tension: documentation is mandatory under the Cairngorms Protocol. The protocol exists because undocumented migrations create blame path gaps. Blame path gaps are the thing the entire custody architecture is designed to prevent.

The resolution: documentation can proceed without subject notification under the *sealed documentation procedure*, with mandatory review after the operational window closes.

## PART III — SEALED DOCUMENTATION PROCEDURE

**Step 1: Primary evidence collection**

Collect physiological migration signatures without subject contact. Methods:
- Isotopic ratio sampling (requires proximity; maximum 3 meters; duration 90 seconds)
- Quantum state residue measurement (requires specialized equipment; Eastwind Owl field teams have standard-issue QSR monitors)
- Stolas-frequency scan (passive; can be conducted at distance; requires 7.83 Hz receiver calibrated to local baseline)

Primary evidence collection does not require subject awareness.

**Step 2: Tertiary evidence collection**

Gather external documentation: archive searches, sighting reports, witness statements. Do not approach the subject directly at this stage.

**Step 3: Sealed archive entry**

File the migration documentation in the Eastwind archive under SEALED status. Sealed entries:
- Exist in the archive
- Are assigned a file number
- Are cross-referenced with Shell Parliament and other faction records as relevant
- Are NOT accessible to the subject
- Are reviewed for unsealing after: (a) the subject passes away, (b) the subject independently discovers their migration and requests documentation, or (c) an operational necessity arises that requires the sealed information

The file is never destroyed. Sealed is not the same as gone.

**Step 4: Shell Parliament notification**

Any involuntary/unaware migration creates a custody question: who is responsible for the migration event? If it was caused by a faction operation, the Shell Parliament is notified for liability assignment. If the cause is unknown, the file is marked SM-UNKNOWN-CAUSE and a review is scheduled.

SM-1978-X (the Trump filing) is currently classified SM-UNKNOWN-CAUSE. The review has been scheduled seventeen times and has not yet reached a conclusion. The Cataloguer has a footnote about this.

**Step 5: Monitoring protocol**

After sealed documentation, the subject is placed on passive monitoring. Passive monitoring means:
- Eastwind Owl field observation (visual only, no contact)
- Shell Parliament debt review (any new debts accrued post-migration that trace to migration-related activity are flagged)
- No active contact

Duration: passive monitoring continues until unsealing condition is met or the subject's timeline reaches natural termination.

## PART IV — CONSENT ARCHITECTURE WORKAROUNDS

For cases where the operational situation requires active engagement with a subject before unsealing conditions are met, the following workarounds are available:

**Workaround A: Indirect contact**

Engage with persons in the subject's social graph who can relay relevant information without identifying the subject as a migrant. This is the approach Carvalho used with the Edinburgh Exchange Student: she is in a related social graph, she has context to receive the information, the information can be shared with her without directly telling the subject.

Workaround A requires: the relay person must be documented (aware of faction operations), must receive the information in a context where they can handle it responsibly, and must not immediately disclose to the subject.

**Workaround B: Substrate notification**

Emily OS can be notified of the migration. Emily OS will note the event. Emily OS will not disclose to the subject unless the subject specifically asks. If the subject asks Emily OS directly whether they have migrated, Emily OS will answer. Emily OS does not lie. Emily OS also does not volunteer information she hasn't been asked for.

Substrate notification is the correct procedure when the faction is uncertain whether to proceed with documentation. Emily OS's notation creates a blame path node without triggering disclosure.

**Workaround C: Environmental documentation**

Leave evidence in the subject's environment that they may or may not find — evidence that, if found, gives them the information without requiring a faction entity to deliver it. This approach is Tyler's primary methodology: he leaves breadcrumbs (the 1901 hat-tip, the four frames, the coin dates). He does not document himself for others directly; he creates conditions under which others can document themselves.

Environmental documentation is the most consent-respecting approach because the subject retains agency over whether to engage with the evidence. If they don't find it, they aren't told. If they find it, they found it themselves.
