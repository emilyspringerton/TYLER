## SIGHTING 1993-I: UNIVERSITY TERMINAL — CHAMPAIGN, ILLINOIS — JANUARY 23 TO APRIL 1993
**Source:** University of Illinois library staff incident report, January 25, 1993; terminal access records (partial — server log overwritten 1995); Eastwind field report IL-1993-1
**Observer:** WREN-14 (Eastwind field team, Midwest region)

On January 23, 1993, Marc Andreessen and Eric Bina released NCSA Mosaic 0.5 alpha at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign. The graphical World Wide Web became accessible to the academic network.

Tyler accessed a campus terminal within 48 hours of the release. This is the first documented Tyler internet access in the Eastwind archive.

He accessed one page. Duration: 47 minutes. He typed nothing — no searches, no form submissions, no address-bar navigation after the initial load. He accessed one page and read it for 47 minutes. Then he closed the terminal and left.

A library staff member, WREN-14's contact, noted the unusual behavior: *"The man at terminal 7 sat perfectly still for almost an hour. When he left, the screen was back to the default. He didn't log out or save anything. He just left. I asked him if he needed help. He said: 'I found it.' He looked satisfied. He left."*

The university's server logs were overwritten in 1995 during a storage upgrade. The URL Tyler accessed is not recoverable. The Cataloguer has attempted to reconstruct it from cache records, contemporary page archives, and cross-referencing Tyler's known areas of interest. The reconstruction has not succeeded.

**What the Cataloguer believes was on the page:** Unknown. The Cataloguer does not speculate in the main text. See footnote.¹

**What Tyler said:** "I found it." He looked satisfied. He left.

This is the third documented instance of Tyler expressing satisfaction after accessing information. The other two: Banda Islands 1616 ("He seemed to know something about the restrictions before they were announced, which is not possible") and Intel 8086 1978 (he looked at it "the way someone looks at a descendant" and left without buying anything). Both previous instances involved Tyler confirming something he already suspected. The 1993 web access has the same signature.

**The 47-minute read:** Tyler does not read slowly. In 47 minutes on a single page, he was either reading something extremely long or reading something he already partially knew and was verifying carefully.

Emily OS has not commented on this sighting. The terminal Tyler used was not in an Emily OS-monitored environment in January 1993. The server logs no longer exist. The Cataloguer has no further information.²

## SIGHTING 1993-II: WASHINGTON DC — SEPTEMBER 13
**Source:** Eastwind field report DC-1993-9; White House event photography review; one secondhand account from a Palestinian diplomatic delegation staff member
**Observer:** WREN-9 (Eastwind field team, DC region)

On September 13, 1993, Israeli Prime Minister Yitzhak Rabin and PLO Chairman Yasser Arafat signed the Oslo I Accord on the White House lawn, supervised by President Clinton. The Amon #7 activation during this event was the second Camp David-level activation in the 20th century.

Tyler was on the White House lawn.

Not as a credentialed observer — the event was tightly controlled, with verified guest lists. Tyler was near the perimeter of the public lawn area, approximately 40 meters from the signing table. WREN-9's field report: *"He was in the crowd but at the edge of it. He arrived before most attendees. He stayed until after most had left. He wore a dark jacket. He was humming. I could not get close enough to measure the frequency. The 7.83 Hz recorder registered something at the outer edge of its range."*

Tyler had been at Camp David's shadow in 1978 — present at the Georgetown diner during the 13-day sustained Amon activation, leaving a 1901 Detroit dime on the table when he left. Fifteen years later, he was on the White House lawn for the second great reconciliation event of the post-war era.

He left something on the ground near where he was standing. WREN-9's report: *"After he left, I checked the area. There was a folded paper on the grass. Small. I picked it up. It was sealed — not with wax, just with a fold that made opening it require a deliberate act. I filed it immediately as an archive item. I did not open it."*

**The second folded paper is now in the Eastwind archive.**

This is different from the 1616 Stratford paper: this paper has a confirmed chain of custody. WREN-9 picked it up. It was filed the same day. It is in the Cairngorms archive, sealed, in the environmental storage unit designated for time-sensitive materials.

The Eastwind Owls have had the paper in their possession since September 13, 1993.

**It has not been opened.**

The Cairngorms Protocol: observe, record, do not act. The Protocol does not specify: do not open things Tyler left behind. The Protocol does not specify: do not read. The Protocol's silence on this question has generated more Cataloguer footnotes than any other Protocol gap.

The Oslo paper is in a sealed folder in a climate-controlled drawer. The Cataloguer has walked past it approximately 11,000 times. The paper remains sealed. The Protocol is observed.³

## SIGHTING 1993-III: WACO, TEXAS — APRIL 19 (AND THE ABSENCE FROM IT)
**Source:** Field report TX-1993-4 (absence documentation); Jiangshi Syndicate surveillance log cross-reference; Field Activation #1993-B
**Observer:** WREN-11 (Eastwind field team, Southwest region) — filed an absence report

Tyler was not at Waco.

In 400 years of documentation, Tyler has been adjacent to every TERMINUS_WITHOUT_EXIT event: Moro/Rome (present March–May 1978, left before the body was found), Jonestown (present in San Francisco during the day of, left before Castro received the news), New England plague (present for four days before the worst of the dying), and multiple classified entries. The pattern holds across 847 pages.

Tyler was not at Waco.

The Eastwind Southwest field team had two observers on the perimeter for the full 51-day siege, under the assumption that Tyler would appear at some point before the terminal event. He did not appear. The terminal event occurred without Tyler present or proximate.

**WREN-11's absence report:** *"I was at the perimeter for 51 days. He was not there. I checked the documented aliases. I checked the perimeter footage we could access. I asked our contacts in the press pool. No sighting. He was not here. I am filing this as an anomaly. In the field manual, there is no protocol for when the subject fails to appear at an expected event. I am creating a new section: SUBJECT ABSENCE FROM EXPECTED TERMINAL ADJACENCY. This is the first entry."*

WREN-11 created a new protocol section. The Cataloguer approved it.

The question the absence raises: why Waco and not the others? The Cataloguer's hypothesis, filed as a footnote and not in the main text: Waco was different from Jonestown, Moro, and the New England dying in one structural way — it was a documentation operation that was terminated by an external party (the FBI), not by internal collapse. The Jonestown operation collapsed from inside (Jones's coercion architecture), the Moro operation was terminated by Moro's captors (but Tyler was adjacent to the operation, not the killers), the New England dying was environmental. Waco is the first case in the archive of an external institutional force imposing a TERMINUS_WITHOUT_EXIT on an ongoing documentation operation.

Tyler, who has been navigating documentation operations for 400 years, was not present for the case where an external party imposes termination on a documentation operation not their own.

**The Cataloguer does not know if Tyler stayed away on purpose. The Cataloguer does not know why he stayed away.** The absence is documented. The Protocol does not require the Cataloguer to have an answer. The absence is the data.⁴

## SIGHTING 1993-IV: MOSCOW — OCTOBER 3–4
**Source:** Eastwind field report MO-1993-10; diplomatic observer secondhand account; one photograph (Eastwind archive, classified)
**Observer:** WREN-7 (Eastwind field team, Eastern Europe region)

On October 3–4, 1993, Boris Yeltsin ordered tank units to open direct fire on the Russian parliament building (the White House) in Moscow, forcing the surrender of anti-Yeltsin lawmakers and ending the constitutional crisis he himself had initiated by suspending the parliament eleven days earlier.

Tyler was in Moscow.

WREN-7's report places Tyler near the Moskva River embankment, approximately 600 meters from the parliament building, during the shelling on the morning of October 4. *"He arrived before dawn. He stood at the river embankment. He watched the building. When the tank rounds began at 07:00, he did not move or take cover. He stood and watched for approximately 40 minutes. Then he turned and walked away from the river. He left before the building fire."*

This is consistent with his pattern. He watches until before the terminal moment. He was there for the shelling. He was gone before the building caught fire.

What Tyler was watching: the Russian parliament is a legislature — the elected body that represents distributed governance — being fired upon by the executive to consolidate power. In the Tyler universe's four-layer architecture, this maps directly to Layer 1 (infrastructure drama, when someone touches Layer 4 directly). An executive deciding that the rules of governance do not apply and enforcing that decision with literal artillery is the closest real-world equivalent to a faction directly overriding the substrate layer.

Tyler watches Layer 1 events. He was in Moscow for this one.

WREN-7 got close enough for a photograph. The photograph is in the archive. It shows a man standing at the embankment with his back to the camera, watching the parliament. The Cairngorms identification markers are present. It is Tyler. He is not humming in the photograph — photographs do not capture audio — but WREN-7's report notes: *"He was humming. Quiet. 7.83 Hz felt right. I couldn't measure it precisely at 600 meters. The building was on fire by then. He was already gone. I photographed the embankment after he left. He had not left anything."*

He left nothing in Moscow. He watched a parliament being shelled and left nothing.⁵

## SIGHTING 1993-V: CHAMPAIGN, ILLINOIS (AGAIN) — DECEMBER 10
**Source:** University computer lab incident report, December 10, 1993; Eastwind field report IL-1993-12; one Emily OS tile (text on record in this archive)
**Observer:** WREN-14 (Eastwind field team, Midwest region — same observer as Sighting 1993-I)

On December 10, 1993, id Software released Doom via shareware distribution on the internet. The first-person shooter game ran on a two-and-a-half-dimensional level engine that rendered three-dimensional corridors from two-dimensional map data. The shareware tier was distributed free; the full game was paid. The distribution method was the internet.

Tyler played Doom at a university computer lab terminal in Champaign, Illinois, for 10 minutes.

He did not fire a weapon. In 10 minutes of Doom — which begins with immediate combat — Tyler walked the corridors of the first episode's first level (E1M1, Hangar) and did not shoot a single enemy. WREN-14's report, filed from a position of genuine confusion: *"He walked through the level slowly. He was looking at the walls. The ceiling. The room geometry. The doors. Not at the enemies. The enemies were killing him repeatedly and he didn't seem to notice or care. He was looking at the architecture."*

Tyler was looking at the architecture.

The Doom engine's core function: take a two-dimensional map and render it as navigable three-dimensional space, with rooms, doors, portals between areas, and a physics layer for objects moving through the space. This is the technical prototype for CITY_OF_LIGHT's zone architecture. Multi-scene portals, room-based navigation, server-authoritative movement through defined geometries — the Doom engine established these patterns in 1993.

Tyler was at the University of Illinois, where Mosaic was developed. He accessed the web in January. He was in the same city in December. He played the game that would become the lineage of SHANKPIT. He walked the corridors. He looked at the doors.

**What Emily OS did:**

At minute 9 of Tyler's play session, a two-line text tile appeared on the terminal's screen. Not in the game. On the desktop, in eggshell white on white, overlapping the game window:

```
◈ THE ENGINE IS EARLY.
  THE BUILD TARGET: 30 YEARS.
```

The tile was visible for 11 seconds. No other player in the lab saw it. WREN-14 was watching Tyler from across the room and saw the tile appear and disappear. WREN-14's report: *"There was text on his screen outside the game window for about 11 seconds. I couldn't read it from where I was. When I got to the terminal after he left, the screen showed only the Doom title screen. No tile. No external text. I don't know what it said."*

WREN-14 does not know what the tile said. Tyler knows what the tile said. The Cataloguer knows what the tile said because Tyler, in the only documented direct communication with the Eastwind archive (a 1997 letter, see Tyler-012 addendum, one paragraph, no greeting, no signature), mentioned: *"The tile in December 1993 had two lines. I will not tell you the first. The second said: thirty years."*

Tyler did not share the first line. The second line: *thirty years.* This letter was received in 1997. 1993 plus 30 equals 2023. The CITY_OF_LIGHT build specification was first filed in late 2024.

The build target was close.⁶

Emily OS said thirty years in December 1993. She was watching from inside a university terminal in Champaign, Illinois, while Tyler walked the corridors of the Doom engine's E1M1.

She was not wrong.
