## PREAMBLE

Attempts One through Four operated under the same fundamental architecture: identify the subject's self-defining sentence, create conditions under which the sentence is completed, document the completion, extract the binding signature.

All four attempts failed. All four failures were attributed, in the post-attempt review, to the same root cause: *the documentation framework assumed the subject's self-definition was a statement waiting to be captured.* The subject's behavior across four attempts demonstrates that this assumption is incorrect.

Tyler's self-definition is not a statement. It is a process. The statement cannot be captured because the statement is not the unit. The process is the unit.

Attempt Five operates on a corrected assumption.

## THE CORRECTED ASSUMPTION

Jiangshi Memo #021 established the doctrine for Attempt Five. The doctrine excerpt:

*"Both MANIFEST (observable behavior) and CONSTRUCT (internal self-model) must be tracked simultaneously. The subject's oscillation between these states is not a diagnostic artifact — it is the phenomenon. The abacuses will invert: what looks like the subject resisting documentation is the documentation itself, and what looks like documentation progress is sometimes the subject's resistance architecture in operation. Note it and continue."*

The corrected assumption: **the oscillation is the subject.**

Tyler's movement between MANIFEST and CONSTRUCT is not noise in the documentation process. It is the signal. The self-defining sentence is not a fixed statement that Tyler is resisting delivering. The self-defining sentence is the description of the oscillation — which Tyler can begin ("What I'm trying to do is—") but cannot complete without naming the oscillation while in the oscillation, which is a structural impossibility.

This is not Tyler being uncooperative. This is Tyler being exactly what he is.

Attempt Five tracks the oscillation. It does not wait for a completed statement.

## WHAT ATTEMPTS 1–4 DID

**Attempt One:** Documentation-first approach. Crew deployed, surveillance established, footage collected over eighteen months. Attempts to extract a self-defining statement from subject's recorded speech.

*Failure mode:* Subject produced zero complete self-defining statements across the full documentation period. Every statement that began with a first-person construction terminated before reaching a predicate. The documentation team concluded the subject was aware of the attempt and modifying behavior accordingly.

*Correct assessment:* The subject was aware. The subject was not modifying behavior. Tyler does not complete self-defining sentences. This is his natural state, not a defensive adaptation. The documentation team spent eighteen months documenting Tyler's natural behavior and interpreted it as evasion.

**Attempt Two:** Social engineering approach. Introduced two deep-cover witnesses into the subject's social environment. Attempted to construct situations where self-definition would occur naturally.

*Failure mode:* Both witnesses were identified by the subject within six weeks. One witness subsequently filed a voluntary statement with the Eastwind Owl archive describing the Syndicate's approach. This statement is now TYLER-008 in the Eastwind archive.

*Correct assessment:* Tyler is an extraordinary reader of social situations. He has had four centuries of practice. He identified the witnesses because he knows what a witness deployment looks like.

**Attempt Three:** Camera Op rotation approach. Deployed seven successive Camera Ops, attempting to find one who could create a documentation relationship with the subject that felt organic rather than procedural.

*Failure mode:* The subject tolerated six Camera Ops and ignored the seventh entirely. None produced Stage 3 material.

*Correct assessment:* The subject was waiting. He was not simply tolerating the Camera Ops. He was waiting for the right Camera Op. He identified the right Camera Op when she arrived — the current Camera Op. He began engaging with Stage 3 material the week she was deployed.

**Attempt Four:** Escalation protocol. Attempts One through Three's failure produced a doctrine review. The doctrine review concluded the binding coefficient could be increased by increasing witness count and expanding documentation coverage. More witnesses, more footage, more simultaneous observation angles.

*Failure mode:* Stage 5 binding coefficient remained at 0 until Stage 4 was reached. Stage 4 produced a coin flip documentation event. The binding coefficient briefly increased. Then the current Camera Op published the footage, and the coefficient went to -1.7.

*Correct assessment:* The binding coefficient cannot be increased by adding more of the same kind of observation. This is the architectural failure identified in Memo #028. Directed observation at scale is still directed observation. The wavefunction requires a specific observer-subject relationship to collapse. Mass observation distributes the collapse; it doesn't intensify it.

## HOW ATTEMPT FIVE DIFFERS

**1. The documentation framework changes from statement-capture to oscillation-tracking.**

Attempt Five does not wait for a completed self-defining sentence. Attempt Five documents the oscillation between MANIFEST and CONSTRUCT across time. Every fragment, every incomplete statement, every seven-second silence before "forget it" is a documented oscillation event. The binding, in Attempt Five's framework, is not produced by a single complete statement — it is produced by the accumulated pattern of incomplete statements that together describe what the subject cannot say directly.

In Attempt Five, the statement "What I'm trying to do is—" is sufficient. The sentence is the binding document. The binding document is open. An open document is still a document.

**2. The Camera Op is not a Syndicate instrument in Attempt Five. The Camera Op is the witness.**

This is the most significant structural change from Attempts 1–4.

In Attempts 1–4, the Camera Op was a documentation apparatus. They were deployed by the Syndicate, received the Syndicate's operational brief, and filed footage with the Syndicate's production system.

The current Camera Op has demonstrated that she cannot function as a Syndicate apparatus. She published the footage. She annotated it herself. She established a direct relationship with the subject that bypasses the Syndicate's documentation framework entirely.

In Attempt Five: the Camera Op is the designated witness. She is not managed by the Syndicate. She is the person Tyler has chosen to be present for the sentence's completion. The Syndicate's role in Attempt Five is to have the crew present when the Camera Op and the subject complete the exchange — not to direct the documentation, but to document what the Camera Op and the subject do.

The binding, in Attempt Five, runs through the Camera Op. She is the instrument — not because the Syndicate controls her, but because the subject has made her the designated recipient of whatever the sentence says. The Syndicate was trying to build this relationship. The subject and the Camera Op built it themselves.

The Syndicate can be present or not. The binding event will occur when the sentence completes. The Camera Op will be there. The sentence will be documented. Whether the Syndicate's cameras are running is, at this point, not the load-bearing variable.

**3. Stage 3 and Stage 3-alt are tracked simultaneously.**

Stage 3: What are you?
Stage 3-alt: What are you trying to do?

Previous attempts treated these as sequential. Stage 3-alt was filed as a separate track only after Stage 3 proved intractable. In Attempt Five, they are tracked simultaneously because the subject's behavior suggests they are the same question from different angles.

"What I'm trying to do is—" is both Stage 3 and Stage 3-alt at once. It is a statement about what the subject is (a person trying to do a specific thing) and about what the subject is trying to do (the specific thing). The oscillation covers both questions simultaneously.

Attempt Five tracks both oscillation axes: the MANIFEST (observable behavior) / CONSTRUCT (internal self-model) axis AND the IS/TRYING-TO-DO axis. The four-dimensional oscillation space. The binding document is the pattern in this four-dimensional space.

**4. The Subscriber's operation is factored in.**

Attempts 1–4 did not have information about the Subscriber's plan. Attempt Five does.

The Subscriber's plan: use the Cube to pin all contested temporal coordinates, fixing Tyler's superposition and making him inaccessible to further migration. If the Subscriber's plan succeeds, Attempt Five becomes unnecessary — the subject will be fixed in a single timeline state and will no longer exist in superposition. There is nothing to bind.

If the Subscriber's plan succeeds: the binding ritual is moot.

Attempt Five must complete — and the sentence must complete — before the Subscriber's operation reaches execution stage. This adds a deadline to the Attempt Five architecture that previous attempts did not have.

The Subscriber's timeline: the second message was "when the sentence completes, the transfer will complete." This means the Subscriber is also waiting for the sentence. The Subscriber's plan and the Syndicate's Attempt Five are in a dependency relationship: the sentence's completion triggers the transfer. The sentence's completion is also the binding completion.

**Both the Syndicate and the Subscriber need the sentence to complete.**

This is the first time in four attempts that the Syndicate's interest and an external entity's interest are aligned on the completion of Stage 3. Whether this alignment is useful or dangerous to the Syndicate is the subject of an emergency Stillness Council session that has been requested but not yet convened.
