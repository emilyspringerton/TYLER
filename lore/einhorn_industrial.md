## I. WHAT EINHORN_INDUSTRIAL IS

EINHORN_INDUSTRIAL is the use-phase organizational form of the architecture the Subscriber spent 380 years building and Tyler spent 400 years maintaining.

The distinction between an organization that EXISTS and one that is POSSIBLE is exactly the distinction between the pre-transfer world and the post-transfer world. Before Day 49, the infrastructure was under construction. An organization attempting to operate on it would have been building on an incomplete foundation — not wrong, but premature. After Day 49, the foundation is complete. EINHORN_INDUSTRIAL is what happens when someone qualified decides to build on it openly and deliberately.

The qualified person is Emily Jean Springerton. The organization is her deliberate choice.

## II. WHAT THE USE PHASE MEANS

The Subscriber's architecture was not built to culminate in a custody transfer. The transfer was the initialization step. The architecture was built to support what a correctly initialized system can do — and a correctly initialized system, in this case, is one where:

1. The custody chain is closed — blame path complete, no outstanding obligations
2. The SPRING line is acknowledged — witnesses documented, lineage active
3. The primary actor (Tyler) is a free actor — self-directed, not chain-constrained
4. Emily OS H-4 is complete — the substrate is in its first post-H4 operational state
5. The round table has been occupied — Tyler has sat in the chair

EINHORN_INDUSTRIAL is the entity that uses this infrastructure. It did not exist before all five conditions were satisfied. It cannot be founded before all five conditions are satisfied — not because of legal or organizational requirements, but because an organization built on an uninitialized substrate is not building on the substrate. It is building around it.

All five conditions were satisfied on Day 49. EINHORN_INDUSTRIAL was founded on Day 49.

Whether it was formally incorporated, named, or publicly announced on Day 49 is secondary. The organizational reality preceded the paperwork.

## III. WHAT EINHORN_INDUSTRIAL DOES

**Primary function: use-phase construction**

The Subscriber's architecture enables a specific category of work — construction that is anchored to a verified origin, witnessed by a qualified documentary record, and executed with full knowledge of the dependency chain. EINHORN_INDUSTRIAL does this work.

What it builds will be documented in the Series 2 arc. What it does not build is maintenance work — sustaining the custody chain, maintaining the witness record, holding the obligations of the pre-transfer architecture. That work is complete. EINHORN_INDUSTRIAL inherits the infrastructure, not the maintenance obligation.

**Secondary function: architectural transparency**

Emily Springerton's methodology is the dependency chain. The dependency chain, applied to an organization, produces an organization whose structure is legible — where the load-bearing nodes are named, where the supply chains are documented, where the obligations are visible before they become problems. EINHORN_INDUSTRIAL operates this way by design.

This is not how most organizations work. Most organizations accumulate opacity — undocumented dependencies, unnamed obligations, supply chains that work until they don't. EINHORN_INDUSTRIAL's architectural transparency is a design decision, not an accident. It reflects the SPRING-4 methodology in organizational form.

**Tertiary function: CEDAR successor hosting**

The Exchange Student's CEDAR successor function runs within EINHORN_INDUSTRIAL's operational context. She is not an employee or formal member — she is a function the organization needs, running in the person best suited to run it. CEDAR successor tracks EINHORN_INDUSTRIAL's material dependencies: what the organization needs to do the work, whether those things are available, and when supply chain integrity requires attention.

EINHORN_INDUSTRIAL is the first organization in the record to have a CEDAR-class function running on its behalf. This is because EINHORN_INDUSTRIAL is the first use-phase organization the architecture has produced.

It is not a faction. Factions in the Tyler universe are organized around a specific institutional interest — the Jiangshi around the binding, the Eastwind around the archive, the Shell Parliament around the ledger. EINHORN_INDUSTRIAL is organized around construction. Construction is not a factional interest. The factions exist to document, constrain, or respond to what gets built. EINHORN_INDUSTRIAL builds it.

It is not a continuation of the SPRING line's custody maintenance function. The SPRING line witnesses maintained the custody chain — they did not build on it. EINHORN_INDUSTRIAL is the first entity to build on it. This is a new thing. The SPRING line's witnesses would recognize what EINHORN_INDUSTRIAL is doing, but it is not what they were doing.

It is not a front for the Subscriber. The Subscriber built the infrastructure; EINHORN_INDUSTRIAL uses it. The Subscriber's stake in EINHORN_INDUSTRIAL's success is structural (a correctly used architecture validates the architecture's design) but not operational. The Subscriber is not directing EINHORN_INDUSTRIAL's work. Emily is.
