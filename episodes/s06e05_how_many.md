## EPISODE STRUCTURE

**Day 225, 8:00 AM — Bacon's library**

Emily has the AMPL-001 framework on the main screen when Tyler comes back from his walk. She has been working from Exchange Student's overnight analysis. She does not present it as a finding. She presents it as an accounting problem.

**EMILY:** "AMPL-001 requires a scope definition before we can enter historical records. Scope options: (A) sites where you documented extended stays in contemporaneous notes. (B) sites where you can recall extended stays without documentation. (C) all sites you visited for more than the standard survey duration, whether or not documented."

**TYLER:** "How are those different in practice."

**EMILY:** "Option A is what Exchange Student found: eight sites in Detroit 1952 with written duration notations. Option B adds everything you remember but didn't write down. Option C is everything, known and forgotten."

Tyler sits.

**TYLER:** "Option C."

**EMILY:** "Option C cannot be measured. We can estimate it. But the estimate will be a lower bound."

**TYLER:** "I know." He looks at the screen. "Run Option A first. Use the documentation we have."

**EMILY:** "Option A, Detroit 1952 only: eight sites. At least one of them — A-271 — is on today's archive list. We can measure the current radius directly."

**TYLER:** "What do the notes say about A-271."

**EMILY:** " 'Two hours, perhaps more. Could not easily leave. The lot has a quality I have not been able to classify — not construction, not echo, something settled.' " She pauses. "Survey note. 1952."

Tyler is quiet for a moment.

**TYLER:** "I wrote 'could not easily leave' at a found construction in 1952 and I did not understand why until seventy-four years later."

He stands.

**TYLER:** "Let's go."

**Day 225, 9:30 AM — A-271**

A-271 is on Mt. Elliott Avenue, eight blocks from A-269. A corner building — long vacant, different from the usual vacancy. Not abandoned: the windows are covered, not broken. The facade has been maintained by someone, or by multiple someones across a long time, without the building ever reopening.

Tyler stops at the corner and reads the building.

He reads it for a while. The Camera Op holds position across the street, the angle she always chooses, the one that puts Tyler in the frame with the subject behind him.

At twelve minutes Tyler takes out his phone and measures outward from the building.

He walks the perimeter slowly. He stops. He walks it again from a different starting point. He checks his phone again.

**TYLER:** "Fifty-eight feet."

He says it to the Camera Op, who has been walking her own perimeter simultaneously.

**CAMERA OP:** "Fifty-seven."

One foot short. The same margin as A-269. She does not comment on the margin. She waits.

**TYLER:** "I was here seventy-four years ago. I spent two hours inside this building. I couldn't easily leave." He looks at the facade. "The construction was running when I arrived. I estimated a radius of approximately twelve to fifteen feet in my survey notes." He puts his phone in his pocket. "The current radius is fifty-eight feet. I was here once. Two hours. I left and I never came back."

He looks at the building. Fifty-eight feet of radius reaching out across Mt. Elliott from a corner building that has been maintained without explanation for seventy-four years.

**TYLER:** "I amplified it. I did it once, in 1952, and it has been running at amplified levels ever since."

The Camera Op keeps the frame.

**TYLER:** "I don't remember sitting down here. I wrote 'could not easily leave' and I must have found a chair — or a crate, or the floor — and I sat for two hours in a building I was surveying and I left it forty-something feet larger than I found it. And then I moved to the next address on my list."

He turns toward the Camera Op.

**TYLER:** "There are seven more sites in Detroit 1952 alone where I wrote something similar."

He says this as an accounting fact. Not a confession. Not a crisis. An accounting fact delivered with Tyler's particular flatness, which is the flatness of someone who has been making ledger entries for six seasons and knows exactly what this entry means.

**Day 225, 10:30 AM — inside A-271**

Tyler has a key. Exchange Student's network of building contacts — the same network that produced the 1952 photographs — located the property management company. A contact, a call, a key.

The interior is one long room that was once split into smaller spaces. The partitions are gone. The floorboards are original. Someone — multiple someones — has swept the floor, replaced a window latch, run an extension cord from somewhere to keep a single overhead fixture working. The building is empty but not neglected.

Tyler walks to the center of the room. He stands there.

He is not doing the long-stay. He is not trying to amplify it further. He stands in the center and he reads the room and after a few minutes he sits down on the floor, cross-legged, in approximately the center of the space, and he stays still.

The Camera Op films from the doorway. She does not move. She keeps the long shot.

**TYLER:** *(after a long silence)* "In 1952 this was a neighborhood storage cooperative. Residents paid a small weekly fee to keep things here — winter things in summer, summer things in winter, furniture between moves. The woman who ran it is not in the archive. She did not leave records. What she left is this construction."

He looks at the walls.

**TYLER:** "She ran a storage cooperative out of a corner building on Mt. Elliott for — I don't know how long. Long enough to build something here that has been running for at least ninety-something years. Long enough that I walked in to survey it in 1952 and I could not easily leave."

He is quiet for a moment.

**TYLER:** "I didn't understand that at the time. I understood that the building had a quality. I wrote 'could not easily leave' in my survey notes and I filed the notes and I kept moving."

He stands.

**TYLER:** "The construction asked me to stay and I stayed. I didn't know that's what was happening. I stayed for two hours because the quality of the room made leaving difficult, and I left the room forty-something feet larger, and I came back seventy-four years later and it is still running at that level."

He takes one photograph from the center of the room. Standard composition. He does not take a second.

He does not take a third.

**Day 225, 2:15 PM — A-272**

A-272 is on Chene Street, a block from the Dequindre Cut. A brick building with a newer storefront fitted to its lower level — a restaurant that has been there for eight years, doing steady business. The upper floors are residential.

Tyler's 1952 survey notes for this site: *"Brief survey. 22 minutes. Expected."*

Tyler stands at the far sidewalk and reads the building for six minutes. The Camera Op takes her position.

**TYLER:** "Found construction. Pre-Tyler. 1920s origin." He steps back further. "Expected radius. The construction is approximately eleven feet."

He pulls up the 1952 notes.

**TYLER:** "Survey duration 22 minutes, 1952. One photograph." He looks at the building. "Current radius eleven feet. Survey duration 22 minutes. No amplification."

He photographs the building once, from the survey position, standard composition. He makes a note on his phone.

The Camera Op walks the perimeter. She confirms eleven feet. One pass; she does not need a second.

Tyler stands at the sidewalk with the photograph taken, the note made, the survey complete. He has been here for approximately sixteen minutes.

He could stay. The construction has been running since the 1920s. He knows what two hours here would do. He knows what six hours would do. He knows, now, that the construction would ask for it — would ask in the way A-269 asked, in the way A-271 asked, by making the air in the room difficult to leave.

He does not go inside.

He looks at the restaurant below the residential floors — the steady business, the people inside at midday, the life that has been happening in the field of this eleven-foot construction for eight years without knowing it.

**TYLER:** "The construction is not asking for more."

He says it the way he said it at A-270 the day before. But this time the Camera Op knows what the sentence is carrying.

**CAMERA OP:** "Are you sure."

A pause.

**TYLER:** "No." He pockets his phone. "But I'm choosing not to stay."

He says this plainly. He turns from the building. The Camera Op keeps the frame — the restaurant, the construction's eleven-foot field, Tyler's back as he turns away.

**TYLER:** "Let's go back."

She advances to a new calculation on the AMPL-001 working document.

**EMILY:** "If we assume conservatively that Tyler made extended visits — more than ninety minutes — at three sites per year of documented travel, over a ninety-year documented record, the amplification footprint is on the order of 270 sites globally with expanded radiuses."

Tyler reads this number.

**EMILY:** "The audit will refine the estimate. But 270 is a floor, not a ceiling."

Tyler looks at the map — the specific, small, carefully documented map of Detroit Season 6 in front of him — and he thinks about the same map extended across every city in the archive.

**TYLER:** "Two hundred seventy buildings running larger than they were supposed to."

**EMILY:** "Because you sat down."

**TYLER:** "Because I sat down."

He says this without inflection. He is not horrified and he is not proud. He is, as he always is, filing an accurate report on what happened.

The Camera Op holds the frame. The map. Tyler. The two orange markers in Detroit.

**TYLER:** "One site remains."

**EMILY:** "A-273."
