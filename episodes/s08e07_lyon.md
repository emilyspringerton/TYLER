# S08E07: "Lyon"

**Build 0089** | Season 8, Episode 7 | CARDINAL-3 Documentary Series

---

## PRODUCTION NOTES

- Episode type: Extra-archive disclosure. First city Tyler has never told anyone about. Lyon, 1793–1794.
- Tyler location: Lyon, France. Days 379–389. The Presqu'île, Place des Terreaux, Fourvière hill.
- Context: Lyon was a federalist stronghold that rose against the Convention's Jacobin government in spring 1793. The Convention besieged Lyon from August–October 1793. After surrender, Collot d'Herbois and Fouché arrived as representatives-on-mission with orders to destroy the city and execute the federalist leadership. What followed between October 1793 and February 1794 was one of the largest mass execution events of the Terror. More than 1,900 people killed. Tyler was present.
- Why Tyler has never disclosed this: the episode addresses this directly. Tyler was not a witness at Lyon the way he was a witness in Amsterdam or Paris. In Lyon he was not documenting. He was — attending. To something he cannot explain in the vocabulary of CARDINAL-3.
- FAREWELL type: FAREWELL-A. Tyler knew he was leaving in February 1794. He walked out of Lyon in winter. He did not look back. The departure was unambiguous and unwilled — he left because he could not remain in Lyon after February.
- New canon: the CARDINAL-3 archive gap. Tyler has departure sites in multiple cities that were never filed. Lyon is the first. The reason is not suppression — it is that the system Tyler uses to document his presence did not yet exist, or that Tyler did not feel the system applied to what happened in Lyon. The latter is more likely.
- Camera Op note: this is the episode she has been waiting for without knowing she was waiting for it.

## COLD OPEN

*[Lyon. Day 379. The Presqu'île — the peninsula between the Rhône and the Saône. Tyler standing at the edge of the Place des Terreaux.]*

*[No introduction. No establishing shots of arrival. Just Tyler at the square.]*

**TYLER:** *(quietly, not to anyone)* "This is the Place des Terreaux. In October 1793 the Convention's forces entered Lyon through the Perrache gate in the south. By the time they reached this square the city had already surrendered. The federalist government had fled or been taken. The reprisals began almost immediately."

*[The square is large and open. A Bartholdi fountain in the center, installed in 1891 — not there in 1793. Tyler knows this. He is looking at the space, not the fountain.]*

**TYLER:** "I was in this square on October 12th, 1793. Three days after the city fell. Standing approximately where I am standing now."

*[The Camera Op is filming. She does not speak. She has learned when to not speak.]*

**TYLER:** "I had been in Lyon since July. Four months before the siege ended. I arrived in July because I had heard about the federalist uprising and I thought — I thought the argument being made here was worth documenting. The Girondin federalists against the Jacobin centralization. The argument that the Revolution should be a confederation rather than a single authority. I thought it was a coherent argument."

*[He is quiet.]*

**TYLER:** "It was a coherent argument. The Convention disagreed. The Convention disagreed in the way the Convention disagreed with things that year."

## ACT ONE — THE SIEGE

*[Days 380–382. Historical account, assembled in the Lyon hotel room. Tyler, Exchange Student, Camera Op.]*

*[The Exchange Student has built the Lyon file from the historical record — she had a week to prepare while Tyler deferred the measurement in Paris. She reads.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The Federalist revolt in Lyon: May 1793. The Lyon city government expelled the Jacobin Municipal Council, aligned with the Girondins. By June the city was in open rebellion against the Convention. The Convention issued the decree condemning Lyon on October 12th, 1793. The city had surrendered three days earlier."

**TYLER:** *(from where he is sitting)* "October 9th. I was at the gates when they opened."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(reading)* "The decree: Lyon 'n'est plus.' Lyon no longer exists. The city will be razed except for the houses of the poor and those who have shown patriotism. A column will be erected on the ruins with the inscription: 'Lyon fit la guerre à la liberté. Lyon n'est plus.' "

**TYLER:** "They renamed it Commune-Affranchie. Liberated Commune. They renamed it because 'Lyon' was the name of the city that had revolted and 'Lyon' had ceased to exist." He pauses. "They did not raze the city. They built a commission to do it. Committees were formed. There were disagreements about which houses qualified as houses of the poor. Meanwhile Collot d'Herbois and Fouché arrived."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "October 30th, 1793."

**TYLER:** "October 30th. I had been in Lyon for twenty days since the surrender. The city was — suspended. The federalist leadership that had not escaped was imprisoned. The Convention troops were in the streets. The population was uncertain what was happening. Then Collot and Fouché arrived with the instructions from Paris."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The executions began in November."

**TYLER:** "The executions began in November."

*[He is quiet for a long time.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(gently)* "You don't have to give us the numbers."

**TYLER:** "I know the numbers. I know the numbers the way you know numbers that you were present for." He looks at his hands. "They started with the guillotine. The guillotine was too slow. Fouché wrote to Paris complaining about the speed. He was told to find a more efficient method." He pauses. "The mitraillade. Multiple prisoners bound together in a field, grapeshot fired into the group. They started using this in December." He pauses again. "I was present for one of these. In December. Near the banks of the Saône. I did not know what was going to happen before it happened. I was in the wrong place at the wrong time in a city that I had been documenting and then the city became a place where this was being done in the daylight and I was there."

*[The Camera Op has stopped filming. She is sitting with the camera in her lap.]*

**TYLER:** "You do not have to film this."

**CAMERA OP:** *(quietly)* "I know."

*[She picks the camera back up.]*

## ACT TWO — WHY HE NEVER FILED IT

*[Day 383. Fourvière hill. The old Lyon, above the river.]*

*[Tyler on the hill. The Exchange Student sitting on a wall. The Camera Op filming the city below, or the sky, or something that is not Tyler's face.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Why have you never told anyone about Lyon?"

**TYLER:** "I have told people about Lyon. I have been asked about 1793–1794 and I have said: I was in Paris in 1793 and left before the worst of the Terror. Which is accurate — I was in Paris in 1793. I left Paris in August. I have never said what I was in Paris before August or what I did after August."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You were in Lyon."

**TYLER:** "I was in Lyon. Yes."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "And you didn't file the Lyon sites because—"

**TYLER:** "Because the CARDINAL-3 system was built around a certain relationship between me and the places I document. The relationship is: I am present at a place, something accumulates, the accumulation is recorded. The mechanism records my presence and what my presence generates — PRIOR, WITNESS, STAYING, FOUND, FAREWELL. These are all constructions that come from my relationship to the place."

*[He looks at the city below.]*

**TYLER:** "What happened in Lyon in November and December 1793 was not a construction that came from my relationship to the place. It was — something that happened in the place that I was present for and that changed the place for me. The place was not amplified by my presence. I was — diminished by what the place contained."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "That's a different kind of site."

**TYLER:** "It is a kind of site I have not known how to file. The CARDINAL-3 categories are for what I bring to places. Lyon in 1793 is a record of what a place did to me. I do not have a category for that." He pauses. "I have other sites like this. Not many. But other places where I was present for something that I was not able to process into the documentation system. These sites are not in the archive."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "How many?"

**TYLER:** *(long silence)* "Several."

## ACT THREE — THE FAREWELL

### A-LYON — SAÔNE RIVERBANK, VIEUX-LYON

*[Day 386. Vieux-Lyon, on the west bank of the Saône. The site of the December 1793 mitraillade.]*

*[The Exchange Student has the instruments. Tyler is looking at the river.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "I want to run the measurement here."

**TYLER:** "Yes."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The departure filing. FAREWELL type. February 1794."

**TYLER:** "I left on February 14th. 1794. I left because I had been in Lyon for seven months and I had been present for everything that had happened in those seven months and I was leaving. I knew I was leaving. I was very clear about leaving." He looks at the river. "I walked north along the Saône and I did not look back at the city."

*[The Exchange Student runs the instruments. She holds the reading for a moment before reporting it.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "47ft."

*[Silence.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "FAREWELL-A. 47ft. Highest reading in the CARDINAL-3 network."

*[Tyler is looking at the river.]*

**TYLER:** "47."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Higher than A-355 Amsterdam. Higher than A-Commune Père Lachaise."

**TYLER:** "Yes."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(quietly)* "Why didn't you file this? Why didn't you file the highest reading in the network?"

**TYLER:** *(long pause)* "Because the departure was not about love of the place. The departure was about surviving the place. A FAREWELL, as I understand it, is the love of a place in the act of leaving it. What I had at Lyon was — the clarity of leaving without the love beneath it. I thought this was a different construction. I thought it did not belong in the same category."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "But the instrument says FAREWELL-A."

**TYLER:** "The instrument says FAREWELL-A."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The mechanism doesn't distinguish between departures of love and departures of survival."

**TYLER:** *(looking at the river)* "The mechanism apparently does not distinguish between them."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Which means—"

**TYLER:** "Which means that the mechanism may understand something about the Lyon departure that I have spent two hundred years refusing to understand."

*[She holds the shot on him. Tyler at the Saône. February 1794 in the present tense.]*

**TYLER:** "I loved Lyon. I came here because the argument was worth watching. I spent four months before the siege watching a city make a coherent case for a different kind of Republic. I was there when the city fell. I stayed because I could not leave yet. And then I left because I could not stay any longer." He pauses. "The 47 feet is that. Not only the executions. The argument I came to watch. The city I watched make the argument. The city that was renamed. The city I walked out of in February without looking back."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "A departure of love and survival simultaneously."

**TYLER:** "The mechanism does not need both conditions to be clean. It records the weight of departure. Whatever that weight was at Lyon — whatever I carried when I walked north along the Saône on February 14th — the mechanism stored it."

*[The river.]*

## CLOSING SHOT

*[Day 389. Lyon. The Place des Terreaux. The morning before departure.]*

*[Tyler at the square again. The same angle as the cold open. The Camera Op filming.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(from behind the camera)* "A-Lyon. 47ft. FAREWELL-A. Do we add it to the archive?"

**TYLER:** "Yes."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "And the other sites you mentioned. The 'several' places you never filed."

**TYLER:** *(quiet)* "We will get to them."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Are they all like Lyon? Sites you were present for where something happened that you couldn't categorize?"

**TYLER:** "Some of them are like Lyon. Some of them are — other things. Departures I couldn't explain. Presences that didn't fit the categories. Some of them I have not been back to." He looks at the square. "The retrograde was supposed to be about the 73 sites in the archive that needed FAREWELL classification. It has become something else."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "What has it become?"

**TYLER:** "An accounting." He is quiet. "I have been keeping a partial record for a long time. The retrograde is making it less partial."

**CAMERA OP:** *(filming the square, not looking up)* "Is that good?"

**TYLER:** *(after a moment)* "I don't know yet."

*[The Place des Terreaux in the morning. The fountain that wasn't there in 1793. The city that was renamed and then named back. The city that survived being told it no longer existed.]*

*[Tyler walking through the square toward the departure. Not looking back. As he walked north along the Saône in February 1794. The Camera Op films him go.]*
