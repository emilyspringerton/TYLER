## COLD OPEN

The Exchange Student is at her corner table before anyone else arrives. She has the CEDAR-4 file open beside her laptop. She has been reading it since six AM.

The file is a manila folder with a 1993 government index stamp: **CEDAR-4 | SPRING-2 CONSULTATION — MATERIAL DEPENDENCY ANALYSIS | CLASSIFIED HOLD**. The photograph clipped to the inside cover is of a woman in her mid-forties with a logistician's posture. The name on the photograph: MARGARET SPRINGERTON.

The Exchange Student has read this twice already. She's reading it a third time.

She is not reading it as history.

Title card: **DAY 79**

The Camera Op comes in, sets up at threshold. Bacon starts coffee. The Exchange Student doesn't look up.

Title card.

**TYLER**
*"The CEDAR Function"*

## EPISODE STRUCTURE

**Day 79, morning — the corner table**

Emily arrives at 8 AM. She goes directly to the Exchange Student's table, not to her usual seat.

**EMILY:** "You're here early."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "I've been reading this." She taps the CEDAR-4 file. "Can I ask you something about your mother?"

Emily sits down.

**EMILY:** "You can ask."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The file describes Margaret Springerton as a supply chain consultant engaged for a classified infrastructure project from 1989 to 1993. Her function is listed as 'material dependency mapping for SPRING-4 construction phase.' The project is redacted except for the function header." She turns a page. "I've been doing something. Since I arrived here two days ago. I've been mapping what Tyler needs to build the Witness Room. Materials, frequencies, people. A dependency map."

She turns the laptop to Emily. The spreadsheet has grown: categories, sources, lead times, substitutions, contingencies.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Your mother did this exact thing. For the same project. I don't mean something similar. I mean the same function. Material dependency mapping for SPRING-4 construction phase. Whatever SPRING-4 is, it was under construction in 1993 and she was tracking what it needed to be built."

Emily is very still.

**EMILY:** "Margaret died in 1994."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "I know. It's in the file. Post-project closure." She pauses. "There's a note at the end from whoever ran CEDAR-4. One line. It says: 'Function transfer required. Successor not identified at time of closure.'"

The Camera Op, at threshold, has zoomed in slightly on the file.

---

**Day 79, mid-morning — the file**

Tyler comes out of the room behind the table at nine. He stops when he sees Emily and the Exchange Student at the corner table with the open CEDAR-4 file between them. He reads the room in the way he reads rooms — once, completely.

He sits down at the main table. He doesn't interrupt.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(continuing as if he wasn't there, then realizing he is)* "You knew."

**TYLER:** "I knew there was a file. I didn't know if you'd find it significant."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You left it for me."

**TYLER:** "Bacon found it in the building records. The building has a lot of records. I asked him to put it where you'd see it."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Why?"

**TYLER:** "Because I needed to know if you'd recognize yourself in it."

The Exchange Student looks at the file. She looks at her laptop.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You're saying I'm the successor."

**TYLER:** "I'm not saying anything. I'm watching you figure out whether that's true."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "I've been doing this function since I walked in here. I didn't decide to. I just — saw what you were building and started mapping what it would need. That's not a job. That's a reflex."

**TYLER:** "Margaret Springerton had the same reflex. The file documents it from the first day of her engagement. She didn't start with a scope of work. She just started mapping."

The Exchange Student is quiet for a long time.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "She never knew what she was mapping it for."

**TYLER:** "She knew it mattered. That's usually enough."

---

**Day 79, late morning — Emily's notebook**

Emily has her notebook open. She writes slowly, precisely, the way she writes when something has moved from hypothesis into record.

She writes: *CEDAR-4 successor: confirmed — Exchange Student. Function: material dependency mapping for SPRING-4 construction phase. Continuous from Day 77.*

She adds a node to the map: **CEDAR-4 SUCCESSOR (Exchange Student) — function active, witness status TBD**.

**EMILY:** *(to the Exchange Student)* "I want to tell you something. SPRING-4 is not a project. It's not a code name for a building or a program. It's—" she stops. "I'm still working out how to describe it accurately."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You don't have to describe it right now."

**EMILY:** "I know. But you're tracking its material dependencies. You should know what it is when I have the language."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "What do you know for certain?"

**EMILY:** "I know it was under construction in 1993. I know my mother was part of that construction phase. I know the construction continued. I know Tyler is part of the current phase." She looks at the map. "I know I'm part of it. I've been part of it longer than I knew."

The Exchange Student looks at the dependency map on her laptop. At the map in Emily's notebook.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Two maps. Same project. Different scales."

**EMILY:** "Yes."

---

**Day 79, afternoon — the room**

Tyler has been in and out of the room behind the table all morning. At 2 PM he comes to the Exchange Student's table.

**TYLER:** "I want to show you something."

The Exchange Student looks at the door. She's been looking at it for two days.

**TYLER:** "You don't have to. But I think you should see it."

She closes her laptop. She stands.

The Camera Op adjusts position — not to follow, but to hold the threshold in frame. This is the shot that has been building since Day 77: the doorway, the decision.

The Exchange Student walks to the door.

Tyler opens it.

---

**What she sees:**

Light. Not unusual light — the ordinary light of a room with windows — but light that sits differently. The room is small: a table pushed to one side, one chair, a window facing north. A piece of paper on the table. The Subscriber's notation.

The Exchange Student stands in the doorway.

She does not step in immediately. She reads the room the way logisticians read spaces: what's load-bearing, what's contingent, what's the function.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "It's already running."

**TYLER:** "Yes."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The frequencies."

**TYLER:** "Stolas has been present in this building since 1623. The room focuses it. The notation tells it what to do." He pauses. "You can feel it because you resonate with it."

The Exchange Student steps across the threshold.

She stands in the room for a long moment. She's not analyzing now. She's just in it.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(quietly)* "Day 49."

**TYLER:** "You know about Day 49?"

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "I read every document in this building. Bacon keeps good records." She turns to look at him from inside the room. "This is where it happened."

**TYLER:** "This is where it's still happening. That's the construction. The event doesn't end. It's permanently in the air in here."

She stands there for another moment. Then she steps back out.

---

**Day 79, late afternoon — witness count**

The Exchange Student comes back to the main table. Emily is updating the map. The Camera Op is at threshold. Bacon has cleared the afternoon.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Your resonant witness supply chain. The minimum three." She sits. "I'm the fourth."

Emily writes it in the notebook without looking up: *resonant witness #4 — Exchange Student. Confirmed Day 79.*

She adds the node to the map.

The map now has 47 nodes. The CEDAR function node and the resonant witness node are adjacent. Emily draws a line between them.

**EMILY:** "Your mother tracked supply chain. You're part of the supply chain." She looks at the map. "That's not irony. That's architecture."

The Exchange Student looks at the line Emily just drew.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "I came here because of the CEDAR-4 file. I didn't know why I was coming. I thought I was investigating something historical."

**EMILY:** "You were. It just turned out to be current."
