# S08E10: "Avignon"

**Build 0092** | Season 8, Episode 10 | CARDINAL-3 Documentary Series

## PRODUCTION NOTES

- Episode type: The oldest measurement. Pre-archive site. The Black Death.
- Tyler location: Avignon, France. Days 408–417. The Palais des Papes, the Rocher des Doms, the old city.
- Historical context: January 1348 through July 1349. The Black Death reaches Avignon on approximately January 25, 1348. Pope Clement VI is in residence at the Avignon Papacy. The city has approximately 50,000 inhabitants. By the end of the epidemic, roughly half are dead. Clement VI issues two papal bulls against the flagellants and one against persecutors of Jews (who were being blamed for the plague). The Pope stays in Avignon. Tyler stays in Avignon.
- What Tyler was doing in Avignon: he had arrived in Marseille in October 1347, witnessed the arrival of the Genoese ships carrying the plague, left Marseille in November, walked north, arrived in Avignon in January 1348 as the plague was reaching the city. He did not flee. He is not sure why he did not flee.
- The measurement: the instruments find something at Avignon. Not the profile they expected. A-Avignon is not REFUGE (even density). It is not STAYING (attention peaks). It is a construction type the Exchange Student has never seen: WITNESS-LONG. Extended witnessing at a catastrophic event over a multi-year duration. Different from WITNESS (brief, present-for-a-specific-event) — this is 18 months of witnessing without departure. The construction is enormous and old and the density has been building for 676 years.
- The reading: 89ft. A new network maximum, exceeding A-Lyon (47ft) by nearly double. But the Exchange Student's note: the 89ft is the current reading after 676 years of passive accumulation. The 1349 reading may have been smaller. Or larger. They cannot separate the original construction from the accumulation.
- Tyler on why he stayed: "I have watched arguments. I have watched revolutions. I have watched battles. I had not watched a city die from a disease. I did not know what to do with the information. I stayed because the information was still arriving and I could not leave while the information was still arriving."
- Tyler on not getting sick: "I don't get sick. I have never had a fever. I have not had a disease since — I don't know when. This is part of what I am. I was in Avignon while half the population died and I did not get sick and this was noticed."
- The departure (July 1349): Tyler does not know if he was present at the departure. The reading suggests FAREWELL-B — small, 15ft. He left Avignon when the epidemic was subsiding. He does not remember the departure clearly.
- New understanding from Avignon: the mechanism records witnessing differently depending on duration. A WITNESS construction from a brief event (hours, days) is one thing. 18 months of continuous catastrophic witnessing is WITNESS-LONG — fundamentally different density profile.

## COLD OPEN

*[Avignon. Day 408. The Palais des Papes — the vast Gothic palace of the Avignon Papacy, center of the city. Tyler looking at it.]*

*[The Camera Op filming. No one has spoken yet.]*

**TYLER:** "The Palais des Papes was under construction in 1348. The old palace was nearly complete. The new palace had been started by Clement VI in 1342. When I arrived in January 1348, the new wing was being built. Construction stopped during the epidemic. They didn't resume until 1350."

**CAMERA OP:** "You watched the construction stop."

**TYLER:** "I watched the construction stop, yes. I watched — the workers who were building the palace get sick and die. Some of the workers who were building the palace. Others survived. There's no pattern to who survived and who died in the Black Death. The plague's selection criteria are — if there are selection criteria, we didn't understand them in 1348 and we don't have complete understanding now."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Why did you stay?"

*[He looks at her.]*

**TYLER:** "I have thought about this for six hundred and seventy-six years and I still don't have a satisfactory answer."

## ACT ONE — WHY TYLER STAYED

*[Day 409. The Rocher des Doms — the rocky hill above the old city, with a view over the Rhône and the surrounding countryside.]*

**TYLER:** "I had watched the plague arrive in Marseille. October 1347. The Genoese merchant ships from the Black Sea. The sailors were sick. Some were dead. The disease was visible — the swellings, the fever, the speed. I had never seen anything move through a population at that speed." He looks at the Rhône. "I left Marseille in November 1347 because the city was beginning to understand what had arrived and the panic was starting and I could not stay in a city while the panic started. Panic is not useful information."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "But you came north to Avignon."

**TYLER:** "I came north to Avignon because the plague was also coming north to Avignon and I thought: if I watch the plague arrive in Avignon, I will understand something I did not understand in Marseille. Marseille was the arrival of the thing. Avignon would be the thing itself — a large city, the papal city, one of the most significant cities in Europe in 1348. If the plague hit Avignon—"

**CAMERA OP:** "What would you understand?"

**TYLER:** *(quiet)* "I didn't know. I thought I would know when I was watching it." He is quiet. "I was wrong about that."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You stayed for eighteen months."

**TYLER:** "I stayed for eighteen months because the information was still arriving and I couldn't make myself leave while the information was still arriving. This is — a version of what happens to me at events I witness. I cannot leave until the event is resolved. At arguments and battles the resolution comes in days or weeks. The Black Death in Avignon took eighteen months." He pauses. "I watched — I watched the city process the death of half its people. Not just the dying. The processing. The bodies in the streets at first, then the coordination for removing bodies, then the burial grounds overflowing, then the mass graves. The city's administrative response to more dead than the infrastructure could handle. The Pope issuing the bull from inside the palace — he wouldn't leave. He stayed in his chambers with fires burning to purify the air. He blessed the Rhône so that corpses could be dropped into it directly, because the burial grounds were full."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(quietly)* "You watched all of this."

**TYLER:** "I watched all of this. I walked the city every day. I was not sick. I was — I was there."

## ACT TWO — THE MEASUREMENT

### A-AVIGNON — PLACE DU PALAIS, AVIGNON

*[Day 412. Place du Palais — the large square in front of the Palais des Papes.]*

*[Exchange Student with the instruments. Tyler and Camera Op nearby. The Gothic palace filling half the sky.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(preparing the instruments)* "I want to run this. I don't know what we'll find. Strasbourg was 1848 — 176 years of accumulation. Avignon is 1348 — 676 years. If the mechanism recorded passively, there has been 676 years of whatever it recorded accumulating here."

**TYLER:** "Or it decayed. The constructions may decay without return visits."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Amsterdam didn't decay. A-340 had been accumulating since 1628 — 396 years — and we found it at full strength. Strasbourg was readable at 176 years."

**TYLER:** "Amsterdam was a STAYING construction. Active accumulation. I returned to Amsterdam. Strasbourg I never returned to."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Strasbourg was still readable."

**TYLER:** "Yes. Run it."

*[She runs the baseline.]*

*[She runs it again.]*

*[The instruments do something they have not done before. The Exchange Student looks at the reading for a long time.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "There's something here." *(long pause)* "There's something here that is — very large."

**TYLER:** "How large?"

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The reading is 89ft." *(pause)* "But the profile is — I have never seen this profile. It's not FAREWELL. It's not STAYING. It's not REFUGE. It's not PRIOR." She adjusts the instruments. "The density is distributed — not evenly like Strasbourg. Not peaked at a single event. It's — it looks like a WITNESS construction, but the scale is wrong. WITNESS constructions are brief. This is — dense and broad at the same time. High density across the entire measurement zone. 18 months of measurement zone."

**TYLER:** "WITNESS-LONG."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(writing)* "WITNESS-LONG. Extended catastrophic witnessing. Not the brief WITNESS construction from a single event — 18 months of continuous witnessing without departure, during a catastrophic event. The mechanism appears to record witnessing differently at this duration." She looks at the reading. "89ft. Possibly more — the instruments are having difficulty with the depth. This is very old. The construction has been here since 1349. It may have been accumulating."

**TYLER:** "Or the accumulation is the 676 years."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "There's no way to separate the original construction from the accumulation. We can't know what the 1349 reading was."

*[Tyler is looking at the palace.]*

**TYLER:** "89 feet."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "89ft. New network maximum."

**TYLER:** *(slowly)* "Lyon was 47. Amsterdam A-355 was 30-plus. A-Commune was 34."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Avignon is 89ft. Two times the previous maximum."

*[Very long silence.]*

**CAMERA OP:** *(filming the palace)* "Six hundred and seventy-six years."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "676 years of a WITNESS-LONG construction sitting in this square."

**TYLER:** "I watched this city process the death of half its population. For eighteen months. I was here every day. The mechanism recorded — that." He pauses. "The mechanism recorded what I was present for. And I was present for all of it."

## ACT THREE — THE DEPARTURE

### A-AVIGNON DEPARTURE — JULY 1349

*[Day 415. Avignon. The old city gate on the north side.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The departure. July 1349. Do you remember leaving?"

**TYLER:** "I remember — the city had quieted. Not normal. But quieter. The dying had slowed. The population was what it was and the plague was withdrawing. I remember that the construction on the Palais had resumed — I could hear the stonecutters again, which I had not heard for over a year." He looks at the gate. "I remember having a bag. I remember walking out through a gate — I don't know if it was this gate. And then I was north of Avignon on a road and I didn't know where I was going."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The same as 1848 Paris."

**TYLER:** *(slowly)* "Yes."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You were absent from your own departure."

**TYLER:** "I was absent from the Avignon departure. I had spent eighteen months watching the city die. By July 1349 there was — not very much of me left to make a departure. I walked out." He looks at the gate. "I don't know when I became present again."

*[The Exchange Student runs the departure measurement at the gate.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "A-Avignon departure. July 1349." *(reading)* "15ft. FAREWELL-B."

**TYLER:** "15."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "FAREWELL-B. Unconscious departure. Consistent with the 1848 reading — the departure follows an extended catastrophic witness event and the subject is absent from the act of leaving." She looks at her notes. "Pattern confirmed: extended catastrophic witnessing → FAREWELL-B departure. The 18 months of witnessing leaves the subject unable to be present at the departure."

**TYLER:** "What I gave Avignon was 89 feet of presence. What I took with me was a 15-foot departure." He is looking at the gate. "The city kept the 89 feet. I left with the 15."

**CAMERA OP:** *(quietly, filming the gate)* "The city kept what you gave it."

**TYLER:** "The city kept what I gave it. Yes."

## CLOSING SHOT

*[Day 417. Avignon. The Rocher des Doms, above the city. The Rhône below. The Palais des Papes visible. The Pont Saint-Bénézet — the famous broken bridge — crossing the Rhône.]*

*[Tyler at the edge of the hill. The city below him. The river.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "89ft at the palace square. 15ft departure at the north gate. A-Avignon total: approximately 104ft across two sites."

**TYLER:** *(looking at the city)* "The bridge."

*[The Pont Saint-Bénézet. Built in the 12th century. By the 17th century, most of it had washed away. Four arches remain, extending from the Avignon bank into the Rhône and stopping in the middle of the river.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The bridge was complete in 1348."

**TYLER:** "The bridge was complete in 1348. I crossed the bridge many times. I crossed it going into the lower city along the river. I crossed it coming back." He looks at the four arches. "By the 17th century it had mostly collapsed. Four arches left." He is quiet. "I crossed a bridge that no longer exists in its complete form. The bridge I crossed is not the bridge that's there now."

**CAMERA OP:** "Does that bother you?"

**TYLER:** "No. The four arches are the same four arches. The rest is gone, but those four are the same stone." He looks at the Rhône. "Things change. Partly. The parts that don't change are still the parts that were there."

*[The broken bridge. The river. The palace. The city that has been holding 89 feet of Tyler's witnessing for 676 years.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Where next?"

**TYLER:** "The monastery in Burgundy. Where I went after Avignon." He looks at the city one more time. "I was in a monastery in Burgundy for two years after I left here. I don't remember the monastery's name. But I remember the location. I want to go there." He pauses. "I want to find out if two years of — being very still, after Avignon — generated a construction."

**CAMERA OP:** "Another REFUGE?"

**TYLER:** "Another REFUGE, possibly. Or something else. Something between REFUGE and WITNESS-LONG. Being still after catastrophe is not the same as recovering from a departure. It might be its own category." He picks up his bag. "I want to find out."

*[Tyler walking down from the Rocher des Doms. The Camera Op filming the broken bridge.]*

*[The four arches in the Rhône. Extending from the bank into the water and stopping. The rest of the bridge gone. What remains is still the same stone.]*
