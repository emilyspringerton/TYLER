## EPISODE STRUCTURE

**Day 191, morning — the methodology problem**

Emily and the Exchange Student are at the table. Tyler has been at it since 6 AM.

**EMILY:** "What are you doing?"

**TYLER:** "Deciding which photographs are constructions."

**EMILY:** "All of them?"

**TYLER:** "I don't know yet." He looks at the spread. "Seven remaining. They're not all the same kind of thing. I took them all between 1950 and 1967, and I knew when I took them that I was taking them for a reason. But I didn't always know what the reason was."

He picks up photograph 3 — a corner of a building, 1961, with a partial street sign.

**TYLER:** "This one I took because the light was right. That's not a construction."

He puts it down.

**TYLER:** "This one—" Photograph 4, a door on what might be a residential block, 1955. "—I took because something was present. I could feel the weight of an event. I didn't stop to understand what it was. I just documented it."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You took some of these by instinct and some with purpose."

**TYLER:** "Yes. And now I need to sort them."

---

**Day 191, midday — the sort**

They work through the seven remaining photographs together. Tyler describes what he remembers about each one. The Exchange Student takes notes. Emily marks the map.

**Photograph 3** — corner building, 1961, Gratiot Avenue area. Tyler: *"Light was right. Beautiful building, specific moment of day. I don't feel a weight here."* Emily: *not a construction candidate.* Exchange Student: *marks "aesthetic documentation."*

**Photograph 4** — door, residential block, 1955. Tyler: *"Something is present. I remember stopping. I remember the coin was heavy in my pocket."* Emily: *candidate.* Exchange Student: *marks "event-present, unidentified."*

**Photograph 5** — Conant Street, factory gate, 1963. Tyler: *"Conant Street. I annotated this one myself — 'something started and stopped deliberately.' That's the same category as Hastings, but different. Hastings was interrupted by external force. Conant Street — whatever ended here chose to end. A deliberate closure."* Emily: *candidate, different construction type.* Exchange Student: *marks "deliberate ending — requires research."*

**Photograph 6** — bridge over the river, 1964, not the Ambassador. Tyler: *"Eastern Market side. There used to be a crossing point here before the current infrastructure. I think I photographed the old crossing geography. The bridge in the photograph is infrastructure — I was photographing through it, same as Hastings."* Emily: *candidate, passive construction possible.* Exchange Student: *marks "geography under infrastructure."*

**Photograph 7** — empty lot, 1958, weeds and a chain-link fence. Tyler: *"I have no strong memory of this one. I don't feel a weight. I took it because I was on that block and I photograph everything. Not a construction candidate."* Emily: *not a candidate.* Exchange Student: *marks "incidental documentation."*

**Photograph 8** — a storefront window, 1960, reflection of the street in the glass. Tyler: *"I photographed this window because I could see two things at once — the inside and the reflection of the outside, simultaneously. I wanted to document the doubling. Not an event-present photograph. A technique photograph."* Emily: *not a candidate.* Exchange Student: *marks "technique/formal document."*

**Photograph 9** — alley behind a church, 1957. Tyler: *"This one is heavy. Heavier than any of the others. I remember standing in this alley for forty-five minutes. I didn't know why. I left without fully understanding what I'd documented."* Emily: *strong candidate.* Exchange Student: *marks "event-present, strong — requires priority research."*

**Day 191, afternoon — the results**

Emily lays it out on the map.

**EMILY:** "Four candidates: photographs 4, 5, 6, and 9. Three eliminations: 3, 7, 8. We're down to four active construction possibilities."

**TYLER:** "Plus the two active constructions. Plus Bacon's building and Ward C complete." He looks at the map. "Eight constructions total when it's done. If all four candidates confirm."

**EMILY:** "That's a network."

**TYLER:** "Yes."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "What does a network of eight do that four separate constructions don't?"

Silence. Tyler doesn't answer immediately. He picks up the map and looks at it from above — the positions of Bacon's building, Ward C, the Ambassador Bridge crossing, Hastings/Vernor, and now four candidate sites marked in pencil.

**TYLER:** "I don't know yet. I know that the CEDAR function produces a network — it doesn't produce isolated events. I know that whatever I'm building here is related to what Emily built, and to what the Subscriber initiates, and to what the network of witnesses sustains." He sets the map down. "The answer to what eight constructions does — I think that's a Season 6 question."

**EMILY:** "Season 6."

**TYLER:** "We haven't finished Season 5. Let me finish the season."

She writes it down: *Season 6 question: what does the complete network do?*

---

**Day 191, late afternoon — photograph 9, identified**

The Exchange Student has been on her laptop since the sort ended.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Photograph 9. Alley behind a church, 1957. I found the address." She turns the screen. "Bethel African Methodist Episcopal Church, Saint Antoine Street. The alley behind the sanctuary. In 1957 — this is the year of the Detroit NAACP chapter reorganization following the 1955 internal dispute. Bethel AME was a key meeting site. The alley was used for overflow, for conversations that needed to happen outside the formal meeting structure."

**TYLER:** "Outside the formal structure."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The decisions about what the chapter would become were made in that alley. Not inside. The inside was formal — minutes, votes, proceedings. The alley was where people said what they actually meant."

Tyler looks at photograph 9 for a long time.

**TYLER:** "A room where consequential decisions were made informally, without record, without documentation of any kind. The decisions shaped a decade of civil rights organizing in this city. And they happened in an alley in 1957 and nobody wrote them down."

**EMILY:** "That's the event."

**TYLER:** "That's the event." He pauses. "This is the heaviest one. I stood in that alley for forty-five minutes in 1957. I'm going to go back to Saint Antoine Street."

Emily marks photograph 9: *Bethel AME alley, Saint Antoine Street — strong candidate, event identified.*
