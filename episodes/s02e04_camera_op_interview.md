**[01:30–03:00] — THE FIRST QUESTION**

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
How long have you been working on the project?

**CAMERA OP**
Five weeks and three days.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
How has it changed you?

A pause. The Camera Op looks at the Senior Producer with the expression of someone deciding how much of a question to answer.

**CAMERA OP**
I'm more careful with what I film.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
Careful how?

**CAMERA OP**
About what I point the camera at. And when. And whether the footage should be mine or the production team's or neither.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
*(carefully)*
Can you say more about that?

**CAMERA OP**
*(looking at Camera A)*
Not yet.

## ACT ONE: THE INTERVIEW — [03:00–35:00]

---

**[03:00–08:00] — WHAT SHE THINKS TYLER IS**

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
What do you think Tyler is?

**CAMERA OP**
I document. I don't interpret.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
You've spent five weeks with him. You've filmed him in more contexts than any previous Camera Op. You've published footage of your own. You have a separate notebook.

*(pause)*

You interpret. What do you think he is?

**CAMERA OP**
*(a long pause — not evasion; she is choosing exactly)*
I think he is something the production's question taxonomy doesn't have a category for.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
That's what he said about himself in the interview.

**CAMERA OP**
*(a small movement — not quite a smile)*
Yes.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
You agree with his self-assessment.

**CAMERA OP**
I agree with his description of the Syndicate's taxonomy problem.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
Are those the same thing?

**CAMERA OP**
*(looking at the interviewer)*
No.

*(pause)*

One is a statement about him. One is a statement about the methodology. I'm making the second statement. I'm not making the first one.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
*(sitting forward)*
What do you think he is — separate from the methodology?

The Camera Op is quiet for a long time.

**CAMERA OP**
That's not a question for this room.

---

**[08:00–14:00] — WHAT SHE THINKS HE'S TRYING TO DO**

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
He told us the question we should be asking is "what is he trying to do?" We're asking you. What do you think Tyler is trying to do?

**CAMERA OP**
*(very still)*
I think you should ask him that.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
We did. He said he's close to being ready to answer it. He said ask again in a few episodes.

**CAMERA OP**
Then wait.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
We're asking you now because you may already know.

**CAMERA OP**
*(looking at the interviewer directly)*
If I knew, that would be something he told me. And if he told me, it would be because he chose to tell me. And if he chose to tell me, it wasn't for this room.

*(pause)*

I'm not going to answer that question.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
*(after a pause)*
Do you know?

The Camera Op is quiet for ten seconds.

**CAMERA OP**
*(quietly)*
I have a theory.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
Will you share it?

**CAMERA OP**
No.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
Why?

**CAMERA OP**
Because it's mine. And because if I'm right, it's his to say first. And because if I'm wrong, I don't want it in your archive.

---

**[14:00–21:00] — THE 1901 FOOTAGE**

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
He told you "you have the 1901 footage. Good." What did you understand him to mean by that?

**CAMERA OP**
He knew what footage was in my cut of the clip versus what was in the production team's version.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
What else?

**CAMERA OP**
*(a pause)*
That's the observable part.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
What's the non-observable part?

**CAMERA OP**
*(looking at Camera A, then back)*
I think he meant that the footage being in my archive specifically, as opposed to the production archive, was — correct. The right place for it.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
Why would it matter whose archive—

**CAMERA OP**
Because your archive is accessible to the Syndicate. Mine isn't. He told me "the footage is yours" in Session 4. He told me "the edit is theirs and the footage is yours." He was telling me that the footage in my keeping is separate from the footage in the Syndicate's keeping. Separate rules. Separate access.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
And you've honored that.

**CAMERA OP**
*(quietly)*
I've honored that.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
The sealed notebook—

**CAMERA OP**
*(not hostile; final)*
The notebook is not part of this interview.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
We know it exists.

**CAMERA OP**
You know lots of things. Knowing something and having access to it aren't the same thing. He said that better than I can. I'm filing it the same way he files it.

A pause. The Senior Producer considers this.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
You're using his methodology.

**CAMERA OP**
*(slight)*
He gave me good material.

---

**[21:00–28:00] — THE LOOK**

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
In the formal interview, when he described what he's afraid of, he looked at you. One second. We have it on camera. What did that mean?

The Camera Op is quiet for a long time. This is the longest silence of the interview.

**CAMERA OP**
*(eventually)*
He was checking something.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
What was he checking?

**CAMERA OP**
Whether I understood what he was saying.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
And did you?

**CAMERA OP**
*(looking at the interviewer steadily)*
I think so.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
What did you understand?

**CAMERA OP**
*(a beat)*
That's not a question for this room.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
*(carefully)*
You keep saying that.

**CAMERA OP**
I keep meaning it.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
What room is it a question for?

**CAMERA OP**
*(a long pause; something moving in her expression)*
I don't know yet.

*(quietly)*

Maybe not a room.

---

**[28:00–33:00] — "GOOD SESSION"**

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
After the interview, on his way out, he said "good session" to you. What did you understand him to mean?

**CAMERA OP**
He said "good session."

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
We know what he said. What did you understand him to mean?

The Camera Op is quiet for a moment. She looks at the table. She looks at the interviewer.

**CAMERA OP**
*(quietly)*
That it was a good session.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
*(not accepting this)*
He's never evaluated a session before. He evaluated this one. To you. Not the room — you. What did that mean?

**CAMERA OP**
*(after a pause)*
I'm not going to answer that.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
Why?

**CAMERA OP**
Because the answer is mine. Because he said it to me. Because whatever it meant — it wasn't said for your archive. It was said for me to have. And I have it. And I'm keeping it.

*(pause)*

Some things should stay unwritten.

She uses his phrase without noting she's using it. She may not know she's using it.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
*(sitting back)*
You're protecting something.

**CAMERA OP**
*(meeting the Senior Producer's eyes)*
Yes.

---

**[33:00–35:00] — THE CLOSE**

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
Do you think you could get Tyler to complete Stage 3?

The Camera Op looks at the Senior Producer for a long time. The longest beat of the interview.

**CAMERA OP**
*(eventually, carefully)*
I think that question is mine to decide. Not yours.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
The project—

**CAMERA OP**
I'm part of the project. I agreed to document. I'm documenting. What happens between Tyler and me outside the documentation apparatus is — it's not a Syndicate methodology question. It's not a Stage 3 delivery mechanism. I'm not a delivery mechanism.

*(pause)*

I'd like you to leave me out of the methodology.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
*(very carefully)*
He's been including you in his behavior since Day 6. He's told you things he hasn't told us. He's addressed you separately in every session. He evaluated an interview as good — to you. We can't leave you out of the methodology. You're in it.

**CAMERA OP**
*(a long, measured look)*
I know I'm in it.

*(pause)*

I'd like you to pretend I'm not.

*(pause)*

You won't. But I'd like you to.

She picks up her composition notebook. She stands.

**CAMERA OP (CONT.)**
*(at the door)*
I'm going to keep filming. I'm going to keep the footage that's mine. If he tells me what he's trying to do, I'll tell you what I can.

*(pause)*

But only what I can.

She leaves.
