### ACT ONE — A-293

**[EXTERIOR — GRAY'S INN, HOLBORN. MORNING. DAY 273.]**

*[Gray's Inn Road. The hum is distinct here — the Camera Op will note in Entry 52 that it had a specific quality at this site, differentiated from Wood Street, different from anything in Vienna. The camera is on Tyler's back as he approaches the gate. He stops outside it.]*

Gray's Inn has been a working inn of court since the fourteenth century. The gatehouse stands. The gardens behind it open to the public in the mornings. The quadrangle is original in its geometry — rebuilt several times, not moved.

Tyler stands outside the gate.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(quietly)* "The gate would have been different in 1623. The current gatehouse structure is largely later. But the ground plan is—"

**TYLER:** "The ground plan is the same."

*[He walks through the gate.]*

*[Inside the quadrangle. The hum is slightly louder. The Camera Op moves to follow. The Exchange Student has the tape measure. Tyler stands in the center of the quadrangle, turns once slowly, and stops facing the hall.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Should I measure?"

**TYLER:** "Yes."

*[She begins walking outward from the quadrangle's center. North toward the inner gate. At fifty feet she is still within the enclosure. At one hundred feet she has passed the inner gate. She continues along Gray's Inn Road. One hundred and fifty feet. The road crossing is at one hundred and seventy feet.]*

*[She hesitates. The measurement is asking her to cross the road.]*

*[She crosses.]*

*[At one hundred and eighty-eight feet, she finds the edge. She stands there. The Camera Op is at the quadrangle entrance, filming the Exchange Student through the gate at one hundred and eighty-eight feet, a figure in the morning commuter crowd.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(calling back)* "One hundred and eighty-eight feet."

*[Tyler does not look surprised. He nods once.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(returning, writing)* "One hundred and eighty-eight feet from a single notation. No return visits in the file. One date. One hundred and eighty-eight feet, four hundred and three years later."

*[The Camera Op finds Tyler's face. He is looking at the hall.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "How long were you here?"

**TYLER:** "One afternoon."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "One afternoon."

**TYLER:** "From late morning until dark. I didn't count the hours. I didn't know counting would matter."

She writes.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "One afternoon and it's been running since November 1623. A-291 in Vienna was one hundred and seven mornings — two hundred and ten feet. One afternoon here: one hundred and eighty-eight feet."

**TYLER:** "It wasn't a typical afternoon."

*[A long pause. Morning light across the quadrangle. A barrister in robes passes through without looking at any of them.]*

**CAMERA OP:** "What happened here?"

*[Tyler reaches into his right-hand pocket. He takes out the coin. He flips it — not a high flip, a quick, precise motion. He reads the face. The Camera Op catches the arc and the light on metal but not the face. Tyler's timing is exactly right. It is always exactly right. He puts the coin back.]*

**TYLER:** "I left something here."

*[He is careful. Not evasive. There is a difference and he writes it into every sentence.]*

**TYLER:** "I had been carrying something for a long time. I came here to — to put it somewhere it could be held properly. By someone who was ready for it." He pauses. "I spent the afternoon making sure I had done it right. That it was stable. That what I had handed over was going to hold."

**CAMERA OP:** "Did it?"

*[The smile. Slightly too knowing. The camera can't resolve what it knows.]*

**TYLER:** "It held for four hundred years."

*[Silence. The hum.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Were you aware you were building here?"

**TYLER:** "No. I knew I was here for something important. I knew I was going to be checking and rechecking the — the architecture of what I had done." He looks at the hall. "I was sitting in this quadrangle making sure the load-bearing structure was sound. I was holding the geometry of what I had done in my attention for several hours. I was very careful. I was not thinking about constructions. I was thinking about whether I had gotten it right."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "But you were here for hours. In your specific way."

**TYLER:** "Yes."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Paying attention to the thing you had handed over. Whether it was stable."

**TYLER:** "Apparently the mechanism doesn't distinguish between what you are attending to and whether it builds. It builds from the quality of attention. Not the subject."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You were being very precise for one afternoon."

**TYLER:** "Yes."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "And one hundred and eighty-eight feet reflects that."

**TYLER:** "Apparently."

*[He writes in the leather journal. The Camera Op holds the shot: Tyler writing at the quadrangle, the gate behind him, Gray's Inn Road beyond.]*

### ACT TWO — IN THE QUADRANGLE

**[INTERIOR — GRAY'S INN, QUADRANGLE. LATE MORNING. DAY 273.]**

*[They have stayed longer than planned. Tyler is sitting on the steps in front of the hall. The Camera Op is across the quadrangle. The Exchange Student is cross-referencing the second wave of London sites in the file. The hum is continuous.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The single-event mechanism is different from the others we've categorized. Blue Bird was also a single event — one evening, one quartet set. But Blue Bird took seventy-four years to identify. This one the CARDINAL-3 data found from a single notation in the journal and a Cairngorms cross-reference."

**TYLER:** "The data found it because someone was looking for it. I wasn't looking for it when I wrote it down. I wrote the date because I write dates. Because it was November 14, 1623, and I was here, and that is what I had always done."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You wrote it in the journal before you knew the journal would become part of the analysis."

**TYLER:** "I write because I want there to be a record. Even if I'm the only one who reads it. Even if no one reads it." A pause. "Someone read it."

*[The Exchange Student holds the leather journal carefully. Four hundred years of someone writing things down so there would be a record.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Is this a new category? Single-event?"

**TYLER:** "I don't know. Reims was a single morning. Blue Bird was a single evening. This was a single afternoon. The mechanism may be the same as unintentional construction. The afternoon was different from a music set or a rose window in what it required of me. Not what I was doing. What it required."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "What did it require?"

**TYLER:** "Precision. For several hours. I was holding something very specific in my attention and checking the architecture of it. I had to be right. I couldn't afford to be imprecise about a single node in the dependency chain."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "And the construction reflects that."

**TYLER:** "Apparently. One hundred and eighty-eight feet of me being very careful for one afternoon in 1623."

*[A pause. The hum. Morning light moving across the quadrangle as the sun shifts.]*

**CAMERA OP:** "You said 'most important until Day 49.'"

*[Tyler looks at the camera — direct, brief, one second, exactly two. Not more than two. Two is the taunt. He holds it for exactly two.]*

**TYLER:** "Yes."

**CAMERA OP:** "Day 49 was—"

**TYLER:** "Day 49 was Day 49."

*[The smile. Slightly too knowing. The camera can't resolve it.]*

**CAMERA OP:** "And this afternoon was before that."

**TYLER:** "Everything was before Day 49. That's what Day 49 means."

*[The Exchange Student writes this down. She is no longer asking if she should write things down. She writes things down.]*

### ACT THREE — A-294

**[EXTERIOR — RUSSELL SQUARE, BLOOMSBURY. AFTERNOON. DAY 273.]**

*[Three blocks from the Bloomsbury flat. The Camera Op notes in Entry 52 that Tyler could have walked here any of the three nights they had been in Bloomsbury — the square is visible from the end of the street. He waited until after Gray's Inn. The hum is present, quieter here, distributed differently.]*

Russell Square is a garden square in the center of Bloomsbury. Victorian, large, plane trees planted in the 1840s. Academic and publishing buildings surround it — the world Tyler moved through in the second half of the nineteenth century.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "A-294. Russell Square. The London file has four entries for this location: 1856, 1871, 1888, 1905. The last notation reads: *The plane trees are large now. The square is the same. Seventeen visits. The counting is approximate.*"

**TYLER:** "I lost count sometime in the 1870s. I was in this area regularly for about fifty years. I came through Russell Square because the feeling was here and it was on the way to where I was going."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "On the way."

**TYLER:** "Slightly out of the way, actually. But the feeling made it worth the detour." He looks at the square. "I haven't been here since 1905. That's a hundred and twenty years of running with nothing added."

*[The Exchange Student begins measuring from the center of the square garden outward. The mechanism is quieter here — more distributed, accumulated across decades rather than concentrated in a single afternoon. She walks the perimeter.]*

*[Tyler sits on a bench. Not necessarily the same bench from 1856, but the same position — the geometry he knew. He sits and he is very still.]*

*[At forty-seven feet from the center of the garden, the Exchange Student finds the edge.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Forty-seven feet."

**TYLER:** "Forty-seven feet." He looks at the plane trees. "After a hundred and twenty years with nothing added."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "A-291, Vienna: two hundred and ten feet from one hundred and seven mornings. A-293, Gray's Inn: one hundred and eighty-eight feet from one afternoon. A-294, Russell Square: forty-seven feet from seventeen visits across fifty years."

**TYLER:** "The afternoon at Gray's Inn cost more than the fifty years here. In terms of what the mechanism required of me."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The size reflects the intensity, not the duration."

**TYLER:** "I think so. At Gray's Inn I was not present the way I am present when I walk through a garden square on a Tuesday afternoon. I was holding something very precise. The quality was different."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "And Russell Square was—"

**TYLER:** "Russell Square was good. Tuesday afternoons in a square with plane trees. 1856 to 1905." He pauses. "It was a good fifty years."

*[The Camera Op notes the difference in his register. Gray's Inn: careful, the weight of precision. Russell Square: the word "good," with something like ease in it. Not absence of feeling. Ease.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Still running at forty-seven feet."

**TYLER:** "Still running." He pauses. "A hundred and twenty years with no one adding to it and it's still forty-seven feet. It's been patient."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The mechanism is patient."

**TYLER:** "The mechanism is very patient." He pauses. "I keep being grateful for that. That it doesn't require continuous attention. It holds what was put there. It waits."

*[He is quiet for a moment. The square. The plane trees. The afternoon light.]*

**TYLER:** "I keep not knowing what I was building. At Gray's Inn I was not building. I was checking the architecture of something I had built somewhere else. And the checking built one hundred and eighty-eight feet. Here I was walking through a square because I liked the feeling, and it built forty-seven feet over fifty years." He pauses. "I keep not knowing. And then someone measures it."
