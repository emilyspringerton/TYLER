## COLD OPEN

Day 231. Descent into Charles de Gaulle.

Tyler is at the window seat. He is looking out at the city below — the Seine, the rooftops, the grid. The Camera Op is in the middle seat. She films him looking at it.

He does not say anything for a long time.

The city comes up through the clouds and he watches it arrive.

**CAMERA OP:** "When were you last here?"

Tyler doesn't look away from the window.

**TYLER:** "1943."

**CAMERA OP:** "Not 1931?"

**TYLER:** "In 1943 I came back through. I didn't go to the 6th arrondissement. I didn't go near Rue de Fleurus."

The Camera Op adjusts the angle slightly. She has learned not to follow up immediately. She films the city below.

**TYLER:** "There was a war on. It seemed—" He pauses. "Inappropriate to go measure a construction while there was a war on."

A beat.

**TYLER:** "That sounds more reasonable said aloud than I expected it to."

The wheels come down.

Title card: **DAY 232**

Title card.

**TYLER**
*"Paris"*

## ACT ONE: RUE DE FLEURUS

Day 232. Morning.

The 6th arrondissement is narrow streets and tall doors and the light is different than Detroit. The Camera Op is filming Tyler walking. He knows where he is going. He doesn't use his phone for navigation.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You know the way."

**TYLER:** "I've walked it."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Ninety-five years ago."

**TYLER:** "The streets haven't moved."

They walk. The Paris Correspondent meets them at the corner of Rue de Fleurus and Rue Guynemer. She is in her forties, she has a tablet with measurement instruments already running, she has clearly been here before. She and the Exchange Student greet each other in French.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(to Tyler, in English)* "This is our Paris contact. She's been monitoring the Rue de Fleurus site for two years. She didn't know what she was monitoring until I sent her the CARDINAL-3 parameters three weeks ago."

**PARIS CORRESPONDENT:** "I knew there was something unusual about the block. We had it flagged in our archive under 'unclassified ambient elevation.' I did not know it had a name."

**TYLER:** "Does it have a name now?"

**PARIS CORRESPONDENT:** She looks at the Exchange Student. "AMPLIFICATION-CLASS UNINTENTIONAL CONSTRUCTION, A-274. That's what the protocol designates it."

**TYLER:** "That's a long name."

He looks down the street. The building is visible from where they are standing. Stone facade, tall windows, the particular Parisian proportion of a building that was built to last.

He doesn't move toward it immediately.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "We should start the measurement perimeter. The Paris Correspondent has baseline readings going back eighteen months. That gives us a rate comparison."

**TYLER:** *(still looking)* "What's her eighteen-month reading?"

**PARIS CORRESPONDENT:** She checks her tablet. "Consistent elevation across the block, roughly thirty to forty meters in all directions. No expansion detected in eighteen months. Which is unusual for an active elevation source."

**TYLER:** "It's not active. I haven't been here since 1931."

**PARIS CORRESPONDENT:** A pause. "Then it's been running on the 1931 deposit for ninety-five years."

Tyler nods. He puts his hands in his pockets. He moves toward the building.

---

The measurement takes ninety minutes. The Exchange Student and the Paris Correspondent work the perimeter with a systematic grid protocol. The Camera Op films the measurement, films Tyler standing outside the building, films the numbers as they come in.

Tyler stands at the edge of the perimeter and watches.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(calling to Tyler)* "North face. Can you walk me to where you think the edge is?"

Tyler walks north. He stops at a specific point on the sidewalk, forty feet before the Paris Correspondent's marker.

**TYLER:** "Here."

**PARIS CORRESPONDENT:** She looks at her instrument. Looks at her tablet. Looks at the Exchange Student. "That's — he's standing at the inner threshold."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "How far is the outer edge?"

**PARIS CORRESPONDENT:** She measures. "Fifty meters north of the facade."

She converts.

**PARIS CORRESPONDENT:** "163 feet."

Silence.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** She writes it down. She looks at Tyler. "A-269 was 23 days for 71 feet. This one—"

**TYLER:** "Was more than 23 days. The twenty-three days was one stay. I was at the salon before that. Multiple Saturdays."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "How many Saturdays before the twenty-three-day stay?"

Tyler thinks about it.

**TYLER:** "Eight or nine. Maybe more. She had very good arguments."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** She is already writing. "Eight Saturdays plus twenty-three days. That's a significant total duration."

**TYLER:** "And the Saturdays lasted into the small hours. She didn't serve dinner until eleven."

The Camera Op holds the frame on the Paris Correspondent, who is standing with her tablet, looking at a number she has been seeing in her archive for two years without understanding it.

**PARIS CORRESPONDENT:** "163 feet. For a deposit made in 1931. Ninety-five years without reinforcement."

**TYLER:** "It doesn't need reinforcement once it's running." He pauses. "The ones I built deliberately need maintenance. The ones I made by staying just — run."

**PARIS CORRESPONDENT:** Quietly: "Why?"

**TYLER:** "I don't know yet. That's why we're doing the audit."

## ACT TWO: INSIDE

Day 232. Afternoon.

The building at 27 Rue de Fleurus is residential apartments now. The Exchange Student had arranged access through the building management two weeks prior — she told them a documentary team was researching the building's historical connections, which is accurate.

The caretaker meets them in the lobby. The apartment where the salon was held is on the second floor, left side. The current residents agreed to allow a brief visit. The Exchange Student thanks the caretaker. Tyler reads the commemorative plaque by the entrance: *Gertrude Stein (1874–1946), Écrivaine et collectionneuse d'art américaine, a vécu et travaillé ici de 1903 à 1938.*

He reads it for a while.

**CAMERA OP:** "You knew her."

**TYLER:** "She was one of the most interesting people I've met." He doesn't say "in my life" — he never uses that phrase. "She had a very clear theory about what art was for. She didn't soften it for polite company. The first Saturday I went she argued with a painter for two hours about the function of the visible surface and she won."

**CAMERA OP:** "Who was the painter?"

A pause.

**TYLER:** "I shouldn't say. He's in the historical record and this would revise it."

The Camera Op makes a sound that might be a laugh.

They go upstairs.

---

The apartment door is open. The current residents have made themselves absent — the Exchange Student arranged for that consideration. The camera enters the apartment.

The room is different from 1931. The walls are painted white now where they were covered in paintings then. The proportions are the same. The windows face the garden.

Tyler stands in the doorway.

He doesn't enter immediately. He stands there for — the Camera Op checks the timecode later — forty-one seconds.

Then he crosses the threshold.

**CAMERA OP:** *(quietly)* "What does it feel like?"

**TYLER:** "Like the room knows I'm here."

He walks to the window. He looks out at the garden below.

**TYLER:** "The paintings were on every wall. She had a Cézanne above that window and two Picassos on the wall behind where I'm standing. The Saturday evenings were — crowded. She knew everyone she invited and she invited widely. There was always bread and there was always argument."

He turns from the window.

**TYLER:** "I came on a Saturday and I stayed. I didn't go back to the hotel. I came back the next day and the next. She didn't ask me to explain it and I didn't explain it. She gave me a blanket."

A pause.

**TYLER:** "After three weeks she said: 'You should see more of Paris. You've seen this apartment very thoroughly.' And I thanked her and I left."

**CAMERA OP:** "And you wrote 'two weeks.'"

**TYLER:** "I wrote it short. I always write it short."

**CAMERA OP:** "Why?"

He looks at her.

**TYLER:** "Because I didn't understand why I stayed and the notation was for the archive and I didn't want to file something I didn't understand."

**CAMERA OP:** "And now?"

**TYLER:** "Now I understand why I stayed." He looks around the room. "Something was already here when I arrived. I made it larger by staying. The room asked me to stay and I stayed because the room asked. I didn't know the vocabulary. I just knew I wasn't ready to leave."

He picks up his coin. He looks at it — the 1901 dime.

**TYLER:** "The construction asked and I answered. I've been answering that question in one form or another since—" He stops. "For a long time."

He puts the coin back.

**TYLER:** *(to the room)* "163 feet. You've been running for ninety-five years."

He says it quietly. He is not performing it for the camera. He is telling the room.

The Camera Op holds the frame. She is inside a 163-foot construction that a man she has been filming for seven seasons made by sitting in a room and having good arguments and eating bread in the small hours ninety-five years before she was born.

## ACT THREE: COUNCIL SESSION #6

Day 232. Evening.

A café on Rue de Vaugirard, two blocks from Rue de Fleurus. Tyler, the Camera Op, the Exchange Student, the Paris Correspondent. The Paris Correspondent has left after the afternoon — she has more measurement protocol to run at two other sites in the coming days. Tyler is drinking coffee. The Exchange Student is reviewing the day's data.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "163 feet, steady for eighteen months. No active reinforcement since 1931. I'm going to estimate the construction deposit — how much amplification was added in 1931 — by comparing it to the A-269 rate." She calculates. "Tyler was at A-269 for roughly eleven hours total, two visits. A-269 went from ~20 feet to 71 feet. A-274: total estimated duration sixty to seventy hours. That would project—"

**TYLER:** "What's the projection."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Roughly 130 to 150 feet. But the current measurement is 163."

**TYLER:** "There's a gap."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Thirteen to thirty feet unaccounted for. Either the baseline at A-274 was higher than standard, or—"

**TYLER:** "Gertrude had her own resonance."

The Exchange Student looks up.

**TYLER:** "She wasn't Tyler. She was something else. She held salons for thirty years in that room. That has its own construction quality. I amplified a found construction." He pauses. "I didn't know that's what I was doing."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(writing)* "A-274 was already a found construction before Tyler's 1931 visits."

**TYLER:** "Gertrude Stein ran a salon in that room for thirty years. Of course it was already there."

A silence while the Exchange Student writes and the Camera Op holds the frame and Tyler drinks his coffee.

Then the Exchange Student's phone buzzes. She reads the message. Her expression changes in a way that the Camera Op notices and films.

**TYLER:** "What is it?"

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** She looks up from the phone. "Council Session #6 concluded this afternoon. Paris time, approximately while we were in the building."

A pause.

**TYLER:** "And?"

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** She looks at him. "They have a term."

The Camera Op does not move. Tyler sets down his coffee.

**TYLER:** "What is it?"

The Exchange Student looks at the message. She looks at him. She makes a decision.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "I'm not going to tell you here. Not tonight. Not in the city where they measured what you made. You should have the night first."

Tyler looks at her.

**TYLER:** "Is it accurate?"

A pause. The Exchange Student considers the question.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "I think it's the first time the Syndicate has had a word that isn't entirely wrong."

**TYLER:** "That's not the same thing as accurate."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "No. But for the Syndicate, it may be as close as they've gotten."

Tyler picks up his coffee. He looks at it. He looks out the window at the Paris evening, the street lamps coming on, the particular quality of light on stone buildings that has been consistent since before he was first here.

**TYLER:** "Tomorrow."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Tomorrow."

The Camera Op holds the frame. There is a reclassification term on the Exchange Student's phone. The term exists. Tyler knows the term exists. He will read it tomorrow.

Tonight he has come back to a room he left in 1931 and found it still running.

He drinks his coffee.
