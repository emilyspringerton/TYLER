### ACT ONE — A-284

**[INTERIOR — MUSIKHAUS VOGT, VIENNA. MORNING.]**

*The shop is still here. Instruments in the window. Same arrangement Tyler described in the register, as far as he can tell. Inside: Herr Vogt, seventy-one, adjusting the peg box on a violin.*

**TYLER:** "He's been here since 1903."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Is he the reason you're here?"

**TYLER:** "I bought a string mute here in 1923. He was forty-six. He had two apprentices and a dog. The apprentices are gone, I assume. I don't know about the dog."

He opens the door. The bell above it rings.

**HERR VOGT:** *(looking up)* "Guten Morgen."

**TYLER:** "Guten Morgen. I was here in 1923. You sold me a mute for a viola."

*Herr Vogt looks at him for a long moment.*

**HERR VOGT:** "You play?"

**TYLER:** "No. I wanted to know what it felt like to mute something."

*Herr Vogt considers this, then returns to the peg box.*

**HERR VOGT:** "Then you came to the right place."

*Tyler sits on the bench near the window. He doesn't touch anything. He stays ninety minutes. The Camera Op records from outside through the glass, twice. At forty minutes, Herr Vogt plays a scale on the violin he is repairing — one full scale, C major, not for anyone, just to check the tuning. Tyler listens. He doesn't move.*

---

**[EXCHANGE STUDENT — LATER, ON THE STREET]**

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "What ran?"

*Tyler holds the measuring tape at the curb.*

**TYLER:** "Thirty-one feet."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "That's A-284."

**TYLER:** "Herr Vogt is still there. He was there when I arrived. The shop knew I had been before. I stayed for a man repairing a violin. That is a presence."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Did the rule apply? You understood the pull?"

**TYLER:** "Completely. A man. A violin. A scale in C major at forty minutes. Nothing ambiguous."

**CAMERA OP:** "He didn't ask who you were."

**TYLER:** "No."

**CAMERA OP:** "But he looked at you for a long time."

**TYLER:** "He recognized the bench."

*Silence.*

**CAMERA OP:** "The bench?"

**TYLER:** "I always sat on the same bench. Not every time. But often."

### ACT TWO — A-285

**[EXTERIOR — MARIAHILFER STRASSE. AFTERNOON.]**

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The register entry reads: *Café Bernstein, Mariahilfer 76. The regular table. Herr M. present. 14:00 bis 17:30. Dreiundzwanzig Tage verblieben.*"

**TYLER:** "Twenty-three days remaining. Before I had to leave."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You were counting."

**TYLER:** "Everyone was counting."

*The café is there. Different chairs. Same awning. Through the window: Herr Mayer, the owner, visible behind the counter. Tyler identifies him from across the street.*

**TYLER:** "He's there."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "But?"

**TYLER:** "The regulars are gone. Every Thursday for eleven years, there were eight people at the long table near the back. Writers, mostly. A composer. One journalist I didn't like. Herr M. would bring coffee without being asked. He knew what everyone took. He knew when to not bring coffee. That's a skill."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Are those people—"

**TYLER:** "Gone. Some emigrated. Some didn't. I don't have all the data."

*He is quiet.*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Is this a presence or an absence?"

**TYLER:** "I don't know."

*This is notable. The Camera Op turns from the window to look at him.*

**TYLER:** "The table is there. Herr M. is there. The specific people are not. If I stay, what am I staying for?"

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "What does the mechanism say?"

**TYLER:** "The mechanism says: the pull is toward Herr M. Not toward the Thursday people. Specifically toward him."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Then?"

**TYLER:** "Then I stay for Herr M. Not for their absence. The rule says: stay for presence, refuse for absence. Herr M. is a presence. I stay for him."

He goes inside.

---

**[INTERIOR — CAFÉ BERNSTEIN]**

*Tyler sits at the counter, not the long table. Herr Mayer brings coffee without being asked. Tyler takes it the way he always took it. Two hours and forty minutes. Three other customers come and go. At one point Herr Mayer sits across from Tyler and they speak in German, low, the Camera Op cannot get audio.*

*The Camera Op writes in her log later: I don't know what they said. I don't think it matters that I don't know.*

---

**[EXCHANGE STUDENT — LATER]**

*Forty-seven feet. Tyler holds the tape.*

**TYLER:** "I was right."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Was it hard? The rule?"

**TYLER:** "It required a decision. A-283 required a decision — say no to the room. A-285 required a decision — say yes to the man, not the table, not the Thursday people. The rule clarifies the question. It doesn't answer it automatically."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "That's not how you described it when you revised it."

**TYLER:** "I said: stay for presence. I didn't say: it would be obvious which thing is the presence."

*He writes in the register.*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT (V.O., reading later):** "*A-285. Café Bernstein. 47 feet. 2 hours, 40 minutes. Herr M. is the presence. Not the table. The table is furniture. The Thursday people are present in Herr M. — he still knows what everyone takes. He brought my coffee. He did not offer to pour it. He set it down. That is: he still knows I prefer to do it myself. Presence is not location. Presence is what still knows you.*"

She puts the register down.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Presence is what still knows you."

**CAMERA OP:** "He said that in Vienna."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "He said it in 1938."

### ACT THREE — THE PLATFORM

**[EXTERIOR — WESTBAHNHOF, VIENNA. DAY 259]**

*They are not here to measure. They are here because Tyler offered to describe it.*

**TYLER:** "F. left from this platform. July 11th. He had his papers and two bags and a coat that was too warm for July."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You were there?"

**TYLER:** "I was there. He'd given me the Ichthyosapiens records the night before. He had a contact in Zürich. He had two more contacts after that. He didn't tell me the route."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Were others leaving?"

**TYLER:** "Many others. The platform was crowded. Families. Children. I remember a woman with a potted plant wrapped in newspaper. She was carrying a fern."

*The Camera Op turns. She recognizes the fern.*

**CAMERA OP:** "Like the one in A-280."

**TYLER:** "Different fern. Different apartment. Different person. But I saw the fern and I thought: she's carrying what's alive. That's the right decision."

He looks at the platform.

**TYLER:** "F. said: if the mechanism runs here, don't tell me. I don't want documentation of a departure."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Does it run?"

*Tyler is silent for a moment. He produces the tape measure from his coat.*

**TYLER:** "I didn't promise not to measure it after he was gone."

*He measures.*

*Twelve feet.*

**TYLER:** "Twelve feet. Eighty-six years of it running."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Is this A-286?"

**TYLER:** "Yes."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Is it a presence or an absence? F. is gone."

**TYLER:** "F. was here. He was present. He said goodbye to me on this platform and he left for Zürich and he survived the war and I had contact with him in the 1950s and he is in the record. The platform has him. Not the fact that he left. The fact that he was here. The going was a presence. He went *to* something, not *from* something. The distinction matters."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "How do you know he went *to* something and not *from* something?"

**TYLER:** "Because he was carrying the records. You only carry records if you intend to use them."

*Long pause.*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "So the platform holds the carrying of the records."

**TYLER:** "The platform holds the last time I saw him on the ground. He was present. He carried something important. He left. The mechanism recorded all three."

*He writes the measurement in the register.*

**CAMERA OP (V.O.):** "Entry 49."

### ACT FOUR — A-287

**[EXTERIOR — KANDLGASSE VOLKSSCHULE, VIENNA. DAY 263]**

*A school. Empty — July, summer recess. Tyler looks at the building.*

**TYLER:** "Two teachers I knew taught here. Frau Goldstein, mathematics. Herr Langer, literature."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Are they still here?"

**TYLER:** "No."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Gone how?"

**TYLER:** "Dismissed. Both dismissed by the time I arrived. March 1938 was the Anschluss. By April, the dismissals were completed. I don't know where they are."

*The building is present. The school is operational — there are signs of recent use, exercise books in a window, chalk diagrams still on a board visible through the glass.*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The school is still here."

**TYLER:** "The school is still here. The teachers are not."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The pull?"

**TYLER:** "Toward the school. Not specifically toward Frau Goldstein or Herr Langer. Toward the building. As if the building has them."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Does it?"

Tyler is looking at the window with the chalk diagrams.

**TYLER:** "That's a mathematics problem on that board. The hand is different. Someone else wrote it. But it's the same board."

*He is still for a long time.*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Is the pull for presence or absence?"

**TYLER:** "The building is present. The institution is present. The teachers are absent. I don't know which thing the pull is toward."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You said that about A-285."

**TYLER:** "I did. And at A-285 I could identify it: Herr M. is the presence. Here — I don't know what the presence is. The pull is toward the building and I don't know if I'm being asked to witness the institution or to stay for the absence of the people who taught inside it."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "What does the rule say for that case?"

**TYLER:** "The rule says: if you don't understand the pull, leave."

*He stays for ninety minutes anyway.*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You just said the rule says leave."

**TYLER:** "I stayed for the mathematics problem. I looked at it for ninety minutes. Someone is still teaching long division here. That is a presence. Whoever wrote that problem is present in it."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "That's not what you said before."

**TYLER:** "I know. The rule is that when you don't understand the pull, you leave. I thought I didn't understand it. Then I understood it. It took ninety minutes."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "So you applied the rule correctly."

**TYLER:** "I think so. But I'm noting it in the register as a borderline case. Because I almost left, and if I had left I would have left for the wrong reason. I would have applied 'don't understand' when the correct answer was 'wait, you will understand in ninety minutes.' That is a different thing. That's new."

*He writes in the register.*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT (V.O., reading):** "*A-287. Volksschule, Kandlgasse. Borderline. I almost applied the 1931 rule to a case that was not a 1931 case — it was a 90-minute understanding problem, not a refusal problem. Stayed for the long division on the board. 28 feet. Note: understanding takes time. The rule should read: leave when you don't understand the pull AND time does not clarify it. Vienna is teaching me the edges of my own rule.*"

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "He's revising the 1938 rule."

**CAMERA OP:** "In 1938."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "While we're measuring it."

### CLOSING — DAY 264

**[EXTERIOR — RENTED ROOM WINDOW. EVENING.]**

*The Exchange Student has the register. 7 sites measured now — 4 from the previous episode, 3 from today: A-284, A-285, A-286. A-287 borderline and filed. Four remaining.*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "How many of the four remaining are like A-287? Ambiguous?"

**TYLER:** "Probably all of them. Vienna is not Paris. Paris in 1931 was different. Vienna in 1938 is a city that has been changed. The sites I came to — many of them are like A-287. The institution is present. The specific people are not. I am going to have to decide, for each one, which thing I am staying for."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "And if you can't decide?"

**TYLER:** "Then I leave. And the refusal trace runs. And I note: ambiguous. Refused. Because a refusal for ambiguity is not nothing — it says: I was here, I felt the pull, I could not determine its purpose, and I chose not to stay for a purpose I could not name."

**CAMERA OP:** "A third category of refusal."

*Tyler looks at her.*

**TYLER:** "That would be the third category."

**CAMERA OP:** "Pre-awareness refusal — you didn't know the mechanism. Purpose-based refusal — you knew, and the purpose was wrong. And now: ambiguity refusal — you can't determine the purpose."

**TYLER:** "If it happens. It hasn't happened yet. I'm noting the possibility."

**CAMERA OP:** "What's the fourth category?"

*Tyler picks up the register.*

**TYLER:** "I'll tell you when Vienna is finished."
