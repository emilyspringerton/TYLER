## EPISODE STRUCTURE

**Day 207, morning — the Cataloguer's report**

Emily arrives at 9 AM. Tyler has printed the email. Three pages.

**EMILY:** "What is that?"

**TYLER:** "The Cataloguer went back through my photograph archive." He hands her the pages. "All 340 photographs. She re-indexed them using the three categories we now have: aesthetic documentation, event-present, found construction." He pauses. "She found seven."

**EMILY:** "Seven found constructions."

**TYLER:** "In the full archive. Not counting Bethel AME, which I already knew about. Seven additional pre-existing constructions in the 340 photographs. Meaning I documented eight found constructions between 1947 and 1969 without understanding what I was looking at."

He sits down.

**TYLER:** "The eleven selected photographs for this project include one found construction — Bethel AME. In the remaining 329 photographs, the Cataloguer found seven more. Total: eight found constructions in the archive. Plus four active candidates I'm still working with."

Emily sets the pages on the table and reads them carefully.

**Day 207, midday — the seven**

The Exchange Student has been cross-referencing the Cataloguer's identifications against historical records since 10 AM.

The seven found constructions the Cataloguer identified, with preliminary research:

**Archive photograph A-112** (1951, Gratiot Avenue near Chene): A church basement. The Cataloguer's note: *weight signature matches Bethel AME; event-present indicators in Tyler's photographing behavior (multiple exposures, extended time on site)*. Exchange Student: Detroit Urban League chapter meeting site, late 1940s–early 1950s. Possible found construction.

**Archive photograph A-198** (1955, Eastern Market): A loading dock behind a produce vendor. Cataloguer note: *Tyler returned to this location twice; second visit 1958. Repeated return without apparent documentary reason = event recognition behavior*. Exchange Student: unclear; research ongoing.

**Archive photograph A-221** (1957, Corktown, Michigan Avenue): A back room of a bar. Cataloguer note: *Tyler photographed the interior and then the exterior threshold separately. Threshold documentation = recognition of container.* Exchange Student: Corktown neighborhood organizing history in this period — possible.

**Archive photograph A-267** (1960, North End neighborhood): A vacant lot with a single tree. Cataloguer note: *Tyler photographed the tree from four angles over two visits. The tree is not architecturally interesting. It is geographically interesting.* Exchange Student: The lot was the site of a house demolished in 1952. Investigating who lived there.

**Archive photograph A-288** (1962, Delray): Industrial canal bank. Cataloguer note: *Tyler photographed the water surface, not the industrial infrastructure. Same orientation behavior as bridge crossing — photographing through infrastructure to geography.* Exchange Student: Delray industrial history includes significant labor organizing in the 1940s.

**Archive photograph A-301** (1964, Jefferson Avenue near Belle Isle): A park bench. Cataloguer note: *Tyler sat on this bench for an estimated two hours based on the film exposure timestamps. This is the only location where Tyler's own presence was that extended without a documented purpose.* Exchange Student: no immediate historical correlation found; research ongoing.

**Archive photograph A-319** (1966, Hamtramck): A school gymnasium, empty. Cataloguer note: *Empty gymnasium, off-season, no apparent visual interest. But Tyler used a different lens — one he reserved for interiors where he wanted to capture the full space, not a detail. He was documenting the room as a whole. This is the construction documentation lens.* Exchange Student: Hamtramck community organizing, Polish and Arab-American communities. School gymnasiums as meeting sites.

---

**Day 207, afternoon — Tyler's reaction**

Tyler reads the Exchange Student's research notes.

**TYLER:** "Seven. In nine cases where I can't identify the event yet, I'm going to assume the Cataloguer is right about the signature. She's been reading my photographs for twenty years." He sets the notes down. "I spent twenty-two years taking photographs of things that were already built, and I called it documentation."

**EMILY:** "You were documenting. You just didn't have the category."

**TYLER:** "No. I had the category. I just didn't have the word for it." He picks up photograph A-267 — the tree on the vacant lot. "I know why I came back to this twice. I knew something was there. I didn't know how to describe what I was looking at." He looks at it. "I thought I was taking photographs of events. I was also taking photographs of completed constructions. The photographs look the same from the outside. You can only distinguish them by what they feel like from the inside."

**EMILY:** "And you always knew from the inside."

**TYLER:** "Yes."

He is quiet for a moment.

**TYLER:** "The network is larger than I thought. I've been planning for eight constructions — four complete, four candidates. But if seven of these archive photographs are found constructions, the network already has fifteen nodes. Before I build anything else."

**EMILY:** "Does the size of the network change what it does?"

**TYLER:** "I don't know. That's the Season 6 question." He looks at the map. "Right now I need to know which of the seven the Cataloguer identified are confirmed. Some of them will be. Some may not be. I need to visit them." He pauses. "The crossing completes in eight days. When it does, I'll have time."

---

**Day 207, late afternoon — the crossing**

Tyler walks to the Ambassador Bridge at 4 PM. Not to the midpoint — he stands on the US approach, looking at the span.

He does this every three to four days now. The crossing construction is in its final calibration phase. He can feel it completing from the shore.

**TYLER:** "Eight days."

Camera Op films him watching the bridge from the approach. She has a new position today: lower than before, framing Tyler against the span rather than the water. She has been repositioning with the construction phase — her angles have been changing as the construction nears its endpoint.

**TYLER:** "When it completes, it will be the largest construction in the network by witness count." He is talking to Emily. "Seven hundred thousand passive witnesses by Day 215. Every one of them a person who crossed the bridge not knowing they were holding something."

He watches the traffic on the span.

**TYLER:** "I think the passive witnesses are the point. Not incidental to the construction — structural. The crossing holds because 700,000 people are holding it without knowing. That's what makes it different from Bacon's building and Ward C. Those are small, precise, known-witness constructions. The crossing is something else."

He turns away from the bridge.

**TYLER:** "Hastings/Vernor is the same type. The highway traffic doesn't know. They're holding the music that was never ready to be public." He pauses. "I keep building things that are held by people who don't know they're holding them."

**EMILY:** "Is that by design?"

**TYLER:** "I don't know yet."
