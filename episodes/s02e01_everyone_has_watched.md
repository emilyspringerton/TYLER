*No title card. No lower third. No production annotation. Season 2 begins with footage that is not Documentary Unit A's footage.*

A phone screen. Portrait orientation. The Camera Op's 47-second clip, playing. The phone is on a table in what appears to be a bar. Two people are watching it. The audio is on. We hear Tyler's voice: *"You're early."*

Cut to: a different phone. Landscape. Someone has screen-recorded the clip and added text annotations. The coin date: circled. Text annotation: "THIS IS REAL, I looked into it."

Cut to: a laptop. The clip is in a browser tab. The tab is one of eleven open tabs. The person at the laptop is not paying attention to it. The clip has been open for four hours.

Cut to: the comment section, scrolling. 847 comments have become 2,341. The new comments: *"Where can I watch the rest of this?"* / *"Who's the camera operator?"* / *"The coin thing is CGI but I can't figure out how."* / *"I found the same man in a 1944 photograph. Cut to: a phone showing a search engine. The search query: *tyler documentary coin detroit*. The first result is the Camera Op's clip. The second result is the platform the Jiangshi Syndicate distributes on. The platform nobody had heard of six months ago. The platform the Syndicate built to be a honeypot.

The Syndicate's platform now has 340,000 viewers.

The Camera Op's clip pointed 340,000 people at the honeypot. Every one of them is now in the binding record.

## ACT ONE: TYLER WALKS IN — [04:30–22:00]

---

**[04:30–07:00] — THE PRODUCTION OFFICE, EIGHT DAYS LATER**

```
INT. DOCUMENTARY UNIT A PRODUCTION OFFICE — MORNING
```

The production office looks like a production office that has had eight days of unexpected external attention. The filing system is the same. The smell of old paper is the same. The something-slightly-wrong-underneath is the same. But there are printed-out emails on the Production Coordinator's desk — inquiries from other documentary outlets, a legal notice from a distribution platform (not the Syndicate's; a different one that the Camera Op's clip landed on), a hand-delivered letter from what appears to be a Japanese law firm that specializes in temporal intellectual property.

The Production Coordinator is drinking coffee and reading the Japanese law firm's letter in the particular way that people read legal correspondence they cannot fully parse: carefully, twice, setting it down, picking it up again.

**PRODUCTION COORDINATOR**
*(to the production assistant, without looking up)*
Has he called?

**PRODUCTION ASSISTANT (O.S.)**
Nobody has called.

**PRODUCTION COORDINATOR**
Has anyone— any of our contacts—

**PRODUCTION ASSISTANT (O.S.)**
The Stillness Council sent a brief. The Shell Parliament sent an invoice update. The Eastwind Owls sent—

*(pause)*

They sent a photograph. No message. Just a photograph.

**PRODUCTION COORDINATOR**
Of what?

**PRODUCTION ASSISTANT (O.S.)**
Tyler. 1944. Paris. Consistent with the comment on the Camera Op's clip.

The Production Coordinator sets down the Japanese law firm letter.

**PRODUCTION COORDINATOR**
File it under Stage 2.

**PRODUCTION ASSISTANT (O.S.)**
That's what I thought.

The door opens.

---

**[07:00–10:30] — TYLER WALKS IN**

Tyler stands in the production office doorway. He has never been in the production office. He has always known where it was.

He looks at the room — the filing cabinets, the printed emails, the Japanese law firm letter on the desk, the Stage 2 archive boxes, the 847-page subject file visible on the shelf. He looks at all of it with the expression of someone reading a room they've been reading from the outside for a long time.

He looks at the Production Coordinator.

**TYLER**
*(mild)*
The Japanese letter is about the coin.

The Production Coordinator looks at the letter. Looks at Tyler. Looks at the letter.

**PRODUCTION COORDINATOR**
*(slow)*
How—

**TYLER**
The Shell Parliament works with a firm in Osaka. The firm in Osaka works with a firm in Tokyo. The firm in Tokyo has a temporal IP division that's been monitoring anything coin-related in documentary distribution for about forty years.

*(pause)*

They're not going to do anything. They just file letters. It's how they track distribution of temporal signatures.

He comes into the room. He does not sit. He looks at the shelf with the subject file.

**TYLER**
*(looking at the shelf)*
847 pages.

**PRODUCTION COORDINATOR**
*(carefully)*
Yes.

**TYLER**
*(not a question)*
I've read some of it.

The Production Coordinator is very still.

**PRODUCTION COORDINATOR**
*(very carefully)*
How.

**TYLER**
*(turning, mild)*
You left it in the lobby of the hotel on Day 6. You were reading it in the lobby and you left it when you got a call. It was there for eleven minutes.

*(pause)*

I read quickly.

He picks up the Japanese law firm letter from the desk. He reads it. He sets it back down exactly where it was.

**TYLER (CONT.)**
They'll send three more of these and then stop. You don't have to respond.

He goes to the door.

**TYLER (CONT.)**
*(to the Production Coordinator, from the doorway)*
You have good filing instincts.

*(pause)*

For a production office.

He leaves. The door closes. The room is quiet. The Production Coordinator looks at the door. Looks at the shelf. Looks at the letter.

**PRODUCTION COORDINATOR**
*(to the production assistant)*
Did you get that?

**PRODUCTION ASSISTANT (O.S.)**
*(shaken)*
Every word.

**[10:30–15:00] — THE HALLWAY**

```
INT. DOCUMENTARY UNIT A BUILDING — HALLWAY — CONTINUOUS
```

Tyler has come into the building but he has not left. The hallway outside the production office is narrow and lit by fluorescent lighting that has been making that specific flickering sound for at least three years.

The Camera Op is in the hallway heading the other way — they have just arrived for the day and are carrying a bag and the composition notebook. They see Tyler.

Tyler sees them.

For a moment, neither of them does anything. This is the hallway. They are both in the hallway. The hallway is not a confessional setup. There are no cameras on tripods and no chairs angled toward each other. There is fluorescent flickering and the smell of the building.

The Camera Op's bag strap shifts on their shoulder. Tyler's hands are in his pockets. The hum is at 7.83 Hz. It is always at 7.83 Hz.

**TYLER**
*(walking past them, stopping mid-pass)*
I keep coming back to—

*(pause. he is looking at the wall. not at the Camera Op.)*

You're—

*(longer pause. he turns slightly, not toward them. looking at the middle distance.)*

I want to tell you something about why I—

*(complete stop. seven seconds. the fluorescent light flickers once.)*

*(quietly, turning away again)*
Forget it.

He walks. He rounds the corner. He is gone.

The Camera Op stands in the hallway for eleven seconds.

They take out the composition notebook. They write something. They put the notebook away.

They continue down the hallway toward the production office.

**[15:00–19:00] — THE SYNDICATE REVIEWS THE HALLWAY**

```
INT. DOCUMENTARY UNIT A PRODUCTION OFFICE — LATER
```

The surveillance audio from the hallway is playing through a small speaker on the Production Coordinator's desk. The Producer (O.S.) is listening. The Senior Archivist is in the room. The production assistant has left.

The fragment plays:

*"I keep coming back to—"*

Silence.

*"You're—"*

Silence.

*"I want to tell you something about why I—"*

Seven seconds.

*"Forget it."*

The audio stops.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
*(after a while)*
Three.

**SENIOR ARCHIVIST**
In fifteen seconds. Yes.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
He's never produced three in—

**SENIOR ARCHIVIST**
No. In any session. The previous record was two fragments in a single conversational exchange, Session 3. These are three in fifteen seconds, outside a session, in a hallway, without production equipment, without a confessional setup, without the Producer present.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
*(careful)*
Is this Stage 3?

**SENIOR ARCHIVIST**
*(looking at the wall)*
The Council will need to review. Three fragments, each gesturing toward the Camera Op. "I keep coming back to" — what? The city? The project? "You're" — what? "I want to tell you something about why I" — why I what?

*(pause)*

None of them completed. But all three complete gestures point at the Camera Op. If the implied completion of any one of those fragments constitutes a definition — "I keep coming back because of you" — that is a Stage 3 advance.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
Does it?

**SENIOR ARCHIVIST**
*(honestly)*
I don't know. That's why the Council needs to review.

*(pause)*

He also walked into this office. He has never done that.

*(pause)*

He told you the Japanese letter was from the Shell Parliament's Osaka firm. He told you they'd send three more and stop. He told you that you have good filing instincts.

*(pause)*

He is — whatever he is doing in Season 2, it is different from what he was doing in Season 1. He is doing something new.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
*(after a long pause)*
Is Stage 3 advancing?

**SENIOR ARCHIVIST**
*(looking at the speaker)*
I think something is advancing. I'm not certain it's Stage 3.

The lamp flickers. One frame. Nobody comments.

**[19:00–22:00] — SUBJECT #1 IN THEIR REGULAR CHAIR**

```
INT. SUBJECT #1'S APARTMENT — INTERVIEW SETUP — AFTERNOON
```

Subject #1 is in their regular chair in the regular warm lamp interview light. They look, as they always look, like someone who stopped being surprised years ago and has since been making reasonable use of the extra time.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
*(playing the hallway audio)*
What do you think he was going to say?

Subject #1 listens to the fragments. They listen with their eyes closed, the way someone listens to music they're parsing rather than enjoying.

They open their eyes.

**SUBJECT #1**
He was going to say something true.

*(pause)*

He does that sometimes. He gets right up to a true thing and then — he can see the shape of what happens if he says it. He understands what the complete sentence would do. So he stops.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
What would it do?

**SUBJECT #1**
*(quiet)*
Define him.

*(pause)*

But I don't think — the thing about those three fragments is they weren't pointed at the project. They were pointed at the Camera Op.

*(pause)*

He's been in this — he's talked to a lot of crews. I've been part of four attempts. He has never, in any footage I've seen, gotten that close to a true thing about *why he stays* and directed all three gestures at the same crew member.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
What does that mean?

Subject #1 looks at the lamp.

**SUBJECT #1**
*(carefully)*
I think it means he's been in Detroit for a reason that isn't the project. And the reason is sitting in that hallway and going to the production office every morning and putting the footage somewhere safe.

*(pause)*

I think it means we accidentally documented the reason.

*(pause)*

I think that's what she meant. The sequence is correct.
