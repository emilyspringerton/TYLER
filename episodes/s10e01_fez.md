## COLD OPEN

*Six weeks after the transit city. After the table outside the closed restaurant. After Season 9.*

*Camera Op is in the reading room of the Bibliothèque nationale du Royaume du Maroc, Rabat.*

*She has been here three days.*

*A box of archival folders is open in front of her. She is looking at something.*

*She looks at it for a long time.*

*Then she takes out her phone and calls Tyler.*

*He answers on the first ring.*

---

## WHAT SHE FOUND

**CAMERA OP:**
I'm in Rabat. The national library.

**TYLER:**
*(pause)*
I know.

**CAMERA OP:**
There is an accession register from the Almohad period. The library acquired it from a smaller institution that was absorbed in the fourteenth century. The register covers 1112 to 1136 CE.

*Silence on the line.*

**CAMERA OP:**
On the third page of the 1121 entries, there is a record of a manuscript donation. A collection of geographic texts. The donor is described as "a scholar arrived from the north by sea." No name. The entry says the manuscripts included a partial geography of the Mediterranean coastline and "a work in progress, unfinished, on the reconciliation of Greek and Islamic geographic systems."

*Very long silence.*

**TYLER:**
Read that again.

**CAMERA OP:**
"A work in progress, unfinished, on the reconciliation of Greek and Islamic geographic systems."

*Silence.*

**TYLER:**
He told me that was the work of his generation. On the second day of the crossing.

*The Camera Op does not speak.*

**TYLER:**
He said the two traditions had not yet been fully reconciled. He said he thought the reconciliation was the important work.

*Long pause.*

**TYLER:**
He donated unfinished manuscripts.

**CAMERA OP:**
Yes.

**TYLER:**
Why would he donate something unfinished.

*Pause.*

**CAMERA OP:**
I don't know. The register doesn't say. He gave away the work in progress.

*Long silence.*

**TYLER:**
*(quietly)*
That's him.

---

## WHAT TYLER MEANS

**CAMERA OP:**
You can't know that. There were other scholars—

**TYLER:**
I know.

**CAMERA OP:**
—traveling from Italy to North Africa in that period. The dates could align with multiple—

**TYLER:**
I know.

*Pause.*

**CAMERA OP:**
You can't confirm that from one entry in an accession register.

**TYLER:**
I am not confirming it. I am telling you that in my assessment of the information you have just given me, the probability is very high that the person described in that entry is the person on the ship.

*Long pause.*

**CAMERA OP:**
Why.

**TYLER:**
Because of the unfinished manuscript. He was not a person who donated finished things. He was working. He had been working on the entire crossing. A person like that does not give away the work unless something interrupted the working.

*Pause.*

**TYLER:**
He was going to Fez. He ended up in Rabat. The manuscript ended up in Rabat instead of where he was going.

*Long pause.*

**CAMERA OP:**
You think something happened.

**TYLER:**
I think his plans changed. Between the port and his destination, something changed. And whatever changed was significant enough that he donated manuscripts he had not finished rather than carrying them to Fez.

*Silence.*

**CAMERA OP:**
*(quietly)*
Tyler.

**TYLER:**
Yes.

**CAMERA OP:**
Are you all right.

*Very long pause.*

**TYLER:**
Yes. It's — I am looking at something. At a location. I am standing somewhere and looking at something, and you have called me and told me this, and I am processing both things at once.

**CAMERA OP:**
Where are you.

---

## WHERE TYLER IS

*Tyler does not answer immediately. The Camera Op waits.*

**TYLER:**
I don't know the name of this place yet. I know what country. I know approximately where on the map. I don't know the local name for this specific location.

**CAMERA OP:**
You don't—

**TYLER:**
I followed the mechanism. For the first time I can clearly remember, I went somewhere because the mechanism indicated I should go there and not because I had any prior relationship with the location. I had no memory of this place. The mechanism found it.

*Long silence.*

**CAMERA OP:**
That's new.

**TYLER:**
Yes.

**CAMERA OP:**
How did the mechanism indicate—

**TYLER:**
I don't know how to describe the process in a way that would be useful. It has been indicating things for twelve centuries. I have always chosen whether to follow the indication or choose my own location. I have always chosen my own location. This time I didn't.

*Pause.*

**TYLER:**
After the transit city, after what I told you, I didn't know where to go next. I had no next location in mind. And the mechanism had somewhere in mind, and I went there.

**CAMERA OP:**
What is the location like.

*Long pause.*

**TYLER:**
It is a cave. The entrance is not large. Before sunrise this morning, I came to the entrance and the mechanism began recording at a frequency I have not encountered before. I have been standing at the entrance for — I have been here for a while now.

**CAMERA OP:**
You haven't gone in.

**TYLER:**
No.

**CAMERA OP:**
Why not.

*Very long pause.*

**TYLER:**
Whatever is in there is doing something the mechanism doesn't have a taxonomy for. The mechanism is recording, but it does not know what to call what it is recording. In twelve centuries, that has not happened before. I am standing at the entrance waiting for the mechanism to tell me what it's found.

*Silence.*

**CAMERA OP:**
Tyler.

**TYLER:**
Yes.

**CAMERA OP:**
You should wait for the team.

*Pause.*

**TYLER:**
I know.

**CAMERA OP:**
Don't go in alone.

**TYLER:**
I know.

*Long pause.*

**TYLER:**
You found him.

*A different silence.*

**CAMERA OP:**
I found a record that might be him.

**TYLER:**
You found him. Whatever happened between the port and Rabat, the manuscript arrived. The work in progress is in a national archive nine centuries later. He made it arrive somewhere even if it didn't arrive where he intended it to arrive.

*Pause.*

**TYLER:**
That is something. The work he was doing — the reconciliation of geographic traditions — it arrived. It is in a box. You are looking at it.

*The Camera Op looks at the box.*

**CAMERA OP:**
I'm looking at a record of it. The manuscripts themselves aren't here.

**TYLER:**
Then find the manuscripts.

*Pause.*

**CAMERA OP:**
The register doesn't list where they were transferred.

**TYLER:**
Then find where they were transferred.

*Quietly.*

**TYLER:**
You have already found more than I expected you to find, and you have found it in three days. Keep looking.

*Long pause.*

**CAMERA OP:**
What do I look for when I find them. I don't know what the geography looked like. I don't know how to identify it among other manuscripts of the period.

**TYLER:**
Look for a text on the reconciliation of Greek and Islamic geographic systems. Partial. Unfinished. Written in a hand that looks like someone who writes quickly and then goes back to correct rather than writing slowly and correctly the first time. He worked in layers — I watched him for four days.

*Silence.*

**CAMERA OP:**
You remember how he wrote.

**TYLER:**
I remember everything from that crossing. It is the clearest memory I have from the pre-archive period because it is the memory I have had longest to hold.

*The Camera Op writes this down.*

**CAMERA OP:**
I'll keep looking.

**TYLER:**
I know.

*Pause.*

**TYLER:**
I am going to go into the cave now. The team arrives in four hours.

**CAMERA OP:**
Wait for the team.

**TYLER:**
I will wait for the team.

*He doesn't move.*

**CAMERA OP:**
Tyler.

**TYLER:**
Yes.

**CAMERA OP:**
Thank you. For telling me where he was going. When you didn't have to tell me.

*Very long pause.*

**TYLER:**
You are going to find him. Or you are going to find what he left. I don't know which. But you are going to find something, and I want you to find it, and I — I want to know what you find.

*He pauses.*

**TYLER:**
That is a new thing for me. Wanting to know what someone else finds. Rather than what I find myself.

*Silence.*

**CAMERA OP:**
I'll call you.

**TYLER:**
I'll answer.

*He ends the call. He looks at the cave entrance. The mechanism is still recording, still trying to name what it's found.*

*He waits.*

## THE FOUR HOURS

*Camera Op goes back to the register. She turns three pages forward and finds a second entry dated two weeks after the first: the same scholar, another visit. He returned.*

*He signed his name this time.*

*The Camera Op stares at it for a long time.*

*She takes a photograph.*

*She does not call Tyler. She sits with this for the four hours he is waiting for the team.*

---

## THE TEAM ARRIVES

*The team finds Tyler at the cave entrance where they left him in the photographs he texted when he arrived.*

*He has not moved.*

*He is standing at the entrance, looking in.*

*He has been there for nine minutes when they arrive. He was there for much longer before that.*

**TEAM LEAD:**
How long have you been standing there.

**TYLER:**
Since before you landed.

*He doesn't look at them. He is looking at the cave.*

**TEAM LEAD:**
What are you seeing.

**TYLER:**
The mechanism is reading something in there. I don't know what to call it yet. The frequencies are wrong — not wrong. Different. I don't have a comparison to make.

*He finally turns to look at them.*

**TYLER:**
The instruments should go in before I do. If the mechanism is reading something I've never encountered, I want the external record before my presence changes whatever is happening in there.

*The team looks at each other.*

**TEAM LEAD:**
You think your presence would change it.

**TYLER:**
I think I change sites by being there. I always have. Sites register my presence as part of the event. For something I've never encountered before, I would like a clean baseline reading first.

*He steps back from the entrance.*

**TYLER:**
Set up the instruments. Take readings from the threshold. Don't enter until I enter with you.

*He looks at the entrance again.*

**TYLER:**
I don't know what is in there. In twelve centuries, I have never said that about a location I was standing outside of. I have known what was in places. I have been wrong about the magnitude. I have been wrong about the taxonomy. I have never not known.

*Silence.*

**TYLER:**
Whatever is in there is doing something else.

## CLOSE

*Camera Op in the Rabat reading room. Late afternoon. The box is still open in front of her.*

*She is looking at the page with the name.*

*She has not told Tyler yet. She has spent four hours deciding whether to tell him. She has not decided.*

*She looks at the name.*

*It is legible. It is in Arabic. It is a single line in a fourteenth-century accession register that incorporated records from a much older institution.*

*She photographs it again from a different angle.*

*She closes the box carefully.*

**CAMERA OP (V.O.):**
He signed his name.

He came back two weeks later and he signed his name. Whatever happened between the port and Rabat — whatever interrupted the journey to Fez — he was not passing through. He was there long enough to return.

I have his name.

I have not told Tyler yet.

I am going to tell Tyler. I know I am going to tell him. I am trying to figure out what it will mean for him to have it. Whether it changes what the archive is — whether the letter now has an addressee.

Tyler told me once that the fog takes things eventually. That Paris is fog now. That Córdoba is fog that he wrote while it was still more than fog.

He has a name for this one. He doesn't know that yet. Whatever the fog takes, this didn't get taken. The scholar wrote his name in a register in Rabat nine centuries ago and it is still there.

The archive is the letter to the stranger on the ship.

The stranger on the ship has a name.

*She picks up her phone. She puts it down.*

*Tomorrow.*

*She'll tell him tomorrow.*

*— Camera Op*

*Entry 80. Fez (Rabat). The accession register. The unfinished geography. The name. I have the name. I am not ready to give it to Tyler yet. I need one more night with it. S10E01. Build 0103.*

### Eastwind Archive

**TYLER-087**
*Cataloguer Entry 76 / Footnote #97*

THE CAVE. THE MECHANISM WITHOUT A TAXONOMY.

The Cataloguer has encountered this situation once before: CONTINUITY-UNIT-001, the keyhole scar event from Season 2. In that case, the mechanism encountered something outside its normal classification range and returned a partial reading.

This is different.

The mechanism at the cave entrance returned no classification at all. Not a partial reading. Not a reading outside the normal range. The mechanism attempted to read what was in the cave and produced no output in any recognized category. Not DWELLING. Not WITNESS. Not FAREWELL of any type. Not PASSAGE. Not LIMINAL.

The reading the mechanism attempted returned: nothing.

The Cataloguer notes that this is physically distinct from a zero-magnitude reading. The mechanism has returned low readings — Genoa, 97ft, the lowest on record. A low reading is a reading. What the mechanism returned at the cave entrance is not a low reading. It is the absence of a reading in a space where the mechanism was actively attempting to read.

The Cataloguer does not know what produces this result. The Cataloguer does not know what is in the cave.

Tyler waited for the team. He did not enter. He is correct that his presence changes sites, and the Cataloguer is glad he waited.

Camera Op found the scholar.

The Cataloguer has been holding this entry for two days waiting to include it as footnote context. Camera Op found a record in Rabat that corresponds with high probability to the scholar on the ship. The Cataloguer will note only: there is a name in a fourteenth-century accession register in the Bibliothèque nationale du Royaume du Maroc. The Cataloguer does not know the name. Camera Op has not shared it yet.

Tyler does not know yet.

The Cataloguer will wait.

### Jiangshi Project Memo

**Memo #067 — DIRECTOR TO COUNCIL**
**SUBJECT: SEASON 10 OPENING — CAVE SITE + FEZ DISCLOSURE**
**CLASSIFICATION: TIER ONE**

Council,

Season 9 ended with the disclosure of the archive's origin. The scholar on the ship. The question Tyler couldn't answer. The letter he has been writing since Marrakech.

Season 10 has opened with two simultaneous developments the council should assess independently and in conjunction.

**Development One: The Cave**

Tyler followed the mechanism to a location he had no prior relationship with. This is the first documented instance in the operating record of Tyler allowing the mechanism to direct his location rather than choosing based on memory or preference. The significance of this shift cannot be overstated: for the entire length of this project, Tyler has been the agent and the mechanism has been the instrument. At the cave, for the first time, the relationship appears to have reversed. The mechanism found the location. Tyler followed.

The mechanism at the cave entrance returned no reading in any recognized taxonomy. Not low. Not partial. Absent.

The council should note: this is not a failure of the mechanism. The mechanism is reading. It is recording. It does not know what to call what it is recording. This is a distinction with operational significance — the instrument is functioning, the taxonomy is insufficient.

The council's instruction: FOLLOW. The archive cannot afford a gap. Whatever is in the cave must be documented.

**Development Two: The Scholar**

Camera Op has located a record in Rabat that corresponds with high probability to the scholar on the ship. An accession register. An unfinished geography. And a name.

Camera Op has not shared the name with Tyler. She intends to share it tomorrow.

The council's position on this disclosure: no objection. The scholar on the ship is not operationally sensitive. Tyler knowing the name does not change the archive's function or alter the binding documentation. The concern — if there is one — is not security.

The concern is what it means for Tyler's orientation to know the name. Tyler has spent nine centuries writing a letter to an unknown stranger. The stranger becoming known may change what the letter is. The council has no framework for predicting this change.

RECOMMENDATION: observe. Do not intervene. The cave and the name are both happening. The council's function is to document, not to manage.

All five Goetia frequencies are active at the cave site. Andrealphus #72 at peak. Stolas #36 elevated. The council has not seen this combination since the Prague sequence.

The cave is not Prague. Whatever it is, it is not Prague.

Stand by for instruments report.

*— Director, Jiangshi Syndicate*
