The library has not been in the documentary before. The Camera Op documents it carefully — wide establishing shot first, then detail. Books floor to ceiling. Not decorative books: working books. **TYLER:**
The Subscriber was in this room.

Many times. They kept materials here between visits. Some of them are still here. The document I'm reading — *(he sets it down)* — is one of theirs. It's been here since 1743. I understand it better now than I did last week.

**BACON:**
Sit. Read it when you're ready. I'll wait.


He finishes. He sits for two minutes without moving.

Then:

**TYLER:**
Bacon.

*(from the doorway — he has been there)*
Yes.

**TYLER:**
The second message. The one in the custody file. "When the sentence completes, the transfer will complete."

Yes.

**TYLER:**

**TYLER:**

"Clean builds first. Then custody. Then everything else."

*(He looks up from the document.)*

That's it. That's the second message.

*(after a moment)*
"The table is set."

*(looking at the table in the room behind the round table — visible through the open door)*
That's what the table is. Not a waiting thing. A set thing.

Because if they knew where it came from, they'd use it out of obligation. If they don't know, they use it because it's correct.

**BACON:**
The Subscriber built the phrase the same way they built the chain. Requiring genuine use rather than obligated use.

Tyler looks at the open door.

**TYLER:**
Everything they built works that way. The sentence — had to be genuine. The custody — had to be voluntary. The phrase — had to be used without knowledge of the source. Every load-bearing element in the architecture is load-bearing specifically because it cannot be performed under obligation. It has to be performed because it's right.

**TYLER:**
I want to say something for the record. Not for the Syndicate — the archive is closed. Not for the Parliament — the account is settled. For the record in general.

**CAMERA OP (O.S.):**
Running.

**TYLER:**
The Subscriber built something that required 380 years and four SPRING-line witnesses and one time-displaced person carrying an obligation through every year they could reach. They built it correctly. It ran correctly. It completed correctly. I spent four hundred years not fully understanding what I was carrying. I understand it now. What I was carrying was the beginning of something, not the end. The chain was the setup. What was behind the lock is the architecture — intact, running, available.

He pauses.

**TYLER:**
I don't know what comes next. The second message says I don't need to. The architecture will run whether I'm using it or not. I'm going to use it. Not out of obligation — that ended on Day 49. Because it's correct. Because the Subscriber built it correctly and the correct response to a correctly built architecture is to build on it.

He looks at Bacon.

**TYLER:**
That's what I decided on Day 56. Not where I'm going next year. What I'm doing with what was left.

**BACON:**
*(quietly)*
Clean builds first.

**TYLER:**
Then custody. Then everything else.

*(quietly, not for the record, but the camera is running)*
Clean builds first.
