## COLD OPEN

Day 227. 7:45 AM.

The Exchange Student has been awake since 4:00 AM.

She has the CARDINAL-3 archive spread across three monitors and the dining table. Paper printouts — the scan renders on the side-by-side screens, the paper copies to handle, annotate, mark. Tyler comes downstairs at 7:45 and finds her with a red pen and a 1938 Vienna survey register.

She looks up.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Vienna, 1938. Eleven notations in four months. Six of them read 'extended stay' or variant. One reads: 'The room on Praterstraße. Could not easily leave, three days.'"

She holds up the page. Tyler reads it across the room.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "I cross-referenced every city where CARDINAL-3 flagged anomalous visit durations. Not just Detroit. Every city in the primary documentation record, 1908 to 1995. Anywhere Tyler's notation suggests a stay longer than the standard survey protocol."

She has a map on the table. Paper, hand-annotated. Cities in red ink.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Paris, 1931. Vienna, 1938. Lisbon, 1943. Chicago, 1952. Osaka, 1967. Buenos Aires, 1974. Lagos, 1981. Seoul, 1989. There are more — I only stopped because I ran out of CARDINAL-3 data. The 1995-forward record is incomplete."

Tyler comes to the table. He looks at the map.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Each of these cities has at least one site where CARDINAL-3 logged an anomalous duration. In most cases, multiple sites. In Paris, six."

Tyler picks up the 1931 Paris register. His own handwriting. He reads it.

A long pause.

**TYLER:** "I was there for three weeks."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The notation says two weeks."

**TYLER:** "I wrote it short."

She writes something in her notebook.

Title card: **DAY 227**

Title card.

**TYLER**
*"The First City"*

## ACT ONE: THE METHODOLOGY

Day 227. Morning.

The Camera Op arrives at 8:15. She has been filming since she walked through the door and she doesn't mention Day 226 and Tyler doesn't mention Day 226. The Camera Op films the Exchange Student's map. She films Tyler's hands holding the 1931 Paris register. She films the red pen annotations.

**CAMERA OP:** "How do we run the audit?"

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "CARDINAL-3 by city, cross-referenced against extended-duration notations. For each anomalous site: identify the address, current status, send a field operative if accessible, or pull remote sensing data. Measure current resonance radius if possible."

**CAMERA OP:** "That's the procedure. What's the first city?"

The Exchange Student doesn't answer immediately. She's been building to this.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Paris. 1931. Six sites with anomalous durations. The longest: twenty-three days in a single building. Rue de Fleurus, 6th arrondissement. Tyler wrote 'two weeks.' The CARDINAL-3 field cross-reference puts him in the building for twenty-three days by exit records."

**CAMERA OP:** "You verified against exit records?"

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "CARDINAL-3 always had more than they shared with the archive. We have the full record now."

Tyler is standing at the window. He has been standing at the window for most of this conversation.

**TYLER:** "I knew the woman who lived there."

Silence.

**TYLER:** "She was a writer. She held a salon on Saturday evenings. I went the first Saturday and I kept coming back."

**CAMERA OP:** "For twenty-three days."

**TYLER:** "She had very good bread."

**CAMERA OP:** "Tyler."

**TYLER:** "I don't know if it was the bread."

He turns from the window. He comes back to the table and he sits down and he picks up the Paris register and he reads the notation aloud.

This is the first time Tyler has read a survey notation aloud for the camera.

He reads it slowly: *"Rue de Fleurus. The salon. Bread and argument, very long evening. Could not easily leave. Returned four more times. Something in the room."*

He puts the register down.

**TYLER:** "Something in the room. That's what I wrote."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "That's the notation."

**TYLER:** "I wrote that and I moved on to the next city and I filed it as background information and I never went back."

He looks at the Camera Op.

**TYLER:** "The building is still standing?"

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Converted to apartments. The ground floor was a gallery until 2018."

**TYLER:** "What's the current radius?"

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "No measurement yet."

**TYLER:** "That's the first city."

## ACT TWO: THE RECLASSIFICATION MEMO

Day 227. Afternoon.

The Jiangshi Syndicate's reclassification memo arrives via the Eastwind intermediary at 2:17 PM.

The Exchange Student intercepts it first. She reads it. She comes to the table where Tyler and the Camera Op are working through the 1938 Vienna register.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The Jiangshi reclassification memo is here."

Tyler and the Camera Op look up.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Previous classification: DOCUMENTATION SUBJECT. New classification—" She pauses. She looks at the document. "New classification: pending Council Session #6."

A beat.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "They don't have a new word yet."

The Camera Op is very still. She has been filming this documentary for six seasons and she has been filing information about the Jiangshi Syndicate's classification of Tyler for most of that time. She holds the frame.

**TYLER:** "What's the memo say about the audit?"

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "They want to observe it. Not run it. They're ceding the methodology to us."

**TYLER:** "Why?"

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** She reads: *"The Syndicate's documentation apparatus was built to observe an unknown subject. The subject is no longer fully unknown. We do not have the architecture to classify what the subject appears to be. We are requesting a pause in direct documentation while we consult internally."*

Tyler picks up his coffee. He drinks it.

**TYLER:** "They built a system for something they didn't understand and when they got close to understanding it they found out their system was too small."

**CAMERA OP:** "That sounds familiar."

Tyler looks at her.

**TYLER:** "The CARDINAL-3 archive was built to track a documentary subject. It turns out it was tracking a construction network that spans a hundred years and at least eight cities and probably more. The data was there. The category was missing."

**CAMERA OP:** "And now?"

**TYLER:** "Now the category is there and the data is there and the question is whether the person reviewing both is the same person who logged 'something in the room' in 1931 and moved on."

**CAMERA OP:** "Are you the same person?"

A pause that is not filled with a complete sentence.

**TYLER:** "I'm the person who's going back to Paris."

---

## ACT THREE: THE AUDIT PROTOCOL

Day 227. Evening.

Tyler, the Camera Op, and the Exchange Student are at the table with the map.

The Exchange Student has formalized the audit protocol in three pages of notes. She explains it.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Phase one: CARDINAL-3 review. All cities, all extended-duration notations, 1908–1995. I estimate 200–400 sites. Phase two: current measurement. For accessible sites, direct measurement. For inaccessible sites, proxy measurement via exchange network. Phase three: COEFF-002 calibration — Shell Parliament needs at least 30 confirmed measurements with current radius and 1952 estimated radius to establish the amplification rate model."

**CAMERA OP:** "What's the timeline?"

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Paris is two weeks. Vienna is two weeks. The rest depends on how many sites per city. Call it eighteen months to two years for Phase one and two."

**CAMERA OP:** "Tyler?"

**TYLER:** "Two years is fine."

A beat.

**TYLER:** "I've been doing this for a hundred years without knowing what I was doing. Two years to find out what I actually made seems proportionate."

He looks at the map. Cities in red ink. The Exchange Student's annotations in her precise handwriting. The Paris entries circled.

**TYLER:** "What was the writer's name?"

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The salon. Rue de Fleurus. The woman you knew."

**TYLER:** "Gertrude. Gertrude Stein." He says it like he's just remembered. "The bread was very good. She had a philosophy about bread."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "What was the philosophy?"

**TYLER:** "That it should be eaten with argument and friends and not alone. She was right."

He looks at the map again.

**TYLER:** "Something in the room. I wrote that in 1931. I think what was in the room was what's always in the room. I made it larger by staying."

The Camera Op holds the frame. She does not say anything. She films Tyler looking at the map of every city where he sat down and something asked him to stay and he stayed.

He is looking at ninety years of his own stays laid out on paper in red ink.

**TYLER:** "Paris first."
