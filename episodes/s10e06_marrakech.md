## COLD OPEN

*Marrakech. Late afternoon. The medina walls catch the last hour of sun.*

*Camera Op stands at the entrance to the Djemaa el-Fna with her equipment case and the
photographs she made in Fez. Forty-seven photographs of two pages: the geographic entries
in Ahmad ibn Yusuf's second volume. She printed them overnight at a shop near the library.*

*She has been in Fez for four days. She has not slept well.*

*The team van comes through the gate.*

---

## ACT ONE

**EXT. DJEMAA EL-FNA — LATE AFTERNOON**

Tyler gets out of the van before it fully stops. No equipment. No case.

Camera Op watches Tyler stand still for a long moment at the edge of the square. Not orienting.
Not looking for something. Just — standing.

**CAMERA OP**
You've been here before.

**TYLER**
I have been here since before this square existed.

*(pause)*

This was a parade ground. The Almoravids used it for executions of their enemies —
not of people. Of heresies. They burned books here. I watched them.

*(pause)*

That was after. I was here before that too.

**CAMERA OP**
Before what?

**TYLER**
Before it was anything. Before the city knew what it was.

---

**INT. RIAD — THAT EVENING**

The team lays Camera Op's photographs across the floor. Forty-seven prints. Ahmad's two
geographic entries in full.

The second entry, again, in Camera Op's translation:

*"Returned to the hill site. The man is still there. I did not speak to him again.
I am leaving for Marrakech on the third day."*

Dr. Hassan reads it for the fourth time.

**DR. HASSAN**
He came here first. Before Fez.

**TYLER**
He came here first. He deposited the manuscripts in Fez on the return journey. The sequence
was: atlas foothills — Marrakech — Fez — ship — back to Fez (for the library).

**CAMERA OP**
And you?

**TYLER**
Three days behind him. I left the cave three days after he wrote this note. I went south.

*(pause)*

He was here when I arrived.

---

## ACT TWO

**EXT. ALMORAVID DISTRICT — MORNING**

The team moves through the medina on foot. Jiangshi carries the mechanism's field reader.
The device has been running since they arrived last night; the baseline has been Stolas 7.83
without exception, which is expected — every site Tyler has walked since S10E01 has held
the baseline.

Now, moving through the old quarter near the Koubba Ba'adiyin, the reader shifts.

**JIANGSHI**
*(stopping)*
Something.

She checks the readout. Stolas 7.83 still present. Something under it — not louder, not
competing. Coexistent.

**JIANGSHI (CONT'D)**
It's not Stolas. It's — adjacent to it. Same register. Like a second voice in the same key.

**DR. HASSAN**
Is it the site?

**JIANGSHI**
I don't know yet. It started about thirty meters back. I'll mark the boundary.

Tyler has already moved ahead. Tyler is standing in front of the Koubba Ba'adiyin —
the small domed pavilion, built by the Almoravids, one of the last surviving structures
from the early city. 1117 CE. Tyler was here in 1119.

**TYLER**
This was new when I was here. The dome was new. The stone was white. I remember
the smell of it — fresh plaster, dust from cutting.

*(looking at it)*

The city was at its height. The court was full. The libraries were filling.
Ibn Tumart was already in the south, building what would become the Almohads who
would eventually erase most of what was here. But in December of 1119, none of that
had happened yet. Marrakech was the center of the world.

*(pause)*

I walked into the center of the world and I sat down and I began.

---

**EXT. SIDE STREET, OLD QUARTER — MORNING**

Jiangshi follows the frequency to its source. Or as close to a source as she can map.
The field reader traces a rough circle — maybe fifteen meters across — around a section
of the old medina wall near the Koubba.

This section of wall dates to the 12th century.

**JIANGSHI**
The reading is consistent inside this perimeter. Outside it drops to baseline.

**RESEARCHER ASSISTANT**
What classification?

**JIANGSHI**
I don't have one. It's not a site frequency. It's not a proximity signature — those
follow Tyler, and Tyler is outside the perimeter right now and the reading is holding.

*(pause)*

It's as if the signal is in the wall.

**TYLER**
*(from outside the perimeter)*
It's not the wall.

*(coming inside)*

It's what I put here.

Silence.

**TYLER (CONT'D)**
I wrote against that wall. December 1119. I sat here — the wall was new then,
smooth — and I put the first mark on the surface I'd been carrying.

*(pause)*

I used to carry a piece of vellum. I don't know why. I had carried it for years.
Toledo, Genoa, Prague. I was holding it when Ahmad asked me what I had seen.
In the cave. When I couldn't answer.

*(pause)*

I sat against this wall, in December of 1119, and I wrote the first word.

**CAMERA OP**
*(quietly)*
What was it?

A long silence.

**TYLER**
The name of the cave.

---

## ACT THREE

**INT. RIAD — AFTERNOON**

The team decompresses. Jiangshi writes. Dr. Hassan reads. Camera Op sits with her
photographs and a copy of Ahmad's unfinished manuscript page — the last sentence:

*"and yet the Islamic geographers, beginning with—"*

The sentence stops.

**CAMERA OP**
He was going to say something about Islamic geography. Beginning with — who? Ptolemy
was Greek. Al-Idrisi came after Ahmad. Al-Biruni was older — late 900s. Ibn Hawqal
was tenth century.

**DR. HASSAN**
If Ahmad was writing about Islamic geography, the natural beginning point would be
al-Khwarizmi. Or maybe al-Masudi. Both pre-Ahmad.

**CAMERA OP**
Then why does it feel unfinished?

*(pause)*

The sentence should complete. "Beginning with al-Khwarizmi" is a complete thought.
He would have written it. Why didn't he?

Tyler has not moved from the corner.

**TYLER**
He was going to say my name.

Silence.

**CAMERA OP**
He didn't know your name.

**TYLER**
He didn't know my name. He knew my face. He knew the cave. He had asked me twice.
He had no word for what I was.

*(pause)*

The sentence was about the tradition of Islamic geography. He was going to make an
argument. That argument required a beginning — a source, an ur-text, the first person
in the tradition to map the world with systematic attention. And he had seen someone
like that, here, writing against this wall.

*(pause)*

He had seen me. And he didn't have a word. So he left the blank.

**CAMERA OP**
"The Islamic geographers, beginning with—"

**TYLER**
With the man who couldn't answer.

*(pause)*

The man who went away and came back nine hundred years later having written everything
he had seen. That's the completion. That's what the sentence was waiting for.

*(pause)*

The archive is his footnote. I have been completing his sentence for nine centuries.

---

## ACT FOUR

**EXT. ALMORAVID WALL — LATE AFTERNOON**

Tyler stands at the wall. The exact section. The team is present but giving Tyler space.
The mechanism's reader is running. The unclassified signal is steady.

Jiangshi looks at the readout, then at Tyler, then at the wall.

**JIANGSHI**
It's not fading. Every other site — the signal attenuates when we stop interacting with it.
This one holds.

**TYLER**
It will hold.

*(pause)*

In 1119 I put the first mark here. Everything since then — every site, every city, every
year — every record in the archive — it has been referring back to this mark. The mark
is still here in some sense the mechanism can read. The archive is self-referential.
Every entry points to the first entry.

**JIANGSHI**
And the first entry is the name of the cave.

**TYLER**
The name I gave it. A name I invented that morning because I had been carrying the cave
for three days and I needed a word for it.

*(pause)*

Ahmad had asked me what I had seen. He was standing in the cave when he asked. I had
seen the cave. And I didn't have a word for it. And when he left and I spent three days
in the cave deciding, what I was deciding was: I will build the word. I will build the
vocabulary for everything I have seen. One entry at a time, from here, going forward.

*(pause)*

And the first entry in the vocabulary was: what I had seen standing in that cave.
The word I finally had, sitting against this wall three days later, was the name I gave
the cave.

**DR. HASSAN**
And Ahmad's sentence—

**TYLER**
His sentence is in the archive. It appears as Footnote #101. It will appear again here.

*(gesturing around)*

The Islamic geographers, beginning with the man in the Atlas foothills in December of 1119
who could not answer, who went south and began answering, one site at a time, for nine
centuries.

*(pause)*

He would have recognized it. If he had read it. If he could have.

**CAMERA OP**
He never read it.

**TYLER**
He is in it. He is the question. The archive is addressed to him. Every entry.

*(pause)*

He asked what I had seen. The archive is what I had seen. The last entry will be this:
arriving here, standing at this wall, understanding finally what I have been doing.

*(pause)*

I have been answering.

## CLOSE

*Marrakech. The old quarter. Dusk.*

*Jiangshi's field reader: Stolas 7.83, stable, plus the unclassified signal — the same
level it has been all day. Steady. Waiting.*

*Camera Op stands at the wall with her camera. She does not photograph it.*

*Tyler sits.*

---

**TYLER (V.O.)**
The first word was the name of the cave.

I did not know, when I wrote it, that it would be the first word.
I wrote it because I had been carrying it for three days and I needed
to put it somewhere outside myself. That is all the archive ever was.
A place to put what I was carrying.

*(pause)*

Ahmad asked what I had seen.

I have been writing it down ever since.

*(pause)*

He was here when I sat against this wall. I did not see him.
He saw me.

*(pause)*

Later, on the ship, he asked again. Still I could not answer.
He left for Fez. He wrote about me. He left a gap in the manuscript.
He died in 1121. His manuscripts were deposited at al-Qarawiyyin.
Nine centuries passed. Camera Op found the pages.
I read what he had written.

*(pause)*

The gap was the gap. He left it for me.

I was the word he was looking for.

I have been writing it for nine centuries.

*(pause)*

I still have not finished.

### TYLER-092 (Eastwind Entry 85 / Footnote #102)

*Filed by Tyler. MARRAKECH-001, Day 2.*

MARRAKECH-001. First entry: December 1119 CE. Return visit: Day 2 survey.

The archive began here. Sitting against the Almoravid wall. New stone — 1117 CE construction,
white plaster, still settling. The name I gave the cave was the first word. The cave name
is the first record. Every record since refers to it.

Ahmad ibn Yusuf (geographer, Rabat 1121 CE) was in this city when I sat against this wall.
He saw me. He did not speak. His manuscript's last sentence — "and yet the Islamic geographers,
beginning with—" — was going to name me. He did not have the word. The archive is the word.

Classification: ZERO-TAXONOMY / EXECUTION-SITE / ARCHIVE-ORIGIN.
Mechanism signal: Stolas 7.83 (baseline) + unclassified second signal (archive-state; first
reading of the execution site; signal persists without Tyler present once established).
Distance: 0ft. Explanation: MARRAKECH-001 is the origin-zero of the archive. All archive
distances are measured relative to this point. CAVE-001 is 0ft (pre-archive, decision); this
site is 0ft (archive-origin, execution). Two zeros, different meanings.

Footnote #102: The archive is addressed to Ahmad ibn Yusuf. The first word was the name of
the cave where he asked the question I could not answer. Every entry since December 1119 is
a continuation of the sentence he could not finish. The archive is his footnote. I am his
blank.

### Camera Op Entry 85

*Field log. Marrakech. S10E06.*

Arrived in Marrakech with forty-seven photographs of Ahmad's two geographic entries. We
spent the first night in the riad going through them again, looking for anything missed.
Nothing missed.

The next morning Tyler found the wall. I don't know how Tyler found it — the mechanism
was guiding Jiangshi along a frequency gradient, but Tyler moved ahead of the gradient.
Muscle memory from 900 years ago.

The wall is still there. 12th century stone, heavily repaired, still standing.

Tyler told us about the first word and I did not photograph the wall. I could not explain
why except that it felt like the wrong apparatus for that moment. I have a camera for
recording. This was not for recording. This was for witnessing.

I photographed Ahmad's manuscript in Fez because it needed to be documented. I stood at
the archive's origin point and I put the camera down.

We will return tomorrow to complete the mechanism survey. Jiangshi needs full perimeter
measurements. Tyler says the signal will hold.

I believe Tyler.

Ahmad wrote: "I am leaving for Marrakech on the third day." He got here before Tyler.
He saw Tyler sit against the wall and begin. He didn't say anything. He just watched.

Nine centuries later I also just watched.

I think that is what this site requires.

### Jiangshi Project Memo #072

*Project: TIDES OF PARADOX | Filed: Marrakech field station, Day 2*

**MEMO #072 — ARCHIVE-STATE SIGNAL. NEW SITE CLASSIFICATION. MARRAKECH-001.**

Site designation: MARRAKECH-001.
Location: Almoravid district, old medina, section near Koubba Ba'adiyin.
Construction date of surrounding structures: 1117 CE. Age at Tyler's first visit: ~2 years.

Mechanism signal: Stolas 7.83 (baseline, unchanged) + unclassified second signal
(archive-state reading; first appearance at this site; persists independent of Tyler's
proximity once established — distinct from CAVE-001 decision signal, which requires
Tyler's engagement to activate fully).

The CAVE-001 decision signal and MARRAKECH-001 archive signal coexist when Tyler
is present at both sites. When Tyler is at neither, both decay to baseline over time.
This site's signal began holding on Day 2, after Tyler spoke about the first word.
It has not decayed since. Jiangshi hypothesis: the mechanism has been waiting for
this site to be identified. Now identified, it locks.

**NEW CLASSIFICATION — MARRAKECH-001:**

ZERO-TAXONOMY / EXECUTION-SITE / ARCHIVE-ORIGIN

"Execution-site" is not a prior classification. It refers to the execution of the decision
made at CAVE-001: the act of writing, the first mark, the beginning of the archive.
CAVE-001 = decision. MARRAKECH-001 = execution. These are two halves of one origination event,
separated by ~40km and three days.

**DISTANCE NOTE:** Both CAVE-001 and MARRAKECH-001 are classified as 0ft. This requires
explanation. CAVE-001 is 0ft because it predates the archive (no construction, no measurement
possible, zero-taxonomy). MARRAKECH-001 is 0ft because it IS the archive's zero-reference —
all archive distances are measured relative to this point. The mechanism uses this site
as the baseline for all historical distance readings. Every 412ft and 191ft and 97ft
in the archive is 412ft and 191ft and 97ft from here.

**Summary:** We have the origin. Decision at CAVE-001. Execution at MARRAKECH-001.
Three days between them. Ahmad watched the execution from this city and did not interfere.
The sentence he was writing has been completing for nine hundred years.

It is still completing.
