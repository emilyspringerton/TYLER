---

The editor frantically tries to assemble a coherent timeline. Clips reorder themselves on the timeline after every export.

**EDITOR**
I keep cutting the same confession three different ways and none of them feel like the truth anymore.

---

INTERCUT — DOCUMENTARY CAMERA B (HANDHELD)

Close on: condensation pooled at the base of a forgotten coffee mug. A printed waveform graph with red circles around 1.983 Hz, 3.582 Hz, and 7.554 Hz. **PRODUCER (O.S., whisper)**

### [I.B — Tyler enters]

Tyler walks in carrying responsibility like a well-worn coat. No guilt. Only precision.

*(calm, measured)*

**TYLER (CONT.)**
Guilt is emotional debt. Responsibility is structural maintenance.
I broke the year. I carry the year. Simple arithmetic across versions.

The monitors behind him flicker — each showing a slightly different take of him saying the exact same line.

**ON SCREEN:**
EMOTIONAL INTEGRITY: GUILT = 0% | RESPONSIBILITY = 100%

## ACT III — THE CAMERA AS AN ENGINE

---

### [III.A — Revelation]

Tyler stands. The lights dim as if the room itself is listening harder.

**TYLER**
Filming isn't documentation anymore.
It's propulsion.
Every second you keep rolling, you pour more narrative fuel into the loop.
I am responsible for starting this.
You are responsible for keeping it alive.

He looks straight into the main lens.

**TYLER (CONT.)**
So turn it off.
Or admit you like the story more than the exit.

---

### [III.B — Blackout proposal]

Tyler proposes the experiment: all devices off. No recording for sixty seconds.

For eleven seconds the screen goes completely black.
Then subtitles appear in total darkness, glowing:

**ON SCREEN (darkness)**
CAMERA STILL RUNNING IN LAYER 9
RESPONSIBILITY INCREASING

The lights return. Tyler is now holding the clapperboard from Episode 3.
