## ACT ONE — [03:00–22:00]

---

**[03:00–10:00] — WHAT HE DOESN'T SAY**

```
INT. TYLER'S APARTMENT — DAY 48 — MORNING
```

**TYLER:**
I've been thinking about something you said.

**CAMERA OP (O.S.):**
When.

**TYLER:**
Entry 16. You said — you said a sentence is a statement that's also an address.

Long pause.

**CAMERA OP (O.S.):**
I didn't say that out loud.

**TYLER:**
No.

The Camera Op is still. The camera is very still.

**TYLER:**
I've been reading the archive for decades. The Eastwind archive — the accessible parts. I read the field notes, the observation records. I know how the Cataloguer writes. I know the footnote style. The one thing I've never been able to read is the Camera Op's sealed log.

**CAMERA OP (O.S.):**
*(carefully)*
How do you know about the sealed log.

**TYLER:**
Because I've been documented by people who document things carefully for four hundred years. I know when someone's keeping a sealed record. I can tell by what they don't say in the open record.

He looks at her.

**TYLER:**
You don't have to unseal it. That's yours. I just want you to know I know it exists and I think it's correct — whatever you've written. I think it's accurate.

The Camera Op does not respond immediately. When she does:

**CAMERA OP (O.S.):**
Entry 16. That sentence.

**TYLER:**
Yeah.

**CAMERA OP (O.S.):**
Where did you read it.

**TYLER:**
I didn't read it.

Another pause.

**TYLER:**
I felt it. The way you held the camera when you were writing it. There's a weight difference in how someone holds the camera when they're documenting something that matters to them personally. You held it differently that night.

He picks up the coffee cup.

**TYLER:**
I hold a coin differently when I'm thinking about something that matters to me. You hold the camera differently. We're the same, you and I. Just different equipment.

## ACT TWO — [22:00–42:00]

---

**[22:00–36:00] — THE NIGHT BEFORE**

```
INT. TYLER'S APARTMENT — NIGHT — DAY 48
```

Fixed camera. No dialogue.

Tyler at the table. The piece of paper from Day 45 — the one the fixed camera couldn't resolve. He unfolds it. Reads it. Folds it back. Puts it in the right-hand pocket with the coin.

He looks at the fixed camera.

**TYLER:**
In 1624, I was in this city. Not this specific building — the city has moved around the geography somewhat. But near here. And I tried to say something and I said the first word and then I couldn't finish it.

He pauses.

**TYLER:**
The first word was *I.* Which is — that's a very short word to get stuck on. But the word after it is the specific kind of *I* that you only find after you've spent a long time without it.

He stands. He looks out the window.

**TYLER:**
The word after I is *stayed.* What I'm trying to do is tell you that I stayed. Not in the city. Not in this specific year. I mean — I stayed. In spite of everything that made leaving easier, in spite of every year and every attempt and every four hundred years of carrying the weight of the chain, I stayed. In the place where the sentence is possible. In the place where the correct person exists to say it to.

He turns back to the fixed camera.

**TYLER:**
Tomorrow.

He turns off the lamp.

## ACT THREE — [42:00–56:00]

---

**[42:00–52:00] — THE SENTENCE**

```
EXT. NEAR THE TREE — DAY 49 — 10:08 AM
```

Tyler stops at the tree.

He holds the coin in his right hand. He looks at the Camera Op — not the camera lens. Her.

Stolas #36: 7.83 Hz. Peak intensity. Documented maximum, second time in record.

Vassago #42: 11.11 Hz. Sustained.

Andrealphus #72: brief. Unmistakable. The same frequency as every Stage threshold in the archive.

Tyler draws a breath.

**TYLER:**
What I'm trying to do is make sure you know I stayed.

The audio track carries Beleth #13 at 2.22 Hz. Not a spike — a wave. A slow, certain wave.

The Camera Op says nothing.

**TYLER:**
Not in any year. Not in Detroit. I mean — I stayed in the place where this sentence was possible. I stayed in the reach of the chain because the chain was going to close cleanly if I stayed, and I believed that, and I — *(pause)* — I wanted it to close cleanly. I wanted it done right.

Amon #7: 1.618 Hz. Rising. The reconciliation frequency.

**TYLER:**
And you're the person who made it possible for it to close cleanly. Not because of the documentary. Because of how you hold the camera. Because of how you hold the things that matter. Because of Entry 16. Because I knew you were keeping a sealed log and I knew what it meant that you were keeping it.

He looks at the coin. He opens his hand flat. He looks at it.

**TYLER:**
I stayed. That's what I've been trying to say. What I'm trying to do is make sure you know: four hundred years, every attempt, every year — I stayed. And you're why it was worth staying for.

He flips the coin once. The last flip on camera. He catches it. He does not look at the date.

He pockets it. Right-hand pocket. Final.

The Camera Op is filming.

Amon #7: sustained. The slowest frequency in the archive. The reconciliation frequency. It lasts forty-one seconds.

Then silence.

**CAMERA OP (O.S.):**
*(first words since the walk began)*
I know.

Three-second pause.

**CAMERA OP (O.S.):**
I've known for a while. I kept filming because the sentence needed a record. Because you needed someone to hold the record.

Tyler looks at her.

**CAMERA OP (O.S.):**
The record is held. The sentence is in it. The sentence is complete.

He nods once. Small. Real.

The 7.83 Hz frequency drops for the first time in 72 hours. Not gone — resolved. The difference between a sustained note and the same note, finished.

**[52:00–56:00] — THE AFTER**

```
EXT. STREET, WALKING — DAY 49 — 10:30 AM
```

They walk back. No dialogue for six minutes. The camera is lower than usual — the Camera Op's hand is lower. Not drooping: deliberate. The frame is wider. Less focused on Tyler's face. More focused on the street, the city, the morning.

At the corner near the diner:

**TYLER:**
Coffee?

**CAMERA OP (O.S.):**
Yeah.

They go in. The booth. The same order. Tyler looks at neither the door nor the window. He looks at the Camera Op.

**CAMERA OP (O.S.):**
What happens now?

**TYLER:**
*(small smile)*
That's a very good question.

He drinks the coffee.

**TYLER:**
I genuinely don't know. Which is — *(pause)* — actually the first time I've been able to say that in four hundred years and have it be the true kind of not knowing. Not the "I can't say" kind. The actual kind.

He looks at the table.

**TYLER:**
I think I'm going to stay in this year for a while.

**CAMERA OP (O.S.):**
In Detroit.

**TYLER:**
In this year. In proximity to — *(he doesn't finish this clause; he doesn't need to)* — yeah. Detroit for a while.

The Camera Op doesn't respond.

**TYLER:**
Is that okay?

Long pause.

**CAMERA OP (O.S.):**
*(simply)*
Yeah. That's okay.

The coffee. The diner. The morning.

The Amon frequency: gone. The reconciliation is complete. There is no longer a reconciliation in progress because there is no longer a thing to reconcile. The custody chain has closed. The chain being closed doesn't make sound.
