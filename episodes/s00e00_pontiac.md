The establishing shot is not glamorous. This is on purpose.

Three-story buildings, half of them occupied. A parking lot that used to be something. A new green awning going up on a corner storefront — two workers on scaffolding, one holding the bracket, one with a drill. The awning says STINKIES COMMISSAIRE in block letters on a matte black background.

The lettering is being measured. Not yet level. The worker with the drill adjusts.

Street-level: a woman in work clothes is directing from the sidewalk. She has a clipboard. She is pointing upward. Her name is not important yet.

At the far end of the block, walking at a pace that does not hurry and does not drag, is a man.

He notices the awning. He slows, but does not stop. He reads the sign — STINKIES COMMISSAIRE — and something in his expression does a calculation. Not a smile exactly. The precursor to a smile, held back for reasons he probably has a name for.

He keeps walking. He walks toward the store.

**[00:45–02:00] — INTERIOR: STINKIES COMMISSAIRE, STORE 0 — CONTINUOUS**

```
INT. STINKIES COMMISSAIRE — STORE 0 — MORNING
```

The store is soft-open. The shelves are ninety percent stocked. The floor still has that particular smell — sealed concrete, recent — that takes three weeks to leave a new space. Behind the counter: a COUNTER PERSON, mid-twenties, reading something on their phone with the particular attention of someone who is actually reading, not scrolling.

The product arrangement:

On the counter nearest the door: a stack of soap bars. Off-white. Kraft paper band. STINKIES stamped in block type. No price tag. The stack has that slightly handmade quality of something bought in very large quantities that has not yet been sorted into display-ready units.

On the shelf behind the counter: Mountain Man Coffee (12oz bags, matte black) and Powderhorn Coffee (12oz bags, same aesthetic, slightly different text layout). A hand-lettered sign: *WHOLE BEAN · GROUND TO ORDER.* A grinder sits on the shelf that is clearly new and has not been used many times yet.

Left wall: a glass-door cooler with Miller High Life — 6-pack, 12-pack, 30-pack. A small printed card: *21+ · MICHIGAN SDM LICENSE #PENDING.* The license is in process.

Center: cheese sauce, hot dogs (chest freezer, recently delivered). A promotional card for the STINKIES NACHO KIT leans against the freezer lid.

The man walks in.

He pauses at the threshold for a half-second — the way a person pauses when they're entering a space that is newer than it looks, when the geometry hasn't settled yet. Then he walks to the counter.

He looks at the soap. He picks up a bar. He turns it over. He reads the stamp: STINKIES. He sets it on the counter on the customer side. He looks at the Counter Person.

**THE MAN**
Mountain Man.

The Counter Person looks up from their phone.

**COUNTER PERSON**
Whole bean or ground?

**THE MAN**
Ground.

A beat. The Counter Person stands, moves to the grinder. The man watches the process without impatience. While the grinder runs, he looks out the window. He watches the workers on the scaffolding. The awning bracket goes in. Level now.

The Counter Person hands over a bag of ground coffee. Rings up: $14.

The man pays. Cash, exact change.

He is handed a receipt. He looks at it. He looks at it for a moment longer than receipts usually warrant.

The date on the receipt: February 14, 2027.

The register is displaying correctly. The date in the top right of the screen reads June 27, 2026. The receipt disagrees. The Counter Person doesn't notice. The man folds the receipt and puts it in his jacket pocket. He picks up the soap bar — free, already on his side of the counter — and puts that in his pocket too.

He looks at the window once more. The awning is level.

**THE MAN**
*(to no one in particular, or to the room, or to the architecture)*
Looks right.

He walks out.

The hum starts approximately two seconds after he's gone. Low. Sustained. 7.83 Hz. The grinder was louder, so the Counter Person didn't hear it start. By the time they look up from the register, it's already present, already continuous, already at the frequency it was always going to be.

The Counter Person goes back to their phone.
