### ACT ONE — A-288

**[EXTERIOR — BURGTHEATER, DR.-KARL-LUEGER-RING. MORNING.]**

*[The hum is present. The Camera Op is positioned across the ring road. Wide shot — Tyler and the Exchange Student small against the imperial facade.]*

The theater is open but dark. Summer maintenance season. Through the main entrance a workman is moving a lighting fixture. The institution is present and continuous.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The register says *das Zögern.* No measurement. You left before you finished deciding."

**TYLER:** "I was here from 1895. I knew the building. I knew the repertoire. I knew — by July 1938 I knew who was still here and who had been forced out. There had been a great many dismissals in April and May. Actors, directors. The theater was still operating." He pauses. "The institution was present. Certain people I knew were still in it. Others were not."

They enter the atrium. A long corridor. The morning light from the side windows falls in regular intervals across the floor.

*[The hum intensifies slightly. Stage direction only — not noted by any character.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "And the pull?"

**TYLER:** "The pull existed. I stood in this atrium for sixty-eight minutes. I asked, as precisely as I knew how to ask: is this toward the building? Toward the people who remained? Toward the performances that are still happening? Toward the people who were forced out?" He looks at the corridor. "I could not separate them. The institution is present. The absences are present in the institution. I couldn't isolate what the mechanism was responding to."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "What did the 1938 rule say?"

**TYLER:** "The rule said: if you don't understand the pull, wait. I waited sixty-eight minutes. Understanding did not come."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Then?"

**TYLER:** "Then I applied the extension: if understanding does not come with time, leave. File: ambiguous. Refused." He pauses. "The third category. The first time it applied."

---

**[EXTERIOR — RING ROAD, AFTER.]**

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "A-288. Nine feet."

*She writes it. The number is small for sixty-eight minutes. The mechanism records the duration of the waiting, not the depth of the confusion.*

**TYLER:** "The trace records the refusal. Not my inability to name the presence. Nine feet for sixty-eight minutes of trying to read what the place needed."

**CAMERA OP:** "Did it cost something? Leaving."

*Tyler turns the tape measure in his hands.*

**TYLER:** "More than A-283."

**CAMERA OP:** "Why more?"

**TYLER:** "At Wollzeile 7 I knew exactly what I was refusing. The purpose was absent — I said no on purpose. Here I couldn't name what I was refusing. There might have been a presence worth staying for. I don't know. I still don't know." He looks back at the theater. "Ambiguity is its own weight. The hesitation was not uncertainty about the rule. It was uncertainty about what the rule was being applied to."

*Long pause.*

**TYLER:** "I came back the next morning. I stood outside for ten minutes. The ambiguity was still there. I filed it and moved on."

**CAMERA OP:** "You tried twice."

**TYLER:** "I tried twice. The third category: you wait, and if understanding doesn't come, you leave, and if you come back the next day and the understanding is still not there, you file it and move on."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(quietly)* "The rule is still being written."

**TYLER:** "Vienna is still writing it."

He writes in the register.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT (V.O., reading):** "*A-288. Burgtheater. 9 feet. 68 minutes. Ambiguity refusal. Third category — confirmed. Understanding did not come on first or second visit. Filed: ambiguous. The hesitation is its own kind of record. I don't know what was there. The trace knows I tried.*"

### ACT TWO — A-289

**[EXTERIOR — NASCHMARKT, LINKE WIENZEILE. AFTERNOON.]**

The market is loud and hot. Stand 22 is a flower and herb vendor. The current vendor — a man in his forties — is arranging sunflowers in tin buckets.

**TYLER:** "She had good parsley. She had it organized by how close it was to ready — you could tell from how she placed it. The best was always third from the left, back row."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Is she still here?"

**TYLER:** "She would be —" He calculates. "One hundred and thirty-one this year. She is not here." He looks at the current vendor. "Something of her is still here. The stand knows how to be a certain kind of stand. He has his own arrangement. But there's — continuity. In how the stand is approached."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The pull?"

**TYLER:** "Clear. Completely clear. The stand still knows someone who comes for parsley and doesn't cook with it."

*He stands at the edge of the stall for ninety minutes. He buys parsley he will not use. The current vendor wraps it without asking if he needs it wrapped.*

---

**[BRIDGE NEARBY, AFTER.]**

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Twenty-nine feet."

**TYLER:** "I was staying for the continuity of a particular kind of knowing. Frau B. knew her customers the way Herr M. knew his. She knew what I came for. She never asked why. The stand has been that kind of stand since the 1920s. The current vendor — he's that kind of vendor. He didn't ask why I wanted parsley either." Tyler writes in the register. "Presence is what still knows you. The stand knows the parsley customer."

**CAMERA OP:** "Even after eighty-eight years."

**TYLER:** "Even after. The knowing survives the person who originally did the knowing. It passes into the place. Into how the stall is operated." He looks at the parsley in his hand. "I'll leave this on the railing."

He leaves it on the bridge railing. The Camera Op holds the shot on the parsley, then on Tyler walking away.

### ACT THREE — A-290

**[EXTERIOR — JUDENGASSE, FIRST DISTRICT. MORNING. DAY 266.]**

A residential building in the first district. Renovated facade but the proportions are continuous with 1938.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Three visits. April 23rd, May 11th, July 14th. Then: *I didn't know what I was building.*"

**TYLER:** "A family I had known for eleven years lived here. By early May they were gone. I first came here in late April — before I knew what the shape of the summer was going to be. I came because the pull was there. Stayed for an hour. Filed it and moved on." He pauses. "I came back in May. The pull was still there. I stayed for half an hour. I came back in July. I stayed for twenty minutes."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Shorter each time."

**TYLER:** "I didn't know what I was building. I was coming back to confirm that the pull was still there. That the place still held what it had held. I thought I was checking." He looks at the building. "I was depositing."

The Exchange Student measures.

*She starts at the building and walks outward. She pauses at forty feet, continues. She walks to sixty feet. She comes back, measures more carefully.*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Sixty-four feet."

*Tyler is still.*

**TYLER:** "That's not right for three visits at those durations."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The visits compounded. The mechanism accumulates between returns." She writes. "You came back on May 11th. The April deposit was still running. Your second visit added to what was already there — it didn't restart. By July, you were depositing into a construction you had already been building for two months."

**TYLER:** "The return amplified it."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "More than that. The return is its own event. The act of coming back — the mechanism records it as a separate deposit from the duration of the stay. You were here for one hour, then thirty minutes, then twenty. Decreasing duration. But the total is sixty-four feet. Because the return itself deposited."

*Tyler produces the coin from his right pocket. He turns it in the light — the date shifts at the edge of the Camera Op's focus, just past her reaction time. He reads it. He puts it back.*

**TYLER:** "I was coming back to cities because I needed to know they were still themselves. Still holding what they'd always held. I thought that was witness. I thought coming back to confirm was just — documentation."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "It was construction."

**TYLER:** "Yes."

He writes in the register.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT (V.O., reading later):** "*A-290. Judengasse 8. 64 feet. Three visits. April 23, May 11, July 14. Duration decreasing. Total increasing. I didn't know what I was building. I came back because the pull was still there. The return deposited. The returns were the construction. I have been coming back to places for a very long time. This is what that was doing.*"

**CAMERA OP:** *(quietly)* "Is this the fourth category?"

**TYLER:** "This is the beginning of understanding it."

### ACT FOUR — A-291

**[EXTERIOR — STADTPARK, JOHANNESGASSE ENTRANCE. MORNING.]**

*[The hum is present here. It has been present every morning in this park. The Camera Op's log, Entry 50, notes it for the first time — "I should have written this down weeks ago."]*

The park is mid-morning. Dog walkers, a woman reading on a bench further along, the particular light through the large trees that have been here for over a hundred years. Tyler goes directly to a bench at the eastern edge of the main path, near the rose garden.

He sits down.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The register entry has no address. Just: *täglich. Morgens. Immer.* And a separate slip of paper, different day."

**TYLER:** "I wrote it the last morning. August 14th. I was leaving Vienna the next day. I sat here one final morning and I wrote it down before I went."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You came here every morning?"

**TYLER:** "From May 1st. Some mornings earlier than others. This bench when it was available, the next closest one when it wasn't. Usually this one." He looks at the path. "I came here before anything else. I sat until I could be in Vienna for that day. Then I went."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "One hundred and seven days."

**TYLER:** "I lost count around June."

*The Exchange Student starts measuring.*

*She walks from the bench, outward along the path. She passes 50 feet and continues. 100 feet. She continues. 150 feet. She is now a significant distance from the bench. She turns, walks back, takes a different angle. She repeats the measurement on the diagonal.*

*At 210 feet she finds the edge.*

*She stops. She stands there for a moment.*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Two hundred and ten feet."

*She says it quietly.*

Tyler is still on the bench, watching the path.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Two hundred and ten feet. One hundred and seven mornings at various durations. The largest Vienna site. The second largest in the full audit after A-273."

**TYLER:** "I was here longer, cumulatively, than I was at any single site. Short durations but the return—"

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The return deposited every time."

**TYLER:** "Yes."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "This is why you didn't name the fourth category. You had to finish measuring Vienna to know the shape of it."

**TYLER:** "I knew what the category was when I saw A-290. But I needed A-291 to understand the scale. To understand what it was doing."

---

**[TYLER — ON THE BENCH. THE CAMERA OP STANDING AT 210 FEET, FILMING BACK.]**

*[The shot from 210 feet: Tyler on the bench, the park behind him, the path extending. He is very still. He looks like someone who has sat in this spot a great many times.]*

**TYLER (to the Exchange Student, quietly):** "The first three categories are about refusal. When to leave, why to leave, what the trace records when you leave. Pre-awareness. Purpose-based. Ambiguity. The archive now has all three."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "And the fourth."

**TYLER:** "The fourth is about what happens when you keep returning to the same place. Not for a specific event. Not for a specific person. Not because of a deliberate construction protocol. Because the place holds something that requires your regular presence to remain held. And because—" He pauses. "Coming back to it confirms that you are also still there."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You were checking if Vienna was still Vienna."

**TYLER:** "And if I was still — " He stops. Starts differently. "I came here every morning because Vienna required me to be careful and small and present. And I needed somewhere that cost nothing. The bench asked nothing of me. The park asked nothing. I came and I sat and I read the light and the trees and then I went and tried to be somewhere useful for that day. And every morning I came back."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "And each time you came back, it deposited."

**TYLER:** "Each time. Because returning is the act. The mechanism doesn't respond only to the duration of a single stay. It responds to the pattern of coming back. To the evidence that you were here yesterday and you are here again today. The mechanism treats the return as confirmation of the original deposit — and then adds its own layer."

*The Camera Op walks back toward the bench. She takes her time. She has the camera on its strap, not raised.*

**TYLER:** "The fourth category is—" He looks at the path, the trees, the morning light working its way across the grass. "I don't have the word for it."

*The Camera Op arrives at the bench. She sits down next to him.*

*For a moment neither of them speaks.*

**CAMERA OP:** "I do."

**TYLER:** *(looking at her)* "What?"

**CAMERA OP:** "Staying."

*Silence.*

**TYLER:** "I came back every morning. I didn't stay — I left every morning and came back."

**CAMERA OP:** "You kept coming back to the same place. Every morning. For one hundred and seven days. That's staying. It doesn't require you to be physically present continuously. It requires you to keep choosing the same place." She looks at the path. "You were staying in Vienna. Not at this bench. In Vienna. The bench was where you renewed it every morning."

*Tyler says nothing.*

**CAMERA OP:** "The fourth category is how you stay somewhere when staying is hard. You come back. Every morning, you come back. The mechanism records each return as a continuation of a single act. The act is: staying."

*[A very still moment. Three seconds. Tyler looks at the Camera Op. Two seconds of direct eye contact — the camera catches it, holds it, releases it. The two-second mark. Tyler's gaze moves back to the path.]*

**TYLER:** "That's—"

*He does not finish.*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(writing)* "I'm putting it in the register."

**TYLER (quietly):** "Write it down."

---

**[EXCHANGE STUDENT — READING THE FINAL REGISTER ENTRY OUT LOUD.]**

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "*A-291. Stadtpark. 210 feet. 107 mornings. täglich. Morgens. Immer. The fourth category: Staying. Defined: the construction that builds from the pattern of return. Not the duration of a single stay. Not a specific event or person. The act of coming back — regularly — because the place holds something that requires your presence to remain held. Rule: each return deposits. The mechanism accumulates between visits. It is patient. It waits for you to come back.*"

*She closes the register.*
