## BACKGROUND

*Riad. The Camera Op and Tyler sitting in the courtyard. Morning light beginning. Tea.*

**CAMERA OP:**
Tell me about 1127.

**TYLER:**
The Almoravid dynasty. The city was thirty years old — Marrakech was founded in 1062 by Ibn Tashfin. By 1127 it was the capital of an empire that stretched from the Maghreb into Andalusia. It was one of the largest cities in the western Islamic world. The Koutoubia mosque didn't exist yet — that comes later. The city I arrived in was still building itself.

**CAMERA OP:**
How long were you there?

**TYLER:**
Four years. 1127 to 1131.

**CAMERA OP:**
And you've known about this site since when?

**TYLER:**
Since the archive. The Eastwind system recorded a Marrakech entry in 1622 — the first year of formal documentation. Someone in the Eastwind organization had a record going back to 1127. I've always assumed it was based on something I told them. I don't remember telling them. But I don't remember a lot from 1127.

**CAMERA OP:**
What do you remember?

**TYLER:**
The heat. The scale of the mosque construction happening in the city — the original mosque, before the Koutoubia. The smell of the tanneries. The arguments about theology and mathematics. Ibn Tashfin's scholars were doing serious mathematical work — optics, astronomy. I remember being in rooms full of people who were thinking precisely about things I found worth thinking about.

**CAMERA OP:**
DWELLING.

**TYLER:**
*(pause)*
Possibly. Or — I was very different in 1127. I had been on the earth for a shorter time. I don't know if the mechanism worked the same way it does now. I don't know if I was the same person who generates the constructions I generate now.

**CAMERA OP:**
What do you mean?

**TYLER:**
The mechanism is calibrated to Tyler. Or I am calibrated to the mechanism. I don't know which is the origin and which is the consequence. By 1127 I had already been — I had been alive for some time. But I had not yet been alive for the time I have subsequently been alive. I don't know if the constructions I generated in 1127 look like the constructions I generate now.

**CAMERA OP:**
That's a question we're about to answer.

## THE DEPARTURE — A-MARRAKECH-1131

*Southern medina. The location of the Bab Agnao gate, one of the original gates of the city. Tyler walks to it.*

*He stands at the gate for a moment before the instrument.*

**CAMERA OP:**
What kind of departure was it?

**TYLER:**
*(long pause)*
I don't know. I don't remember leaving the way I remember leaving Prague or Lyon or Amsterdam. I remember being in Marrakech and I remember being not in Marrakech. The departure itself is — unclear to me.

**CAMERA OP:**
FAREWELL-A would mean you knew you were leaving permanently.

**TYLER:**
I knew I was leaving. I don't know if I knew it was permanent. In 1131 I had not yet understood the permanence of my departures. I knew I left places. I didn't yet understand that I never fully returned.

**CAMERA OP:**
You hadn't developed the concept.

**TYLER:**
I hadn't needed to. I had been leaving places all my life and I had always been somewhere new and not yet somewhere old. The idea that a departure meant something — that it was irreversible, that it cost something — I don't think I understood that in 1131.

**CAMERA OP:**
So what type?

**TYLER:**
I think it's FAREWELL-C. Established pattern. I left the way I always left. Confident that the pattern of departure-and-arrival would continue. Not conscious of permanence. Not absent from myself. Just — leaving the way I always left.

**FAREWELL-C ratio established:**

A-Marrakech-1131: FAREWELL-C, 25ft. Prior presence: 117ft. Ratio: 0.214 ≈ 0.21.

Compare to FAREWELL-A ratio: 0.36.

FAREWELL-C extracts approximately 21% of prior accumulated presence. FAREWELL-A extracts 36%. The difference: FAREWELL-C is an established-pattern departure — the subject believes they will return, so the mechanism leaves more behind. FAREWELL-A is a conscious-permanent departure — the subject knows they will not return, so the mechanism extracts more.

The departure type determines how much is taken versus left. Conscious permanence takes more. Expected return takes less.

**Implication for network size:**

If FAREWELL-C and FAREWELL-B sites exist throughout the archive (which they do — approximately 300 of 900 sites are non-FAREWELL-A departure types), those sites may contain substantially more than 64% remainder. FAREWELL-C sites leave 79% behind. If this holds for all non-A types, the global remainder is substantially larger than estimated.

Revised estimate: global unmeasured remainder across all 900 sites, factoring in type-weighted ratios, may exceed 30,000ft.

*Cataloguer Footnote #87: 1127 is the oldest archived entry. It is not necessarily the oldest construction. Tyler was alive before 1127. Tyler does not know how long before 1127. The mechanism, if active before 1127, has been generating constructions for an unknown period before the archive's oldest entry. The CARDINAL-3 network's true minimum age is unknown. It is older than 899 years.*

The consistency question.

Prague (1351) DWELLING profile: accelerating density, work-site generates more per month than residence, ratio of work-to-residence approximately 1.3:1.

Marrakech (1127) DWELLING profile: accelerating density, work-site generates more per month than residence, ratio of work-to-residence approximately 1.27:1.

The mechanism operates consistently across 224 years. The same subject, the same construction type, produces the same internal ratios separated by two centuries.

This suggests the mechanism is not a product of Tyler's age or experience at any particular moment. It is a stable function operating on Tyler's presence. The function produces consistent outputs for consistent input types.

Alternately: Tyler is the mechanism, and Tyler is consistent in what he brings to chosen presence regardless of the century.

The distinction is not measurable. Both interpretations predict the same observations.

*Cataloguer Footnote #88: The FAREWELL-C departure at Marrakech in 1131 — Tyler departing without understanding permanence — is the first documented instance of the gap between what Tyler understood about his departures and what the mechanism understood. The mechanism recorded a departure as significant. Tyler did not understand why it was significant. This gap, first seen at 1131, has been a consistent feature of the retrograde. The mechanism has always known something Tyler did not.*
