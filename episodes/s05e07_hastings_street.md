## EPISODE STRUCTURE

**Day 183, morning — what Tyler knows**

Emily arrives at 9 AM. Tyler is at the table with photograph 2 and a Detroit street atlas from 1952 — Bacon's, from the building's reference shelf.

**EMILY:** "Hastings Street."

**TYLER:** "Yes."

**EMILY:** "June 1943. What happened?"

**TYLER:** "The riot." He looks at the photograph. "June 20, 1943. The Detroit race riot. 34 people dead, hundreds injured, the city in federal occupation for three days. Hastings Street was in the middle of it."

**EMILY:** "But your annotation says 'something ended here.' Specific to this corner."

**TYLER:** "The riot ended a lot of things. Most of them got recorded — the dead, the damaged buildings, the insurance claims. But there are things that ended at specific addresses that aren't in any record. Not because they were hidden. Because the people who knew what was there were the ones who lost it, and they left."

**EMILY:** "What's at this corner in the photograph?"

**TYLER:** "In 1952 — a boarded-up storefront. Empty for nine years." He pauses. "I went to 1952 to document what was still there. This corner was already gone. What I photographed was the absence."

---

**Day 183, late morning — the Exchange Student and the archives**

The Exchange Student has been at Bacon's building for three days, cross-referencing historical records with the CEDAR function's dependency mapping.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "I found something." She brings a folder to the table — photocopies from the Detroit Public Library archives, a 1943 newspaper index, and two pages from an oral history project conducted in 1978 by Wayne State University students.

She opens to a specific page.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The 1978 oral history. An interview with a man named James Elwood, then 62, born on Hastings Street. He talks about a gathering place at the corner of Hastings and Vernor — not a bar, not a church, not a formal club. He calls it 'the room where we practiced.' He says musicians would come on Sunday afternoons and work on things they weren't ready to play publicly yet. Nothing was recorded. That was the point."

She puts the page in front of Tyler.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "He says it stopped in June 1943 and never started again. He doesn't say why. The riot is the context the interviewer provides. But James Elwood doesn't say 'because of the riot.' He says 'it stopped and people left and when they came back it wasn't the room anymore.'"

Tyler reads the passage twice.

**TYLER:** "The room held something that wasn't finished. Music that was never ready to be public. A practice that existed only in the act of practicing."

**EMILY:** "Like photograph 5. Conant Street. 'Something started and stopped deliberately.'"

**TYLER:** "Conant Street is an uncompleted thing that was put down. Hastings is different. This wasn't put down deliberately. It was interrupted. The people who held it dispersed. The room couldn't continue without them and they couldn't return to it."

**EMILY:** "So the event isn't a completion. It's an interruption."

**TYLER:** "Yes. A Witness Room for an interrupted thing. The room holds the moment before the interruption — the last Sunday, the last practice session, the last time the room was what it was meant to be."

---

**Day 183, midday — the complication**

The Exchange Student opens her laptop.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Here's the problem." She turns the screen. "The building at Hastings and Vernor doesn't exist anymore. The entire block was demolished in 1962 during the Black Bottom urban renewal project. There's a highway ramp there now."

Silence.

**EMILY:** "The container is gone."

**TYLER:** "Yes."

**EMILY:** "How do you build a Witness Room when the container has been demolished?"

He looks at the photograph. The 1952 image: boarded storefront, already nine years empty. The room had already been interrupted by 1952. He photographed the building because it was still standing. It isn't anymore.

**TYLER:** "I don't know yet."

This is the second time in Season 5 he has said he doesn't know yet. Emily writes it in the notebook without comment. The archive will note it.

**TYLER:** "The event happened in a specific place. The place is gone. The question is whether the event is still tethered to the geography when the container no longer exists."

**EMILY:** "Is it?"

**TYLER:** "I think so. The crossing at the Ambassador Bridge has been happening since before the bridge — the bridge is infrastructure, not the container. The container is the crossing point in the river. The highway ramp is infrastructure. The container might still be the geography." He pauses. "I need to go there."

---

**Day 183, afternoon — Hastings and Vernor**

The highway ramp. Concrete and traffic. The air smells like exhaust and the river a mile east.

Tyler stands at the corner — or where the corner was. The geography is still there: the intersection of Hastings Street (now gone, absorbed into the expressway approach) and the ghost of Vernor Highway. The land is under concrete. The crossing point is buried.

He stands there for forty minutes.

The Camera Op films from the expressway shoulder, a position she found herself — not the corner, not the median, the shoulder of the approach ramp, looking down at Tyler standing on the sidewalk remnant beside the concrete.

When Tyler returns to the group:

**TYLER:** "It's there."

**EMILY:** "The event."

**TYLER:** "The geography is buried but the event is present. Interrupted things don't dissipate — they wait. The music that was never ready to be public has been waiting under this concrete for eighty years."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Can you build on top of a highway ramp?"

**TYLER:** "No. The container is the geography, not the infrastructure. I need to get below the infrastructure." He looks at the concrete. "Or find a way to work with the container from the surface."

He takes out photograph 2. He looks at it, and at the expressway approach, and at the geography beneath.

**TYLER:** "This one is going to require a different method. I don't know what it is yet. I'm going to think."

Emily notes it: *Hastings/Vernor — event present, container buried under I-375 approach ramp. Construction method TBD. Tyler studying.*
