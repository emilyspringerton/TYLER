**Day 215, morning — at Bacon's building**

Back at the building. Tyler makes coffee. He has slept three hours. He looks awake.

**EMILY:** "What happened at 5:12?"

**TYLER:** "The calibration sealed. I've been watching it approach completion for two weeks — the passive witness pattern was converging, the resonance was tightening. Today it crossed the threshold. It's self-sustaining now without calibration." He pours coffee. "It will hold as long as people cross the river."

**EMILY:** "What happens if the bridge closes?"

**TYLER:** "The geography is the container. Not the bridge. The bridge is infrastructure." He pauses. "If the bridge closes, people will find another way to cross. The crossing point in the river doesn't change. It's been a crossing point since before the bridge existed." He drinks the coffee. "The crossing holds."

**EMILY:** "How long?"

**TYLER:** "I don't know. Longer than I'll be alive to check."

She writes it down. He looks at the map.

**TYLER:** "Four complete. One found. Four candidates still in the notebook. Seven archive sites to visit." He looks at the crossing position on the map. "The network has more nodes than I thought when I started. It also has more time ahead of it than I thought. I don't think this is a Season 5 project. I think this is a longer arc."

**EMILY:** "Season 6."

**TYLER:** "Season 6, 7, maybe further. The photograph archive goes back to 1947. Some of those constructions I found sixty, seventy years ago. The newest passive construction — Hastings/Vernor — started thirty-one days ago. The oldest — Bethel AME — has been running for sixty-nine years." He sets the coffee down. "I'm not building a network. I'm finding one that was already being built."
