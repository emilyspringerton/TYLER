## ACT ONE — THE VIENNA FILE, REVISED

*[Vienna. Morning. The Exchange Student has the revised CARDINAL-3 Vienna file open on the table — the same café near Judenplatz she's been using as a base since they arrived. The original 12 sites are documented. Three flagged in the margin in her handwriting: **undisclosed. PRIOR probable. Confirm on-site.** Next to them, the three locations.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "I ran the PRIOR filter on the Vienna file the same way I ran it on London. The model is now calibrated. Paris confirmed it; London refined it." She turns the file toward Tyler. "The filter returns three sites that meet the PRIOR threshold. Pre-documentation. Pre-1748."

**TYLER:** *(reading the locations)* "Those are correct."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You knew."

**TYLER:** "I knew."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You withheld three in Paris. Three in Vienna."

**TYLER:** "Yes."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Do you know how many in the 18 remaining cities?"

Tyler is quiet for a moment.

**TYLER:** "I have estimates. Not counts." He sets the file down. "I have been in some of these cities during significant historical events. The PRIOR count for some of them will be — substantial."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Define substantial."

**TYLER:** "Amsterdam may have more PRIOR sites than London."

*[She writes this down.]*

### A-V13 — AUGUSTINERKIRCHE PRECINCT

*[The Augustinerkirche — the Augustinian Church, the Habsburg court parish, standing on the Augustinerstraße since the 14th century. A grey stone morning. The spire.]*

*[Tyler and the Exchange Student stand on the pavement outside. The Camera Op is filming them. This scene she has filmed before, she realizes — not this specific morning, but this specific arrangement: Tyler looking at a building while the Exchange Student checks her clipboard.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "A-V13. November 1619. Twelve days."

**TYLER:** "The Bohemian Revolt had started the previous year. The defenestration. The Ferdinand II coronation. By November 1619 Vienna understood it was going to be a long war." He looks at the church. "I was in Vienna for something unrelated — I was trying to reach a contact in the Moravian archdiocese. The contact never arrived. I spent twelve days waiting."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Where did you wait?"

**TYLER:** "Here, mostly. This was the court parish. The Habsburgs used it for significant events. During my twelve days there was a requiem mass for the Archduke. Funeral architecture. The congregation staying for the performance of grief."

*[He looks at the entrance.]*

**TYLER:** "I find requiem masses useful. The duration is fixed. The attention is directed. Everyone in the room is doing the same thing and they all know why. It's the one case where a crowd produces something unified rather than dispersed."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You stayed for the mass."

**TYLER:** "I stayed for several masses. And for the hours between. The church was open. I had nowhere else to be."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Forty-one feet."

**TYLER:** "That seems right for twelve days with a requiem mass in it."

*[Inside, the Exchange Student runs the instruments. The 1619 construction reads clearly at 41 feet — faint at the threshold but consistent. The church has had 407 years of subsequent occupancy on top of it. The signature from twelve November days and a Habsburg requiem is still distinct.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(to herself, writing)* "A-V13. Augustinerkirche. 1619. 12 days. Requiem mass, Archduke. Construction from directed collective grief with individual STAYING component. 41 feet."

### A-V14 — OLD UNIVERSITY PRECINCT

*[The Bäckerstraße and Sonnenfelsgasse intersection — once the site of Vienna's original Dominican monastery and later the Old University of Vienna (founded 1365). The buildings have been rebuilt but the street geometry is unchanged. The Exchange Student stops at the corner with her instruments.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "A-V14. 1529. Nineteen days."

**TYLER:** *(standing at the corner)* "The first Ottoman siege. Suleiman's army was outside the walls from September 27th to October 15th. Nineteen days." He looks down the street. "I had nowhere to go. The gates were closed."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You were in Vienna when the Ottomans besieged it."

**TYLER:** "I was in Vienna when the Ottomans besieged it." He is quiet. "I was in this precinct because the university complex was being used as a staging area for city defense coordination. It was a place where people knew what was happening. It felt important to be somewhere that knew what was happening."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "For nineteen days."

**TYLER:** "The siege lasted nineteen days. I was here for all of them." He pauses. "I wasn't useful. I don't have military knowledge relevant to 16th century siege defense. I was — present. I occupied a corner of the courtyard and I knew what was happening."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You stayed because it mattered."

**TYLER:** "I stayed because I did not have a better reason to go somewhere else." He considers this. "Which is, I think, the FOUND category. The place offered — not comfort, exactly. Accuracy. It was an accurate place to be during a siege."

*[The Exchange Student runs the instruments. 67 feet — the largest of the three Vienna retroactive sites. 19 days of concentrated presence during active military crisis, in a place calibrated to information and coordination.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Sixty-seven feet. It reads differently from A-291 — the Stadtpark STAYING. That was patient accumulation. This is — "

**TYLER:** "Compression. Everything in the same nineteen days."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "A-V14. 67 feet. 1529. Siege duration. FOUND construction — place offered accuracy during crisis. Mechanism: compressed duration with high-intensity presence."

*[She files it. She looks at Tyler.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "I want to note: the three Vienna PRIOR sites span 1499, 1529, and 1619. You were in Vienna for three major European historical crises. The Siege. The start of the Thirty Years War. And we haven't measured the third one yet."

**TYLER:** "The third one is quieter."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "1499 is before any of the crises."

**TYLER:** "1499 was the beginning. I came to Vienna for the first time in 1499. I was — " He stops. "I wanted to see the city that Maximilian was building. I had heard it described. I arrived and I was here for four days and I left. Nothing happened. I ate bread and walked around and looked at a city that was becoming something."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "And left 23 feet."

**TYLER:** "Apparently." He sounds, for a moment, genuinely surprised. "Four days in 1499. I was curious."

### A-V15 — GRABEN

*[The Graben — Vienna's main pedestrian street since Roman times, the original site of the city's moat, drained and turned into a market in the medieval period. The Pestsäule (Plague Column, 1693) stands at one end, but the Graben itself is far older than the column.]*

*[Tyler stands in the middle of the Graben. Morning foot traffic passes him. He is standing on a Roman moat that became a medieval market that became a baroque public space that became a 21st century pedestrian shopping street. He is standing on five layers of Vienna.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "A-V15. 1499. Four days. Twenty-three feet."

**TYLER:** "I walked the Graben three times a day for four days." He looks at the stones underfoot. "It was the center of the city. I did not know it would still be the center of the city five hundred and twenty-seven years later. I thought: this is where the city is. I should walk through it and understand what kind of city this is."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "And what kind of city was it?"

**TYLER:** "Becoming. It was a city that had decided to become something and had not yet become it." He looks at the Pestsäule — which didn't exist in 1499. "The column wasn't there. Maximilian had plans for a new cathedral wing. The Habsburg ambition was visible but not built yet. The city knew it was going to be important. It had the hum of potential rather than the hum of establishment."

*[He pauses.]*

**TYLER:** "I come back to cities at their becoming moments, sometimes. Before they're what they become. The construction I left at 1499 Graben is from four days of paying attention to a city that hadn't arrived yet."

**CAMERA OP:** *(quietly)* "Does that happen a lot? Being in a city before it becomes itself?"

**TYLER:** "It happens. Usually by accident. You can't always know which visits will be the early ones."

*[The instruments confirm: 23 feet at the 1499 Graben location. Below the paving stones, below the 1693 plague column foundations, below the 18th century renovation layers, the construction is there. 527 years running. From four days of walking through a city that was becoming what it was going to be.]*

The instruments confirm 31 minutes of camera work on the Graben during the S07E05 production days, close enough to the 1499 site center to register.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "A-V13, A-V14, A-V15. Vienna retroactive complete. Revised Vienna total: 15 sites, 587 feet including departure-trace." She closes the file. "Camera Op WITNESS confirmed at all three."

## ACT TWO — THE COUNT

*[The café. The three of them. The complete Vienna file. The Exchange Student has the full CARDINAL-3 revision on the table.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "I want to do the count. The full network count, with retroactive corrections." She opens the revised file. "Detroit: 31 sites. Paris: 9 sites (6 original, 3 PRIOR). Vienna: 15 sites (12 original, 3 PRIOR). London: 47 sites. The other four cities you mentioned — Istanbul, Buenos Aires, Osaka, Cape Town — those are filed separately, not yet audited with the PRIOR model."

**TYLER:** "The four haven't been retroactively audited."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Which means the retroactive PRIOR count for all four is currently zero — but probably isn't actually zero."

**TYLER:** "Probably not."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The 18 remaining cities haven't been visited at all under CARDINAL-3."

**TYLER:** "Correct."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The network total you gave us in Paris — 900 sites — is that with or without PRIOR retroactive corrections?"

*[Tyler considers this for longer than expected.]*

**TYLER:** "With." He pauses. "I have been keeping an informal count for a long time. The count I gave you — 900 — includes what I know to be PRIOR sites. What I know. The actual total, once the model is applied retroactively to all 26 cities, will be higher. I have — " He stops. "There are sites in my archive that I have never tried to locate with instruments. Cities I passed through once in the 12th century and never went back to. Those are not in the 900."

*[Silence.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You have undocumented construction sites from the 12th century."

**TYLER:** "I have been alive since approximately 1100 CE. The mechanism has been running the entire time. I did not understand what it was doing for most of that time. The 900 is what I can account for."

*[The Camera Op holds the shot on Tyler. He is looking at the table.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(quietly)* "The 1127 Marrakech site — the earliest confirmed."

**TYLER:** "The earliest I can confirm. There are earlier ones. I came through the Iberian peninsula in the late 11th century. I was in the Levant in 1096 — I'm not going to discuss why — and I was in those places for sustained periods. The mechanism was running. I did not know it was running. Those sites are in cities we are not documenting under CARDINAL-3 and I have never tried to locate them."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "What is the actual total?"

**TYLER:** "I don't know."

*[He says it simply. Not as a deflection. As a fact he has been carrying for a long time.]*

**TYLER:** "The count I can give you is the count I can verify. The actual total — I don't know. I've been alive for roughly nine centuries. I've been in a great many places. I paid attention to a great many places before I understood what attention did."

*[The Camera Op does not zoom out. She stays on Tyler's face.]*

**CAMERA OP:** "And the 18 remaining cities."

**TYLER:** "Those are cities I can verify. Cities where I have clear records, CARDINAL-3 documentation, known visit dates. The 18 are the documented remainder."

**CAMERA OP:** "There are other cities."

**TYLER:** "There are other cities." He pauses. "This is not a complete inventory of what I've done to the world. This is a complete inventory of what I can account for."

## ACT THREE — THE DEPARTURE

*[Day 334. Vienna. The last morning.]*

*[The Exchange Student is packing the field equipment. Tyler is at the window — the same window of the same café they have been using since they arrived. The hum here has been present every morning.]*

*[The Camera Op is filming him at the window. She has filmed him at many windows. The window framing is becoming one of the recurring images of the documentary — Tyler looking out, what he sees unseen, the glass between him and the city.]*

**TYLER:** *(without turning)* "Vienna is different from Paris."

**CAMERA OP:** "How?"

**TYLER:** "Paris I go to because Paris asks me to. Paris has always asked me to come back. The construction I left in Paris is from arguments and bread and light — pleasant things. Things I wanted to do." He pauses. "Vienna I go to because Vienna requires me. The 1938 register. The 1619 requiem. The 1529 siege. Vienna has needed me to be present for difficult things. The city and I have an arrangement: I show up when it's terrible, and I stay."

**CAMERA OP:** "And the 1499 visit."

**TYLER:** *(a small pause — remembering)* "The first visit. I was curious. Before the arrangement was established."

**CAMERA OP:** "Before the count."

**TYLER:** "Before the count." He turns from the window. "That's actually accurate. The 1499 visit was before any of the crises. Before I understood that Vienna was the kind of city that would require me. I came because I wanted to see it. I didn't know yet what the city and I were going to be to each other."

*[He picks up his coat.]*

**TYLER:** "That happens sometimes. The first visit is innocent. You don't know what the place is going to ask of you. You're just curious. And then the place and you develop a history, and the first visit becomes the beginning of the history retroactively. The 23 feet at A-V15 are from four days of innocent curiosity. I'm glad they're there."

---

*[The station. Vienna Hauptbahnhof. The Exchange Student has the complete revised file. Paris: 9. Vienna: 15. London: 47. Detroit: 31. Four other cities, unaudited. Network total: undetermined.]*

*[Tyler buys a coffee from the platform kiosk. The Camera Op films the train board.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You said you'd tell us the next city."

**TYLER:** "Amsterdam."

*[She writes it down.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The CARDINAL-3 Amsterdam file?"

**TYLER:** "Extensive. I've been going to Amsterdam since 1628. The Dutch Golden Age. I was there for the VOC years, the Rembrandt years, the Spinoza years. I spent significant time in Amsterdam in the 1630s and 1640s." He sips his coffee. "The Amsterdam PRIOR count will be — all of them. The CARDINAL-3 Amsterdam documentation starts 1792. Everything before is PRIOR by definition."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "All of them."

**TYLER:** "The mechanism ran unobserved in Amsterdam for approximately 160 years before CARDINAL-3 began documenting it. What they documented was already ancient by network standards."

*[The train is announced. The Camera Op catches the board: Amsterdam Centraal.]*

**CAMERA OP:** *(voiceover, later)* "He said Amsterdam the way you say the name of a place you have a long history with. Not excited — he doesn't do excited. But something in the way he said it. Like arriving somewhere he's been meaning to return to."
