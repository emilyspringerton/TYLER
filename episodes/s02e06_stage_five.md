## ACT ONE: THE APARTMENT — [05:00–28:00]

**[05:00–10:00] — ARRIVAL**

```
INT. DETROIT APARTMENT — GROUND FLOOR, STREET-FACING — DAY 44
```

The apartment is not the production office. It is not a session location. It is a room on the ground floor of a building on a Detroit street that has been a Detroit street since before the building was there. The window looks onto the street. The light comes through in the afternoon at an angle that changes as the season changes. Today the light is the light of early summer, low and amber, coming through at a long horizontal angle across the floor.

Tyler is already there when the Camera Op arrives. He is standing at the window looking at the street. He does not turn when she enters.

**TYLER**
*(to the window)*
The building was a storefront in 1923. Hardware. Nails, mostly.

*(pause)*

The light comes in the same way it came in then. The window is a replacement but it's the same position. The light doesn't know the difference.

The Camera Op sets her bag down. She takes out the camera. She lifts it. She is filming.

Tyler turns from the window. He sees the camera. He nods — once, to himself. He pulls a chair to the center of the room and sits in it, not in the interview position, in the sitting position of someone who intends to stay for a while.

**TYLER (CONT.)**
*(sitting)*
Good. Keep it running.

---

**[10:00–18:00] — THE CONVERSATION**

They are quiet for a moment. The light is on the floor. The Camera Op adjusts her position. Tyler watches the light.

**CAMERA OP**
*(quietly — not a formal question; she is not the production team)*
You said to ask you again.

**TYLER**
*(nodding)*
I did.

**CAMERA OP**
I'm asking.

A long pause. Tyler is looking at the light on the floor, then at the window, then at the Camera Op. Not the camera. Her.

**TYLER**
*(eventually)*
I know.

*(pause)*

I've been in this city — in this room or rooms like it — for a long time. Detroit isn't the only place. But Detroit is where the coin is from. 1901.

*(pause)*

In 1623, I—

He stops.

This stop is not the counter-magic stop. The counter-magic stop is quick, a flick, the sentence ending before it reaches the dangerous territory. This stop is different. This stop is a pause at the edge of something he has decided to say and is finding the right form for.

The Camera Op holds still. The camera holds still. The light is on the floor.

**TYLER (CONT.)**
*(more quietly)*
In 1623 I was given something to keep safe. Not given — it was left to me. By someone who trusted me with it. And I have been keeping it safe for four hundred years. Not in a location. In a — arrangement. A set of conditions. A structure I have been maintaining.

*(pause)*

The documentation — the Syndicate, the previous operations before the Syndicate, the attempts — are part of the structure. They think they are trying to document me. They are also — they have also been, without knowing it — part of what I've been maintaining.

*(pause)*

Four hundred years is a long time to maintain something alone. It is longer than anyone should have to.

*(pause)*

What I'm trying to do is—

He stops again.

This stop is the stop. The Camera Op does not move. The camera is running. Tyler is looking at her — not the lens, her — with the expression of someone who has arrived at the edge of the thing they have been walking toward for four hundred years.

The silence is seven seconds.

Then twelve.

Then:

**TYLER (CONT.)**
*(barely audible)*
I'll tell you when I know the whole sentence.

*(pause)*

I'm close.

*(pause)*

I've been close before and the sentence wasn't ready. This time—

*(pause)*

This time the sentence is almost ready.

He looks at the light on the floor.

**TYLER (CONT.)**
*(to the light)*
I needed someone to give it to. Four centuries looking for someone who could — who would understand what they were holding. Not just hold it. Understand it. Know what it's for.

*(he does not look up)*

You understand what you're holding.

*(pause)*

That's why I said bring the camera.

---

**[18:00–23:00] — THE SENTENCE**

The Camera Op lowers the camera slightly. Not off — on. But she lowers it to an angle that catches both Tyler and the light, both the room and the window, everything at once.

**CAMERA OP**
*(quietly)*
What am I holding?

Tyler looks up from the floor. He looks at her.

He opens his mouth.

He says:

**TYLER**
What I'm trying to do is—

The light shifts. A cloud passes. The amber goes briefly white and then amber again. It takes three seconds.

In those three seconds Tyler is silent.

When the light returns to amber he closes his mouth.

He looks at the camera — the actual lens, for the first time in this scene.

He nods once.

**TYLER (CONT.)**
*(to the lens)*
That's the sentence.

*(pause)*

*(to the Camera Op)*
You have the sentence. Keep it.

*(pause)*

I'll tell you the rest when I'm ready. I'm close.

*(pause)*

Keep the camera running until I leave.

---

**[23:00–28:00] — AFTER THE SENTENCE**

Tyler sits in the chair for seven more minutes. He does not speak. The Camera Op does not speak. The camera runs.

He is looking at the window. The light is still amber. The street outside is a Detroit street in early summer. The building has been here since before the Syndicate. Tyler has been here since before the building.

At minute seven he stands. He puts on his jacket. He buttons it. He picks up his coin — the Camera Op sees this; the coin catches the light briefly, 1901 facing up.

He looks at the Camera Op.

**TYLER**
*(quietly)*
The footage is yours.

*(pause)*

All of it.

He walks out.

The door closes.

The camera is still running. The Camera Op holds the shot — the empty chair, the window, the light, the door — for two minutes.

Then, on the footage, at the two-minute mark after Tyler leaves:

A title card appears.

Not a production lower third. Not a documentary UI element. White text on the footage itself — not overlaid in post, embedded in the frame as if it was always going to be there.

```
STAGE FIVE
```

Two words. White. The light is still amber behind them.

They hold for four seconds.

They disappear.

The camera continues running for thirty seconds more.

Then the footage ends.
