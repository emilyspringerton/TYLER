### COLD OPEN

**[EXTERIOR — MOORGATE, CITY OF LONDON. DAY 285. EARLY MORNING.]**

*[The hum, low and specific, under the City's morning traffic. The Exchange Student has the map open on a clipboard. The Camera Op is filming from the corner. Tyler is standing at the Moorgate junction, looking north along the street toward where the old gate was.]*

*[The City at 7:30 AM: office workers, cyclists, the specific London sound of many people moving with deliberate purpose. The construction is here. It is always here. Nobody moving through Moorgate notices it.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(checking the clipboard)* "The notation date is April 1577. 'Moorgate. Early morning. The gate still standing. I have always liked gates.'"

*[Tyler does not turn from the street.]*

**TYLER:** "I wrote that?"

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Page 23 of the leather journal."

**TYLER:** "I have always liked gates." He pauses. "That's true."

*[He turns and looks at the Exchange Student, then at the Camera Op. The smile: slightly too knowing. One second toward the camera. Then he looks at the street.]*

**TYLER:** "The Moorgate stood about thirty feet north of here. The City wall ran east from it, toward Bishopsgate. If you stood here in April 1577, you could see both the gate and the open land of Moorfields beyond."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "What was in Moorfields?"

**TYLER:** "Washing lines. Pasture. Market gardens. Archers practicing." He pauses. "London didn't have a park, properly, until the next century. Moorfields was the nearest thing — open land outside the wall you could actually walk on."

*[He takes the coin from his right-hand pocket. Reads it. Pockets it. The camera catches the motion and is slightly late for the face.]*

**TYLER:** "The gate is where the inside and outside met. You stood at it and you could see both things at once. April 1577 is — I think I had been in the City for about four months by then. Long enough to start having a sense of the routes."

**CAMERA OP:** *(from behind the lens)* "You were living here."

**TYLER:** "For a stretch."

*[He walks to the point on the pavement where the notation position would have been — he knows it the way he knows all the positions, precisely and without explaining his knowing.]*

**TYLER:** "Here."

---

**[EXCHANGE STUDENT measures. She walks the radius outward, marks the edge.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "39 feet."

*[Tyler nods. 39 feet. Running since April 1577. 449 years.]*

**TYLER:** *(reading from the journal)* "April 1577. Moorgate. Early morning. The gate still standing. I have always liked gates. I sat here some time watching the people coming in and going out. The road north from Moorfields goes to Islington and beyond. Most people were coming in from the north, from the market gardens and the outer places. One woman had geese. The geese did not want to come through the gate. I waited to see what she would do. She waited the geese out. It took some time." He closes the journal. "The geese are in the notation."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "I noticed the geese."

**CAMERA OP:** "The geese are in the construction?"

*[Tyler considers this.]*

**TYLER:** "The geese are in the notation because I found them interesting to watch. Whether they are in the construction — the construction holds what I was paying attention to. I was paying attention to the geese."

**CAMERA OP:** "39 feet from waiting to see what she would do with the geese."

**TYLER:** "39 feet. Yes."

*[The Exchange Student marks A-305 on the map: PRIOR. UNINTENTIONAL. 39FT. APRIL 1577.]*

### ACT ONE — THREE PRIOR SITES

**[EXTERIOR — ALDERSGATE STREET, CITY OF LONDON. DAY 286. MORNING.]**

*[The hum is present. The Exchange Student leads them north along Aldersgate Street from the junction with Gresham Street. Tyler is careful today. The Camera Op has noticed this — she is filming him, not the street.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "September 1563."

*[Tyler walks slightly ahead of them, his hands in his jacket pockets. He knows where he's going.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(reading from her clipboard)* "The notation: 'September 1563. Aldersgate Street. The street was almost empty. Two or three left who could not leave. I sat with the air for a morning.'"

*[Tyler stops. He is at a point on the left side of Aldersgate Street, south of the junction with Long Lane. He stands very still.]*

**TYLER:** "Here."

*[He does not take the coin out. He does not open the journal. He just stands.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(quietly)* "I'll measure."

---

*[She measures. The Camera Op holds the frame on Tyler standing in the street.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(from some distance away)* "71 feet."

*[Tyler closes his eyes for one moment. Opens them. The Camera Op is still filming him.]*

**TYLER:** "71 feet."

*[He is quiet for a moment.]*

**CAMERA OP:** "What happened in September 1563?"

*[Tyler is careful. He knows the answer. He is choosing how to hold it.]*

**TYLER:** "The plague. A bad year. A very bad year in London." He pauses. "Most people left when they could. The Court left. The lawyers left. Anyone with means to go elsewhere went elsewhere. The City had about — I don't know — a tenth of the people it normally had. Maybe less, in some streets."

*[The Camera Op keeps the frame on him.]*

**TYLER:** "Some people couldn't leave. They stayed — not because they wanted to, because they had no means to go. They were here when everyone else went. They kept the city alive while everyone waited for it to be safe to come back."

*[A beat.]*

**CAMERA OP:** "And you stayed."

**TYLER:** "I stayed." 

*[He looks at the street. The City rushing past: office workers, a courier, someone with a coffee, a group of people speaking quickly to each other. The hum.]*

**TYLER:** "I didn't get sick. I stayed. I was useful in some ways that don't need to be documented. But mostly I — I walked these streets in September 1563 when they were almost empty, and I paid attention. Not to what was gone. To what was still here." He pauses. "The people who couldn't leave. The particular quality of a city at one-tenth capacity. The way the sound was different."

*[The Exchange Student has returned. She is writing without speaking.]*

**TYLER:** "I said I sat with the air for a morning. That's what I wrote. I didn't know what I was doing. But the city was — it was very present. In the way a thing is present when it's reduced to what it cannot shed. And I sat in it."

*[He looks at the measurement mark at 71 feet. He looks back at the street.]*

**TYLER:** "71 feet from the people who didn't leave. For 463 years."

**[They walk to the third unvisited PRIOR site. Day 287.]**

**[EXTERIOR — BREAD STREET, OFF CHEAPSIDE. CITY OF LONDON. DAY 287. MID-MORNING.]**

*[The hum. Bread Street runs south from Cheapside — a short street, barely eighty yards, modest by London standards. Tyler has been here since before they arrived: the Camera Op finds him standing at the Cheapside end of the street, looking south.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(reading from the clipboard)* "March 1573. 'Bread Street. A market morning. A baker had his bread on the ground because something was wrong with his board and he couldn't stop selling to fix it. He had a boy helping him. The boy was maybe eleven. The boy kept putting the bread back on the board and the board kept falling and the baker kept putting the bread back on the ground and going on selling. This happened four times. I watched it for — I think thirty minutes. It is still making me happy as I write this.'"

*[Tyler is smiling. The smile: slightly too knowing, unresolvable. He is reading the annotation with the Camera Op able to see him.]*

**TYLER:** "The board kept falling."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Four times, you wrote."

**TYLER:** "At least four. I may have stopped counting."

*[The Camera Op lowers the camera briefly. She is smiling too. She brings it back up.]*

**TYLER:** "The baker was very annoyed. The boy thought it was funny. The baker thought it was not funny. The customers found it somewhere in between. Every time the board fell, the bread went on the ground, and the baker would say something I'm not going to repeat, and the boy would try not to laugh, and they would arrange the bread on the board and the board would eventually fall again." He pauses. "I think the board had a leg that had cracked. He needed to take a nail to it. He kept not doing that."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Why?"

**TYLER:** "He had customers. You don't stop selling to fix your board when you have customers. The bread on the ground was a secondary problem."

*[He walks to the position where he stood in March 1573. The Camera Op keeps the frame.]*

**TYLER:** "Here. I stood at the corner and watched."

*[The Exchange Student measures.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "44 feet."

**TYLER:** "44 feet from a man and his boy and a broken board. For 453 years."

*[He pockets his hands. The hum.]*

**CAMERA OP:** *(quietly)* "All three PRIOR sites measured."

**TYLER:** "All three."

*[A pause.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Eight London PRIOR sites. All measured."

*[She marks A-307 on the map. The map of London now has eight off-center-dot PRIOR symbols. The City of London geography — the square mile — has five of them. The Exchange Student looks at the map for a long moment, holding the clipboard at arm's length.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The PRIOR sites cluster. Not across London. In the City."

**TYLER:** "Yes."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Five of the eight PRIOR sites are within half a mile of each other. In the square mile."

**TYLER:** "The City was where I was. In those years. I was using it as a home geography."

### ACT TWO — THE DAY AFTER

**[EXTERIOR — ST. PAUL'S CATHEDRAL CHURCHYARD. DAY 288. LATE MORNING.]**

*[The hum is strong here. The Cathedral's west face rises above them. The churchyard — a small strip of green along the Cathedral's north side — is quiet despite the tourist traffic on the Cathedral steps. The Camera Op is still and filming. Tyler is standing in the churchyard with his jacket off, holding it over one arm. He has been here since early morning.]*

*[The Exchange Student arrives with the measuring equipment.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "This is A-308."

**TYLER:** "Yes."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(checking the clipboard)* "The notation date is November 15, 1623. One day after Gray's Inn."

*[The Camera Op holds very still.]*

*[Tyler is looking at the Cathedral. He is careful. Not evasive. Careful.]*

**TYLER:** "November 15, 1623. The day after the most important afternoon of four hundred years." He pauses. "Or the most important until Day 49. I said that at Gray's Inn. I said it because it was true."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "What does the notation say?"

*[Tyler takes the journal from his jacket. He finds the page. He reads it to himself first. Then to them.]*

**TYLER:** *(reading)* "November 15, 1623. St. Paul's churchyard. I did not know what to do with myself. I came here. I sat on the ground near the east wall — they had booksellers against the wall then, in little stalls, the whole east end of the churchyard. I was not buying books. I sat near the booksellers and let the hours go. I came back three days after. Then the week after. Then I stopped counting the returns." He closes the journal. "That's it."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Not 'I did not know what I was doing.'"

**TYLER:** "No. That notation is different." He looks at the Cathedral. "By November 1623 I had the first layer. I knew something had happened. I was just — I didn't know what to do with myself. The journal acknowledges that. I came here because the Cathedral was here and it was large and quiet and I didn't need it to be anything other than large and quiet."

*[He looks at the spot near the east wall, where the booksellers' stalls were. The booksellers' stalls are gone. The geography is the same.]*

**TYLER:** "I didn't know I was building anything. I knew I needed somewhere to be. This was somewhere to be."

*[The Exchange Student measures.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "88 feet."

*[Tyler nods. Something in his face that is not guilt. It is responsibility held honestly.]*

**TYLER:** "I came back how many times, and each return added to it, and I never knew. Until now." He pauses. "88 feet from not knowing what to do with myself."

**CAMERA OP:** *(very quietly)* "From the day after Gray's Inn."

**TYLER:** "From the day after."

*[The hum. The Cathedral's bells, on the hour. Tyler looks at the Camera Op — not directly at the lens; obliquely, toward her, two seconds maximum — and then looks away.]*

**CAMERA OP:** "I know what happened at Gray's Inn."

**TYLER:** "I know you do."

*[A long pause. He puts his jacket back on.]*

**TYLER:** "The construction here is an AMPLIFICATION. One seeding event — November 15, 1623 — and then returns. Many returns. I stopped counting the returns in 1640 or thereabouts. It kept compounding." He looks at the churchyard. "88 feet. This is not the biggest construction in the City. A-292, Wood Street, is 440 feet. A-293, Gray's Inn, is 188 feet. But this one — this is from not knowing what to do with myself." He pauses. "I find that particular."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "It's the only construction in the audit that the journal names as: *I did not know what to do with myself.*"

**TYLER:** "Yes."

*[He looks at the east wall. The bells of St. Paul's still fading.]*

**TYLER:** "The day before was Gray's Inn. The day after was here. That's the sequence."

*[He says nothing more about Gray's Inn. He says nothing more about what happened the day before. He is being careful. The Camera Op knows what she is filming. She keeps the frame.]*

*[The Exchange Student marks A-308 on the map: AMPLIFICATION. NOVEMBER 15, 1623 + RETURNS. 88FT. She draws it near A-293 (Gray's Inn, 188ft, PRIOR) — the two constructions made within 24 hours of each other, less than two miles apart.]*
