## COLD OPEN — [00:00–02:00]

---

**[00:00–00:30] — THE LOT WAS EMPTY YESTERDAY**

```
EXT. DETROIT STREET — MORNING
```

Establishing shot. A city block in Detroit. Coffee shop. A laundromat. A chain-link fence around a vacant lot that has been vacant for a generation.

Except.

The lot is not vacant. There is a school.

Three stories. Brick that is wrong for the decade — older, denser, the kind of brick they stopped making before the laundromat existed. Tall windows in proportions nobody builds anymore. Double front doors with institutional hardware.

A sign above the doors reads: DETROIT PREPARATORY INSTITUTE. The letters are institutional. Standard signage font. Nothing extraordinary. The kind of sign you stop noticing immediately because it looks like every other school sign.

Students are arriving. Backpacks. Phones. The ordinary morning rush of a school day that has apparently always been happening.

A man in a plaid jacket stops on the sidewalk across the street and stares.

**NEIGHBOR (V.O., later interview):**
It wasn't there Thursday. I walked past the lot every morning for eleven years. You learn not to look at it too hard — it's depressing, a lot like that. Then Friday morning there's a school.

*(pause)*

The weeds at the base of the fence were old. Like the school had been there long enough for the weeds to grow around it. But it wasn't there Thursday.

*(pause)*

I asked my wife if she remembered the school. She said yes. She said it had always been there. I asked her what it was called. She said she didn't remember. I didn't push it.

## ACT I — THE CREW FILMS THE SCHOOL

---

### [I.A — Establishing the school on camera]

```
INT./EXT. DETROIT PREPARATORY INSTITUTE — VARIOUS — MORNING
```

The crew works methodically through the school. This is Stage 2 material — archival footage. The school's existence is significant because Tyler has been sighted here three times in the past week. The school did not exist before this week. These facts are placed in adjacent boxes in the Producer's operational logic, labeled NOTABLE, and not yet connected.

The Camera Op films:

*The library — three floors of books, organized by a system that is not Dewey decimal and not alphabetical but that feels internally consistent to the point where you trust it.* The librarian, a woman of indeterminate age with the posture of someone who has been right about something for a very long time, looks at the camera for exactly two seconds when it pans past her. She does not smile.

*The hallways — students between classes, the universal language of adolescence under mild academic duress.* The Camera Op catches, in passing, the two students from the fence — now moving in opposite directions down the same hallway, not looking at each other, with the specific charged non-looking that means something happened. The Camera Op notes this. Does not report it.

*The cafeteria — the smell of institutional cooking, which is the same in every decade.* A table in the corner where one student is reading what appears to be an archival document with footnotes visible at the bottom of each page. The Camera Op holds this shot for longer than necessary. The student does not look up.

*Room 204 — a closed door that reads CUSTODY in the font of official government documents.*

The Camera Op holds the shot on room 204 for eleven seconds. Then moves on.

---

At the fire exit map visible through the lobby glass, in Emily OS's font, pointing to exits that may or may not lead where exits usually lead.

## ACT II — THE CUSTODY OFFICE

---

### [II.A — Tyler arrives]

```
EXT. DETROIT PREPARATORY INSTITUTE — NORTH ENTRANCE — AFTERNOON
```

Tyler walks in from the north. The Camera Op has the shot. He looks like he has been here before. He also looks like he has never been here before. These are not contradictory. It is the walk of someone who knows exactly where they are and has been somewhere nearby enough times that arrival is familiar even when the specific location is new.

He checks his phone. He puts it in his pocket. He looks at the school for a moment with the expression of someone who has received exactly the message they expected and is deciding whether to find this annoying or interesting.

He goes in.

The Camera Op follows, maintaining distance. Filming. The Jiangshi Stage 2 documentation protocol is: follow and record. Do not prompt. Do not engage. The subject will move toward whatever is significant. The subject always does.

Tyler navigates the school without hesitation. He does not look at the room numbers. He does not ask anyone for directions. He walks directly to the third floor. He walks directly to room 204. He looks at the door marked CUSTODY for a moment.

He smiles. Not the practiced one. Something more specific.

**TYLER**
*(to the door, not to anyone visible)*
Right.

He knocks. He opens the door.

---

### [II.B — Hana]

```
INT. ROOM 204 — CUSTODY OFFICE — CONTINUOUS
```

The office is correct. A desk. Filing cabinets. A computer that has more files open than any single desktop should be running. A mug on the desk that reads HANA in handwritten marker — the kind of mug that has been through enough dishwasher cycles to establish that the person is planning to stay.

HANA is behind the desk. She is approximately the same age as the students in the hallway, which is the wrong age for someone with the posture of a functionary who has been handling this particular case for a long time. She has a file in front of her.

The file is labeled: TYLER — CUSTODY STATUS — UNCLAIMED.

**HANA**
*(without looking up)*
You're late.

**TYLER**
I wasn't aware I had an appointment.

**HANA**
You were aware. You weighed it. You decided to come anyway. That's not the same as not being aware.

*(she looks up)*

She looks at him the way someone looks at a very long document they have been waiting for the relevant person to come in and sign. Professional. Unhurried. Mildly exasperated in the way that professionals who work with complicated cases are mildly exasperated — not at the person, at the situation.

**HANA**
Your custody status has been unresolved since 1623. We've been maintaining the file.

**TYLER**
I appreciate the attention.

**HANA**
I'm not looking for appreciation. I'm looking for a custodian on record.

Tyler looks at the file. He looks at the room. He looks at the fire exit map on the wall behind Hana — Emily OS's font, the same as everywhere else in this building. He looks at the window that overlooks the library entrance below.

At the library entrance: a brief figure. Seen for less than two seconds. Looking up at something the library does not contain.

Tyler looks away from the window.

**TYLER**
What's the formal process?

**HANA**
Self-definition. You complete a statement about what you are, what you're responsible for, and what you're prepared to accept as the consequence of that responsibility. We file it. The custody chain becomes legible.

**TYLER**
*(considering)*
I'm a—

*(a pause)*

He stops. The pause is not uncertainty. It is the specific pause of someone who has been making this stop for four hundred years and can perform it without effort.

**HANA**
*(flat)*
You just did it again.

**TYLER**
I know.

**HANA**
Forty-seven times in this century. Across four documented attempts. You start the sentence. You don't finish it.

**TYLER**
I'm aware of the pattern.

**HANA**
The pattern has a cost. The custody file stays open. The interest compounds. The factions keep running operations. The binding ritual keeps advancing.

*(she looks at him steadily)*

You know all of this. You've read the documentation.

Tyler looks at the window again. The library entrance is empty.

**TYLER**
What if I'm not refusing? What if I'm just not ready?

**HANA**
Not ready is a choice. Every day it's not ready, the custody file is open. An open custody file is a contested asset. A contested asset generates operations. I don't enjoy the operations.

*(a beat)*

Neither do you.

Tyler looks at her. Something in his expression shifts — not the practiced warmth, not the smile, but something quieter. The look of someone who is having a conversation they have been waiting for without knowing they were waiting for it.

**TYLER**
Four centuries. You've been maintaining this file for four centuries?

**HANA**
The office has been maintaining it. I've been here since the school appeared.

**TYLER**
When was that?

**HANA**
*(returning to her files)*
You know when that was.

Tyler looks at the mug with her name on it. The filing cabinets. The computer with too many files open.

He says nothing. He leaves.

## ACT III — FOUR FRAMES

---

### [III.A — The afternoon light]

```
EXT. DETROIT PREPARATORY INSTITUTE — NORTH ENTRANCE — LATE AFTERNOON
```

The crew is wrapping for the day. The Camera Op is getting establishing shots — the school at the golden hour, which makes the wrong-era brick look like it belongs to the century it was built in, whenever that was.

They pan the building. Third floor windows. Second floor. First.

Library entrance.

A figure.

The Camera Op tightens the frame. Four frames — one second, approximately, of footage.

TYLER. Standing at the library entrance. Not entering. Not leaving. Standing with the stillness of someone whose stillness is a decision. He is looking at something. Not at the camera. At the third-floor library window.

In the window: a reflection. The building across the street. A figure in that reflection. A figure in clothing that resolves, at this distance, as approximately 1920s. Standing at an angle that suggests they are watching the library entrance.

The figure in the reflection is not Tyler. It is not anyone the Camera Op knows. It is not anyone who should exist in the present tense of a 2025 afternoon.

Four frames. Then Tyler steps back and is no longer visible.

The Camera Op holds the shot on the empty library entrance for eleven seconds.

---

### [III.B — The love triangle]

The Camera Op is panning back to the van when the frame catches:

The two students from the fence. The ones who were moving in opposite directions in the hallway with the charged non-looking. They are on the school's front steps. One of them says something. The other one's expression changes — the specific change that means something that was uncertain has become certain, in the direction that hurts.

This is not the Camera Op's story. This is not the documentary's story. This is something that is happening in the same city block, at the same moment, for reasons entirely separate from the binding ritual that the Camera Op is filming and is starting to understand the nature of.

The Camera Op films it. Three seconds. Then cuts away.

In those three seconds: something detonated. Quietly. In the way that important things detonate.

The Camera Op will think about this footage for weeks.
