## THE CITY IN 1351

*Tyler and Camera Op in Old Town Square, now oriented toward the site of the original building where Tyler stayed.*

**TYLER:**
Charles IV had been king for three years. He was twenty-five years old and he was already making Prague into something that hadn't existed before in this part of the world. The university had opened the year before I arrived — the first university in central Europe. The cathedral was under construction. The city was full of architects, theologians, scholars, people arguing about the right proportions for a flying buttress.

**CAMERA OP:**
You fit in.

**TYLER:**
I understood the arguments. I had been having them in various forms for two hundred years. *What is the correct relationship between the vertical and the horizontal? What does the arch achieve that the post-and-lintel doesn't? Is there a geometry that is sacred in itself or is the sacred quality a projection?* I had been in those arguments since before I started filing constructions.

**CAMERA OP:**
What did you do here?

**TYLER:**
I worked on the cathedral. As a craftsman. Stone. Not design — the architects were settled on the design by then. But the cutting and the fitting.

*Beat.*

**TYLER:**
I had been doing almost nothing for two years. I had been pulling weeds in a monastery garden because I could not do anything that required thought. Prague was the first time I had done skilled work in three years. The first time my hands were doing something that required my mind to be present.

## THE NETWORK IMPLICATION

*Exterior. Bridge. Camera Op writing in her log while Tyler looks at the river.*

**CAMERA OP (V.O.):**
Prague is the highest total for any single city in the retrograde. Two years. Five sites. 144 feet if you include the bridge departure. 106 feet of DWELLING. 38 feet of FAREWELL-A.

Compared to Paris: 6 sites, 95ft total. Compared to London: 47 sites over multiple periods. Compared to Avignon: 89ft over 676 years.

The DWELLING construction changes the network calculus. The sites I would have predicted to be the largest — Avignon, Lyon, the long-residency sites — are not the largest. The largest is a voluntary two-year stay in a city Tyler chose for himself and inhabited fully.

The network isn't measuring time. The network is measuring the quality of Tyler's attention.

The implication is uncomfortable: every STAYING site in the global network — the long flat-density readings that look like decades of passive presence — may be much smaller than the DWELLING sites they outnumber by factors of ten. A city Tyler lived in for a century without fully choosing to be there might generate less than two years in Prague.

The network is measuring something about will. About consent. About the difference between being somewhere and choosing to be somewhere.

Tyler has been on the earth for a very long time. He has chosen to be somewhere, fully, less often than the duration of his presence would suggest.

Prague is 144 feet. That's the number.

**Construction type: DWELLING (new)**

DWELLING is generated by voluntary inhabitation — a subject who chose to be present, is glad to be present, and is engaged with the place as a place rather than as a refuge, a waystation, or a suspension.

Profile characteristics:
- Accelerating density gradient (opposite of CARRIED's discharge model; builds toward the end rather than front-loading)
- Per-month measurement increases as the period continues — the subject becomes more present, not less
- Correlated with active engagement (work, social connection, chosen activity)
- DWELLING (finite) subtype: the final months before a known, chosen departure produce higher density than earlier residence. Awareness of finitude intensifies presence.

Comparison to REFUGE:
- REFUGE: even density, recovery gradient — the subject is coming back to themselves
- DWELLING: accelerating density — the subject is already themselves and going deeper

Comparison to STAYING:
- STAYING: flat even density — the subject is present by default, not by choice
- DWELLING: the gradient reveals choice. Choosing generates more than defaulting.

**Network implication:**

The network measures the quality of presence, not only its duration. A DWELLING site of two years may exceed a STAYING site of two decades. The retrograde's highest measurement is not the oldest site (Avignon, 1348) or the longest stay, but the most fully inhabited.

Every flat-density STAYING reading in the global network should be re-examined for what the subject was choosing, or not choosing, during accumulation.

### DWELLING: FORMAL PROPOSAL

The Council proposes formal recognition of DWELLING as a construction type:

**DWELLING**
- Generated by voluntary inhabitation — the subject chose to be present
- Characterized by accelerating density gradient (deepening over time, not front-loaded)
- Work and residence components distinguishable: work presence generates higher per-month density, residence generates consistent base
- DWELLING (finite) subtype: terminal months of a known, chosen departure produce elevated density — awareness of finitude intensifies the construction
- Subject must be fully reconstituted (not in suspension, not in recovery) for DWELLING to activate

Distinction from STAYING: STAYING is flat even density (presence by default). DWELLING accelerates (presence by choice). The gradient reveals the will.

---

### NETWORK QUALITY MODEL

The Prague data requires a revision to our understanding of the CARDINAL-3 network.

Prior model: network measures presence + duration. Large readings = long presence or intense events.

Revised model: network measures the quality of presence. DWELLING in a city for two years generates more than STAYING in a city for a century. Catastrophic witnessing (WITNESS-LONG) generates significant readings through passive accumulation over geological time. But DWELLING generates through active engagement in a relatively short window.

The highest readings in the network may not be the oldest or the longest. They may be the most fully chosen.

Implication for the retrograde: we should seek, not only the catastrophe sites and the departure sites, but the DWELLING sites. The places Tyler chose freely and inhabited fully. These may be the true maximums of the network — overshadowed in the archive by events, while the DWELLING sites went unfiled precisely because they felt too ordinary to document.

---

### FAREWELL INTENSITY RATIO

Proposed rule: FAREWELL-A intensity scales with prior DWELLING depth.

Prague data point: DWELLING 106ft → FAREWELL-A 38ft. Ratio 0.36.

If this ratio holds across FAREWELL-A sites:
- A-Lyon (FAREWELL-A 47ft): suggests prior DWELLING ≈ 130ft at Lyon over the period
