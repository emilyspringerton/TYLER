## ACT ONE: TYLER LOOKS AT THE FRAMES — [03:00–25:00]

---

**[03:00–05:30] — HE ARRIVES**

Tyler arrives at the review suite at exactly the agreed time. He is not early. He is not late. He has the quality, in these moments, of someone who finds punctuality interesting.

He looks at the board.

He stands in front of it for approximately forty seconds, looking at all four frames in sequence, left to right, then right to left.

He does not say anything.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
*(eventually)*
We'd like to walk through these with you.

**TYLER**
*(without turning from the board)*
Of course.

---

**[05:30–10:00] — FRAME 1: 1901**

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
This is Detroit, 1901. Do you recognize this photograph?

Tyler is quiet for a moment. He is looking at the photograph with the expression of someone recognizing a version of an experience they have had.

**TYLER**
*(to the frame, not the Producer)*
The light was different that day. In the photograph. It was overcast when this was taken. You can't see it in the frame — the photographer chose a tight composition, the sky's cut off. But the light on my jacket is diffuse. No direct sun.

*(pause)*

It had been raining.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
*(carefully)*
Is that you?

**TYLER**
*(looking at the frame)*
That's a man tipping his hat at a camera.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
Is that—

**TYLER**
*(warm, not answering)*
The camera he's tipping his hat at is a Brownie No. 2. Box camera. You can see the outline of the photographer's shadow at the bottom left of the frame — the shadow is longer than the man's. Overcast day, low angle. Late afternoon.

*(pause)*

The dime in my — the coin I carry. The 1901 date. Detroit. Late afternoon, overcast. It's been raining.

*(pause)*

That's a lot of detail for someone who doesn't know if that's him.

He looks at the frame. The frame looks like a man in 1901 looking at a camera that can't possibly have reached 2023.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
Is that—

**TYLER**
*(looking at the next frame)*
What's the second one?

---

**[10:00–15:00] — FRAME 2: 1988**

Tyler looks at the hotel register.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
Detroit, 1988. Room 412. The register entry.

Tyler reads it. He reads the whole page, not just the entry. His eyes move across the other names on the register. He stops at one.

**TYLER**
*(mild)*
Room 308 checked out the same night. The name is Bakker, initial M. The Bakker in room 308 is not a person. It's a forwarding address. The Syndicate uses it for staged witness placements.

*(pause)*

You were already here in 1988. Not this crew — a previous operation.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
*(after a pause)*
We—

**TYLER**
I know what the entry says. Room 412. I know the handwriting.

*(turning to the Producer's position, not the lens)*
You want to know if the handwriting is mine. I know what "consistent across a 71-year gap" means to you. I've read the Stage 2 methodology.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
*(carefully)*
Is it your handwriting?

**TYLER**
*(turning back to the frame)*
The T is the same.

*(pause)*

Room 412 is a corner room. The window looks onto the alley behind the hotel. The neon from the bar sign across the alley comes through the gap in the curtain at about 2 AM and makes a red line on the ceiling.

*(pause)*

What's the third one?
