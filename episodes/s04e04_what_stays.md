## EPISODE STRUCTURE

**Day 82, morning — the question Tyler doesn't ask**

Emily arrives at 9 AM. She sits across from Tyler. The coin goes back in his pocket.

**EMILY:** "You've been in there every day since Day 76. Six days."

**TYLER:** "Yes."

**EMILY:** "When does a construction stop being built and start being complete?"

He looks at her. This is what he's been sitting with.

**TYLER:** "When it doesn't need me in it to work."

**EMILY:** "Has it reached that?"

He's quiet for a moment.

**TYLER:** "Close."

---

**Day 82, late morning — what Emily's map needs**

Emily opens the notebook. 47 nodes. She goes through the outer ring — contingency nodes, supply chain tracking, frequency documentation. Then she gets to the center: the SPRING-4 primary architecture node.

It has one blank sub-node. She's been leaving it blank for two days.

**EMILY:** "I have a node I don't know how to fill in. It's under primary architecture. The construction is a space where Day 49 is permanently present — I have that. The frequencies, the witnesses, the Subscriber notation — I have all of that. What I don't have is—" she taps the blank. "How does it stay without you. What holds the Day 49 event in the room when you're not working in it."

**TYLER:** "The witnesses."

**EMILY:** "More than that."

He considers.

**TYLER:** "The event doesn't need a holder. It doesn't go anywhere. Day 49 happened in that room. It happened everywhere, but it happened there specifically — the building was here, Bacon was here, the frequencies were already running. The room is a container that was in the right place at the right moment. What I've been doing is clarifying the container. Cleaning the glass."

He looks at the room door.

**TYLER:** "It was already holding it. I'm building the thing that lets people know the room is holding it."

Emily writes in the blank sub-node: *the room was already the container — construction = clarity, not creation.*

The node is no longer blank.

**EMILY:** "48 nodes."

---

**Day 82, midday — the Exchange Student's final dependency**

The Exchange Student closes her laptop. She looks at the dependency map on her screen — 34 items, most of them checked.

She prints a single page. She walks it to the main table.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Material dependency map, current status. Everything required for continuous full-amplitude Stolas operation in the Witness Room is either present, confirmed, or has a verified backup source. The only open item is—" she points to a line at the bottom of the page "—long-term witness continuity. You need the minimum three to be consistently available. Not permanently — but consistently. Regular enough that the room stays calibrated."

Emily reads the line. She adds it to the notebook: *witness continuity — Exchange Student tracking.*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "I don't know how you maintain that over time. I can map sources. I can't make people stay."

**EMILY:** "No."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Margaret couldn't either. The file ends with 'function transfer required' because the material dependency couldn't be guaranteed after her departure. That's the real reason for the successor notation — not the mapping function. The witnesses."

The table is quiet.

**EMILY:** "She found what the construction needed. She documented it. She couldn't guarantee the supply chain would hold." She looks at the dependency map page. "That's why the function kept running. It was waiting to find someone who could."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "I can map. I can track sources. But long-term witness continuity is—"

**EMILY:** "I know. I'm working on it."

---

**Day 82, afternoon — Tyler and the room**

Tyler goes into the room at 2 PM. He's in there for ninety minutes.

The Camera Op holds position.

At 3:30 PM, he comes out. He stands in the doorway for a moment — not looking at the room, looking at the table. At Emily. At the Camera Op. At the Exchange Student at her corner seat.

**TYLER:** "It's done."

---

**What "done" means:**

He sits down. They wait. This is how it works: he says the thing, and then they wait for him to say the thing that the thing means.

**TYLER:** "The construction is structurally complete. The room holds Day 49 without any additional work from me. It will continue to hold it. The Subscriber's notation is calibrating the frequencies. The witnesses are providing amplitude. The container was already there." He looks at Emily's notebook. "You wrote the last node."

**EMILY:** *(quietly)* "This morning."

**TYLER:** "The map is the completion record. That node closed the construction."

She looks at the notebook. 48 nodes. The blank sub-node filled in this morning, three hours before he said it was done.

**EMILY:** "The map closed it before you knew it was closed."

**TYLER:** "The map knew first. That's how it works."

---

**Day 82, late afternoon — what gets built next**

The Camera Op comes out from behind the camera. She sits at the table. This is the first time she's sat at the table in Season 4.

**CAMERA OP:** "You said 'rooms.' On Day 78. You said you wanted to build more of them. Not the same room — rooms. Places where something that happened is permanently in the air."

**TYLER:** "Yes."

**CAMERA OP:** "Where?"

He takes out the coin. He looks at it — not the year, the feel of it. The 1952 dime. Detroit. The photographs he brought back.

**TYLER:** "Wherever something happened that should stay."

**CAMERA OP:** "That's everywhere."

**TYLER:** "Yes."

He puts the coin back.

**TYLER:** "That's the work."
