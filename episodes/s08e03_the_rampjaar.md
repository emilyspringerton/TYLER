The hand's shadow is more compositionally important than the hand itself."

**CAMERA OP:** "He chose what the light makes, not the light."

**TYLER:** "He chose what the light makes." He pauses. "He was working through the problem out loud. He made a mark on the canvas with a dry brush — no paint, just the gesture — and then he picked up a loaded brush and committed."

**CAMERA OP:** "And every time you've seen it, you've thought: I was in the room when he decided the hand's shadow."

## ACT ONE — THE TWELVE PAGES

*[The Keizersgracht room. The Exchange Student has obtained a facsimile of J.H.P.'s 1672 entry from the Jiangshi Amsterdam archive — forwarded by the Syndicate per Director authorization. She has laid all twelve pages out on the table in sequence.]*

*[The pages are covered in 17th century Dutch mercantile handwriting. Formatted as VOC risk assessment. But the Exchange Student has been translating for two days.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "It's a daily location log. Every day for sixty-seven days, J.H.P. noted where Tyler was, how long he stayed, and what the construction meter read at each location." She holds up page three. "He's formatted it as commodity insurance risk — it reads as: 'Site A: occupancy duration 4 hours, asset exposure LOW, structural stability HIGH.' But 'asset exposure' is his code for construction yield. 'Structural stability' is COEFF-002 precursor — his version of the bilateral measurement."

**TYLER:** *(reading the Dutch)* "He was thorough."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "He was scientific. He had a model — earlier than ours, cruder, but functional. He was running controlled observations. He varied his measurement intervals." She points at page seven. "Day 34. June 12th. He measured you at three different times of day at the same location and noted different yields. He was testing whether construction rate varies by time of day."

**TYLER:** "Does it?"

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(looking at the data)* "His data suggests: marginally, yes. Morning constructions read slightly higher than afternoon constructions at the same site. He couldn't account for it. He called it 'morning premium.' " She pauses. "We haven't tested this. We don't have time-of-day data for our measurements — we always measure total yield, not rate." She writes it down. "Another COEFF-002 revision."

*[Tyler is reading the pages. His expression is something the Camera Op can't quite name.]*

**TYLER:** "He was trying to understand what I was doing."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "He was trying to understand what the mechanism was doing."

**TYLER:** "He was trying to help."

*[The Camera Op catches this: Tyler reading his own surveillance log from 350 years ago, and finding it — not invasive. Useful. The person who watched him most carefully during the worst period he spent in Amsterdam was, it turns out, trying to help.*]

### THE RAMPJAAR CLUSTER: SITES A-343 THROUGH A-352

*[The Exchange Student has translated the location data from J.H.P.'s log into a site map. Tyler confirms each location. The measurements take three days.]*

**A-343**: The Bourse (Amsterdam Exchange, Hendrick de Keyser's building, Rokin area). Tyler present on Day 1 and Day 12 of the Rampjaar period. The VOC and WIC shares were collapsing. He was at the exchange for the price data — not as an investor, as an observer. Understanding the financial mechanism of the crisis. 8ft (FOUND, 2 visits, average 2 hours each, high-intensity observation during financial collapse).

**A-344**: Singel Canal, western ring, near the main defensive sluice. Tyler observed on Day 8 the flooding of the polders — the Dutch opened the sluices to flood their own land, creating the Water Line that stopped the French advance. Tyler was at the sluice as the decision was implemented. 19ft (FOUND, 6 hours — watching a nation decide to drown its own fields to save the city).

**A-345**: The Waag, Nieuwmarkt. The old weigh house, used as a public gathering point during the crisis. Tyler present on Days 18, 19, 20 — the three days around the murder of the De Witt brothers. Not present at the lynching (Gevangenpoort, The Hague). Present in Amsterdam as news arrived. 24ft (WITNESS-adjacent — attention to aftermath of a political murder across three days of public shock and reorientation).

**A-346 through A-351**: Six sites from the 67-day residential cluster — Tyler staying in the same rented room on the Herengracht side, at the same café, at the same market. The STAYING structure of crisis. J.H.P.'s log makes clear: Tyler's daily pattern during the Rampjaar was methodical, not panicked. He established a routine. He knew where to be each day. Vienna had taught him.

*[The Exchange Student runs the measurements. Largest single-day construction yield across the 67-day cluster: Day 37 — the day Amsterdam was formally notified that the French advance had stalled. Construction at the Waag site (A-345) spiked significantly on this day, J.H.P.'s log notes: "asset exposure PEAK / structural stability recovery."]*

**A-352**: Prinsengracht near the Westermarkt. Tyler was at this location on Day 67 — the final day of his measured 1672 cluster. He stayed for four hours and wrote in his register (this register, J.H.P. noted, was unusual — Tyler didn't typically write on-site). Tyler confirms: this was when he filed the first conscious summary of the mechanism. Not the first awareness (that was a gradual development), but the first time he sat down and wrote it out.

## ACT TWO — DAY 67

*[The Prinsengracht. Westermarkt area. Morning.]*

*[The Exchange Student has the instruments. The Camera Op has the camera. Tyler is sitting on a bollard by the canal.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(running the measurement)* "A-352. Sixty-two feet."

**TYLER:** "Sixty-two feet from sitting and writing for four hours in October 1672."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "What did you write?"

*[Tyler looks at the canal.]*

**TYLER:** "I wrote: 'The city survived. I stayed for sixty-seven days. I don't know what I contributed but the mechanism knows. I will call it a construction, provisionally, and I will come back to measure it when I understand the measuring better. I do not yet understand the measuring. I understand that I stayed and the city survived and the account is not settled because the account is a different kind of thing than I thought it was.' " He pauses. "That's not exact — I'm remembering the sense of it. The actual register is in the archive."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You wrote the definition of what we're doing. In 1672."

**TYLER:** "I wrote an early draft. The current definition is better."

**CAMERA OP:** "Is it?"

**TYLER:** "The current definition is more precise. My 1672 version said: 'I don't know what I contributed.' The current version says: I know what I contributed, within measurement error. The precision matters." He looks at the Westermarkt. "Though the underneath is the same: I stayed. The city survived. The account is not settled. The account is ongoing."

### CRISIS-AMPLIFICATION

*[The Exchange Student has been running the numbers. The COEFF-002 model predicts construction yield based on duration, type, and baseline. The 1672 cluster constructions are consistently above model predictions.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The Rampjaar sites are over-yielding relative to model. Not slightly — significantly. A-344, the sluice site: the model predicts 8 feet for 6 hours of FOUND construction. We measured 19 feet. A-352 is over by 30%. The STAYING sub-cluster is over by 15% consistently."

**TYLER:** "The stakes were higher."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The mechanism responds to stakes."

**TYLER:** "I think so. The A-282 in Vienna — the first COMPREHENDED AMPLIFICATION, Bäckerstraße, July 3 1938 — was fifty-two feet for six hours. The model would have predicted less. Vienna 1938 was not a neutral environment." He looks at the data. "When the city is in danger and I'm paying attention to whether it survives, the attention is different. The attention is — loaded. The mechanism appears to record the loading."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(writing)* "Crisis-amplification coefficient. The construction yield scales with the perceived stakes of the attended situation." She pauses. "How does the mechanism know the stakes?"

**TYLER:** "I don't think the mechanism knows the stakes. I think I know the stakes, and my attention is denser when the stakes are higher, and the mechanism records the density of the attention."

*[She writes: **COEFF-002 revision: attention density as primary variable, not duration. Duration is a proxy for cumulative attention; crisis-amplification occurs when attention density exceeds duration-predicted baseline.**]*

## ACT THREE — WHAT J.H.P. SAW

*[Evening. The Exchange Student shows the final page of J.H.P.'s twelve-page log.]*

*[It's different from the preceding pages. The commercial disguise drops. The last page is written in a different hand register — more personal, less formal. It's dated: October 17th, 1672. Tyler's last recorded day of the Rampjaar cluster.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(translating)* "He wrote: 'Subject departed via Prinsengracht boat at mid-morning. Duration of current Amsterdam phase: 67 days. Total construction yield exceeds all previous measurement periods by a factor I cannot model. The Republic survived. The crisis-amplification effect is confirmed by six independent sites. I have been measuring the subject for eleven years. This is the first time I have seen him afraid.' "

*[Silence.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "He wrote: this is the first time I have seen him afraid."

*[Tyler is looking at the page.]*

**TYLER:** "I was afraid. The Republic was going to end. Paris, Vienna, London — those are cities. Amsterdam in 1672 was — the closest thing I had to a home. I'd been coming here for forty-four years. I knew the city in a way I don't know most cities. I was afraid of losing it."

**CAMERA OP:** "And the mechanism recorded the fear."

**TYLER:** "Apparently. Yes." He looks at the measurement data. "Crisis-amplification. The fear is the amplification. I wasn't paying attention to a city at risk. I was paying attention to a place I loved that was at risk. And that's — denser."

*[He sets down the page.]*

**TYLER:** "J.H.P. saw it. He wrote it down. He knew what it was. He didn't have the model words but he knew what he was seeing."

*[The Camera Op holds the shot on Tyler. The Keizersgracht room. The canal light on the ceiling.]*

**CAMERA OP:** "And then he measured the next city."

**TYLER:** *(quietly)* "And then he measured the next city."
