**[EXTERIOR — WHITECHAPEL ROAD. DAY 306.]**

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "A-319. 63 feet. STAYING. 1870s through 1930s."

**TYLER:** "I lived in this neighborhood for long stretches in the late 19th century. The East End in those years had a specific quality — very poor, very crowded, constantly in motion. I walked this road so many times that I stopped counting. I was here during the Ripper inquiry, which I remember as a period of very high fear and very specific police presence. I kept walking the road during the inquiry because walking was what I did when I needed to think and not be thought about."

**CAMERA OP:** "What were you thinking about?"

**TYLER:** "The inquiry. The fear in the streets. And —" He pauses. "There was a woman I knew who lived near here. I was thinking about her. She was not involved in the inquiry. I was thinking about her for the usual reasons. 63 feet is a long staying construction, but I was here for a long time."

*[He walks the road. The Camera Op follows. The Exchange Student marks A-319 on the map. STAYING.]*

**[INTERIOR — HAWKSMOOR CHURCH, SPITALFIELDS. DAY 307.]**

*[The church. Nicholas Hawksmoor's white stone, the 1714 construction. The high interior. The hum: present, and additionally present, as if the building itself is amplifying something.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "A-320. 88 feet. AMPLIFICATION. 1714 to present."

**TYLER:** "Hawksmoor designed six churches in London. I went to all of them when they were consecrated, and I've gone back to all of them continuously since. This one has the best geometry. The proportions —" He looks at the ceiling. "He was building something with the geometry that isn't strictly religious and isn't strictly secular. I've never been entirely sure what he was building. I kept coming back to understand it."

**CAMERA OP:** "Did you understand it?"

**TYLER:** "No. That's what makes it an AMPLIFICATION construction. I keep coming back. Every time I come back and still don't understand it, the measurement grows."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "88 feet."

**TYLER:** "I've been not-understanding Hawksmoor for 310 years." The smile. "Some things reward the not-understanding."

**[EXTERIOR — HAMPSTEAD HEATH, PARLIAMENT HILL OVERLOOK. DAY 309.]**

*[The view from Parliament Hill. London below, the whole city spread from the heath. The skyline: new and old towers, the specific texture of London from above. The hum, fainter here, but the city's accumulation visible.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "A-325. 27 feet. STAYING. Various."

**TYLER:** "I come here when I need to see the whole thing at once. It's happened many times across the centuries. When London was much smaller — when you could see the edge of it from here — and now, when you can't." He looks at the city. "I come here when something in the city has been too close and I need the distance. The specific geometry of the view — hills, river, sky — settles something. I don't know what it settles. I just know that when I come here, the settling happens."

*[The Camera Op films the view. The city below. Tyler at the edge of the hill, looking.]*

**CAMERA OP:** *(after a pause)* "I've come here before. Before the audit. Background shots of London."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "A-325 has a WITNESS component." She checks. "11 feet. Camera Op. Pre-audit. You were here for — according to the log — thirteen minutes, wide angle on the city."

*[The Camera Op looks at the eleven-foot radius around where she would have been standing, looking at Tyler's staying construction.]*

**CAMERA OP:** "I was here before I knew what this was."

**TYLER:** "You were building before you knew you were building." He doesn't turn from the view. "That's the third option running before it had a name."

### ACT TWO — LONDON COMPLETE

**[EXTERIOR — TOWER BRIDGE. DAY 318. LATE AFTERNOON. THE FINAL SITE.]**

*[Tower Bridge. Day 318. The last site in the London inventory: A-339, the final measurement.]*

*[The Exchange Student stands at the center of the bridge with the measuring equipment. Tyler and the Camera Op on either side of her.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "A-339. Tower Bridge approach, north side. 33 feet. STAYING. 1894 to present. Opening ceremony plus continuous returns."

**TYLER:** "I was at the opening ceremony in 1894. The bridge was built to solve a traffic problem and the solution was a bascule bridge that could lift to let tall ships through. I attended the ceremony because it was a significant piece of public engineering and I was in London. I came back the next morning to watch a tall ship go through the opening lift." He looks at the bridge. "I still come here when I'm in London. Usually once per visit. I watch the bridge from the north approach. I have done this since 1894."

**CAMERA OP:** "You watch the bridge."

**TYLER:** "I watch the bridge. 33 feet. 132 years of watching the bridge once per visit." He considers. "That's the right size."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "That's the last one." She checks her clipboard. Then she holds the map — the full London map, complete for the first time. "London. 47 sites measured. A-292 through A-339." She spreads the map on the bridge railing, looking at it. "Plus 21 confirmed WITNESS components. A-297B through the retroactive catalogue." She looks up. "London is complete."

*[Tyler looks at the map.]*

*[The map is dense: the south bank cluster, the City cluster, the East End sites, Westminster, Hampstead, the river marking through all of it. A-292 to A-339. 47 sites. The construction record of Tyler's London, four centuries of presence in this specific geography.]*

*[He looks at it for a long time.]*

**TYLER:** "It looks like London."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "It looks like London as you moved through it."

*[He nods.]*

**CAMERA OP:** "What did London build in you?"

*[He looks at her. The question is the ending of something. He knows it's the ending of something.]*

**TYLER:** *(after a pause)* "The same thing every city builds in me. The knowledge of its specific physics. How it holds memory. Where the crossings are. The knowledge that I'll come back." He pauses. "London has more crossings than most cities. It's been itself for a very long time. It knows how to hold things." He looks at the map. "I've been holding London back."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "47 constructions."

**TYLER:** "47 constructions. Yes." He pauses. "Plus the Camera Op's catalogue."

**CAMERA OP:** "21 plus 2 confirmed."

**TYLER:** "Plus the ones we haven't found yet. The retroactive audit is not finished."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "I have 19 pending sites from the earlier seasons. Before the London segment. She was at Paris sites, Vienna sites. The WITNESS sub-type applies retroactively to the full CARDINAL-3 record."

*[Tyler looks at the Camera Op.]*

**TYLER:** "The archive is very large."

**CAMERA OP:** "The archive is very large."

*[Tower Bridge above them. The Thames. London, complete, visible in all directions from the bridge approach.]*

*[The Camera Op looks at the London map. 47 Tyler sites. 23 Camera Op sites already confirmed. The two records, in black ink and blue, overlapping and adjacent and sometimes coinciding, distributed across the whole of London's geography.]*

**CAMERA OP:** "I've been here as long as you have. In the documentation."

**TYLER:** "You've been here for four years. In the documentation, that's as long as it takes. The mechanism doesn't require centuries." He pauses. "It requires attention."
