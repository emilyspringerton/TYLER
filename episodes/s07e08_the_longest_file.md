### COLD OPEN

**[INTERIOR — EUROSTAR TRAIN, CHANNEL TUNNEL. DAY 268. MORNING.]**

*[The hum is present under the rail sound. The tunnel goes dark. The Exchange Student has the CARDINAL-3 folder open. The Camera Op is filming from across the aisle — the narrow corridor of train seats, Tyler by the window, the folder on the fold-down table.]*

The London file is the last one out of the CARDINAL-3 folder. The Exchange Student sets it on the table.

It is the thickest folder in the set.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The Paris file was eight pages. The Vienna file was eleven." She holds the London file. "This is forty-seven notations."

She sets it down. Tyler doesn't look at it immediately.

**CAMERA OP:** "How many did you expect?"

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The CARDINAL-3 cross-city model estimated two hundred to four hundred audit sites across eight cities. Forty-seven for London alone is—" She pauses. "I expected London to be dense. This is denser than the model."

Tyler is watching the tunnel. The lights move past at intervals.

**TYLER:** "I've been going to London since before I kept records."

*[Silence. The train moves through the dark.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The first notation in the London file is dated 1652."

**TYLER:** "That's not the first time I went."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "I know. That's the first time you wrote it down."

**TYLER:** "Yes."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "How long before 1652?"

**TYLER:** "WREN-3 saw me at Cheapside in 1620. That's in the Cairngorms file, not mine. I was in London in 1623 at Gray's Inn. That's also in the Cairngorms file. I was in London before both of those. I don't know exactly when I started."

*[The tunnel ends. Kent daylight. The Camera Op holds on Tyler's face — the shift from dark to light.]*

**CAMERA OP:** "The file starts where you started keeping records of it."

**TYLER:** "Yes."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The earliest dated notation says: *Wood Street corner. The same feeling. Same as before.* That's the entire entry."

*Silence.*

**TYLER:** "1652. Fourteen years before the Great Fire." He looks at the folder. "The Fire took Wood Street in September 1666. I was there before, during, and after. The corner is still the corner."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "What feeling?"

**TYLER:** "The feeling that something is being held there and I should stay with it for a while."

### ACT ONE — LONDON PHASE OPENS

**[INTERIOR — RENTED FLAT, BLOOMSBURY, LONDON. DAY 271. MORNING.]**

*[The hum is distinct here — differentiated from Vienna's, the Camera Op will note this later. The registers are on the table. The Paris and Vienna registers are the same style — cream paper, soft cover, German manufacture. The London journal is older: dark leather, smaller, worn at the corners. Tyler sets it open to the first page.]*

The handwriting on the first page is in a different script — an earlier form of his current handwriting, more formal. The Exchange Student leans in to read it.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The first page is undated. It says—" She traces the letters. "It's in a seventeenth-century hand. 'Of certain places in the City and Southwark to which I find occasion to return, and a brief accounting thereof. This to serve as a record against future loss.'"

**TYLER:** "I was concerned I would forget. Or that the record would be lost if I didn't write it. I had been keeping journals for some years by 1652 but London got its own." He closes the journal and opens it again near the middle. "By 1780 the handwriting changes. I'd been to London often enough that I stopped writing in the older style."

The Exchange Student has the CARDINAL-3 file laid against the journal. She's cross-referencing.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Forty-seven sites. Three geographic clusters: the City of London, Southwark south of the river, and Bloomsbury. Four pre-1700 notations. Twenty-one in the nineteenth century. The density peaks between 1850 and 1910."

**TYLER:** "After 1850 I knew London better. I had more reason to be there and I had a clearer sense of where to go when I arrived."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You were choosing to return."

**TYLER:** "Yes. Not consciously returning to deposit. But the instinct had gotten better at locating where the feeling was present. I followed the feeling. I went back."

**CAMERA OP:** "And now you're measuring it."

**TYLER:** "Now I'm measuring it."

*He picks up the leather journal.*

**TYLER:** "We start at Wood Street corner."

### ACT TWO — A-292

**[EXTERIOR — WOOD STREET / CHEAPSIDE JUNCTION, CITY OF LONDON. MORNING. DAY 271.]**

*[The hum is clear — the Camera Op holds the frame on the corner before Tyler enters the shot. He arrives from the Cheapside direction, walks to the corner, and stops. Morning commuters pass. No one looks at the corner the way Tyler does.]*

The corner of Wood Street and Cheapside. A fragment of medieval wall is incorporated into the ground-floor cladding of the modern office building on the north side — visible if you know to look, unremarkable if you don't. A few paces along is the tower of what was once St. Alban's Wood Street: the church burned in the Great Fire, was rebuilt by Wren, was bombed in the Blitz in 1940, was deconsecrated and converted. The tower stands. The wall is still the wall.

Tyler stands at the corner for a long time before speaking.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "First notation: 1652. 'Wood Street corner. The same feeling. Same as before.' No duration recorded."

**TYLER:** "I was not yet in the habit of recording duration. I was in the habit of noting: this place again. Still the same." He looks at the wall. "I was here in the old city geometry — before the Fire remade this area. The street widths were different. Some of the buildings were timber. The wall was already old in 1652."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "There's a Fire notation. September 3rd and 4th, 1666, and then October 7th."

**TYLER:** "Three visits in five weeks. September 3rd was the second day of the Fire. I came to confirm the corner was still — to see if the geography was still the geography under the fire. I stood at the north end of Wood Street where the fire hadn't reached yet and I could feel it." He pauses. "September 4th was the third day. The Fire had taken the church by then. I was here before I had to leave. October 7th, I came back. The tower was standing. The wall was standing. The corner had shifted slightly in how the rebuilding began. But the geography was still the geography."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Three deposits in five weeks."

**TYLER:** "I didn't know that was what I was doing. I was confirming. I needed to know if the place had survived."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "What would you have done if it hadn't?"

*[A pause. The morning traffic. The hum continuous.]*

**TYLER:** "I don't know. I've never been to a place I'd been returning to and found it gone."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The geography survives."

**TYLER:** "The geography survives. The church burned twice. The tower is still a tower. The corner is still the corner."

*The Exchange Student takes the tape measure and begins walking outward from the corner.*

She walks north on Wood Street. At 50 feet, she continues. At 100 feet, she passes the first cross-street. At 200 feet she is midway along the block. She continues. At 300 feet she glances back — Tyler is very small at the corner — and continues. At 340 feet she is at the far edge of the next block.

She continues.

At 380 feet she stops. She looks at the tape. She shakes her head slightly. She continues.

At 440 feet, she finds the edge.

She stands there. She doesn't say the number immediately.

*The Camera Op is at the corner with Tyler, filming the Exchange Student at 440 feet, a small figure at the far end of the street.*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(calling back)* "Four hundred and forty feet."

*Tyler turns the coin over once in his right pocket — not a full flip, just a contact-check. The Camera Op doesn't catch it.*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(returning, writing)* "A-273 was three hundred and forty feet. This is four hundred and forty. It's the largest staying construction in the audit. The first one to exceed A-273."

**TYLER:** "The mechanism has been running here since at least 1620. Probably longer. A-273 has been running since before 1929."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "And A-273 is 340 feet. This is 440 feet. The London mechanism is—"

**TYLER:** "Older."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Older and denser. Forty-seven notations vs. one entry for A-273. You were returning to Wood Street regularly across fourteen decades of records. Before the records. The returns have been compounding for four centuries."

*[The impossible detail arrives, delivered as unremarkable information:]*

**TYLER:** "The pre-Fire street geometry ran three feet further east here. The Wren rebuilding straightened it. I can feel the difference when I'm at the corner — the old line and the new line. The construction holds both."

*The Exchange Student writes this down without commenting on it.*

**CAMERA OP:** "The construction holds the old geometry."

**TYLER:** "The geography holds both. The construction is inside the geography." He looks at the wall fragment. "The wall has been in that precise position since the twelfth century. Not the stones — the position. The stones have been replaced. The position has not moved."

He opens the leather journal and writes.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT (V.O., reading the journal entry later):**
*"A-292. Wood Street / Cheapside. City of London. Four hundred and forty feet. The mechanism has been running here since before my records begin. First notation 1652: 'the same feeling, same as before' — the feeling was already familiar when I first wrote it down. The Fire came here in 1666. I was here before and during and after. The tower is still standing. The wall is still the wall. The geography did not move. The Wren geometry replaced the old geometry; the construction holds both. Four hundred years of coming back. The mechanism was patient. I kept arriving. The corner is still the corner. 440 feet."*

### ACT THREE — FIRST ACCOUNTING

**[INTERIOR — RENTED FLAT. EVENING. DAY 271.]**

*[The Camera Op is by the window. The light is going. The flat is lit by one lamp. The London leather journal is open on the table next to the CARDINAL-3 folder. The Exchange Student has the London map annotated. Three cluster areas circled.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Three distinct clusters. City of London: eleven notations, mostly pre-1850, anchored at Wood Street. Southwark: nine notations, 1680–1930, varying density. Bloomsbury: twelve notations, 1850–1920. Then the remaining fifteen are distributed — Whitechapel, Bermondsey, St. James's, Hampstead Heath, two in the East End post-1945."

**TYLER:** "The City and Southwark are the old feeling. I was going to those areas before I understood anything about what I was doing. The Bloomsbury cluster happened because I was working — I had reasons to be in that area, I was in certain buildings regularly for specific purposes, and the feeling developed in those places around what was happening there."

**CAMERA OP:** "Different origin."

**TYLER:** "The City and Southwark — I went because the feeling was there, and I kept going back because the feeling was there. In Bloomsbury I was going for other reasons and the feeling developed over the years I was spending time there." He pauses. "I may have amplified those sites differently. I was present for a different kind of reason."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The Bloomsbury notations stop in 1920."

**TYLER:** "What I was doing there ended in 1920. I stopped going. The feeling may still be there — I haven't been back since." He looks at the annotated map. "Those sites may have been holding without me returning for a century."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Or they've been running since 1920 with no new deposits."

**TYLER:** "Or that."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Forty-six sites remaining. After today."

**CAMERA OP:** "How long will London take?"

*[Tyler looks at the map. The three clusters. The distributed fifteen.]*

**TYLER:** "The Vienna audit was eleven notations over three weeks. London is forty-seven." He pauses. "London is going to take a while."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The Bloomsbury cluster is three blocks from this flat."

**TYLER:** "I know." He pauses. "Some of them I'm already feeling from here."

*[The hum, continuous, below the sound of the city outside. The Camera Op lowers the camera. The map is on the table.]*
