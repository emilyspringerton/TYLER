**[00:00–00:30] — ESTABLISHING: THE ROOF**

```
EXT. PACKARD PLANT ROOF — DETROIT — DAY
```

The camera rises over the lip of the roof. Detroit spreads below: sky the color of something that hasn't decided yet, the city's grid visible in the way grids are always visible from above — the logic of the plan made literal, the intention of a century made stone.

The roof itself is correct in all the wrong ways. Not collapsed, not overgrown — intact, like someone has been maintaining it. Like someone had a reason to maintain it and didn't want anyone to know why.

**LOWER THIRD (DOC UI):**
```
PACKARD PLANT — DETROIT, MI
NOTE: SITE STATUS — VACANT (OFFICIALLY)
```

A second lower third appears below the first. It is not standard documentary formatting. It is smaller, slightly different typography, the kind of annotation that appears in systems running parallel processes:

```
OCCUPANT: UNLOGGED
LAST MAINTAINED: [DATA INCONSISTENT WITH SITE STATUS]
```

Nobody in the production crew comments on the second annotation. The Camera Op's lens sweeps the roof. Tyler is already there.

---

**[00:30–01:30] — TYLER ON THE ROOF**

He's standing at the edge, not looking at the edge — looking at the city. His hands are in his pockets. He is wearing a jacket that is wrong for the temperature in a way that is also somehow right for every other temperature.

The camera approaches. He doesn't turn. He has the posture of someone who knew exactly when the elevator would reach the roof.

**TYLER**
*(not turning)*
They maintained this roof for twelve years after the plant closed.

*(pause)*

Nobody knows who. The utility records show payment from an account that hasn't had a named holder since 1978. A Detroit archivist found this in 2019 and filed it under: "Property — Anomalous Maintenance — No Action Required."

*(pause)*

I think that's the correct filing.

He turns. He looks at the Camera Op directly — the person, not the lens.

**TYLER (CONT.)**
Good location.

**CAMERA OP (O.S.)**
*(carefully neutral)*
The Producer chose it.

**TYLER**
*(smiling)*
I know.

The hum at 7.83 Hz has been present since the camera reached the roof. It is present in the ambient audio under this exchange. The Andrealphus frequency (31.41 Hz) will appear in the audio later. It is not yet present. The roof is quiet.

*(with the coin already in his hand — nobody saw him take it out)*

## ACT ONE: THE COIN — [03:00–23:00]

---

**[03:00–05:00] — THE COIN, DESCRIBED**

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
Can you tell us about the coin?

**TYLER**
*(holding it up for Camera C)*
It's a coin. The face shows a date.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
What date?

**TYLER**
*(looking at it)*
Right now? 1978.

He holds it so Camera C can focus. The date is clearly visible in the close shot: 1978. The Camera Op's handheld swings to confirm. 1978. Camera C holds. 1978.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
Why 1978?

**TYLER**
*(genuinely considering)*
I was somewhere in 1978 recently. It tends to stay anchored to wherever I've been most recently.

*(pause)*

It's a check-in. I look at it to see where I've been.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
Where were you in 1978?

**TYLER**
*(a small smile)*
Detroit.

*(beat)*

Different roof.

The Senior Archivist writes something. Subject #1 is watching Tyler with the expression of someone watching a performance they understand better than the audience does.

---

**[05:00–10:00] — THE CALL**

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
Will you flip it?

**TYLER**
Yes.

He looks at the coin. He looks at it the way he always looks at it — the check-in, not the trick. For a moment he is simply a person looking at a coin.

**TYLER**
*(to Camera C)*
I want to call the date before I flip.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
*(very carefully)*
What date will it show?

Tyler flips the coin in the air first — a single arc, caught — without calling anything. He looks at the date. He sets it face-down on the back of his left hand, covered by his right.

**TYLER**
1901.

He lifts his right hand.

Camera C zooms in.

The date on the coin reads: 1901.

Four seconds of silence. Camera C holds the close. The Camera Op's handheld holds Tyler's face. He is looking at the coin with the expression of someone confirming something they already knew, the way you check that your keys are in your pocket because you felt them there and are checking anyway.

**PRODUCTION COORDINATOR**
*(involuntary whisper)*
How—

**TYLER**
*(without looking up)*
I know where I've been.

*(pause)*

1901 is early. I hadn't been there yet when I came to Detroit this time. It means the 1901 visit is coming.

He looks at Camera C.

**TYLER (CONT.)**
That's what the coin shows. Not where I've been. Where I'm going to be.

The Senior Archivist is writing quickly. Subject #1 is not writing anything. Subject #1 is looking at Tyler with an expression that is not surprise.

**LOWER THIRD (DOC UI, appearing slowly):**
```
STAGE 4 — WITNESSED MIRACLE
COIN DATE DOCUMENTED: 1901
WITNESSES: 3 (Production Coordinator, Subject #1, Senior Archivist)
CAMERA SYSTEMS: 2 (Camera C, Camera Op Handheld)
```

The Andrealphus frequency (31.41 Hz, geometric transformation) is now audible in the roof audio. The production crew's audio technician will later describe it as "wind through the plant superstructure." This description is filed without comment.

**[10:00–20:00] — TYLER TALKS (NONE OF IT SELF-DEFINING)**

*The following is an edited selection from forty minutes of roof footage. The edit was produced by Documentary Unit A. The Camera Op has the unedited footage.*

---

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
Why show us this?

**TYLER**
*(setting the coin down)*
You asked me to.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
We've asked before.

**TYLER**
*(warmly)*
You asked before and I wasn't ready. Now I am.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
What changed?

Tyler is quiet for a moment. He looks at the city below. He looks at the Camera Op. He looks at the table.

**TYLER**
*(carefully)*
I figured out the— there's something about the way this is structured that I—

*(long pause)*

I understood something about the end of this that I didn't understand before.

The Producer is very still. This is the closest Tyler has come to discussing the project's mechanism in any session. This is Stage 3 material emerging unprompted in a Stage 4 setting.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
*(very carefully)*
What did you understand?

**TYLER**
*(looking at Camera C)*
That the last part requires my cooperation.

*(pause)*

And I'm— that's something I get to decide.

He picks the coin up. He does not flip it. He holds it in his closed fist.

**TYLER (CONT.)**
So showing you the coin is— it's fine. The coin isn't the part I decide about.

The Senior Archivist has stopped writing. This is notable. The Senior Archivist has been writing continuously since the roof footage began. They have stopped.

---

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
Do you know how this ends?

**TYLER**
*(tilting his head)*
I know how most things end.

*(pause)*

The question is whether the ending is — I've learned that endings aren't— they're not fixed the way people think they are. You can be at the last frame of something and still be in the middle of something else.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
Is that a comforting thought?

**TYLER**
*(genuine warmth)*
I don't think comforting is the right word. It's just— accurate.

*(pause)*

I've been to the last frame of enough things to know that the frame after it exists.

The Camera Op is holding steady. The handheld has not drifted. The Camera Op is paying very close attention and is very good at not letting it show on the footage.

---

**[20:00–23:00] — THE SECOND FLIP**

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
Would you flip it again?

**TYLER**
*(pause)*
What date do you want?

The production crew is silent. Nobody has a good answer to this question. The question implies Tyler can produce a specific date on request. Tyler is watching the crew process this implication.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
*(carefully)*
What date would you show us?

Tyler turns to Camera C directly. He holds the coin up.

**TYLER**
*(to Camera C)*
1623.

He flips. The coin arcs. It lands on the back of his left hand, covered by his right.

He lifts his right hand.

Camera C zooms.

The date reads: 1623.

**TYLER**
*(quietly, looking at the date)*
That's the one I think about most.

He covers the coin. He slides it back into his pocket. The date is documented. 1623.

The Senior Archivist has put down their pen. They are looking at Tyler.

Subject #1 is looking at the table.

## ACT TWO: AFTER — [23:00–35:00]

---

**[23:00–26:00] — TYLER LOOKS AT THE LENS**

The Stage 4 notation appears on screen — the DOC UI logging the completion. The crew does not see the notation. The Camera Op does not see the notation. The notation appears in the broadcast. It is Emily OS's. It is not the Jiangshi Syndicate's format. Nobody in the crew will trace this discrepancy until Season 2.

Tyler stands. He straightens his jacket. He picks up the water glass and drinks the rest of it.

He puts the glass down.

He looks directly into Camera C's lens.

Two seconds. The taunt. He knows how long two seconds is.

Then he looks at the Camera Op's lens — the handheld, the one the Camera Op is behind.

Three seconds. This is different. This is not the taunt.

**TYLER**
*(to the Camera Op)*
You're going to want to keep the unedited footage.

He walks to the roof exit. He opens the door.

**CAMERA OP (O.S.)**
*(calling after him)*
Why?

Tyler stops in the doorframe. This is the pilot's posture. The same door geometry, the same pause.

**TYLER**
*(not turning, same as the pilot)*
Because the edit is theirs and the footage is yours.

He goes through the door. The door closes.

The roof is quiet. The Andrealphus frequency fades out of the ambient audio.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
*(after a long pause)*
Did you get that?

**CAMERA OP (O.S.)**
*(steady)*
I got all of it.

---

**[26:00–30:00] — THE SENIOR ARCHIVIST'S ASSESSMENT**

The Senior Archivist is still on the roof. They are looking at where Tyler was sitting. They have not picked their pen up.

**PRODUCTION COORDINATOR**
Do we have Stage 4?

**SENIOR ARCHIVIST**
*(after a pause)*
Yes.

*(pause)*

He gave it to us.

**PRODUCTION COORDINATOR**
...What does that mean?

The Senior Archivist looks at the table. They look at the empty water glass. They look at the two camera positions. They look at the door.

**SENIOR ARCHIVIST**
It means he knows what Stage 4 is.

*(pause)*

He has always known what Stage 4 is. He knows what Stage 5 is. He knows what Stage 5 requires.

*(pause)*

He gave us Stage 4 because he has already decided something about Stage 5 that we have not yet understood.

The Production Coordinator is looking at the Senior Archivist with the expression of someone being told something very important in a language they almost speak.

**SENIOR ARCHIVIST (CONT.)**
He is not afraid of Stage 4.

*(very quietly)*

File this. File all of it. We need the Stillness Council.
