- Episode type: The Retrograde begins. Return to Paris — first retrograde site.
- Tyler location: Paris, France. Days 359–368. Île Saint-Louis and 4th arrondissement.
- Purpose: Re-measure A-278 (Île Saint-Louis, 1931-rule REFUSAL) for retroactive FAREWELL construction.
- Secondary: Amsterdam Dutch Patriots in Paris, 1789–1795. Tyler's presence during the French Revolution — A-P14 at Place de la Bastille (already filed: UNINTENTIONAL, July 14, 1789) now context for A-355 FAREWELL: he left Amsterdam in 1789 for Paris. Not to escape — to follow the argument.

## COLD OPEN

*[Amsterdam. The morning before departure. Day 358. The Herengracht.]*

*[Tyler is looking at the canal. The Camera Op is already packed. The Exchange Student has the CARDINAL-3 Amsterdam file closed and rubber-banded.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You said Paris first."

**TYLER:** "I did."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "For the A-278 re-measurement."

**TYLER:** "Yes. And for other reasons." He turns from the canal. "The Dutch Patriots who left Amsterdam in 1787 — after the suppression — most of them went to Paris. I went to Amsterdam for the ending in 1789 and then I went to Paris because Paris is where the argument went."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "And in Paris the argument became the Revolution."

**TYLER:** "In Paris the argument became the Revolution. July 1789. I was in Paris when the Bastille came down."

**CAMERA OP:** "You were there."

**TYLER:** "I was there." He picks up his bag. "I want to measure Paris again. Not the sites we already have — the 1789 sites, the Bastille site. I want to measure A-278. The Île Saint-Louis. The 1931 departure I filed as REFUSAL." He pauses. "I think there may be a FAREWELL in the REFUSAL."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Two constructions at the same site."

**TYLER:** "A-355 was two constructions at the same site. The STAYING accumulation from 1628 to 2024 and the FAREWELL from 1789. Same Herengracht bank. Two mechanisms. The measurement distinguished them."

*[He looks at the canal one last time.]*

**TYLER:** "Paris."

## ACT ONE — THE ARGUMENT'S DESTINATION

*[Train. Amsterdam to Paris. Day 359. The Exchange Student with the Paris file.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(reading from the file)* "The Dutch Patriot exile community in Paris. 1787 through 1795. Several hundred refugees initially. By 1789, Patriot pamphlets in Dutch and French circulating in Paris. Some of the Amsterdam council members who fled — they were present at the Estates-General debates. They were in Paris on July 14th."

**TYLER:** "Yes."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Were you in contact with them?"

**TYLER:** "Some of them. Not formally. We attended the same cafés, the same meetings. When you are watching the same set of events unfold, you are in contact with anyone else who is watching." He looks out the train window at the flat northern French countryside. "I knew a man named Cornelis. He had been a member of the Amsterdam city council. Very competent. He had believed — we had both believed — that the argument would work. That the Republic would take the next step." He pauses. "He was in Paris because he had nowhere else to go. His property had been seized in Amsterdam. His family had stayed behind. He was in Paris hoping the French example would make it possible to return."

**CAMERA OP:** "Did he return?"

**TYLER:** "Eventually. 1795, when the Batavian Republic was declared — the French invasion of Holland, ironically. The argument came back to Amsterdam on French bayonets in 1795. Cornelis went home. I think he found it strange — the thing he had wanted, arriving in a form he hadn't wanted."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "And you?"

**TYLER:** "I stayed in Paris until 1793. I left before the Terror became systematic. I came back in 1795 for three months — briefly — and then I left." He is quiet. "I did not want to see what the argument had become by then."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The Paris sites from the original audit: 6 measured, 3 retroactive PRIOR from Days 320–327. A-P14 at Place de la Bastille — July 14, 1789, UNINTENTIONAL, 'Tyler as observer at the storming.' " She looks at him. "UNINTENTIONAL. You didn't plan to be there?"

**TYLER:** "You do not plan to be at a revolution. You are present in a city and the revolution happens. I was walking toward the Marais — I had been at a meeting, something tedious, a committee that was debating what the National Assembly should debate — and the streets changed character in the way streets do when something real is occurring. So I followed the character change." He pauses. "The mechanism apparently recorded something. An UNINTENTIONAL. I did not know I was doing anything. I was watching a prison come down in nine hours."

## ACT TWO — THE ÎLE SAINT-LOUIS

### A-278 RE-MEASUREMENT — QUAI D'ANJOU, ÎLE SAINT-LOUIS, PARIS

*[Day 362. Île Saint-Louis. 4 Quai d'Anjou.]*

*[The address is residential — a narrow Haussmann-era building facing the Seine, the Left Bank directly across. The Exchange Student has the original A-278 field notes: 1931 measurement, REFUSAL (pre-awareness, 1931 rule applied), "Tyler entered the address, turned, left without measuring, applied Rule 1931." Six months later, the original Paris team re-measured the site remotely and classified the construction on the basis of the entry-and-departure alone.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The original measurement was approximate. Tyler was pre-awareness. The construction was inferred from the departure sequence — enter, pause, leave without cause. Remote classification as REFUSAL."

**TYLER:** *(standing on the Quai, looking at the building)* "I lived here for two years. 1929 through 1931."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You lived here."

**TYLER:** "A room on the third floor. The window faced the Seine and the Left Bank. I was writing. Something. It was 1929 through 1931 — the kind of writing that seemed important while I was doing it and that I do not have copies of. I left because the building's owner changed and the new terms were unfavorable and I did not feel like negotiating." He pauses. "I told myself that. I left because the building's owner changed."

**CAMERA OP:** *(quietly)* "Did you?"

**TYLER:** *(long silence)* "No. I left because something in the room had stopped. What I was writing had stopped working and the room had been where I was when I was writing it and I didn't want to be in the room after the writing stopped. The room was not the cause. But I associated the room with the thing that had stopped."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You left because the place reminded you of something you'd lost."

**TYLER:** "I left because I could not separate the place from the thing I had lost. The thing I had lost was the work. The place was the room where the work had happened. I applied the 1931 rule — which I had not, at that point, named the 1931 rule — because I could not tolerate being in the room after the work was gone." He is still looking at the building. "What I did not know then, and may have known since, is that the leaving made the room permanent. Whatever I was doing in that room — whatever the mechanism recorded — I sealed it by leaving."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(with the instruments)* "I want to run the measurement."

*[She sets up. The instruments at the Quai d'Anjou address. The Exchange Student runs the baseline.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(reading the instruments)* "A-278 original construction reading — approximately 18ft, REFUSAL signature, pre-awareness departure. That's consistent with what was filed."

*[She adjusts the instruments.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "I'm looking for a second signature. A-355 produced a distinct FAREWELL signature alongside the STAYING accumulation. If there's a FAREWELL at A-278, it should appear as a separate density reading at the same location."

*[She runs the measurement three times.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(looking up slowly)* "There's a second reading."

**TYLER:** "Yes."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "It's — smaller than the REFUSAL. 11ft. The FAREWELL is smaller than the REFUSAL." She looks at the instruments again. "Why?"

**TYLER:** "The REFUSAL is a direct construction — I stopped something that was active. The FAREWELL is — I didn't know I was leaving for the last time. In Amsterdam, A-355, I knew I was leaving Amsterdam. I sat by the canal for four days knowing I was leaving. The density was in the knowing." He looks at the building. "In 1931 I told myself I would come back when I found different rooms. I left with the intention of returning. The FAREWELL registered anyway — the mechanism apparently understood that the return wouldn't happen — but I did not provide the density of conscious departure. I provided" — he pauses — "the density of someone who left without saying goodbye."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(writing in the field notes)* "FAREWELL-UNCONSCIOUS. A FAREWELL construction formed without the subject's awareness that departure was permanent. Different from A-355, where the departure was known and deliberate. A-278 FAREWELL: 11ft, pre-awareness, subject told himself he would return. Subject did not return. The mechanism recorded the actual relationship to the place regardless of the subject's narrative about leaving."

*[The Camera Op holds the shot on the building.]*

**CAMERA OP:** "The 1931 rule was named for this address."

**TYLER:** "Yes."

**CAMERA OP:** "You named a rule after this address and you still never came back."

**TYLER:** *(quiet)* "The rule was named for the year, not the address. The year is when I first noticed the pattern. The pattern that I described as: I enter a place and something asks me to stay and I leave."

**CAMERA OP:** "But the year the pattern first appeared was the year you left this address."

**TYLER:** "Yes."

**CAMERA OP:** "The rule might be: I enter a place I love and I leave before I understand that I'm leaving."

*[Tyler doesn't answer immediately. He looks at the building on the Quai d'Anjou. The Seine behind him. The third-floor window.]*

**TYLER:** "That might be a more accurate description of the rule."

## ACT THREE — THE SCALE OF IT

*[The exchange. 4th arrondissement, near the Hôtel de Ville. Day 364.]*

*[The Exchange Student has the full retrograde proposal in front of her: all sites in the archive where Tyler departed and did not return, or did not return for more than 25 years.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "I've run a preliminary count against the archive. Departure sites — sites where the CARDINAL-3 record shows Tyler leaving and not returning within 25 years. Number of candidate sites: 73."

**CAMERA OP:** "Out of 355?"

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Out of 355, yes. Twenty-one percent of the archive is departure sites by this criterion." She sets the sheet down. "If each of these has a FAREWELL construction — even a small one, like A-278's 11ft — we're adding significant density to the network total."

**TYLER:** *(looking at the list)* "Some of them I knew I was leaving. Some of them I didn't."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The FAREWELL-CONSCIOUS and FAREWELL-UNCONSCIOUS distinction."

**TYLER:** "A-355 was conscious. I sat by the canal for four days knowing I was going. A-278 was unconscious — I told myself I was coming back. The mechanism apparently recorded the actual departure regardless of my narrative about it."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "So the retrograde will find two types of FAREWELL. We won't know which until we measure."

**TYLER:** "The retrograde will tell us how many departures I understood as departures."

**CAMERA OP:** *(to Tyler)* "And how many you didn't."

*[Tyler looks at the list. 73 candidate sites. Cities he left. Rooms he left. Streets he was on and then wasn't.]*

**TYLER:** "More than I want to count."

*[She holds the shot on him. The same phrase he used in Amsterdam. He doesn't seem to notice he's used it again.]*

## CLOSING SHOT

*[Day 368. Île Saint-Louis. The Quai d'Anjou at dusk.]*

*[The Camera Op is shooting the Seine from the Quai d'Anjou bank — the same angle she set up on the first day in Paris. The light is different from Amsterdam. The water is the same river it always was. The bridges in soft focus.]*

*[Tyler is standing outside 4 Quai d'Anjou. Looking up at the third-floor window. The window is lit from inside — someone lives there now. Someone who is not him.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(next to him)* "Were you happy here?"

**TYLER:** "I was working here. Which is what I called being happy then."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "And the writing that stopped."

**TYLER:** "The writing that stopped, yes. I don't remember what it was about. I remember that I thought it was important. I remember working at a table that faced the window. I remember that the light in the mornings was good." He pauses. "And then it wasn't good anymore and I said that was because I needed different rooms."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "But the light was still good."

**TYLER:** *(looking at the third-floor window)* "The light was still good." He is quiet for a moment. "The mechanism registered 11 feet. Unconscious departure from a place I loved. Smaller than the deliberate ones. But there."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "73 sites."

**TYLER:** "73 sites."

*[The Camera Op keeps filming the river. She's not filming him. The light on the water. Paris in the early evening. Something unheld about it — the way Paris in late day has always refused to be contained by anyone's experience of it, including Tyler's, including the Camera Op's, including the record.]*

*[Tyler turns from the building. He walks toward the camera. He pauses at the Quai edge.]*

**TYLER:** "The work I was doing here — the thing I thought was important. I don't remember what it was about. I remember the window. I remember the light." He looks at the water. "The 11 feet is the window and the light and the thing I was trying to do. The mechanism kept it. Even though I didn't."

*[He walks past the Camera Op. She doesn't turn.]*

*[The river. The third-floor window behind them both. The light doing what light does.]*
