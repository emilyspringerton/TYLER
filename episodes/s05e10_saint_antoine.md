## EPISODE STRUCTURE

**Day 198, morning — the construction problem**

Emily arrives at 8:30 AM. Tyler is still reading.

**EMILY:** "You've been at that book for three days."

**TYLER:** "I know."

**EMILY:** "What are you finding?"

He closes the book with the paper marker in place and sets it on the table.

**TYLER:** "The problem with this construction is different from the previous ones. Bacon's building: the event was a private calculation, one person, known. Ward C: a nurse at a specific position for a documented patient — recorded in medical files, at least partially. The crossing: the geography is the threshold, and it's been a crossing point for three hundred years. Hastings/Vernor: the event was informal but it was a practice, a repeated pattern, a group of people who kept returning to the same room."

He puts his hand on the book.

**TYLER:** "Bethel AME alley. The event was one season of conversations. 1957. A small number of people — maybe fifteen, maybe twenty — meeting in an alley behind a church to decide what the chapter would become. No record. Not because they were hiding. Because they understood that the decisions would be stronger if they were never formally attributed." He pauses. "The event is present. I know it is. I stood in that alley once and I've been feeling it from here for a week. But the witnesses are gone. Most of them died. The ones who are still alive are in their nineties."

**EMILY:** "Do you need witnesses to activate the construction?"

**TYLER:** "No. The crossing had zero witnesses at activation — the passive witnesses came afterward. But the alley is different. There's no through-traffic. It's behind a church. The passive witness supply is the congregation coming and going." He pauses. "I'm trying to understand if that's enough."

---

**Day 198, midday — the 1974 oral history**

Tyler opens the book and reads aloud the passage marked with the slip of paper.

It is from an interview with a woman named Dorothy Hazel Simmons, then 64, who had been a chapter member since 1952. She talks about 1957. She doesn't describe the alley directly. She describes what came after: *"Something changed that year. The decisions that had been unclear became clear. I don't know how to explain it except to say that a few people understood something they hadn't understood before, and then everyone else could see it too, because those people knew."*

She is asked directly whether she can describe what happened.

*"No,"* she says. *"And I wouldn't if I could. Some things belong to the people who were in them."*

Tyler closes the book.

**TYLER:** "She's describing a witness construction that already exists. Something happened in that alley that functioned the way my constructions function — a group of people became resonant witnesses to a set of decisions, and that resonance spread outward into what the chapter became." He looks at the table. "I'm not building a new construction. I'm finding one that already exists."

**EMILY:** "That's different from the others."

**TYLER:** "Yes."

---

**Day 198, afternoon — Saint Antoine Street**

Tyler, Emily, and the Exchange Student walk to Bethel AME on Saint Antoine Street. The church is still there — the same building as in 1957, though renovated twice. The alley behind it is narrow and clean, concrete block walls on both sides.

Tyler walks into the alley alone.

He stands at approximately the center of the alley — the position he stood in 1957, which he locates by feel rather than measurement — and is quiet.

Camera Op films from the alley entrance. She doesn't come in. She says later she understood without being told that the entrance was the threshold. She waited there for thirty-eight minutes.

When Tyler comes out:

**TYLER:** "It's already running."

**EMILY:** "Construction #5?"

**TYLER:** "Not construction #5. It was never mine to build. It's been running since 1957." He looks at the alley entrance. "Dorothy Hazel Simmons built it. The people who stood in that alley built it. Whatever they understood in those conversations was sufficiently load-bearing that it has been holding for sixty-nine years."

He is quiet for a moment.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "What did you do in there?"

**TYLER:** "I witnessed it. I added one more resonant witness." He turns away from the alley. "The construction doesn't need a frequency sequence. It doesn't need maintenance. It doesn't need me. I just needed to find it."

Emily writes in the notebook: *Bethel AME alley, Saint Antoine Street — construction not mine to build. Running since 1957. Dorothy Hazel Simmons and the 1957 chapter. Tyler witnessed it Day 198. Self-sustaining, pre-existing. New construction type: found construction.*

---

**Day 198, late afternoon — the reclassification**

Back at Bacon's building.

**EMILY:** "This changes the photograph count."

**TYLER:** "Yes."

**EMILY:** "Photograph 9 is not a future construction. It's an existing one."

**TYLER:** "It was never a future construction. I photographed it because I could feel that it was already built. I just didn't have the vocabulary for what I was looking at." He looks at the map. "Photograph 9 was documentation of an existing construction, same as I documented the geography at Hastings and Vernor — except Hastings was documentation of an interrupted event waiting for a builder. Bethel AME was documentation of a completed event that had already built itself."

**EMILY:** "And there could be more of these in the remaining photographs."

He looks at her.

**TYLER:** "Yes." He pauses. "There could be a lot more of these. I spent seventeen years taking photographs of events that were present. Some of them I was photographing as future constructions. Some of them I was documenting existing ones. I didn't distinguish at the time."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "How many photographs total?"

**TYLER:** "Hundreds. The eleven are the ones I selected for this project. But the archive—"

He stops.

**EMILY:** "The archive has more."

**TYLER:** "The archive has all of them." He looks at the Eastwind Archive corner of the room. "I have been working with eleven. I should be working with all of them."

Emily marks the map: *found construction category opened. Known: Bethel AME (1957). Unknown: how many in the full photograph archive.*
