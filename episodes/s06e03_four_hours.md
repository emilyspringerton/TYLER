## EPISODE STRUCTURE

**Day 222, 9:15 AM — in the car**

Tyler has the 1952 photographs on his phone now — Exchange Student scanned the archive set. He is looking at one of them.

The photograph: a narrow two-story building, set between a vacant lot and a larger commercial structure. Flat-front brick. A window on the upper floor with a curtain partially drawn. At street level: a door, a small sign (unreadable at this resolution), a single step. The photograph is taken from across the street, standard survey distance. It is a composition, not a document.

**CAMERA OP:** "What's there now?"

**TYLER:** "Vacant lot."

He does not say: *that's not unusual*. It is not unusual. Several of the 1952 sites are vacant lots now or new construction or parking. The city changed and the buildings that stood in 1952 are often gone.

He does not say this. He is still looking at the photograph.

**CAMERA OP:** "What was in the building?"

**TYLER:** "A community room. Informal music. Neighborhood pianists, students, practice sessions. It wasn't organized — it wasn't a venue. There was a woman who coordinated the room use, Mrs. —" He stops. He looks at the photograph. "I don't remember her last name. She let me in."

Long pause. Car noise.

**CAMERA OP:** "How long were you there?"

**TYLER:** "Four hours and forty minutes."

He does not add anything to this.

---

**Day 222, 9:50 AM — A-269, East Ferry Avenue**

The vacant lot is between a residential building on one side and a brick structure that may predate the lot on the other. The lot itself is clear — no rubble, no vegetation beyond grass at the edges, the compacted earth of a space that has been empty long enough to settle. A space that has accepted its emptiness.

Tyler stands at the sidewalk boundary for a moment before stepping in.

The Camera Op positions at the far corner of the lot. She doesn't direct this from Tyler; she finds the angle. She has been finding angles for five seasons.

Tyler walks to the center of the lot. He is not walking to the geographic center — he is walking to a different center, one the Camera Op's footage will show but that the Camera Op's footage does not explain: the center of whatever field is present at this site. He steps to it without navigating. He just goes there.

He stops.

He reads the site.

He reads the site for longer than A-267 took. He reads it for longer than A-268 took. He reads it the way he read Hastings/Vernor — which was different from the other reads, which was the specific stillness of recognition.

At four minutes he says:

**TYLER:** "There's a found construction here."

He does not say: *and it's mine.* He says: *found construction.* Pre-Tyler. Something here before he arrived in 1952.

**CAMERA OP:** "Like A-267?"

**TYLER:** "Structurally, yes. Something installed here at some point before 1952, not by me. Running independently." He doesn't move from the center point. "The origin resonant event is from the early 1930s. Something happened at this site in the early 1930s that started a construction and kept it running."

He is quiet for a moment.

**TYLER:** "But the radius is wrong."

---

**Day 222, the wrong radius**

**CAMERA OP:** "What do you mean, wrong?"

**TYLER:** "A found construction that has not been deliberately worked on — that has had no anchor visits, no reinforcement, no deliberate amplification — at this age, at the level of activity I can read here, should have a resonance radius of approximately fifteen to twenty feet." He looks at the edges of the lot. "I'm reading forty-five feet. The construction is substantially larger than it should be."

The Camera Op does not say anything. She keeps filming.

Tyler looks at the photograph on his phone. He looks at the lot. He looks at the photograph.

**TYLER:** "I was here in 1952 for four hours and forty minutes."

Silence.

**CAMERA OP:** *(quietly)* "How many photographs did you take?"

**TYLER:** "Three."

**CAMERA OP:** "The survey protocol is one photograph per site."

**TYLER:** "Yes."

He says this plainly. He is not defending the deviation. He is reporting it.

**CAMERA OP:** "Do you know why you took three?"

**TYLER:** "The first photograph is the survey photograph. It's the one that's in the 1952 archive — the composition is standard. The second photograph..." He looks at the lot again. "I said I couldn't get the composition I wanted."

**CAMERA OP:** "And the third?"

**TYLER:** "I don't know why I photographed it a third time."

This is the first time Tyler has said *I don't know why* about a specific deliberate action in the 1952 survey. The survey was methodical. He knew what he was doing at each site. He photographed once. He left.

He photographed this site three times. He stayed for four hours and forty minutes. He let a woman whose last name he cannot now recall let him into the building and he sat in the back and listened to a pianist he did not know practice a piece he has never heard again.

He stayed.

**TYLER:** "Someone was practicing for a church recital. The following Sunday, she was going to play this piece at her church — I don't know which church. I don't know if she did. I never went back to this building after that day. I photographed it twice more from the outside and I left Detroit three weeks later."

He takes the coin out of his pocket. He holds it without looking at it.

**TYLER:** "The radius is forty-five feet. The construction has been running since the early 1930s and it's the size of a construction that's been worked on."

He turns the coin over. 1901.

**TYLER:** "I think I made it larger."

---

**Day 222, the question**

**CAMERA OP:** "You made a found construction larger."

**TYLER:** "I think I did. I didn't start it. I didn't build anything. I just — stayed. I listened to someone practice for four hours. I took three photographs. I left."

**CAMERA OP:** "That's not what you did at the Blue Bird Inn."

**TYLER:** "At the Blue Bird Inn I started a construction without knowing I was starting it. The Lateef set — I paid attention to something that was ending, and the attention left something behind. A-268 is mine." He pauses. "This is different. This was already here. I didn't start it. I stayed in a room with it for four hours and forty minutes and whatever was here..." He looks at the edges of the lot again. "It grew."

**CAMERA OP:** "Does that have a name?"

**TYLER:** "Not yet."

**CAMERA OP:** "But it's a category."

He does not answer immediately. He puts the coin back in his pocket.

**TYLER:** "I need to understand it better before I name it. The problem is — if this is a category, I need to go back through every site I spent significant time at and ask whether I amplified a found construction without knowing I was doing it." He looks at the lot. "That's a very large amount of material."

**CAMERA OP:** "Larger than the unintentional constructions?"

**TYLER:** "Unintentional constructions require a specific kind of attention at a specific kind of moment — the unrepeatable moment. Not every place I've spent time qualifies." He is thinking. "This doesn't seem to require the irreplaceable-moment condition. This seems to require something simpler: duration. Sustained presence. I was here for four hours. The construction was already here. The two things — my presence and the existing construction — interacted over four hours and forty minutes and now the radius is forty-five feet."

**CAMERA OP:** "You make them larger by being there."

**TYLER:** "Possibly. I don't know the mechanism yet." He pauses. "I'll come back."

The Camera Op holds on Tyler for a moment, then lets the camera settle on the lot. The empty ground. The grass at the edges. Forty-five feet of resonance radius around something she can't see.

He hasn't said *I'll come back* about any of the other sites. He said it about this one.

---

**Day 222, 11:30 AM — at Bacon's building**

Exchange Student has already pulled the full 1952 archive entry for A-269 by the time they return. She has cross-referenced CARDINAL-3's notes.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "CARDINAL-3 has a note about this site. October 1952. It says: 'Subject observed at East Ferry location for extended period — fourth revisit to this site during the survey period. Behavior consistent with previous survey methodology except duration: four hours forty minutes (previous sites: fifteen to forty minutes). No apparent trigger for extended stay. Subject entered building at 10:20 AM. Photographed exterior on departure at 3:07 PM. Second exterior photograph at 3:14 PM. Third exterior photograph at 3:31 PM. Departed at 3:32 PM.' CARDINAL-3 closed the note: 'Purpose of extended visit undetermined.'"

Tyler reads this. He is very still.

**TYLER:** "CARDINAL-3 was watching."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "This whole time, yes. All eleven sites."

**TYLER:** "They logged the four hours and forty minutes."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "And the three photographs. They noted the deviation from survey protocol. They didn't know what it meant."

Tyler looks at the CARDINAL-3 note on Exchange Student's screen. Then he looks at Emily.

**EMILY:** "Purpose of extended visit undetermined." She is not reading from the screen. She is looking at the map on the table — the Season 6 map she has been updating since the Blue Bird Inn, the one that now has UNINTENTIONAL and REINFORCEMENT categories marked in different colors. She picks up her pen. "What color should A-269 be?"

**TYLER:** "I don't have a name yet."

**EMILY:** "What shape do you want to use?"

He considers this. Then:

**TYLER:** "A question mark. For now."

Emily draws a question mark on the map at A-269.
