## COLD OPEN

Day 234. Morning.

Tyler is reading the 1928 Paris survey register — the earlier notebook, before the 1931 one. The Camera Op finds him at the table again. This seems to be his thing in Paris: mornings with his own handwriting.

**CAMERA OP:** "What year is that?"

**TYLER:** "1928. Three years before the salon period. I was in Paris for different reasons."

**CAMERA OP:** "What reasons?"

Tyler closes the notebook.

**TYLER:** "I was having an argument."

**CAMERA OP:** "With who?"

**TYLER:** "Someone from the Ichthyosapiens. We disagreed about the appropriate use of a particular kind of documentation. The argument took a week and we were both right about different parts of it and wrong about different parts of it and by the end we had reached a provisional agreement that required extensive revision three years later."

**CAMERA OP:** "And you stayed at a hotel on Rue de Rivoli."

**TYLER:** "I stayed at a hotel on Rue de Rivoli. The argument was mostly conducted in a room on the third floor with inadequate lighting. I remember the inadequate lighting very clearly."

**CAMERA OP:** "CARDINAL-3 flagged it."

**TYLER:** "Of course CARDINAL-3 flagged it. An extended-duration stay in a hotel room in Paris in 1928 while conducting a faction dispute. That's very flaggable."

Title card: **DAY 234**

## A-276: RUE DE RIVOLI

The hotel is still operating — a different name now, renovated twice since 1928, but the third floor has the same geometry. The Paris Correspondent has arranged access.

The measurement starts at street level. The Exchange Student has the projection: one week of Tyler presence, hotel context (high transient traffic, no established found construction, standard baseline). Projected radius: seventy to ninety feet.

The Paris Correspondent completes the grid.

**PARIS CORRESPONDENT:** "86 feet."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Within projection."

**TYLER:** "The argument generated 86 feet."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The attention generated 86 feet. The argument was the context for the attention."

Tyler looks at the hotel facade.

**TYLER:** "We were arguing about documentation methodology for extended-duration subjects. The Ichthyosapiens had a different framework from the Jiangshi — less classification-oriented, more experiential. I thought their framework was insufficiently rigorous. He thought mine was insufficiently alive."

A pause.

**TYLER:** "He was right about more of it."

**CAMERA OP:** "Is that who you were wrong about?"

**TYLER:** "I was wrong about a lot of things in 1928. The Russian translation was 1924." He pauses. "The provisional agreement we reached has actually held better than I expected. He might not know the audit is happening. I should tell him."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Who is he?"

**TYLER:** "I'll introduce you in Osaka. He has a methodology that will be relevant for the 1967 sites."

## A-277: COUR DAMOYE

Day 235. Afternoon.

A-277 is not a building. It is a passage — Cour Damoye, off Rue de la Roquette, 11th arrondissement. A covered arcade that hasn't changed much since the nineteenth century. The CARDINAL-3 notation is from 1933: *"Passage, 11th. Afternoon, sun on stone. Two hours."*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "1933. Two hours. This is our smallest projected deposit in Paris."

**TYLER:** "I was passing through. The light was interesting."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You stayed two hours because the light was interesting."

**TYLER:** "The light was very interesting. The angle in late afternoon in a covered passage — the way it moves. I had nowhere particular to be. I watched it for two hours."

The Paris Correspondent measures the passage end to end. The current radius is 19 feet. Below the twenty-foot standard for a two-hour stay — the baseline was minimal (a covered arcade with transient foot traffic, no established found construction).

**PARIS CORRESPONDENT:** "19 feet. Projection was 20-28. Slightly below range."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Noted. Minimal-baseline sites may run slightly lower. Two hours on a zero-baseline site."

**TYLER:** He is standing in the passage looking at the afternoon light, which is currently doing the same thing the 1933 light did. "The light is still interesting."

**CAMERA OP:** "You're going to make it 20 feet."

**TYLER:** He looks at her. He walks out of the passage.

**CAMERA OP:** *(quietly, to the Exchange Student)* "Did he know what he was doing?"

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "He chose to leave. That's different from not knowing."

## A-278: THE REFUSAL

Day 236.

This is the site the Exchange Student found three weeks ago, flagging it separately from the other anomalous-duration sites.

The CARDINAL-3 record, 1931, shows Tyler arriving at an address on Île Saint-Louis — a private residence, now apartments — and leaving twelve minutes later. Standard survey notation for a brief visit. The anomaly is in the field cross-reference note, appended by a CARDINAL-3 operative in 1967: *"Subject accessed site Day 12 of Paris visit, 1931. Duration: 12 minutes. Entry log shows no reported anomaly. Possible explanation: subject visited socially, host unavailable. Note: site has subsequently shown anomalous ambient elevation of unknown origin."*

The Exchange Student found the 1967 note and cross-referenced: the anomalous ambient elevation had been running since approximately 1931 with no identified source.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The 1967 CARDINAL-3 note says 'host unavailable.' But the site shows a construction deposit consistent with Tyler's parameters. Small — 11 feet — but there."

**TYLER:** He reads the 1967 note. "There was no host. I wasn't there socially."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Then why did you leave after twelve minutes?"

A long pause.

**TYLER:** "I felt something in the building."

**CAMERA OP:** "What kind of something?"

**TYLER:** "The kind of something that asks you to stay." He pauses. "I left."

Silence.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You felt the pull and you left."

**TYLER:** "I was aware, in 1931, that there were places that kept me longer than I intended. I had filed several of those places as background information without understanding the mechanism. I had developed a heuristic: if somewhere asked me to stay and I could not identify a reason to stay, I should leave."

**CAMERA OP:** "A deliberate rule."

**TYLER:** "An instinct codified into a rule. It felt like the appropriate response to not understanding what was happening."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "But the construction left eleven feet anyway."

**TYLER:** "Apparently twelve minutes is enough time if the underlying construction is already—" He stops. He looks at the building. "Is there a found construction here?"

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The island has been continuously inhabited since Roman settlement. The building dates to 1640. Whatever was at this address for four hundred years before you arrived—"

**TYLER:** "Eleven feet for twelve minutes of Tyler standing outside a building in 1931 on top of four hundred years of occupation." He pauses. "I refused and it still left something."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** She writes in her notebook. "I'm calling this one FIRST PRE-AWARENESS REFUSAL. It's the earliest documented instance of Tyler applying a deliberate decision not to amplify a construction — before he had the word amplification, before he knew that's what he was doing."

**TYLER:** "The rule I made in 1931 — if somewhere asks you to stay and you don't know why, leave — I've applied that rule for ninety years."

**CAMERA OP:** "How many times?"

**TYLER:** A long pause. "I don't know. Many times." He looks at the building. "The audit is going to find all of them. Every A-site where I felt the pull and didn't stay. Every eleven-foot deposit from twelve minutes of standing outside."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The 1931 rule was trying to protect you from something you didn't have the vocabulary for."

**TYLER:** "It didn't work very well."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "It kept you from staying. It didn't keep you from being present."

Tyler looks at A-278 for a long time.

**TYLER:** *(quietly)* "That's a kind interpretation."

## A-279: PLACE DES VOSGES

Day 238. Last Paris site.

A-279 is a bench in the Place des Vosges — the oldest planned square in Paris, 17th century, the geometry unchanged since 1612. The CARDINAL-3 notation, 1932, is the shortest in the Paris file: *"PdV. One hour. Coffee."*

The Exchange Student measured the current radius before Tyler arrived: 8 feet.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The baseline here is significant — four hundred years of continuous public use, one of the oldest planned urban spaces in Paris. Tyler's one hour adds approximately eight feet to a construction that was already substantial."

**TYLER:** "I wasn't paying attention. I was drinking coffee and thinking about the Ichthyosapiens argument."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The mechanism doesn't require deliberate attention. The attention is ambient. You were present."

Tyler sits on the bench. The Camera Op films him sitting on a bench in a 17th-century Parisian square where he spent an inattentive hour in 1932 and left eight feet of something running.

**TYLER:** "Eight feet for an hour of not paying attention."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Technically, three hours on Rue de l'Odéon for being wrong about the Russian left twenty-nine feet. The mechanism distinguishes duration more than quality."

**TYLER:** "So being wrong about the Russian was worth three times as much as not paying attention in the Place des Vosges."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Being wrong about the Russian for three hours was worth three times as much as being inattentive for one hour. The hours are the variable."

Tyler drinks his coffee — he has acquired a coffee from somewhere on the way in. He is drinking it on the bench where he drank coffee in 1932 while not paying attention.

**CAMERA OP:** "Paris is done?"

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Six sites. A-274 through A-279. Total documented Paris amplification: 163ft + 29ft + 86ft + 19ft + 11ft + 8ft. Paris phase: complete."

**TYLER:** "Vienna next."

**CAMERA OP:** "The 1938 register."

**TYLER:** "The 1938 register. Eleven notations in four months." He pauses. "I was having a different kind of difficult time in Vienna in 1938. The Ichthyosapiens argument was clean — we both wanted to reach a good agreement. Vienna in 1938 was—" He looks at his coffee. "Vienna was trying not to notice something very large happening very close to me."

**CAMERA OP:** "And you left constructions."

**TYLER:** "I stayed places. The staying was about something else. The constructions were incidental."

He drinks the rest of his coffee.

**TYLER:** "Six sites in Paris. One week to measure what I made here in twenty years of visits. I made it by arguing and eating bread and watching light and staying in rooms that asked me to stay and — once — leaving when the room asked and leaving something anyway."

He looks at the square — the geometry, the fountains, the arcades. The oldest planned square in Paris, unchanged since 1612.

**TYLER:** "It's a very good square."
