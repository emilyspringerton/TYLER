## COLD OPEN — [RECOVERED, NO TIMECODE]

```
EXT. THE FACTORY — DOOR — CONTINUOUS FROM SOURCE TRANSCRIPT
```

*[Where "The Duck Also Has Opinions About the Hoodie" ends: Tyler folding a hoodie, saying he never
comes for anything, he just doesn't leave empty-handed if he can help it. A pizza on fire, off-camera,
that neither he nor the duck looks at. The transcript ends there. This is what the archive found
spliced to the back of it — same reel, no visible cut, filed as one recovery even though the two
halves don't match stock.]*

The door he was looking at opens before he reaches it. Not for him — it swings on its own weight, the
way a door left unlatched in a building with bad foundations does when something shifts.

Outside: not the parking lot a factory should have. A transit stop. A bench. An intersection he has
sat at before, in other footage, in other builds, though nothing in this transcript says so and the
archive isn't going to say it for him.

He sits on the bench. The hoodie stays folded in his lap.

The duck, in the tuxedo, walks out after him. It does not fly. It has legs and uses them, unhurried,
the way something with the power of telekinesis doesn't need to hurry.

**DUCK**
You're not going to ask how I got out here.

**TYLER**
No.

**DUCK**
Most people ask.

**TYLER**
Most people didn't watch you tell a factory you were a government agent covering for a frog.

**DUCK**
The frog's shy. Somebody has to carry that.

Tyler unfolds the hoodie once, checks the two words on the back — STINKIES, then, smaller,
COMMISSAIRE — and folds it again, the same fold as before, like refolding a map you already read.

**DUCK**
You're going to give that away.

**TYLER**
Eventually. That's what it's for.

**DUCK**
To who.

*(A pause. The kind the record calls out when it runs long — this one is not timed, because no
instrument was running, but it reads long on the page.)*

**TYLER**
Whoever's still standing here when I'm not.

## ACT ONE — [RECOVERED, NO TIMECODE]

---

The duck sits on the bench. Not next to him — on the armrest, the way something small and
telekinetic sits when it wants to be at eye level without asking permission.

**DUCK**
I told the factory I have the power of telekinesis. I want to be clear that was true.

**TYLER**
I believe you.

**DUCK**
Nobody believes me. That's usually the bit.

**TYLER**
I've met a coin that reads next February. A hoodie isn't going to be the thing that breaks my day.

The duck considers this. Somewhere behind them, faint, the sound of a pizza still burning — the
factory, or wherever the factory actually was, keeping its one promise from the source transcript,
which is that nobody was ever going to put it out.

**DUCK**
The unicorn thinks the hoodie is a joke. Eighty units to fund a print run to fund a hat to fund a
store that doesn't exist yet.

**TYLER**
The unicorn's a robot. Robots don't fund things. They calculate whether things were worth funding
after the fact, which is a different job and a worse one.

**DUCK**
And you?

**TYLER**
*(the coin, out of the pocket, one flip, caught without looking)*
I don't calculate. I carry. Somebody else does the math on whether it was worth it.

He reads the coin. Doesn't say the date. Pockets it.

**DUCK**
That's not an answer.

**TYLER**
It's the only one I've got that's held up this long.

## TAG — [RECOVERED, NO TIMECODE]

---

```
EXT. TRANSIT STOP — BENCH — CONTINUOUS
```

Tyler stands. The hoodie goes under his arm, not in a bag — carried the way you carry something
you intend to hand off in person, not ship.

**DUCK**
You never told me your name.

**TYLER**
You never asked. You told me about the frog instead.

**DUCK**
Fair.

*(The duck does not follow him. It stays on the bench armrest, tuxedo unrumpled, watching him walk
toward the intersection — toward, the archive notes without asserting it, a store with an awning and
a punch card and a Bar Manager still alphabetizing the shelf.)*

**DUCK**
*(calling after him, not raising its voice, which somehow carries anyway)*
For the record — I *am* the chosen one. That part wasn't the pizza talking.

Tyler doesn't turn around.

**TYLER**
Everybody says that once. Most people are wrong.

*(a beat)*

**TYLER**
I've been wrong about it for six hundred years running. Doesn't stop me carrying the hoodie anyway.

He walks. The bench, the duck, the factory that was maybe never a factory — all of it stays behind,
unresolved, the way the source transcript always said it would: *you are owed something now, for
reading this. I haven't decided what yet.*

The archive hasn't decided either. Filed as recovered, not as closed.
