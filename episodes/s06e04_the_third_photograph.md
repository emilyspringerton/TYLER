## COLD OPEN

Day 224. 7:30 AM.

Tyler has the 1952 photographs spread on the table. Not all eleven — just the three from A-269. He has printed the scan Exchange Student made. The photographs are physical now. He can hold them.

The Camera Op comes in at 7:30 and sees the photographs on the table and sits down across from Tyler without speaking. She can see what they are from across the table. She does not say: *you said you'd come back.* She does not say anything.

Tyler picks up the third photograph.

**TYLER:** "The composition is wrong."

He is not looking at the Camera Op. He is looking at the photograph.

**TYLER:** "The first two photographs are survey photographs. Survey methodology: subject faces the building from across the street, standard angle, centered. The building fills approximately two-thirds of the frame. Sidewalk at the bottom, sky at the top. I have forty-three survey photographs from the 1952 Detroit archive and all of them have the same composition. Including the first two photographs of A-269."

He puts down the third photograph so she can see it.

**TYLER:** "This one."

The Camera Op looks at it. It takes her a moment.

**CAMERA OP:** "That's taken from inside."

**TYLER:** "Yes."

The third photograph shows the street. East Ferry Avenue in October 1952 — the frame of a doorway at the bottom, the brick exterior of the building at the edges, and the street beyond: a parked car, the residential building across the way, the sidewalk, a tree. Not the building's exterior. The street, as seen from the building's threshold.

Tyler photographed the building from outside twice. Then he went back inside — back to the threshold, to the door he entered through — and photographed the street.

**CAMERA OP:** "You were standing in the doorway looking out."

**TYLER:** "I don't remember doing that."

## EPISODE STRUCTURE

**Day 224, 8:15 AM — East Ferry Avenue**

The vacant lot is the same. Morning instead of mid-morning. The light is different. The lot is the same.

Tyler stands at the sidewalk boundary. The Camera Op is already positioned at the far corner — the position she found on Day 222, the same angle. She is consistent in her threshold-finding. The angle is hers now.

Tyler walks to the center of the lot. He stops.

He reads it. Longer than Day 222. The same specific stillness. He is not doing anything except being present.

At six minutes he puts the third photograph in his pocket and takes out the coin.

He holds the coin and he stays.

---

**Day 224, 9:00 AM — the edge**

Forty-five minutes in, the Camera Op starts walking the perimeter of the lot. She is not walking it randomly — she is walking the way she walked the Ambassador Bridge before Tyler placed the notation anchor, the way she walked the approach to Detroit Receiving before Construction #2. She is finding the threshold.

She makes one pass. She makes a second pass, tighter. She stops at a point on the north edge of the lot, six feet beyond the property boundary, on the sidewalk.

She marks it with her heel in the dirt at the sidewalk edge. She keeps filming.

She walks the south side. She marks a point there. She marks the east edge. The west edge is the wall of the adjacent building — she notes it and moves on.

When Tyler eventually steps back from the center and looks at her:

**TYLER:** "What did you find?"

**CAMERA OP:** "Sixty-two feet."

She says this as a measurement, not a question. She is not asking if sixty-two is correct. She has already measured it.

Tyler takes out his phone. He uses the same method he used on Day 222 — the one he has not explained to the camera but which produces a consistent reading. He walks the perimeter from the center outward.

He confirms each of her marks.

**TYLER:** "Sixty-two feet."

He looks at the lot. He looks at her marks. He has been here for forty-five minutes. The construction is seventeen feet larger than it was two days ago.

He was at Hastings/Vernor for four hours and forty minutes in 1952. He was at this site for four hours and forty minutes in 1952. The construction was forty-five feet when he arrived on Day 222. He has been here for forty-five minutes on Day 224 and it is already sixty-two feet.

**TYLER:** "The mechanism is faster when I know what I'm doing."

The Camera Op keeps filming. She does not say: *I found the edge before you measured it.* She does not say: *I always find the edge.* She keeps filming.

---

**Day 224, 9:20 AM — the third photograph**

Tyler takes out the printed photograph.

He is standing inside the lot. The building that stood here in 1952 is gone — vacant lot, settled earth, the compacted ground of a space that has accepted its emptiness. But the footprint is still visible if you know to look: the lot's boundaries, the slight difference in soil composition near the street edge where the building's foundation was, the slab corner at the north end where the floor met the sidewalk.

He is looking for the threshold. The place where the door was.

He finds it at the north edge of the old foundation footprint: a slightly raised section, the remnant of the entry step. He stands on it.

He holds the third photograph at the level it was taken.

The Camera Op watches him do this from across the lot. She moves without being directed. She positions behind him and looks at the back of the photograph and the street beyond it simultaneously — the 1952 photograph and the 2026 street in the same frame.

The angle matches.

**TYLER:** "I photographed the street from the doorway."

He is standing where the door was. He is holding the 1952 photograph that was taken from where he is now standing. Behind him is the vacant lot. In front of him is the street.

**TYLER:** "I don't remember coming back to the door. I remember sitting in the back of the room for four hours. I remember the piece she was playing — not the name, but the character of it, a recital-piece quality, something practiced and precise. I remember leaving. I remember the first survey photograph from across the street." He pauses. "I have no memory of standing in the doorway on the way out and turning to photograph the street."

He looks at the photograph for a long time.

**TYLER:** "I was inside the construction radius. I had been inside it for four hours. When I photographed from across the street — survey position — I was outside the radius. The standard survey distance is outside the field."

He is not asking a question. He is working something out.

**TYLER:** "I came back to the threshold and I photographed the street from inside the field. I don't know why. I don't know if I made a choice. I walked back to the door — after two exterior survey photographs — and I photographed outward."

He looks at the 2026 street from the 1952 threshold. A car passes. A person walking a dog. An ordinary October morning in 2026.

**TYLER:** "The construction had been running since the early 1930s. Someone started it here. It asked for something and a community room grew around it. A woman I can't remember organized the space. Pianists came to practice. And in October 1952 I came for a survey and I stayed four hours and I photographed from outside and then I came back and I stood at the threshold and I photographed outward and I don't know if I was choosing to do that or if the construction was asking me to document it from inside."

Long pause. Car noise.

**TYLER:** "Both possibilities concern me."

---

**Day 224, 10:30 AM — the long stay**

Tyler stays in the lot until 1:30 PM. Six hours.

He is not at the center the whole time. He sits on the ground for a while. He stands at the old threshold. He walks the perimeter — not measuring, just walking.

He does not speak for most of it. The Camera Op films. She keeps finding the edges, periodically re-marking them as they expand.

At 11:40 AM she says, quietly, from the north perimeter:

**CAMERA OP:** "Seventy feet."

Tyler does not respond immediately. Then:

**TYLER:** "I know."

At 1:30 PM he stands and puts the coin back in his pocket.

**TYLER:** "Seventy-one feet."

He looks at the lot one more time.

**TYLER:** "AMPLIFICATION. That's the name."

He says it flatly. Not an announcement. The way he says things he has been holding — the way he held the name of the Camera Op's threshold-finding function for six seasons before naming it aloud.

**CAMERA OP:** "You're naming it now?"

**TYLER:** "I have two data points. A-269 in 1952, in two sessions: four hours forty minutes, then six hours today. The radius went from baseline to forty-five feet to seventy-one feet. The mechanism is clear enough to name."

He doesn't define it beyond that. He doesn't say: *sustained presence at a found construction increases its resonance radius.* He's already named the mechanism. That's enough.

**CAMERA OP:** "What about the third photograph?"

**TYLER:** "I still don't know if I chose to take it." He picks up his camera bag. "I may not ever know. The distinction matters — but what I documented matters too. I documented the construction from inside. The photograph exists. I have it."

He looks at the lot one last time.

**TYLER:** "Let's go to A-270."

---

**Day 224, 2:10 PM — A-270**

A-270 is four blocks north. An empty storefront on a block of mixed-use buildings that has been changing for fifteen years — some renovated, some still waiting. The storefront is clean but not occupied. A paper sign in the window: AVAILABLE.

Tyler reads it in twelve minutes.

**TYLER:** "Found construction. Pre-Tyler. 1940s origin." He steps back from the front of the building. "Expected size. The radius is approximately eighteen feet."

He checks the 1952 survey notes on his phone. Exchange Student has them scanned.

**TYLER:** "Standard survey duration. I photographed it once. I was here for approximately twenty minutes."

Twenty minutes, one photograph. Expected radius. Mechanism confirmed by contrast.

**CAMERA OP:** "What's the construction from?"

**TYLER:** "A decision made here. 1947. I don't have more than that." He looks at the building. "Something was resolved here. A conversation, or the end of one. The construction has been running on that resolution for seventy-nine years."

He photographs the building once. Survey distance. Standard composition.

**CAMERA OP:** "You're not going to stay."

**TYLER:** "Twenty minutes is enough. The construction isn't asking for more."

He says this plainly. He has been saying *the construction asked me to stay* and *the construction isn't asking for more* as casual observations, as if constructions asking for things is not significant — as if he has been receiving requests from constructions for a long time and is only now putting language to it.

**CAMERA OP:** *(quietly, to herself, and to the camera)* "The construction isn't asking for more."

She holds on the storefront. Eighteen-foot radius. A 1947 decision. A paper sign that says AVAILABLE.

**Day 224, 3:45 PM — at Bacon's building**

Exchange Student has updated the Season 6 map by the time they return. The orange question mark at A-269 is gone. It is replaced with a solid orange marker: AMPLIFICATION. Next to it, the measurement: 71 feet.

**EMILY:** "The contrast confirms the mechanism. A-269: long stay, radius far above expected. A-270: standard survey duration, expected radius. The independent variable is duration. You are the controlled element."

**TYLER:** "I've been the controlled element in most of this."

He says it without bitterness. It is an observation.

**EMILY:** "The third photograph."

**TYLER:** "What about it?"

**EMILY:** "You documented the construction from inside. Before you knew what a construction was. Before you knew you had amplified it. You stood at its threshold and you photographed outward from inside the field."

**TYLER:** "Yes."

**EMILY:** "The construction was running for twenty years when you arrived in 1952. It had been asking for a witness and the space around it had been organized accordingly — a community room, a pianist, a woman who didn't know why she kept the space open for neighborhood musicians." She pauses. "You walked in and you stayed four hours and when you left you documented it from the inside."

She does not say: *the construction chose you.* She does not say: *you were always going to do this.* She puts a small mark on the map at A-269's position — a notation she makes sometimes, in her smallest handwriting — and she moves on.

Tyler watches her.

**TYLER:** "Three sites remain."

**EMILY:** "Three sites remain."
