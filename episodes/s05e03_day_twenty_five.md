## COLD OPEN

The Camera Op has been reviewing her footage from the past twenty-five days.

She has it organized by position: footage from the doorway, footage from the center of the ward, footage from the window, footage from the nurse's position. She's been reviewing them separately.

At 7 AM she stops on a clip from Day 105. She's been watching for eleven minutes.

Bacon comes in — he's been bringing coffee to both sites now, which no one asked him to do — and sets a cup next to her.

**BACON:** "What are you looking at?"

**CAMERA OP:** "The footage from the east wall."

She turns the screen toward him. The footage shows Tyler working in the center of the ward. Standard documentary footage: well-composed, steady, capturing the quality of the light.

**BACON:** "What am I looking for?"

**CAMERA OP:** "Nothing. That's the footage from the center position." She switches to a different clip. The same day, same light. Tyler in the same position. "This is from the nurse's position."

**BACON:** "The one on the right looks—"

**CAMERA OP:** "Different."

**BACON:** "Different how?"

She thinks about it.

**CAMERA OP:** "Older. The footage on the right looks like it was shot decades ago. The same camera, the same day, the same subject. But the footage knows it's standing in a position that has been used for witnessing since 1918, and it looks like it."

## EPISODE STRUCTURE

**Day 119, morning — what Tyler says about the deadline**

Emily arrives with the network map notebook at 8 AM. She goes directly to the question she's been waiting to ask.

**EMILY:** "Fifty-seven days until the conversion starts. You estimated forty to forty-five days for the construction. That's a twelve-to-seventeen day buffer. Is it enough?"

Tyler is standing in the center of Ward C. He's been here since 6 AM.

**TYLER:** "Define 'enough.'"

**EMILY:** "If the conversion starts before the construction is complete—"

**TYLER:** "The construction continues during the conversion. Conversion work doesn't interact with what I'm building. Different layers." He looks at the east windows. "The buffer is comfortable. The construction will be complete before Day 139 at the current pace."

**EMILY:** "What changes the pace?"

**TYLER:** "The density of what the room is holding. This ward held more than the room at Bacon's — the event is larger, more layered, there are multiple people's experiences embedded in it. Clarifying that takes longer than clarifying a single event." He pauses. "But the room is cooperating. It wants to be legible."

**EMILY:** "Rooms want things?"

**TYLER:** "They have a quality that functions like wanting. This one has been holding the 1918 event for a hundred and eight years with no one paying attention to it. The attention itself is changing how it responds."

- *Primary material*: both — continuous five-frequency activation (Stolas dominant).
- *Witness requirement*: both — minimum 3 resonant witnesses for full amplitude.
- *Subscriber involvement*: Bacon's — notation present. Detroit Receiving — **no Subscriber notation yet**.

She taps the last item.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The Subscriber left instructions at Bacon's building. The frequency diagram was in the room. Is there equivalent notation here? Because if the Subscriber's notation is load-bearing for the construction — if it's part of the material requirements — then this construction has a missing dependency."

The table is quiet.

**EMILY:** "Tyler."

He has been listening from the center of the ward.

**TYLER:** "I know."

**EMILY:** "Is it load-bearing?"

**TYLER:** "The notation at Bacon's made the construction easier. It wasn't required — the Subscriber put it there as a gift, not a prerequisite. The room can be clarified without it." He looks at the east windows. "But the construction will be less stable without an anchor. The Bacon room has the Subscriber's notation holding the frequencies in calibration. Without something equivalent here, the construction will need more active maintenance."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "More witnesses. More frequently."

**TYLER:** "Yes."

**Day 119, midday — the Camera Op and the footage**

The Camera Op brings her laptop to the main folding table at noon. She shows the split-screen footage to Tyler.

He watches both clips. He doesn't say anything for a while.

**TYLER:** "The footage from the nurse's position is different because you're filming from inside a position that's been used for witnessing since 1918. The camera is inheriting the function of the position."

**CAMERA OP:** "The camera is—"

**TYLER:** "Doing what that position has always done. Standing in it and recording what it sees. The position has memory. Your camera is reading the memory and the footage carries it."

She looks at the clips.

**CAMERA OP:** "The archive opened a separate file for this. Eastwind told me."

**TYLER:** "I know. I read the footnote."

**CAMERA OP:** "What does the archive think it's documenting?"

**TYLER:** "A pattern. You locate significant positions before anyone tells you they're significant. The tree. The nurse's position. The archive thinks this is a distinct function that should be tracked separately from your general witnessing work."

**CAMERA OP:** "Is it a distinct function?"

**TYLER:** "I think you've always had it. You found the tree naturally — you were filming the location you found meaningful and it turned out to be the right location. The archive is noticing that this has happened twice."

**CAMERA OP:** "Has it happened more than twice?"

He looks at her.

**TYLER:** "The production office, Day 1 of Season 2. The diner booth, Season 3 midpoint. The threshold position at Bacon's building — you chose that. Every position you've chosen has been load-bearing. The archive started noticing in Season 4. I've been noticing since Season 1."

She's very still.

**CAMERA OP:** "You knew."

**TYLER:** "I knew you found the right places. I didn't know what to call it until you found the nurse's position and the footage changed."

**Day 119, afternoon — Day 25 progress**

Tyler works through the afternoon. The Camera Op films from the nurse's position. Bacon brings tea at 3 PM without asking. Emily updates the network map.

At 5 PM Tyler stops. The light through the east windows has shifted to the golden angle it reaches on clear late-autumn days in Detroit — the same angle the 1952 photograph captured, the same angle the 1918 nurse would have seen at the end of her shift.

**TYLER:** "Day 25. The construction is past the midpoint. The layered events are becoming coherent — they're organizing around a center. The room is finding its own shape."

**EMILY:** "What does that mean for the timeline?"

**TYLER:** "Faster from here. The first half was clarifying what the room was holding. The second half is consolidating it. I think thirty-seven days, not forty."

Emily updates the map: *Detroit Receiving — revised estimate Day 130–132. Buffer: 44–46 days. Conversion: Day 176.*

**EMILY:** "More comfortable."

**TYLER:** "Yes."

He looks at the nurse's position. At the Camera Op. At the footage running silently on her monitor — the split screen, the two clips that look like different decades.

**TYLER:** "The archive opened the right file."
