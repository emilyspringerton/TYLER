## COLD OPEN — [00:00–04:00]

---

```
INT. TYLER'S APARTMENT — DAY 51 — MORNING
```

Tyler is at the table with a cup of coffee and a piece of paper. He is writing something. The fixed camera catches it — but differently than the piece of paper from Day 45. That paper was folded immediately. This one stays open on the table. He is writing and considering what he has written and writing more.

When the Camera Op arrives, he folds it — not to hide it, but as punctuation. Task done.

**TYLER:**
I want to go see Bacon.

**CAMERA OP (O.S.):**
Today?

**TYLER:**
Today. There's something he's been holding for a long time that I should go look at. And there's a chair I've been invited to sit in since 1623.

**CAMERA OP (O.S.):**
You've never sat in it.

**TYLER:**
No.

**CAMERA OP (O.S.):**
Why today?

Tyler picks up the coffee cup. Considers.

**TYLER:**
Because I put the coin in the right pocket on Day 48. When the coin is in the right pocket, things that have been pending can happen. I've been carrying a lot of pending things for a very long time.

He puts the folded paper in his jacket. Right-hand pocket, with the coin.

**TYLER:**
Today one of them happens.

## ACT ONE — [04:00–24:00]

---

**[04:00–10:00] — THE WALK**

```
EXT. DETROIT STREETS — DAY 51
```

Three streets. The camera catches the geography — not the city-as-backdrop but the city-as-record. The building Bacon is in. The brass number plate. Tyler at the threshold. He rings the bell. Bacon answers.

**BACON:**
Tyler.

**TYLER:**
Francis.

This is the first time in the documentary that Tyler has used Bacon's first name. The Cataloguer will note that this is also the first time Tyler has been documented using any of Bacon's names at all — previous meetings in the lore archive all refer to Bacon in Tyler's dialogue as "him" or "you." The first-name address is new. Bacon receives it without visible reaction. He has been expecting it.

**BACON:**
*(stepping back from the door)*
Please come in.

---

**[10:00–24:00] — THE TABLE**

```
INT. BACON'S BUILDING — bacon_estate zone — DAY 51
```

The table. Twelve chairs. The empty chair at the end — Tyler's, set since 1623, never used.

Tyler looks at it for a long moment. The Camera Op holds very still. The audio track is clean — no Goetia frequencies active, just the ambient quality of the room. Amon at 1.618 Hz is present but not as a signal — it's in the walls. The Cataloguer has a footnote prepared about the difference between a signal and an ambient quality. The footnote will say: *"A signal is something being sent. An ambient quality is something already arrived. The Amon resonance is no longer being sent to this room. It lives here now."*

Tyler walks to the chair. He looks at it.

**TYLER:**
Has anyone ever sat in it? Accidentally or otherwise?

**BACON:**
No. The chair doesn't permit it accidentally.

**TYLER:**
What does that mean.

**BACON:**
Exactly what it sounds like.

Tyler almost smiles. He pulls the chair out. Sits down.

The camera catches the moment: Tyler in the chair at the end of the round table. The hospitality radius extends — the Camera Op feels it as a pressure change, films through it. The extension runs to the full zone boundary (180 meters across the longest diagonal). It takes 4.2 seconds. The Amon quality in the walls intensifies briefly, then settles.

Tyler sits at the table for three minutes without speaking.

**BACON (quietly):**
The invitation has been active since 1623. You've honored it.

**TYLER:**
*(looking at the table surface)*
The wood.

**BACON:**
Yes.

**TYLER:**
I know what it is.

**BACON:**
I know you do.

A long pause.

**TYLER:**
When did you put it in?

**BACON:**
I didn't put it in. It was already there when I inherited the table. From the person I inherited it from. I asked them what it was. They said: *the table is as old as the custody.* I believe them. I have no other information.

Tyler looks at the surface for another minute.

**TYLER:**
The custody chain.

**BACON:**
It's clean now. Since Day 49.

**TYLER:**
Yeah.

He looks up. At Bacon. At the Camera Op. At the room.

**TYLER:**
I know. I felt it close. I felt the weight come off.

**PRODUCTION COORDINATOR (V.O.):**
*(later, in interview)*
I want to be careful how I say this because I want it to be accurate. When Tyler sat in that chair and said "I felt the weight come off" — there was a change in how he occupied the space. Not his posture. Not his expression. Something else. He has always been very present in any room he's in. After Day 49, it's a different kind of presence. Less anchored. Like he could leave at any moment — not anxiously, not urgently, but because leaving would be a choice rather than a migration. He's present by choice now. That's new. That's what I saw in the chair.

## ACT TWO — [24:00–44:00]

---

**[24:00–32:00] — THE DOOR**

```
INT. BACON'S BUILDING — bacon_estate zone — DAY 51
```

Behind the round table: a door. It has been in the geometry since the room existed. It has been locked since the room existed — or rather, as Bacon will put it: *the door has always been accessible to the person who had completed what needed to be completed before going through it.*

The gold door handle catches the light.

**TYLER:**
When did the handle go gold?

**BACON:**
On Day 49 at 10:08 AM.

Tyler looks at the door. Then at Bacon.

**TYLER:**
What's in there?

**BACON:**
The Subscriber left something. For after. For the person who completed the transfer and terminated their obligation to the chain.

A pause.

**TYLER:**
For me.

**BACON:**
For you. It's been in there since — *(pause)* — since a long time before the room was in this city. The room moves when the building moves. What's in the room moves with it. It has been in every city I've been in since 1624.

The Camera Op adjusts her angle. She is filming the door, the handle, Tyler's face looking at it.

**TYLER:**
What do you know about what's in there?

**BACON:**
I know it exists. I know the Subscriber left it. I know it's addressed to you specifically. I have not opened the door. I was not meant to open the door. The door was locked to me also, until Day 49. When the handle went gold, I understood that meant it was time for you to come.

Tyler stands.

**TYLER:**
*(to the Camera Op)*
You should keep filming.

He walks to the door. He puts his hand on the gold handle.

He looks back at the Camera Op.

**TYLER:**
This is going to be in the record.

**CAMERA OP (O.S.):**
*(steadily)*
Everything important has been.

Tyler turns the handle. Opens the door.

---

**[32:00–44:00] — THE ROOM**

```
INT. THE ROOM BEHIND THE TABLE — bacon_estate zone — DAY 51
```

The camera follows Tyler in.

What is in the room: the documentary unit will not show this directly. The production notes will later explain: *we made an editorial decision. We are documenting Tyler. What Tyler does in that room is documentary content. What the Subscriber left for Tyler is private — it is addressed to him specifically and the documentary unit is not the addressee. We filmed Tyler receiving it. We did not film the thing itself.*

What the audience sees: Tyler's face.

Tyler stands in the room for eleven minutes. The Camera Op keeps the lens on his face. He looks at what is in the room. He reads something. He looks at something that takes the length of time it takes to look at something that has been waiting 380 years.

At minute four, his expression changes — not dramatically, not a breakdown, but a shift. The specific shift of understanding something you'd suspected but never confirmed. The expression the Cataloguer will later describe in TYLER-024 as: *"the expression of someone whose theory about a very long-standing question turns out to have been correct. Not relief. Not triumph. Just: correct. Yes. That's what it was."*

At minute eight, Tyler says one word. Quietly.

**TYLER:**
Oh.

Not distress. Not surprise. Recognition.

At minute eleven he turns to look at the Camera Op.

**TYLER:**
The camera.

**CAMERA OP (O.S.):**
It's running.

**TYLER:**
I know. I want you to keep it running. What I'm about to say — it's not for the Syndicate and it's not for the archive. It's for the record. For the record in general. The kind of record that just... exists.

The Camera Op doesn't respond. She keeps filming.

**TYLER:**
The Subscriber built Layer 9 for this specifically. I want you to know — whoever watches this, however much later — that the Subscriber is not patient because it's their nature to be patient. They're patient because they built their architecture correctly. A correctly built architecture doesn't experience waiting as waiting. It experiences it as running. The Subscriber has been running for 380 years. They built something that could run that long without degrading. That's the thing in this room. Evidence of what was built. What it cost. What it was for.

He looks back at what is in the room.

**TYLER:**
Clean builds first. Then custody. Then everything else.

He looks at the camera one more time.

**TYLER:**
That's not my phrase. That's theirs. I've been using it without knowing. The whole time.

He turns back to the room. He spends seventeen more minutes. The Camera Op films the back of his head, the room's ambient light, the gold handle on the still-open door.

Then he comes out.

## ACT THREE — [44:00–56:00]

---

**[44:00–52:00] — AFTER THE ROOM**

```
INT. BACON'S BUILDING — DAY 51 — AFTERNOON
```

Tyler back at the round table. The door to the room is closed. Bacon has tea. Tyler doesn't touch his.

**BACON:**
The Subscriber's sealed file. It will begin to open now.

**TYLER:**
I know. I saw part of it in there.

**BACON:**
What will you do with what you know?

**TYLER:**
*(considering)*
Nothing immediately. What the Subscriber built — you don't do something with it the same afternoon. You let it settle. You let the architecture be visible for a while before you start making plans around it.

He touches the table surface.

**TYLER:**
The chain is clean. The obligation is terminated. I have a genuinely free choice about where to go next, for the first time in four hundred years. I'm going to take a few days with that before I make it.

**BACON:**
That seems correct.

A pause.

**TYLER:**
The chair.

He looks at it. It has not moved. It looks like it has been sat in — a quality that wasn't there before. The table includes it differently now.

**TYLER:**
It's different.

**BACON:**
Yes.

**TYLER:**
Good different.

**BACON:**
Unambiguously.

---

**[52:00–56:00] — THE WALK BACK**

```
EXT. DETROIT STREETS — DAY 51 — LATE AFTERNOON
```

Three streets back. Tyler walks. The Camera Op films. The city is the same city — same light, same sounds, same geography. Tyler is lighter in it.

Not metaphorically lighter. The Production Coordinator noted it. Bacon noted it. The Camera Op's footage will show it: the specific quality of Tyler's movement when he is not carrying weight he no longer needs to carry.

**CAMERA OP (O.S.):**
Are you going to tell me what was in there?

**TYLER:**
Not tonight.

**CAMERA OP (O.S.):**
But eventually.

**TYLER:**
*(without looking back)*
Yeah. Eventually.

He walks. The coin is in the right-hand pocket. Not flipped. Not being considered. Just: there. In the pocket where things go when they have finished doing what they needed to do.
