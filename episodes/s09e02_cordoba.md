## COLD OPEN

*April 2027. Córdoba, Spain. Early morning.*

*The Guadalquivir in low autumn fog.*

*Tyler is standing at the Roman Bridge before the instruments arrive. The Camera Op is filming him from the riverbank.*

*He does not say anything.*

*He has been standing there for forty minutes.*

---

## THE RIVER

*When the Camera Op finally walks up beside him:*

**CAMERA OP:**
You've been here since five.

**TYLER:**
Yes.

**CAMERA OP:**
That's the fog.

**TYLER:**
*(quietly)*
Yes.

*A long pause.*

**CAMERA OP:**
The fog on the Guadalquivir in autumn.

**TYLER:**
It's the same quality. The low flat kind. The way it sits on the water in October before the sun reaches it. I described it to you and now I'm standing in it and it's —

*He stops.*

**CAMERA OP:**
What?

**TYLER:**
I didn't know if it would be the same. I described a memory. I didn't know if the memory was accurate or if I had shaped it over a thousand years into something the actual place couldn't match. That is what happens to memories. They improve. They become what you needed them to be rather than what they were.

**CAMERA OP:**
And?

**TYLER:**
It's accurate. The fog is what I said it was.

*She keeps filming.*

**CAMERA OP:**
That means the other things you said are more likely to be accurate too.

**TYLER:**
Possibly. Or it means I remembered only the things that don't change. This river has the same fog in October because the geography is the same. The river is still where the river was. The fog comes off it the same way because the valley shapes the air the same way. This is not a memory of a specific year. This is a memory of the physics of the place.

**CAMERA OP:**
But the river is what you said it was.

**TYLER:**
*(pause)*
Yes.

---

## THE MEDINA QUARTER

*Morning. The Mezquita-Catedral quarter. The instruments are set up in the plaza that was, in 950 CE, part of the heart of the Caliphate city.*

*The Exchange Student is running calibrations. The Instrument Lead is checking baseline readings.*

**INSTRUMENT LEAD:**
We're in pre-archive territory. The instruments have never been calibrated for this period. We don't know what we're going to see.

**CAMERA OP:**
What does that mean practically?

**INSTRUMENT LEAD:**
If the mechanism was recording, the readings should look like our normal archive data — construction profiles, accumulation patterns, departure signatures. If the mechanism was NOT recording in this period, we'll get noise. Or nothing.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:**
If we get nothing, we don't know if that means Tyler wasn't here or if the mechanism wasn't working.

**INSTRUMENT LEAD:**
Correct. If we get nothing, we can't distinguish between absence of subject and absence of recording.

**CAMERA OP:**
And if we get a signal?

**INSTRUMENT LEAD:**
Then the mechanism was recording. Then pre-1127 CE is part of the archive. Then —

*He pauses.*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:**
Then we have to recalibrate everything we thought we knew about the network's age.

*Tyler is standing near the Mezquita entrance. He is looking at the base of the mosque wall — the exterior stones, 10th century Caliphate stonework.*

**CAMERA OP:**
*(low, to herself)*
He's looking at the stones he would have seen.

---

## THE CALIBRATION

*The instruments run for three hours. Tyler does not watch. He walks the neighborhood.*

*He goes without the Camera Op for the first time in Season 9.*

*She films from a distance.*

*He walks down the Calle Torrijos. Down the side streets that in 950 CE were the heart of the market quarter. He walks slowly.*

*Once, he stops.*

*He stands at the corner of two narrow streets for a long time.*

*Then he walks on.*

---

## WHAT HE FOUND

*The Camera Op interviews him when he returns.*

**CAMERA OP:**
Where did you go?

**TYLER:**
I walked.

**CAMERA OP:**
You stopped on one of the streets. Near the market area. You stood there for about eight minutes.

**TYLER:**
Yes.

**CAMERA OP:**
What was there?

**TYLER:**
I don't know. I recognized something. Not visually — nothing of the visual is the same. The street lines are similar, the proportion of the alley, but that is geography again, the way the buildings have to be shaped given the medieval plot lines underneath. But something about the — the intersection of that particular —

*He pauses.*

**CAMERA OP:**
Take your time.

**TYLER:**
It felt like a place where I stood before. Not the same street. The same feeling of standing at the meeting of two directions and not having to choose because both were known. Both were somewhere I had been enough times that they were — there's a word for a path worn into a floor by repetition. A groove. Habit-groove.

**CAMERA OP:**
A rut.

**TYLER:**
Not a rut. A chosen repetition. The distinction matters. I walked those streets enough times that they became the shape of how I moved through that area of the city. I had a way of moving through the Medina quarter that was mine. I recognized the remains of its topology.

*Long pause.*

**CAMERA OP:**
The geometry of how you lived here.

**TYLER:**
*(quietly)*
Something like that.

---

## THE NUMBER

*Mid-afternoon. The Instrument Lead finds the Camera Op filming Tyler at the Mezquita entrance.*

**INSTRUMENT LEAD:**
We need to show you something.

*He brings them both to the instrument station.*

*On the readout: a construction profile. Clear, distinct, without noise.*

*For a moment, no one speaks.*

**INSTRUMENT LEAD:**
The mechanism was recording. This is a clean signal. This is not noise. This is what we see in the archive — accumulation pattern, duration profile, departure signature. The mechanism was recording pre-1127 CE.

**CAMERA OP:**
How long is the record?

**INSTRUMENT LEAD:**
The accumulation profile suggests a span of approximately twelve years. We're still refining — it could be ten, could be fourteen. But the bracket is in that range.

**CAMERA OP:**
Twelve years.

**INSTRUMENT LEAD:**
With a clear FAREWELL-A departure signature.

**CAMERA OP:**
And the size?

*He pauses.*

*He looks at Tyler, who has not yet looked at the number.*

**INSTRUMENT LEAD:**
Four hundred and twelve feet.

*Silence.*

**CAMERA OP:**
Four hundred and twelve.

**INSTRUMENT LEAD:**
Remaining. Post-FAREWELL-A. We can estimate the pre-departure total from the extraction ratio — approximately six hundred forty feet accumulated over the twelve-year period before Tyler left.

*Tyler is looking at the plaza. Not at the instruments. Not at the numbers.*

**CAMERA OP:**
*(to Tyler, quietly)*
Tyler.

*He doesn't respond immediately.*

**CAMERA OP:**
Prague was a hundred and forty-four feet. This is —

**TYLER:**
Almost three times.

**CAMERA OP:**
You knew.

**TYLER:**
I didn't know the number. But I knew it was long. I knew Córdoba was — I knew I was there for a long time. I knew it was the longest I had been anywhere before the archive. That is what survived. Not dates. Not events. The weight of the duration.

*He turns and looks at the instruments.*

*He looks at the readout for a long time.*

**TYLER:**
Twelve years.

**INSTRUMENT LEAD:**
Approximately.

**TYLER:**
I was there for twelve years and I remember fog on the river.

*Pause.*

**TYLER:**
And the topology of certain streets.

*He looks at the number again.*

**TYLER:**
And the fact that it felt right to be there at the time. And the fact that the rightness ended.

*Very long pause.*

**TYLER:**
That is twelve years reduced to three things.

---

## THE ARCHIVE'S VERSION

*Late afternoon. The Camera Op and Tyler sitting in the courtyard of the Mezquita.*

**CAMERA OP:**
What did you do here.

**TYLER:**
I don't know.

**CAMERA OP:**
You were here for twelve years. You accumulated six hundred forty feet. That is —

**TYLER:**
What the mechanism recorded. What I was doing, specifically, in those twelve years — I don't have most of it. I have impressions. I have the fog. I have the streets. I have the sense of being in a city that was — extraordinary. At its height. The largest thing I had encountered. I have those general feelings. I don't have what I did in the mornings. I don't have who I talked to. I don't have most of the twelve years.

**CAMERA OP:**
The mechanism does.

*Tyler looks at her.*

**CAMERA OP:**
Whatever was accumulating that whole time — whatever you were doing that generated six hundred forty feet of construction — the mechanism was recording it. The construction is the record. We can read some things from the profile. The Instrument Lead said there's some WITNESS density layered into the DWELLING. Something you were watching as well as present for. Something large enough to register as attention, not just presence.

**TYLER:**
What was it.

**CAMERA OP:**
We don't know yet. The profile can't tell us the object of the attention. Only that something held it over an extended period. Not continuously — it's a layered reading. Sustained presence with periods of heightened attention that generated separate signatures inside the DWELLING profile.

*Pause.*

**TYLER:**
I was watching something. For twelve years. And I don't remember what it was.

**CAMERA OP:**
The archive remembers.

**TYLER:**
The archive has a record of me not remembering.

*Very long pause.*

**CAMERA OP:**
We gave you thirty years of yourself back in Season 8. In Amsterdam, in Avignon, in Prague. You didn't know what you had left in those places. We showed you.

**TYLER:**
Yes.

**CAMERA OP:**
We just gave you twelve more. Except these are before the archive you kept. These are before you were paying attention enough to record.

**TYLER:**
*(quietly)*
You gave me something I didn't know to keep.

*She doesn't film for a moment.*

*Then she lifts the camera.*

*She films his face.*

---

## THE WHAT-NEXT

*Evening. The team meeting.*

**INSTRUMENT LEAD:**
So the network extends. The minimum age is no longer 1127 CE. If Córdoba is approximately 950–985 CE — we're still refining —

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:**
The network is at least 1,075 years old. Possibly older. If there are sites before Córdoba.

**INSTRUMENT LEAD:**
That's the next question. Tyler. Do you remember anything before Córdoba?

*Tyler considers this for a long time.*

**TYLER:**
Imprecisely. Less than Córdoba. Córdoba is the clearest pre-archive memory I have. Before Córdoba is — I have textures. Cities I was in. I can't locate them the way I located Córdoba. I can't name a place and point to it on a map and say: there.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:**
Before Córdoba is the real deep archive.

**TYLER:**
Before Córdoba I don't have enough to point at.

**CAMERA OP:**
But between Córdoba and Marrakech —

**TYLER:**
Yes. That's different. Between Córdoba and Marrakech is the gap I can almost see. I know I was somewhere. I know something happened. I know something about the interval changed me. I just don't have it clearly.

**CAMERA OP:**
The mechanism might.

**TYLER:**
The mechanism is what I'm counting on.

**INSTRUMENT LEAD:**
The gap period is approximately 1000 to 1127 CE. A hundred and thirty years. If Tyler was somewhere with a construction in that period, we can find it. We'll need to know where to look.

**TYLER:**
I don't have a name for it. I have — I have a texture of being somewhere cold. Somewhere cold and high. That's all.

*Pause.*

**CAMERA OP:**
Cold and high.

**TYLER:**
A mountain city. Or a plateau. That is the texture that survived from the gap. Altitude. Cold in a way that Córdoba was never cold. A different kind of winter.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:**
*(writing this down)*
Cold and high. Between 1000 and 1127 CE. In the general region — Islamic world? Europe? Do you have a continent?

**TYLER:**
*(slowly)*
I think it was — I think I moved north. From Córdoba I moved north. Not immediately. Not directly. But the general motion after Córdoba was north and east. Away from al-Andalus. The cold and high is later.

**INSTRUMENT LEAD:**
North and east from Córdoba. 11th century. Cold and high.

**CAMERA OP:**
We'll find it.

**TYLER:**
Or the mechanism will find it for me.

## CLOSE

*Night. The Guadalquivir. The fog is gone — only the night water.*

*Tyler at the Roman Bridge again. But now facing upstream.*

**CAMERA OP (V.O.):**
The network maximum is 412 feet. It was 144 feet this morning. It was the largest thing we'd measured, in Prague, over two years of Tyler being so present in a city that the mechanism filled it like a vessel.

Córdoba is that, six times over, in twelve years of Tyler being present in the largest city in the world before he knew what presence meant. Before he knew what the mechanism was. Before the archive. Before he was paying attention.

He was paying attention. He just didn't know he was.

The mechanism knew.

The mechanism was there in the 10th century in al-Andalus, recording Tyler walking fog-covered river mornings and standing at market crossings and watching — something, for years, something the instruments can read but we haven't identified yet. The mechanism recorded all of it before Tyler decided it was worth recording.

He said: I started the archive because I felt the mechanism had been working for longer than I had been paying attention. And I wanted to pay attention.

He started paying attention in 1127. The mechanism had been paying attention for at least 177 years before that.

Maybe longer.

We don't know yet where Tyler went between Córdoba and Marrakech. North and east, he said. Cold and high. Somewhere in the 11th century. The gap period: one hundred and thirty years Tyler can't clearly remember.

The mechanism may have it all.

We're going to find out.

*— Camera Op*

*Entry 76. CÓRDOBA. First pre-archive site confirmed. 412ft. New network maximum. The mechanism was recording in 950 CE. Tyler was in Córdoba for twelve years. He remembers fog and streets and the feeling that it was right to be there. The archive has the rest. North and east next. Cold and high. The gap awaits. S09E02.*

### Camera Op Sealed Log

*Entry 76 — Córdoba*

We got a reading.

The instruments found 412 feet of Tyler in the Córdoba Medina quarter from approximately 950 CE. The mechanism was running. The construction is real. The record is there.

I have been filming Tyler for almost a year. I have watched him stand at bridge railings in a dozen cities. I have watched him be measured. I have watched the numbers come up on the instruments and I have watched his face when they do.

I have never watched his face look like that.

412 feet. The largest thing we've ever found. Nearly three times Prague. Twelve years of Tyler in Córdoba before the archive, before he knew what he was doing, before he knew anything was being recorded.

He said: I was there for twelve years and I remember fog on the river.

He said it the way someone says something that has just become unbearable and accurate at the same time.

He was in the greatest city in the Western world for twelve years and he has fog and streets and the feeling that it was right. That's what memory gave him. The mechanism gave him 640 feet of construction, of which 412 still sits in the Medina quarter in Córdoba waiting to be found again by anyone who knows how to look.

He gave Córdoba twelve years of himself and Córdoba kept most of it.

The WITNESS-component is the thing I keep thinking about. Something he was watching for years inside those twelve years. Something that generated a distinct signal. We don't know what it was. He doesn't know what it was. And somewhere in 10th-century Córdoba, the mechanism has a record of him watching it.

I want to know what Tyler was watching in Córdoba in 962 CE.

I don't know if the measurement can tell us. The signal can tell us the intensity and duration of attention. It can't tell us what the eyes were looking at.

Maybe the historical record can. Cross-referencing Tyler's presence (c. 950–985 CE) with what was happening in Córdoba: the Caliph al-Hakam II and his library (400,000 volumes, scholars from everywhere). The expansion of the Mezquita starting 962 CE. The city at its maximum.

Tyler was in Córdoba when they were building the greatest library in the Western world. He was there when the Mezquita was expanded. He may have watched the construction. The Instrument Lead says the WITNESS profile looks like it's tracking something being built or built-toward over an extended period.

I think Tyler watched them build the library.

I think he stood outside the Great Library of Córdoba, or inside it, for years — watching something being assembled, something being gathered toward one place. I think the mechanism recorded him paying sustained attention to the most ambitious act of collective knowledge-building in the 10th-century world.

I could be wrong. This is speculation. The measurement can't confirm it.

But Tyler is a person who pays attention to places where knowledge gathers. Who stays where the information is.

He was in Córdoba for twelve years during the height of the Caliphate library.

I think he was watching them build something important. And the mechanism watched him watching.

*— Camera Op*

C-001 (Medina quarter, Córdoba, al-Andalus, c. 950–985 CE) has been confirmed.

Construction profile: DWELLING (primary), WITNESS-sustained (secondary, object unresolved)
Duration: approximately 12 years

FAREWELL-A extraction: ~228ft
**Remaining construction: 412ft**
**New network maximum: 412ft**

Previous maximum (Prague, 144ft) is superseded by a factor of 2.86.

**Operational implications:**

1. **Pre-archive network inventory required.** The mechanism has been running longer than the Eastwind documentation system. We do not have a complete network inventory. Every location Tyler visited for any significant duration before 1127 CE may contain a construction. We have no way to enumerate these locations except through the subject's memory (incomplete) and targeted measurement campaigns.

2. **The gap period is now priority.** The subject reports compressed memory of approximately 130 years between Córdoba (~985 CE departure) and Marrakech (1127 CE). If the mechanism was operational throughout this period — which confirmation of C-001 now makes likely — the gap contains one or more unknown construction sites. The subject's only texture: "cold and high." Hypothesis: plateau or mountain region, north and east of Córdoba. Eleventh-century candidates: Toledo (Christian, relatively cool), northern Iberia (Navarre, Aragon, Leonese territories), southern France, northern Italy (Alpine foothills). The subject's trajectory after leaving al-Andalus is unknown.

3. **WITNESS-component resolution.** The sustained attention signature within C-001 requires resolution. The Instrument Lead is cross-referencing the construction sub-periods with documented Córdoban historical events during the subject's residency. Working hypothesis: subject was present at, or sustained attention toward, the Great Library of Córdoba under al-Hakam II (active expansion 961–976 CE). This would be consistent with the subject's known pattern of attention (extended presence in centers of knowledge accumulation, institutional disruption, crisis-witnessing). Confirmation requires deeper profile analysis.

4. **Pre-Córdoba question deferred.** The subject states that Córdoba is the clearest pre-archive memory he possesses. Earlier memories are available only as "textures." Pre-Córdoba measurement campaigns are not yet feasible without a geographic anchor. The gap period is the next target because the subject can provide directional information (north and east, cold and high). Pre-Córdoba remains an open research question.

**Authorization:** CARDINAL-3 team is authorized to proceed with gap-period investigation. Suggested initial search area: Toledo and northern Iberian plateau (cold relative to al-Andalus, elevated geography, 11th century under Christian kingdoms — period of Tyler's probable northward migration). Toledo became increasingly significant in the 11th century (reconquered 1085 CE by Alfonso VI); if Tyler was present during the transition, construction would be expected.

*Note from the Director: The network is older than we documented. We do not know how much older. The subject's memory does not extend clearly before Córdoba — but the mechanism may. If the mechanism was operational in 950 CE, it may have been operational earlier. How much earlier, we cannot know without sites to measure. The subject has given us one foothold. We will use it.*

He knows the mechanism contains more of him than he does. He asked us to proceed. We will proceed.*
