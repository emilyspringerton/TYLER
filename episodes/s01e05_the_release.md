## ACT ONE: THE DINER — [04:00–18:00]

**[04:00–06:00] — WHERE TYLER IS**

```
INT. DETROIT DINER — MORNING
```

Tyler is in a diner. This is not surprising. Tyler is always somewhere reasonable.

He has a coffee. He has a newspaper that is two days old. He is reading the newspaper the way someone reads a newspaper when they already know what's in it: not for information, but for the ritual of the thing, the specific ordinariness of reading a physical newspaper in a diner in the morning.

A server refills his coffee without being asked. He says thank you without looking up. She says you're welcome without looking at him. This is a diner relationship. It has its own physics.

The Camera Op is across the diner, in a corner booth, with a phone. They are watching something on the phone. The camera catches the phone's screen reflected in the window glass: their own clip, playing for what appears to be the fourteenth time this morning.

Tyler is aware of the Camera Op. The Camera Op is aware of Tyler. They have not acknowledged each other. This is a performance of ordinary behavior by two people who are very good at it by now.

**[06:00–10:00] — THE CONVERSATION THAT WASN'T SCHEDULED**

Tyler finishes the newspaper. He folds it. He picks up his coffee. He looks across the diner.

He gets up, crosses to the Camera Op's booth, and sits down across from them.

The Camera Op puts their phone face-down on the table.

**TYLER**
*(looking at where the phone was)*
How many.

**CAMERA OP**
Forty-seven thousand this morning.

Tyler nods. He holds his coffee cup. He looks at the window — at the street, at the light.

**TYLER**
That's — yeah.

*(pause)*

People want to see the rest of it.

**CAMERA OP**
*(carefully)*
There is a rest of it.

**TYLER**
I know.

He is quiet for a moment. The Camera Op does not fill the silence. They have learned not to fill his silences. This took approximately four days and eleven hours of footage.

**TYLER (CONT.)**
*(not a question, spoken to the window)*
You're going to publish the rest.

**CAMERA OP**
*(honest)*
I don't know yet.

Tyler looks at them. Not the camera — them. The Camera Op is not filming this. The handheld is on the table. The Camera Op made a choice about that before Tyler sat down.

**TYLER**
*(warmly, meaning it)*
That's the right answer.

*(pause)*

"I don't know yet" is the right answer.

He finishes the coffee. He sets the cup down.

**TYLER (CONT.)**
There's a version where publishing the rest of it helps me. There's a version where it helps them. There's a version where it helps you.

*(pause)*

I don't know which version you're in yet. Neither do you. That's why "I don't know yet" is right.

**CAMERA OP**
*(quietly)*
What version are you in?

Tyler smiles — the smile that isn't charm. The one that reaches.

**TYLER**
*(standing)*
I'm in the version where you ask good questions at the right time.

*(pause)*

I've been in that version for eighteen days.

He picks up the tab. Both tabs — his coffee and the Camera Op's. He puts money on the table.

**CAMERA OP**
You don't have to—

**TYLER**
*(already walking)*
I know. That's the point.
