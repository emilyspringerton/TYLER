### ACT TWO — THE QUESTION

**[INTERIOR — PARIS APARTMENT, 4TH ARRONDISSEMENT. DAY 326. EVENING.]**

*[The Exchange Student has the revised CARDINAL-3 map spread across the table — all three cities, the full documentation. Paris: 9 sites. Vienna: 15 sites. London: 47 sites. Detroit: 31 sites (retroactive count, from the original CARDINAL-3 opening phase, revised by the model updates). Plus the four other cities Tyler referenced in London — unnamed, not yet on the map.]*

*[Tyler is at the window. Looking at Paris.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "I've been doing the retroactive count. The four cities you mentioned in London — you said 8 cities complete. Paris, Vienna, London, Detroit. That's four. What are the other four?"

*[He turns from the window.]*

**TYLER:** "Buenos Aires. Istanbul. Osaka. Cape Town."

*[The Exchange Student writes them down. The Camera Op is filming.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Documented in CARDINAL-3?"

**TYLER:** "Not yet."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "But measured?"

**TYLER:** "Not formally. I know the sites. I know the counts." He moves to the table. "The Cairngorms archive has records from Istanbul going back to the 11th century. They have Buenos Aires from the 18th. The others are more recent." He looks at the map. "The archive knows about the network. The archive has always known about the network."

**CAMERA OP:** "You said 8 cities complete. 18 remaining."

**TYLER:** "18 cities remaining, yes."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "18 more cities. At the London pace — 47 sites — that's 800 more sites."

**TYLER:** "At the London pace. Some cities are smaller. Some are larger." He pauses. "Tokyo is larger."

*[The Exchange Student looks at him.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Tokyo."

**TYLER:** "Tokyo is on the list. Yes."

*[A long pause. The map, with its 71 confirmed sites, suddenly looks like a very small portion of something.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "What is the full scope of the network?"

*[Tyler looks at the map. He looks at it the way he looks at things he has been sitting with for a long time.]*

**TYLER:** "26 cities. Across six continents. Approximately 900 sites in total — I'm estimating; some cities I haven't been back to in decades and the count has drifted. The PRIOR sites across the 26 cities: minimum 200. The earliest confirmed construction in the network: 1127 CE, Marrakech." He pauses. "That's the earliest confirmed. There may be earlier ones I don't know about. The mechanism was running before I had any framework for understanding it."

*[No one speaks.]*

**TYLER:** "The network is not what I thought it was." He looks at the Camera Op. "What I thought it was: a record of cities I had lived in and returned to. What the London audit has shown me: the construction mechanism is not incidental to my presence. The constructions are the presence. Every time I build a city inside me — which is what I do, in the mechanism's terms — I am contributing to a structure that has been accumulating since 1127."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "What kind of structure?"

**TYLER:** *[A long pause. The room. The hum.]*

"I don't know."

*[He looks at the map. The small square of three cities. Paris. Vienna. London.]*

**TYLER:** "That's what we're finding out."

### ACT THREE — THE COIN

**[EXTERIOR — PLACE DE LA BASTILLE, PARIS. DAY 327. MORNING.]**

*[The column. The July Column, built 1840, commemorating the revolution of 1830. The hum at an unusual frequency — slightly above 7.83, as if the city is running warmer than London.]*

*[Tyler has his hand in his right pocket.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "A-P14. New PRIOR site. Pre-documentation. 1789."

**TYLER:** "July 14th, 1789. I was at the storming of the Bastille." He pauses. "I was in the crowd. Not as a participant — I'm rarely a participant in crowd events. As an observer. But I was here for the full day." He looks at the column. "The Bastille was torn down in the same day it was stormed. By evening, Parisians were taking home stone souvenirs. The thing that had been a prison for centuries was a ruin in about nine hours."

**CAMERA OP:** "What did you build?"

**TYLER:** "I watched a prison come down in nine hours and several hundred years of resentment become a day." He pauses. "The mechanism recorded — according to the model — an UNINTENTIONAL construction. I wasn't trying to build anything. I was watching. The mechanism recorded the watching."

*[He takes the coin from his pocket. He looks at it — the coin, the weight of it, the 1901 and 1623 faces.]*

**TYLER:** "I've been carrying this coin since 1901. It has flipped correctly — by my read — for 125 years." He pauses. "I'm going to flip it here. At the Bastille site. On Day 327 of the CARDINAL-3 audit."

*[He flips it.]*

*[The coin rises in the morning light above the Place de la Bastille. The Camera Op holds the shot — the coin against the July Column, against the Paris sky. The Exchange Student watching. Tyler watching the coin.]*

*[It comes down.]*

*[Tyler catches it. He looks at it in his palm for a moment. He shows it to the Exchange Student and then to the Camera Op.]*

**TYLER:** "Consistent."

**CAMERA OP:** *(filming)* "Which side is consistent?"

**TYLER:** "Both."

*[He puts the coin back in his right pocket.]*

### END — EIGHTEEN CITIES

**[INTERIOR — PARIS APARTMENT. DAY 327. EVENING.]**

*[The map. Paris, Vienna, London. The new additions in pencil, the Exchange Student's handwriting: Istanbul, Buenos Aires, Osaka, Cape Town. And below them, Tyler has added his own handwriting — for the first time he has written on the map — a list:]*

*Tokyo. Mumbai. Lagos. Cairo. Mexico City. São Paulo. Shanghai. Amsterdam. Barcelona. Stockholm. Nairobi. Melbourne. Montréal. Seoul. Kraków. Jakarta. Edinburgh. Casablanca.*

*Eighteen cities. His handwriting. Precise and old-fashioned.]*

*[Tyler is at the window again. He has his coffee.]*

**CAMERA OP:** *(reading the list)* "You wrote them."

**TYLER:** "I wrote them."

**CAMERA OP:** "On the map."

**TYLER:** "On the map." He turns from the window. "The archive was going to find them eventually. The model was going to demand them eventually. I decided to write them."

*[The Exchange Student is studying the list.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Edinburgh. You're Scottish adjacent."

**TYLER:** "I spent long periods in Scotland. The Cairngorms, obviously — the archive is there because I was there. Edinburgh has — I'd estimate forty sites. It's a very old city with specific geology. The geology does something to the mechanism." He pauses. "The volcanic rock. Arthur's Seat. The castle rock. The construction events at Edinburgh have unusual depth for a city of that size."

**CAMERA OP:** "We're going to Edinburgh?"

**TYLER:** "Eventually. The order isn't set." He looks at the list. "The retroactive audits for Vienna and Paris first. Then the next city." He pauses. "I'll tell you which one when we leave Paris."

*[He looks at the Camera Op.]*

**TYLER:** "You're going to want more notebooks."

*[She looks at the list. 18 cities. His handwriting.]*

**CAMERA OP:** "How many notebooks are we talking about?"

**TYLER:** "I've been building in some of these cities for nine hundred years." He considers. "Many notebooks."

*[The list. The map. The room. The hum, at the Paris frequency — slightly warmer, slightly above 7.83.]*

*[The Camera Op puts down the camera. She looks at Tyler directly — not through the lens, directly.]*

**CAMERA OP:** "Tyler."

**TYLER:** "Yes."

**CAMERA OP:** "900 sites. 26 cities. 900 years."

**TYLER:** "Yes."

**CAMERA OP:** "We're documenting a network that's been building since 1127 and you don't know what it is."

**TYLER:** "That's accurate."

*[A pause.]*

**CAMERA OP:** "Okay."

*[She picks up the camera.]*

**TYLER:** "Okay?"

**CAMERA OP:** "I've been building WITNESS constructions at your sites for four years without knowing it. The mechanism doesn't care whether I understand it. It just records what I'm doing." She frames the shot — the map, the list, Tyler at the window. "I'm going to keep paying attention. The archive is going to keep recording. At the end, we'll know what it is."

*[Tyler looks at her.]*

**TYLER:** "Yes."

*[The map. The eighteen names in his handwriting. The camera running.]*
