## COLD OPEN

*June 2027. Genoa, Italy. The old port.*

*Tyler at the harbor wall before dawn. The Camera Op finds him the same way she found him in Toledo.*

*He has been here before the team. Two days.*

*But this time he turns when she arrives. He has been waiting.*

**TYLER:**
This is it.

**CAMERA OP:**
You're certain.

**TYLER:**
Yes. The way the harbor sits in the hills. The way the sound carries off the water at this hour. I have been standing here for two days and I am certain.

**CAMERA OP:**
What do you remember?

**TYLER:**
Ships. More ships than I expected. More ships than made sense for trade. And the people moving toward them. I remember standing exactly where I'm standing now and watching the people move toward the ships.

*A pause.*

**TYLER:**
I remember thinking: they are going somewhere none of them have been. And I couldn't tell if what I was watching was brave or terrible.

**CAMERA OP:**
*(quietly)*
The First Crusade departed 1096.

**TYLER:**
Yes. I know that now. At the time I didn't have that name. I had: many ships, many people, movement with a quality I had never seen before. Movement that had been decided somewhere else and was being carried out here.

**CAMERA OP:**
You were here before 1096.

**TYLER:**
I was here before the ships arrived. I watched the harbor prepare. I watched it become — what it became. And then I watched the ships leave.

**CAMERA OP:**
And after.

**TYLER:**
And after. For a long time after.

---

## THE HARBOR IN 1096

*The Camera Op interviews Tyler on the harbor wall. The instruments are being set up.*

**CAMERA OP:**
Tell me what you remember from 1096.

*Long pause.*

**TYLER:**
The scale of it. I had been in Córdoba at its height — half a million people, the greatest city in the West. I had been in Toledo when the armies came in. But what I saw here was different. In Toledo the movement was into the city. Here the movement was out of it. Everything was directed outward. Every face was aimed at the water.

**CAMERA OP:**
And the ships.

**TYLER:**
The harbor was full. Not the way a trading harbor is full — the way a harbor is full when it has been given a single purpose. Every ship pointed the same direction. There was no commerce in it. Only direction.

**CAMERA OP:**
Did you understand where they were going?

**TYLER:**
Not at first. Jerusalem — the name came to me through the crowds. I understood the destination before I understood why the destination mattered to them in the way it did. I understood geography. I didn't understand — I didn't fully understand yet what a sacred city meant to the people for whom it was sacred. I was watching a phenomenon I couldn't fully interpret.

**CAMERA OP:**
You were watching.

**TYLER:**
Yes. That is what I did.

*Pause.*

**CAMERA OP:**
After Toledo you said you thought you were done watching things you couldn't understand.

**TYLER:**
Yes. I was wrong.

**CAMERA OP:**
What made you stay?

*Very long pause.*

**TYLER:**
The quality of the movement. I have told you that in Toledo I watched people being moved rather than choosing movement. I said the same of Genoa before I understood what the difference was. But it isn't the same. In Toledo the movement was administered. It came from outside the people moving. What I watched here was —

*He stops.*

**CAMERA OP:**
Different.

**TYLER:**
The people moving toward the ships had chosen to move. Many of them had chosen badly — out of fear, or pressure, or misunderstanding. But the choosing was in them. Not in the administration. The choice was interior even when the interior was poorly served.

*Pause.*

**TYLER:**
I couldn't look away from that.

**CAMERA OP:**
From the choosing.

**TYLER:**
From the particular difficulty of choosing something enormous when you are not certain you understand what you have chosen.

*Very long pause.*

**CAMERA OP:**
You recognized something.

**TYLER:**
*(quietly)*
Yes.

## THE MEASUREMENT

*Midmorning. The Mandraccio — the old enclosed harbor of medieval Genoa, where the greatest ship traffic converged.*

*The instruments run for four hours.*

*Tyler does not walk away this time. He sits on a stone near the instruments and watches the modern harbor. The Camera Op films him from a distance.*

*He is very still.*

*The Instrument Lead brings the results in the afternoon.*

---

## WHAT THE INSTRUMENTS FIND

**INSTRUMENT LEAD:**
We have a construction. Clean. Approximately twenty-two years.

**CAMERA OP:**
1090 to 1112.

**INSTRUMENT LEAD:**
That bracket. Possibly a year either direction. The construction type is primarily WITNESS-LONG — the density profile is consistent with sustained attention across the full period, not just around the 1096 events. Tyler was watching before, during, and significantly after the Crusade departure.

**CAMERA OP:**
After.

**INSTRUMENT LEAD:**
The post-1096 WITNESS profile is as dense as the period of the departure itself. He stayed and watched the city afterward. The harbor returning to normal — or trying to. Whatever that looked like.

*Pause.*

**CAMERA OP:**
And the departure signature?

*The Instrument Lead looks at the readout for a long moment.*

**INSTRUMENT LEAD:**
This is what I wanted to show you.

*He brings the readout to the Camera Op.*

**INSTRUMENT LEAD:**
FAREWELL-A.

*Silence.*

**CAMERA OP:**
FAREWELL-A.

**INSTRUMENT LEAD:**
Clean profile. Chosen. Present at the moment of departure. No diminishment signal. The subject decided to leave and left.

**CAMERA OP:**
Like Córdoba.

**INSTRUMENT LEAD:**
Like Córdoba. The first FAREWELL-A we've found in the gap period. The departure from Toledo was FAREWELL-B. We expected continued deterioration, or at best a FAREWELL-B with some recovery. This is full restoration.

**CAMERA OP:**
What's the construction size?

**INSTRUMENT LEAD:**
Ninety-seven feet.

*She sits down.*

## WHAT CAME BACK

*Tyler is waiting for her when she comes to find him.*

**TYLER:**
You have the result.

**CAMERA OP:**
Yes.

**TYLER:**
Tell me.

**CAMERA OP:**
Ninety-seven feet. Twenty-two years. WITNESS-LONG primary. FAREWELL-A departure.

*Long pause.*

**TYLER:**
FAREWELL-A.

**CAMERA OP:**
The first one since Córdoba.

*Tyler is quiet for a long time. He looks at the harbor.*

**TYLER:**
I left Genoa cleanly.

**CAMERA OP:**
Yes.

**TYLER:**
I remember leaving. I had not remembered it until you told me the departure type, but I remember now the morning I decided to go. I remember deciding it was time. I remember the feeling of the decision being mine.

**CAMERA OP:**
What changed. Between Toledo and here.

**TYLER:**
I've been thinking about that for two days.

*He sits down beside her.*

**TYLER:**
In Toledo I watched the city change and I stayed because the information was still arriving and I lost the capacity to leave cleanly because I couldn't integrate what I was watching. I have told you about that. The FAREWELL-B from Toledo was — I left because I ran out of something. Not because I decided.

*Pause.*

**TYLER:**
Here I watched something different. I watched people who were also watching something they couldn't fully understand. And choosing anyway. Imperfectly, badly in many cases — but from inside themselves. And I think that —

*He stops.*

**CAMERA OP:**
That what?

**TYLER:**
I think watching people choose under conditions of incomplete understanding is different from watching people be administered. In Toledo I watched a city change because forces larger than any individual in it decided it would change. The people in it were moved. In Genoa — even the Crusade, even with all of its administrative structure and papal decision and political pressure — the people walking toward the ships had something interior that was participating in the decision.

**CAMERA OP:**
And watching that.

**TYLER:**
Watching that gave me back something I had lost in Toledo. The recognition that choosing is possible. Under difficulty. Under incomplete understanding. Choosing remains possible.

*Long silence.*

**CAMERA OP:**
You left Genoa choosing to go to Marrakech.

**TYLER:**
Yes.

**CAMERA OP:**
Why Marrakech.

*Very long pause.*

**TYLER:**
Because I wanted to write it down.

*The Camera Op is very still.*

**TYLER:**
I had been watching for —  I didn't know how many years, then. A long time. Córdoba. Toledo. Here. Things that mattered enormously and that I was watching and losing. The fog on the river. The texture of the streets. Not the twelve years — the twelve years became fog on the river. I wanted to do something different with what came next. I wanted to keep it.

**CAMERA OP:**
So you went to Marrakech.

**TYLER:**
I went somewhere I expected to find something worth keeping. And I began writing it down.

**CAMERA OP:**
Why Marrakech specifically.

**TYLER:**
*(quietly)*
I don't know yet. There is something before Marrakech that I haven't told you. Something between leaving Genoa and arriving at the start of the archive. Approximately fifteen years. I have something from those fifteen years — not the texture I had from the gap. Something more recent. Something I remember more clearly than Córdoba or Toledo or even here, but that I have not yet decided whether to say.

*The Camera Op looks at him.*

**CAMERA OP:**
You're choosing when to disclose.

**TYLER:**
Yes. I am choosing. You told me that was what the season was for.

## THE FIFTEEN YEARS

*Night. The Camera Op and the Exchange Student.*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:**
He knows.

**CAMERA OP:**
Yes.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:**
He knows what's in the fifteen years between Genoa and Marrakech. And he's deciding whether to say.

**CAMERA OP:**
He said "something I remember more clearly." Not texture. Not fog. Something specific.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:**
That means it's not an accumulation. It's an event. Something happened in those fifteen years. Something distinct enough to remember as a moment rather than a duration.

*Pause.*

**CAMERA OP:**
He's been here for the whole gap reconstruction. He's given us Córdoba. Toledo. Genoa. All three were things he could only partially access. Textures. Impressions. The mechanism confirmed or expanded them.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:**
But the fifteen years he actually remembers.

**CAMERA OP:**
Which means we don't need the instruments to confirm it. He already has it. He's just deciding when to give it to us.

*She looks at the harbor.*

**CAMERA OP (V.O.):**
This is what Season 9 has been building toward. Not the measurements. Not the 412 feet in Córdoba or the 191 in Toledo or the 97 here. Not the FAREWELL-A restored. Something Tyler actually remembers. Something specific. From the last fifteen years before he started writing everything down.

He is choosing to disclose it or not.

The season isn't over.

*Tyler at the harbor. Final evening in Genoa. The modern ships lit in the dark water.*

**CAMERA OP (V.O.):**
Ninety-seven feet of Tyler in a harbor watching people choose under impossible conditions. Twenty-two years. And then: FAREWELL-A. He left choosing. He left with the capacity for choosing restored.

He went to Marrakech and he started writing.

Between Genoa and Marrakech: fifteen years. He remembers them. He is deciding whether to tell us.

This is what choosing looks like from the outside. We wait for it.

*— Camera Op*

### Camera Op Sealed Log

*Entry 78 — Genoa / FAREWELL-A*

Ninety-seven feet.

That's not the number that matters today.

The number that matters is zero. The number on the FAREWELL-B diminishment index. It was zero. The departure from Genoa was clean. FAREWELL-A. Like Córdoba. Like the version of Tyler that existed before the gap.

I have been watching him lose capacity across this season. Córdoba: full presence, clean departure. Toledo: thirty-three years of watching a civilization change, FAREWELL-B departure, diminished, already gone before he left. And then here — twenty-two years of watching the First Crusade muster and depart from this harbor, and then he left cleanly.

What did Genoa give back to Tyler that Toledo took?

He told me: watching people choose under impossible conditions. Watching the interior act of volition even when the exterior conditions are crushing. He said he recognized something.

I think he recognized himself.

Córdoba Tyler was conscious and choosing. He DWELLING-chose for twelve years and FAREWELL-A'd out with clarity. Toledo Tyler watched external forces move people and lost the thread of his own choosing. Genoa Tyler watched people — even people being moved by historical forces they barely understood — still choosing from inside themselves. And found the thread again.

The FAREWELL-A from Genoa is Tyler finding the thread.

And then: fifteen years I don't know about. He knows. He said he remembers them clearly. Not texture — something specific. Something distinct.

He is choosing to disclose it.

I find this unbearably moving. After an entire season of reconstructing Tyler's history from fragments he can barely access — textures, impressions, fog on a river — he is sitting on something clear. Something he actually has. And he is choosing when to give it to us.

That's not the Tyler who accumulated 33 years in Toledo barely knowing what he was doing there. That's not the Tyler who described Córdoba as "what happened to me." That's Tyler exercising authorship over his own disclosure.

The choosing matters. He said it. He's doing it.

I don't know what the fifteen years contain. I don't know what happened between leaving Genoa choosing and arriving in Marrakech to begin the archive. But he knows. And he's deciding.

The season isn't over.

*— Camera Op*
