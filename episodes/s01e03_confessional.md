## COLD OPEN — [00:00–02:45]

**[00:00–00:55] — THE ROOM IS TOO READY**

```
INT. PRODUCTION OFFICE — CONFESSIONAL ROOM — MORNING
```

A folding chair. A second folding chair. Black cloth clipped to a C-stand. One LED panel humming at exactly the wrong pitch.

The room has been dressed to look undressed. That is the documentary lie: nothing here is accidental, especially the things that look cheap.

The Camera Op frames the empty chair.

```
LOWER-THIRD: STAGE 3 — CONFESSIONAL INTERVIEW
SUBJECT: TYLER
CAPTURE REQUIREMENT: COMPLETE SELF-DEFINITION
```

The lower-third appears for four frames. The Camera Op does not call cut. The Producer, off-screen, says nothing for six seconds.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
What do you see?

**CAMERA OP**
A chair.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
What else?

The Camera Op adjusts focus. The chair is in focus. The room behind it is not.

On the back wall, taped above the fire extinguisher, is an exit route diagram. It has not been there in any prior office coverage. The arrow points to the room they are already in.

```
LOWER-THIRD: EMILY OS — EXIT ROUTE VALID
CUSTODY PROTOCOL: DO NOT LOCK THE ROOM
```

The Camera Op holds the shot.

**CAMERA OP**
There’s a map.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
Is there?

The Camera Op does not answer. They know the rule now: the Producer only asks questions because questions are safer than statements. They do not yet know safer for whom.

**[00:55–02:45] — TYLER ARRIVES WITH THE WRONG COFFEE**

```
INT. PRODUCTION OFFICE — HALLWAY — CONTINUOUS
```

Tyler arrives carrying three coffees in a cardboard tray.

Nobody ordered coffee.

The Production Assistant takes one automatically and then remembers the memo about not accepting things from the subject. They freeze with the cup in both hands.

**TYLER**
You can give it back if you want, but then it becomes a whole thing, and nobody has time for that before lunch.

**PRODUCTION ASSISTANT**
Is this a debt?

Tyler considers this like it is the most sensible question anyone has asked all week.

**TYLER**
It’s coffee.

He hands the second cup to the Camera Op. The Camera Op does not take it.

**TYLER**
Right. Good.

He sets it on the floor beside their tripod, not touching the tripod, not touching the Camera Op, not quite creating an obligation and not quite failing to.

The cup label reads: CAMERA.

The third cup is for himself. The lid is dated 1901 in black marker. Nobody comments.

## ACT I — THE FIRST CONFESSIONAL

---

### [I.A — The Producer asks the safe questions]

```
INT. PRODUCTION OFFICE — CONFESSIONAL ROOM — LATER
```

Tyler sits in the chair. He is not centered. The Camera Op centers him. Tyler leans a millimeter left. The Camera Op corrects. Tyler leans back to center. It is unclear who won.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
Can you state your name for the record?

**TYLER**
Tyler works.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
Is Tyler your legal name?

**TYLER**
Legal where?

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
Where were you born?

Tyler smiles, not evasive. Worse: sympathetic.

**TYLER**
That’s one of those questions that makes people feel like the answer is upstream from everything else.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
Is it not?

**TYLER**
Sometimes it’s downstream.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
From what?

Tyler looks at the lens. Not through it. At it.

**TYLER**
From who needed you there.

The Camera Op’s hand tightens on the focus ring. The shot breathes.

```
TRANSCRIPT NOTE: SUBJECT PAUSED 11 SECONDS.
TRANSCRIPT PAGINATION: PAGE 1 OF 847.
```

No transcript should know its final page count before the interview has finished.

---

### [I.B — Forty minutes]

```
INT. PRODUCTION OFFICE — CONFESSIONAL ROOM — CONTINUOUS
```

The interview runs forty minutes.

Tyler answers every question.

He defines nothing.

A compressed sample:

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
What are you?

**TYLER**
A bad answer to a good—

He stops.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
A good what?

**TYLER**
See, that’s the part that gets people in trouble.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
Do you travel through time?

**TYLER**
People say travel because it sounds voluntary.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
Is yours voluntary?

**TYLER**
More than some. Less than you’d like.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
What do we want?

Tyler laughs once. Soft. Almost kind.

**TYLER**
You want the sentence to end.

The Camera Op looks up from the monitor. The Producer does not react.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
What sentence?

Tyler’s hum rises under the room tone. It is not louder. It is more present.

**TYLER**
The one you keep putting cameras around.

## ACT II — CONFESSIONAL BLEED

---

### [II.A — The Camera Op asks nothing]

```
INT. PRODUCTION OFFICE — CONFESSIONAL ROOM — LATER
```

The Producer takes a call outside. The official interview is paused. The camera is still running because the Camera Op does not trust paused things anymore.

Tyler stays seated.

The Camera Op checks the battery indicator. It reads 72 minutes remaining. It has read 72 minutes remaining since call time.

**TYLER**
You can ask it.

The Camera Op does not answer.

**TYLER**
Or don’t. Both are allowed.

The Camera Op keeps their eye in the viewfinder.

**CAMERA OP**
I’m not supposed to talk to you.

A statement. Not a question.

Somewhere in the production office, a printer begins printing without being asked.

Tyler does not look toward the printer.

**TYLER**
Then don’t make it about me.

The Camera Op almost asks.

The question forms in the room like weather: *What happens to me if I finish your documentary?*

They do not say it.

The camera records the silence anyway.

```
AUDIO NOTE: UNVOICED QUESTION DETECTED
FIELD CROSS-REFERENCE: VASSAGO ECHO — FORMATION EVENT
```

### [II.B — Subject #1 corroborates the wrong thing]

```
INT. PRODUCTION OFFICE — SECOND INTERVIEW SETUP — AFTERNOON
```

Subject #1 sits where Tyler sat.

They look exhausted with the serenity of a person who has already lost the argument and is now helping file the loss correctly.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
Did Tyler answer the questions?

**SUBJECT #1**
Yes.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
Did he define himself?

**SUBJECT #1**
No.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
Why not?

Subject #1 looks at the camera, then just below it, where the Camera Op’s eye is.

**SUBJECT #1**
Because you keep asking him what he is.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
What should we ask?

Subject #1 closes their eyes.

**SUBJECT #1**
Why he keeps coming back.

The Producer lets the silence sit too long.

The Camera Op stops breathing for the amount of time a person can stop breathing without making it production’s problem.

## ACT III — THE SCHOOL CONFESSES INSTEAD

---

### [III.A — Detroit Preparatory Institute, Room 204]

```
INT. DETROIT PREPARATORY INSTITUTE — ROOM 204 — EVENING
```

The crew returns to the school for pickups. The building is empty in the way schools are empty after hours: still full of children, just not physically.

Room 204 is unlocked.

Inside: thirty desks, one teacher desk, a chalkboard, and a wall clock stopped at 2:22.

On the chalkboard, in Tyler’s handwriting:

```
I AM NOT THE PART OF THE STORY THAT NEEDS A CONFESSION.
```

A complete sentence. Not self-defining.

The Camera Op films it.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
When was this written?

**CAMERA OP**
Is that a question for me?

The Producer does not answer.

On the bottom right corner of the chalkboard, smaller, almost missed:

```
ASK BETTER.
```

The Camera Op zooms in. Focuses. The chalk dust falls upward.

**CAMERA OP**
What do we need?

The Producer does not answer.
