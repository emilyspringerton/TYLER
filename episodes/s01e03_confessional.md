The camera is already running when the episode begins. No cue, no count-in — the footage just exists, mid-room, in a hotel room that has been configured into a confessional interview set with the quiet efficiency of people who have done this many times.

Warm lamp. Neutral backdrop (a beige curtain hung across the window; the curtain rod is bent). Two chairs, angled slightly toward each other. A small table with two water glasses. The glasses are equidistant from each chair. This is precise in the way that suggests the precision was debated.

The room smells like old paper and something slightly wrong underneath it. The lamp light is too warm for a hotel room but exactly right for a documentary interview. This is because it is an interview lamp, not a hotel lamp. The lamp was brought from the production office.

**[02:30–03:15] — THE QUESTION ABOUT TIME**

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
What is it like to not know what year it is?

**TYLER**
*(considering)*
That's not quite the—

*(pause)*

It's more like — the year is there, I just sometimes can't tell which one it's pointing at.

*(pause)*

Like the difference between knowing where you are and knowing where north is. Both are true. Sometimes they're the same direction.

The Producer writes something. The camera holds on Tyler.

**TYLER (CONT.)**
That doesn't answer your question.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
Doesn't it?

**TYLER**
I don't know. Does it?

The lamp light shifts slightly. One degree warmer. Almost nothing. The Camera Op notices — you can tell because the lens steadies, a half-second of involuntary focus-pulling toward the lamp as if the camera itself wants to check.

**ON SCREEN (environment only — no tile, just the room):**
The lamp is one degree warmer. The room is slightly less forgiving.

**[03:15–05:00] — THE QUESTION ABOUT BEFORE**

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
What were you before any of this?

Tyler smiles. The smile is the practiced one — the emanation of unresolvability. It makes you wonder what he finds funny. You keep expecting to figure out what he finds funny and you never do.

**TYLER**
I'm a—

He pauses.

The room waits.

He tilts his head like he is listening to something. The hum continues at exactly 7.83 Hz. It has been continuing since he sat down. The audio team will later note that the hum is not affected by his pauses, his breathing, his changes in posture. The hum runs independent of the man.

**TYLER (CONT.)**
I used to have a better answer to that. Then I was somewhere where the answer was different and I came back and both answers were true and I couldn't — you can't hold two answers to "what were you before" at the same time.

*(pause)*

It's like asking which way water goes when you're in the water.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**
Which way does it go?

**TYLER**
*(without hesitation)*
Everywhere.

The Producer writes. The camera holds.

**[05:00–08:30] — THE QUESTION THE CAMERA OP ASKED (NOT THE PRODUCER)**

*Note: The Camera Op spoke during Session 8. This was a protocol violation. The following footage was flagged for review. It has not been removed from the edit because removing it would require destroying production materials.*

The Producer has stopped speaking. There is a pause that has lasted approximately 90 seconds — a pause of the kind that happens when someone is deciding something rather than waiting. Tyler has been looking at the Camera Op for most of this pause. Not at the camera. At the person behind it.

**CAMERA OP**
*(quiet, not a question, then correcting themselves into a question)*
You know what's happening here, don't you?

The Producer is silent. This is notable. The Producer is always either asking a question or considering their next question. They are not asking a question or considering one. The room registers this.

**TYLER**
*(to the Camera Op, not the lens)*
Which part?

**CAMERA OP**
The part where it's not a documentary.

Another pause. Tyler is very still. He is not performing stillness — this is the genuine article, the stillness of someone who is deciding how much to give.

**TYLER**
What would it be, if it wasn't a documentary?

**CAMERA OP**
I don't know. I need you to tell me.

Tyler looks at the camera — at the lens, directly, for exactly two seconds. The two-second look. The taunt. Then away.

**TYLER**
*(to the room, not quite either of them)*
It's what they say it is. That part's real. The documentation is real. The footage is real.

*(pause)*

The question is what real footage is for.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)** *(recovering)*
What is real footage for?

Tyler looks at the Producer's general direction. He smiles slightly — a different version of the smile. Not the practiced one. The one that reaches.

**TYLER**
*(warmly)*
You tell me.

The Producer does not answer. The Producer never answers. The Producer asks only questions. This is the first time in 8 sessions that Tyler has given the question back and the Producer has run out of question to return.

The room is quiet for six seconds.

**[22:00–24:00] — TYLER ENDS IT (ALWAYS HIM, NEVER THEM)**

Tyler looks at the water glass. He looks at it with the expression of someone deciding whether to finish the drink or leave it.

**PRODUCER (O.S.)**

**TYLER**
The ones you haven't asked yet.

He leaves. The door closes.
