## EPISODE STRUCTURE

**Day 131, morning — the completion**

Emily arrives at 8 AM with the network map notebook. The Exchange Student arrives five minutes later. Bacon is already here — he drove from his building at 5 AM because Tyler called him the night before and said only: "Tomorrow."

They stand at the edges of the ward. Tyler is at the center.

At 8:23 AM he stops.

He stands for a moment in the particular silence that follows the end of a sustained piece of work.

**TYLER:** "It's done."

---

**What done feels like in a layered room:**

He walks to the east wall. To the nurse's position.

**TYLER:** "The difference between this room and the first one is the layering. Bacon's building — one event, one moment of completion, clean. This room held a hundred and eight years of layered experience. Clarifying it took longer because the material was more complex."

He looks at the Camera Op.

**TYLER:** "But a layered room is more than the sum of its events. When you make a layered room legible, what you're making accessible isn't just one thing that happened. It's the full weight of what this space witnessed. Everyone who was here. Everyone who didn't leave."

**CAMERA OP:** *(from the nurse's position, camera down for the first time)* "The nurse."

**TYLER:** "The nurse. The patients who stayed through the end of 1918. The doctors who came back the day after the ward closed. All of it. The room holds all of it now."

She lifts the camera. She films from the nurse's position: Tyler, at the center of the room he just completed, in the morning light from the east windows.

This is the footage. This is what the CAMERA-OP-ARCHIVE will carry from this day.

**Day 131, late morning — the Subscriber notation gap**

Emily opens the network map. She draws a second solid circle next to the Detroit Receiving node — completion marker, same as Bacon's building.

**EMILY:** "Two complete. Nine candidates remaining." She looks at the Subscriber notation gap note. "The construction is complete without Subscriber notation. You said it would be less stable. How much less?"

**TYLER:** "The Bacon room runs independently — I haven't worked in it since Day 82 and the frequencies are holding. This room will need a resonant witness present at least once a week. Not working — present. Someone standing in it, acknowledging what it holds. That's the maintenance threshold."

**EMILY:** "Four confirmed witnesses. Weekly rotation. That's manageable."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "I've drafted the maintenance protocol." She puts a page on the folding table. "Four witnesses, weekly schedule, coverage for gaps. One of us in Ward C every seven days minimum. The building conversion won't interfere — the new use of the space doesn't affect the construction." She pauses. "I mapped the conversion plans. The ward becomes an administrative area. Lower foot traffic than the original ward. Quieter. Better for maintenance, actually."

**TYLER:** "Good."

**EMILY:** "The Bacon room has no maintenance requirement because the Subscriber's notation is doing the work. If the Subscriber left notation here—"

**TYLER:** "The Subscriber knows what was built here. Whether they leave notation is their decision, not mine." He looks at the room. "The room is complete. If the Subscriber wants to add something later, the room will receive it."

Emily writes: *Detroit Receiving — complete Day 131. Maintenance: weekly witness presence, rotating schedule. Subscriber notation: open (room will receive if offered).*

**Day 131, midday — Bacon**

Bacon has been standing near the door since 8:23 AM. He hasn't said anything.

Tyler walks over to him.

**BACON:** "I want to ask you something."

**TYLER:** "Ask."

**BACON:** "The room at my building. You've been working in it since Day 76. I know it's complete — you said so on Day 82. But I've been living in the building with it for forty-nine days. And I want to know—" he pauses "—what does it mean that the room is in my building. Not for you. For me."

Tyler looks at him.

**TYLER:** "It means your building is the permanent address of what happened on Day 49. Not a recording. Not a memory. The actual event, accessible. Anyone who enters that room can be in the moment of the sentence completing. Because you were there on Day 49. Because your building was there. The room exists at your address because that's where the event happened."

Bacon is quiet.

**BACON:** "I've been in it three times since Day 82. Each time I go in, I know what it is. I know what happened there. But I wanted to know if that was—correct. If I was understanding it correctly."

**TYLER:** "You're understanding it correctly."

**BACON:** "Good."

He goes back to standing near the door. After a moment he adds:

**BACON:** "I'll make sure it gets maintained."

He means for as long as the building stands.

**Day 131, afternoon — the photographs, again**

Tyler spreads the remaining nine photographs on the folding table. Ten were shown in S05E01; one (Detroit Receiving) is now complete. Nine remain.

He looks at them for a long time. The others give him room.

**TYLER:** "The next one isn't in Detroit."

Emily looks up from the notebook.

**TYLER:** *(pointing to photograph 4)* "The crossing. The Ambassador Bridge. I said this one was the most stable — three hundred years of continuous crossing, the container is very old. It's also the most complex. The container isn't a room. It's a threshold between two places." He looks at the photograph. "A Witness Room for a threshold is a different kind of construction. I need to think about what it requires."

**EMILY:** "How long to think?"

**TYLER:** "I don't know yet. The other eight candidates are rooms — buildings, specific spaces. The crossing is different. I want to understand the construction type before I start."

Emily notes it: *Construction #3 candidate: Ambassador Bridge / the crossing. Construction type TBD — threshold, not room. Tyler studying.*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(already opening her laptop)* "I'll start the dependency map."
