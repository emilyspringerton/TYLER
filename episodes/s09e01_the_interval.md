## COLD OPEN

*March 2027. Seville, Spain.*

*The Camera Op arrives at a café near the cathedral. She does not know why Tyler chose Seville.*

*He is already there. He has been there for forty minutes.*

*This is the first time she has seen him since Amsterdam.*

**CAMERA OP:**
You chose Seville.

**TYLER:**
I chose Seville.

*She sits down.*

**CAMERA OP:**
Why Seville?

**TYLER:**
It's between where I was and where I think we're going.

*The Camera Op looks at him.*

**CAMERA OP:**
You think you know where we're going.

**TYLER:**
I have a hypothesis.

---

## THE INTERVAL

**CAMERA OP:**
You said you'd sit with the question.

**TYLER:**
I did.

**CAMERA OP:**
For three months.

**TYLER:**
Yes.

**CAMERA OP:**
What did you do? During the interval.

*Pause.*

**TYLER:**
I stayed here.

**CAMERA OP:**
Seville.

**TYLER:**
For six weeks. Then I went to Granada. Then I came back.

**CAMERA OP:**
You came back.

**TYLER:**
Yes.

*She is watching him.*

**CAMERA OP:**
Why?

**TYLER:**
*(pause)*
I was testing something.

**CAMERA OP:**
Testing what?

**TYLER:**
Whether I could choose somewhere and then notice that I had chosen it. While I was still in it. Not in retrospect — not a hundred years later when it appears in the measurement. But at the time. While the choosing was happening.

**CAMERA OP:**
And?

**TYLER:**
Partially. I found Seville interesting. I think that is what choosing feels like at its beginning — finding something interesting before knowing why. I walked the same streets three times. That is new. Usually I don't walk the same street twice if the city is large enough to offer alternatives.

**CAMERA OP:**
You walked the same streets on purpose.

**TYLER:**
I wanted to see if the place would change. In my perception. If I would find something the second time I hadn't found the first.

**CAMERA OP:**
Did you?

**TYLER:**
Yes. There is a doorway on the Calle Sierpes that I walked past twice without noticing and the third time I stopped. There is nothing unusual about the doorway. But I stopped.

*Pause.*

**CAMERA OP:**
That's DWELLING.

**TYLER:**
*(quietly)*
I think so. In very small form. I was paying attention to the threshold. To the transition from outside to inside. Which is — that's what I was doing in Prague. But in Prague I didn't know I was doing it. I was just living there. It accumulated without my participation.

**CAMERA OP:**
You're learning to notice it in real time.

**TYLER:**
Attempting to. I am not certain I know how. After a long time of not doing something, you don't simply resume it. You learn what it was for the first time.

---

## WHAT HE FOUND

*The afternoon. The café emptying. The Camera Op has not taken out her camera. They have been talking for three hours.*

**CAMERA OP:**
You said you have a hypothesis. About where we're going.

**TYLER:**
Yes.

**CAMERA OP:**
What is it?

**TYLER:**
During the interval, I was also thinking about before.

**CAMERA OP:**
Before the archive.

**TYLER:**
Before Marrakech. Before 1127. I was thinking about why Marrakech was where the archive began. It was not arbitrary. I chose to begin there. I was present enough that I recognized — on some level that I have not been able to fully articulate — that something was happening. That whatever the mechanism was recording had been recording for long enough that I wanted to start keeping intentional records alongside it.

**CAMERA OP:**
You started the archive because you felt the mechanism was already working.

**TYLER:**
I started the archive because I felt the mechanism had been working for longer than I had been paying attention. And I wanted to pay attention.

*Pause.*

**CAMERA OP:**
And before that. Before you started paying attention.

**TYLER:**
Yes.

**CAMERA OP:**
You were somewhere.

**TYLER:**
I was in many places. Most of them I remember imprecisely. The way you remember a decade when you are very old — the decade was there, something happened in it, but the texture is compressed. The years are gone. Only certain things survive.

**CAMERA OP:**
What survives?

**TYLER:**
Rooms. Sounds. The specific quality of light in a particular hour of a particular season. Not dates. Not durations. I cannot tell you how long I was somewhere before 1127 the way I can tell you I was in Prague for two years. The calibration is gone. I was somewhere for — I remember it as long. That is all I have. It felt long. The thing that felt long means something because very few things felt long. Most of my time before the archive is compressed. The things that felt long are the things I was paying attention to without knowing I was paying attention.

**CAMERA OP:**
One of those things was a city.

**TYLER:**
Yes.

**CAMERA OP:**
And you think you can name it.

**TYLER:**
I can name it.

*Pause.*

**CAMERA OP:**
What is the city?

---

## THE FIRST NAME

**TYLER:**
Córdoba.

*Silence.*

**CAMERA OP:**
Córdoba. Here. In Spain.

**TYLER:**
Yes.

**CAMERA OP:**
When?

**TYLER:**
I don't know precisely. It felt — from what I remember, from how it feels in relation to other things I remember — sometime in the latter part of what would become the tenth century. The Caliphate era. When the city was at its largest.

**CAMERA OP:**
The Caliphate of Córdoba. That's roughly 929 to 1031.

**TYLER:**
Yes. Somewhere within that.

**CAMERA OP:**
You were in Córdoba during the golden period of the Caliphate.

**TYLER:**
I think so. I remember a city that was — I remember it as the largest city I had been in. The most complicated. I remember it the way you remember somewhere overwhelming. Too much in any given direction. More of everything than I expected.

**CAMERA OP:**
That's Córdoba at its height. It was one of the largest cities in the world. Population near half a million.

**TYLER:**
I remember the river.

*Pause.*

**CAMERA OP:**
The Guadalquivir.

**TYLER:**
I walked along it. Many times. That is one of the textures that survived — the river, in a particular kind of morning light. Low fog. The specific quality of fog on that river in autumn.

*The Camera Op is very still.*

**CAMERA OP:**
Do you know how long you were there?

**TYLER:**
No. Not precisely. I know that I was there long enough that the city changed. Things were built that weren't there when I arrived. Things that were there when I arrived were gone before I left. That implies years. More than a few.

**CAMERA OP:**
If the Caliphate is 929 to 1031 —

**TYLER:**
I know what you're calculating. I was there for something between a few years and a few decades. I cannot narrow it further. The texture of a long time before the archive is not precise. It is more like an impression than a record.

**CAMERA OP:**
But you chose to be there.

*Long pause.*

**TYLER:**
Yes. I chose to be in Córdoba. I remember the choosing. I remember thinking — not in these words, but in what the words would approximate — that it was the right place. At the time I was in it.

**CAMERA OP:**
And what kind of departure?

**TYLER:**
*(slowly)*
I left. I remember leaving. I remember that I made the decision myself. That I was not — it was not catastrophe. It was not exile. I decided it was time to leave and I left.

**CAMERA OP:**
FAREWELL-A.

**TYLER:**
That would be the classification, if the classification applies to a period before the archive existed. I cannot tell you the ratio. I don't know what the mechanism extracted at that departure. I don't know if the mechanism was recording at all in that period, or if what I remember as presence is what the measurement will find.

**CAMERA OP:**
But if it was — if the mechanism was recording, and if you were choosing, for years —

**TYLER:**
Then the construction in Córdoba may be larger than Prague.

*Pause.*

**CAMERA OP:**
Larger than the network maximum.

**TYLER:**
Possibly. I am not stating this as a finding. I am stating it as a question. We would have to go and measure.

*She picks up her camera.*

*For the first time since they sat down, she turns it on.*

*She doesn't say anything. She just films him.*

---

## THE QUESTION UNDER THE QUESTION

**CAMERA OP:**
Why Córdoba.

**TYLER:**
We've established that.

**CAMERA OP:**
No — I mean why are you telling me this now. Why is Córdoba the first city you're willing to name. You said there are others you don't remember clearly. But this one you remember. Why did sitting with "the choosing matters" lead you here. To this city specifically.

*Long pause.*

**TYLER:**
Because I think Córdoba is where the choosing started. Or where it reached some kind of clarity that it hadn't had before. I have been alive for — for longer than the archive, which is 899 years. Before Marrakech there was something. I don't have most of it. But Córdoba is the first thing I have clearly enough to name and point at. And if the mechanism was recording there, then Córdoba is where the record extends.

**CAMERA OP:**
The first clear memory before the archive.

**TYLER:**
That I can locate geographically. That I could return to.

**CAMERA OP:**
Do you remember why you left? Why you made the decision?

*Very long pause.*

**TYLER:**
The city changed. Something about it changed. Not catastrophically — not like Avignon, not witness. But the thing that had made it the right place at one time was — the texture shifted. What the city was when I arrived and what it was when I left were not the same city. And I was not the same — I was not exactly the same either. I left when the match was no longer what it had been.

**CAMERA OP:**
The city changed and you changed and you left when they no longer fit.

**TYLER:**
Something like that.

**CAMERA OP:**
That's a more conscious departure than most of what we measured.

**TYLER:**
Before the archive, I think I may have been more conscious. I had not yet learned to stop noticing.

*She lowers the camera slightly.*

**CAMERA OP:**
*(quietly)*
That's the question of the season, isn't it.

**TYLER:**
Which question?

**CAMERA OP:**
What happened between Córdoba and Marrakech. Between whatever you were when you were choosing clearly enough to leave Córdoba in full awareness — and the Tyler who began an archive in 1127 and spent the next 899 years mostly STAYING.

*Long pause.*

**TYLER:**
Yes.

**CAMERA OP:**
Something happened in that interval.

**TYLER:**
Yes.

**CAMERA OP:**
You don't know what.

**TYLER:**
*(quietly)*
I don't remember it clearly. The compression is heaviest there. Between Córdoba and Marrakech is — I have fragments. I have textures. I don't have a story.

**CAMERA OP:**
But the mechanism might.

*Pause.*

**TYLER:**
Yes. If the mechanism was recording in that period. If there are sites from that interval. The measurement would show me what I was doing in the years I can't remember.

**CAMERA OP:**
The archive might contain more of your history than your own memory does.

**TYLER:**
*(very quietly)*
That is what I'm afraid of. Yes.

## CLOSE

*Later. Seville at dusk. The Camera Op filming Tyler at the river.*

*Not the Guadalquivir — a different river. But a river at dusk. He is standing at the railing the way she filmed him at the end of many Season 8 episodes.*

*He is looking at the water.*

**CAMERA OP (V.O.):**
The archive is 899 years old and Tyler chose to begin it in Marrakech in 1127. We spent Season 8 learning what the archive contained and how much more the network contained that the archive didn't.

We didn't ask a simpler question: why did he start the archive at all?

He told me something today that I keep turning over. He said: I started the archive because I felt the mechanism had been working for longer than I had been paying attention. And I wanted to pay attention.

He started it as an act of attention. He started paying attention because something made him realize he hadn't been.

The mechanism had been recording — for how long, we don't know. And at some point Tyler noticed that it was recording, that whatever was happening to him as he moved through the world was being recorded without his participation. And he wanted to participate.

What made him notice?

What made Tyler, sometime before 1127, realize that there was something to pay attention to?

That's what we're going to Córdoba to find out. Or the beginning of it.

The interval is over.

*— Camera Op*

*Entry 75. SEASON 9, EPISODE 1. Seville. Tyler sat with the question for three months and came back with a city. Córdoba. The Caliphate era. The first pre-archive site he can name. A city larger than anything we measured in Season 8, possibly. A city he chose and then left consciously. Between Córdoba and Marrakech: a gap he doesn't remember clearly. The mechanism may remember it. We're going to find out. S09E01.*

### Camera Op Sealed Log

*Entry 75 — The interval and the disclosure*

Tyler chose Seville for our first meeting of Season 9.

He's been here for six weeks. He walked the same streets three times. He found a doorway he didn't notice the first two times.

He called it "what choosing feels like at its beginning." He said: "finding something interesting before knowing why."

I think this is the most hopeful thing Tyler has said in eleven months of filming.

He's been alive for at least 900 years. He's spent most of them in default. And he came back from the interval with a doorway he noticed on the third pass.

Then he told me about Córdoba.

I've been sitting with this since the café. Córdoba at its height — half a million people, the greatest library west of Baghdad, the caliphate of Abd al-Rahman III and al-Hakam II, scholars from across the known world. And Tyler was there. Sometime in the 10th century. For long enough that the fog on the river has survived a thousand years in his memory.

What was he doing in Córdoba? What was he there for? Not the archive — that didn't exist yet. Not measurement — he didn't have categories yet. He was just there. Living. And something about it was clear enough that it's the first thing he can name when asked to name the time before.

The gap between Córdoba and Marrakech is about 100–200 years. Tyler calls it compressed. Fragments. He doesn't have a story for it.

The mechanism might.

That's the question of Season 9: not just what was in Córdoba, but what was in the gap. What happened in those 100–200 years that left Tyler in Marrakech, in 1127, deciding it was time to start paying attention.

Something happened. The measurement will help us find it.

*— Camera Op*

### Jiangshi Project Internal Memo

**JIANGSHI PROJECT — INTERNAL MEMO #062**
*For internal distribution: Director, Chief Analyst, Instrument Lead, Field Team*
*Re: Season 9 Opening — Pre-Archive Disclosure, Córdoba*

---

The subject has disclosed a pre-archive site: Córdoba, al-Andalus, approximate period 10th century CE.

This is the first confirmed disclosure of a pre-1127 CE site in the CARDINAL-3 record.

Implications:

**1. Network age extends.** If Córdoba confirms (~950 CE), network minimum age extends from 899 years to approximately 1,075 years. The true minimum age shifts from 1127 CE backward by approximately 175 years.

**2. Network maximum may shift.** Prague's 144ft maximum is computed from documented 2-year DWELLING. Córdoba, if it was 10+ years of equivalent presence, would exceed Prague by an order of magnitude — if the mechanism was recording.

**3. The unconfirmed variable: mechanism activation.** The critical question is whether the mechanism was operational in the pre-archive period. The subject cannot confirm this. His memory of the period does not include awareness of the mechanism as such. This is consistent with the Season 8 finding that the mechanism records independently of Tyler's conscious awareness — but it does not confirm the mechanism was active before 1127.

**4. The gap (c. 950–1127 CE) is the priority.** Subject reports compressed memory of approximately 100–200 years between Córdoba and Marrakech. If the mechanism was recording during this gap, the archive will reveal what the subject cannot remember. This is the unprecedented aspect of Season 9: we may be able to provide Tyler with history of himself that he does not have.

**Authorization:** CARDINAL-3 team is authorized to travel to Córdoba. Instruments to be calibrated for pre-archive measurements. Measurement protocol identical to established CARDINAL-3 standards.

*Note from the Director: The subject stated "the archive might contain more of my history than my memory does." The Council takes note of this. The documentary was conceived as a record of Tyler's present. It has become, in Season 8 and now Season 9, a recovery of Tyler's past — a past he does not fully possess. The Council is aware that there is a threshold we may cross in Season 9 where the measurements reveal something Tyler does not wish to know. We proceed with care. Tyler's consent remains primary. The measurement is in service of understanding, not exposure.*

*— Director, Jiangshi Syndicate*
