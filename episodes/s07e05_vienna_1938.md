## COLD OPEN

Day 239. Morning.

The 1938 register is eight pages.

For seven seasons, Tyler's field registers have been compressed, shorthand, abbreviated. The 1931 Paris register is loose cursive — *Fleurus, three weeks, the Stein salon, bread and argument, the usual* — like someone writing to themselves with time to spare. The 1952 Detroit archive is tighter, purposeful: address, sketch, duration, note. The Detroit surveys from Season 6 are almost purely functional.

The 1938 Vienna register is different.

Each letter formed. No contractions. No abbreviation. No shorthand for the usual or stayed too long again. Just:

*Wollzeile 12. 12. Juni. Drei Stunden. Ich habe versucht nicht zu schauen.*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Translation: Wollzeile 12. June 12. Three hours. I tried not to look."

**CAMERA OP:** "Tried not to look at what?"

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The question."

**TYLER:** "The question was Vienna in 1938."

## SITE SURVEY — VIENNA, 1938

**Day 239–240. Site reconnaissance.**

The Exchange Student has assembled the Vienna file from CARDINAL-3 records, the 1938 register, and four cross-references from the Jiangshi central archive — which, she notes, has more Vienna 1938 material than any city except Berlin.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Eleven notations in four months. For Paris we had six notations across — Tyler, how many years in Paris total?"

**TYLER:** "First visit 1924. Last before 1938 was 1935. Eleven years of regular visits."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Eleven years in Paris produced six sites. Four months in Vienna 1938 produced eleven notations."

**TYLER:** "I wasn't moving much in Vienna."

The Exchange Student opens the register to the first entry.

*Wollzeile 12. 12. Juni. Drei Stunden. Ich habe versucht nicht zu schauen.*

*Schönlaterngasse. 19. Juni. Zwei Stunden. Das Haus kannte mich noch.*

*Bäckerstraße 11. 3. Juli. Sechs Stunden.* I couldn't leave.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The first seven entries are in German. The eighth switches to English midsentence. 'Six hours. I couldn't leave.' Then back to German for the final three."

**CAMERA OP:** "Why did you switch?"

**TYLER:** "I don't remember writing that entry."

Beat.

**TYLER:** "I was in Vienna for about four months. In that time, I tried to be a very small presence. I went to the same streets I had always gone to. I sat in the same cafés when the same cafés were still open to sit in. I tried not to draw attention. I tried to—" He stops. "I tried to continue being what I had always been in Vienna, which was someone who walked around and looked at things and stayed in rooms that asked to be stayed in."

**CAMERA OP:** "And the trying not to notice."

**TYLER:** "I noticed everything. That was the problem. The register is careful because I was using the writing to control where my attention went. Very small careful letters. One entry at a time. One fact at a time." He looks at the register. "I told you in Paris: the constructions were incidental. The staying was about something else."

**CAMERA OP:** "What was the staying about?"

**TYLER:** "Being somewhere."

## SITE A-280
**Wollzeile 12 — 3 hours. June 12, 1938.**

The street is in the first district, a few minutes from St. Stephen's Cathedral. The building is early 20th century — some 19th century stonework at the base. The current usage is mixed residential and commercial.

The Exchange Student measures: **24 feet**.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Twenty-four feet for three hours. That's approximately eight feet per hour — the same rate as A-279 in Paris, the inattentive coffee on the bench."

**TYLER:** "I wasn't inattentive. I was extremely attentive. I was paying very careful attention to a very small number of things in the room."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "What things?"

**TYLER:** "A kettle. A window. A newspaper I didn't read."

**CAMERA OP:** "You were in someone's apartment?"

**TYLER:** "A friend's. She wasn't home. She had left three days before. I was—" He stops. "Using the space."

The Exchange Student writes the measurement in the log. 24 feet. 3 hours. The mechanism ran at Paris rate while Tyler sat in an empty apartment attending to a kettle and an unread newspaper.

**TYLER:** "She asked me to look after the plants. I looked after the plants. Two ferns. They were fine when I left."

The Camera Op holds the shot on the window for a long time.

## SITE A-281
**Schönlaterngasse — 2 hours. June 19, 1938.**

A narrow street in the first district, north of the cathedral. Beautiful Lantern Lane.

The Exchange Student measures: **18 feet**.

The 1938 register entry: *Das Haus kannte mich noch.* The house still knew me.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You'd been here before."

**TYLER:** "Many times. Over many years."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "And on June 19, 1938, you went back."

**TYLER:** "The street was different. The people were different. The house—" He touches the building's exterior, a small gesture, one palm briefly against the stonework. "The house was the same. I needed something to be the same."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "So you stayed two hours."

**TYLER:** "I stayed two hours."

The mechanism was not about the house. The mechanism was about Tyler staying because he needed something to be continuous. The house complied. 18 feet.

**CAMERA OP:** "The register says 'the house still knew me.' Not: I still knew the house."

**TYLER:** "That's right."

**CAMERA OP:** "You were checking whether it still did."

**TYLER:** "I needed to know if there was still something that recognized me. Something that the four months hadn't changed."

He doesn't finish the thought. The Camera Op does not ask.

## THE ICHTHYOSAPIENS — Vienna, 1938

On Day 242, the Exchange Student finds a notation in the Vienna record that has no address.

It sits between the third and fourth site entries. A single line in careful German:

*F. war am Donnerstag. Wir haben gesprochen. Er geht am Freitag.*

F. was here on Thursday. We spoke. He leaves on Friday.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "F. Who is F.?"

**TYLER:** "Someone I'd known for about ten years by then."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "From the Paris file — A-276, Rue de Rivoli, 1928. Eighty-six feet. One week. The Cataloguer's note: faction argument about documentation methodology. Ichthyosapiens contact."

**TYLER:** "F. is an Ichthyosapiens contact. We had a disagreement in Paris in 1928 about how to document the mechanism. I thought documentation should prioritize witness — what is present in the room, the qualitative texture of the staying, what the space does when something is in it. He thought it should prioritize measurement — radius, duration, conditions, reproducibility. Clean data."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The argument was about those two things being in conflict."

**TYLER:** "The argument was about whether measurement captured what witness captures. He said yes. I said the measurement is downstream of the witness — you can only measure radius because someone was present to witness the effect first. He said witness without measurement is impressionism. We disagreed for ten years. It was, as I told you in Paris, a clean argument. We both wanted to reach a good answer."

**CAMERA OP:** "And Vienna."

**TYLER:** "F. had to leave. Vienna in July 1938 was not — there were many people who had to go, who had waited, who had stayed too long waiting. F. needed to go."

**CAMERA OP:** "Was he—"

**TYLER:** "He had every reason to leave and very little choice about how long he had left. I helped him with documentation before he went. He gave me certain records to keep. I have kept them."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "What happened to the 1928 argument?"

Tyler is quiet.

**TYLER:** "He told me, on the Thursday, that I had been right in 1928. That witness was the correct methodology. He said he had spent ten years trying to make the documentation reproducible and presentable to people who required that kind of evidence. And then he said: 'The documentation is not for people who require that. The documentation is for the room.'" Tyler pauses. "And then he left."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The Ichthyosapiens conceded the argument."

**TYLER:** "He didn't concede it. He arrived at it separately. From a different direction, through a different decade, under different pressure. Which is different from conceding." Tyler looks at the notation in the register. "He was right that measurement matters. He was right that the radius is real data. But he arrived at: what the radius is measuring is the witness. They were never in conflict. We had just been standing in different rooms in 1928."

**CAMERA OP:** "And in 1938 you were in the same room."

**TYLER:** "For one Thursday."

**CAMERA OP:** "What did you do with the records he gave you?"

**TYLER:** "I kept them safe. I still have them." He looks out at the street. "He is — he survived the war. I heard from him in the 1950s. But we never argued about methodology again. There was nothing left to argue about. Vienna had answered the question."

## SITE A-282
**Bäckerstraße 11 — 6 hours. July 3, 1938.**

The register entry is the one that switches languages:

*Bäckerstraße 11. 3. Juli. Sechs Stunden.* I couldn't leave.

The Exchange Student measures: **52 feet**.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Fifty-two feet. Largest Vienna site so far. Comparable to A-276 in Paris — the Rue de Rivoli faction argument."

**TYLER:** "I was in a bakery I had been going to for thirty years. That afternoon there were three people inside. None of them spoke to me directly. One of them—" He stops, starts again. "They were there because they needed to be somewhere safe for a few hours. I knew that. I didn't ask. I bought bread. I sat down."

**CAMERA OP:** "And you couldn't leave."

**TYLER:** "The 1931 rule says: if a place asks you to stay and you don't know why, leave. I knew why. I knew exactly why. The room was asking me to stay because I am something that makes a room more of what it is when I am in it. I knew that by 1938 — not completely, not in the vocabulary we have now, but I knew the staying mattered in a way that ordinary staying doesn't. And the room was asking me to stay for a specific reason. And I—"

He stops.

**TYLER:** "I couldn't leave while they needed it."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You understood the pull and stayed anyway."

**TYLER:** "I understood the pull and stayed *because of it*. The rule is about understanding. I understood. The understanding said: stay. So I stayed six hours. I bought bread. I asked for more tea. I sat and pretended to read. I was very careful. And I made the space larger and more present by being in it, and I couldn't leave, and I didn't want to leave, and the three people left around four o'clock."

He pauses.

**TYLER:** "I stayed for another hour after that. In case they came back. They didn't come back. I left at five. I went back the next morning. The bakery was still there. The same woman behind the counter gave me bread without my asking."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "This is a revision of the 1931 rule."

**TYLER:** "The rule was: leave when you don't understand. The implication I had been drawing was: leave unless it's a deliberate construction, a Witness Room, a Bridge — something you've committed to. But this was neither. This was the mechanism asking me to use what I am for a specific, known, present purpose. That's — the rule needed to distinguish between unknown pulls and understood ones. Leave the unknown ones. Assess the understood ones."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "And your assessment was: stay."

**TYLER:** "My assessment was: stay. Six hours. Fifty-two feet. The rule was revised in a bakery on Bäckerstraße on July 3rd, 1938."

The Camera Op holds the building. Vienna in June 2026. The same street. Different people. The construction running from that afternoon still running.

**TYLER:** "The mechanism doesn't record the reason. It records the duration. The reason is in the register. 'I couldn't leave' is not accurate. I could leave. I chose not to."

## SITE A-283
**Wollzeile 7 — 8 minutes. July 9, 1938.**

The register entry: *Wollzeile 7. 9. Juli. Acht Minuten. Nein.*

Wollzeile 7. July 9. Eight minutes. No.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "No."

**TYLER:** "A few numbers down from the apartment I'd been in in June — where my friend's plants were. Different building. I had been to this one before, in earlier years. I knew the family that had lived there. In July 1938 that family was no longer there."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Gone."

**TYLER:** "Gone. I passed the building. I felt the pull. I went inside. I stood in the entrance hall for eight minutes."

**CAMERA OP:** "You'd just revised the 1931 rule. Known pull — assess it. What was your assessment?"

**TYLER:** "The pull was wrong. The mechanism remembers places. It remembers that I've been somewhere. It was asking me to stay in a room where the people I'd been there with were absent. It was asking me to stay for the absence. To make a memorial out of a place by remaining in it after the people were gone."

He pauses.

**TYLER:** "I don't do that. I don't amplify absence. I don't stay in a room to mourn the people who aren't there. The mechanism is for presence — for what is in the room, for what is alive and continuing. If I stay in a room and witness only the fact that it has been emptied, I am leaving a construction that holds grief without people in it. That is not witness. That is a wound."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "So you said no."

**TYLER:** "I said no to the room. I stood in the entrance hall. I said: no. And I left."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "And left eight minutes anyway."

**TYLER:** "The mechanism documented the refusal. Not my no. Not the absence. Just: Tyler was in the entrance hall for eight minutes and declined to stay. The eight minutes ran. REFUSAL-TRACE."

**CAMERA OP:** "But it's different from A-278 in Paris."

**TYLER:** "A-278 in Paris was a pre-awareness refusal — I applied the 1931 rule without knowing what the pull was. Wollzeile 7 is post-awareness, post-revision. I understood the pull precisely. I understood what it was asking. I refused on purpose, for a specific reason: I won't stay for absences. I'll stay for presences, known or unknown. I won't stay for grief without the person in it."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "A second category of refusal."

**TYLER:** "The first category is: I don't understand, leave. The second is: I understand, and the purpose is wrong. Leave. The mechanism records both. Both leave a trace."

He turns away from the building.

**TYLER:** "The family's name is in the record. The Jiangshi Syndicate will have their name. I gave F.'s records to a contact after the war." He is quiet for a moment. "The trace is eight feet running in the entrance hall of a building where I stood for eight minutes and said no. That's what I could do."

**CAMERA OP:** "That's what you could do."

**TYLER:** "That's what I could do."

The Camera Op holds the frame on the door. Careful.
