## THE SUMMARY

**CAMERA OP:**
When we started, the retrograde was a re-measurement. You had filed 73 archive sites and you wanted to re-classify the departure types. That was the brief. That's what you told me we were doing.

**TYLER:**
Yes.

**CAMERA OP:**
Eleven months later, here is what we actually did.

*She looks at her notes.*

**CAMERA OP:**
We classified five departure types. FAREWELL-A: conscious, permanent, aware — you knew you were leaving forever. FAREWELL-B: unconscious — you left but were not present in the leaving. FAREWELL-B2: dissociative — you had left yourself before you left the place. FAREWELL-C: established pattern — you left the way you always left, confident the pattern would continue. FAREWELL-D: suspended departure — you left from a state of suspension, generating almost nothing.

*She pauses.*

**CAMERA OP:**
We found four construction types that are not departures. REFUGE: recovery in stillness after a departure, even density, the place that didn't require legibility. WITNESS-LONG: extended catastrophic witnessing over months, broad even density, the body present while the mind tries to process the impossible. CARRIED: discharge of a prior intensive presence into a new location, front-loaded, what you brought from somewhere else. DWELLING: voluntary inhabitation, fully chosen, accelerating density as you became more present over time.

**TYLER:**
Yes.

**CAMERA OP:**
We found three constants. The FAREWELL-A departure ratio: 0.36. The FAREWELL-C departure ratio: 0.21. The work-to-residence density ratio: approximately 1.3 to 1 — you generate more per month at the site of focused work than at the site of residence, in every century, every city.

**TYLER:**
Yes.

**CAMERA OP:**
We found that the archive is not the network. The network is larger, older, and more varied than the archive. The archive documents events. The extra-archive contains life — the cities you chose freely and never thought to record because they felt too ordinary to document.

**TYLER:**
Prague.

**CAMERA OP:**
Prague. Burgundy. Lyon. Avignon. None of them archived. All of them generating the largest readings in the retrograde.

*She pauses.*

**CAMERA OP:**
We found that the network minimum age is 899 years and the actual age is unknown. You were alive before 1127. You don't know how long before. The mechanism may have been recording for longer than the archive records.

*Pause.*

**CAMERA OP:**
We found that the mechanism knows more about your departures than you do. In 1131, you left Marrakech without knowing you were leaving permanently. The mechanism extracted 21% and left 79% behind. The mechanism understood the departure as significant in a way you didn't. The mechanism has been doing this since at least 1127. Possibly much longer.

*Long pause.*

**CAMERA OP:**
And we found the new network maximum. Prague. 144 feet in two years. You chose to be there. You were fully there. You generated more in two years of choosing than Avignon accumulated in 676 years of passive recording.

*She puts down the notes.*

**CAMERA OP:**
That's what we found.

*Silence.*

**TYLER:**
Yes.

## THE RECKONING

**CAMERA OP:**
I want to ask you something.

**TYLER:**
All right.

**CAMERA OP:**
When you hired me — when you began this — you told me you wanted to re-classify 73 departure sites. That was true. But it was not everything you wanted.

*Pause.*

**TYLER:**
No.

**CAMERA OP:**
What did you actually want from this?

*Very long pause.*

**TYLER:**
I wanted to understand what I had been doing.

**CAMERA OP:**
Meaning?

**TYLER:**
I have been on the earth for — for a long time. I have been in many places. I have left all of them. I have filed some of them. I have tried to understand why certain places stay with me and others do not. Why I can find a ruined Burgundy monastery by walking toward it and can barely remember the name of cities I lived in for decades.

He pauses.

**TYLER:**
I wanted to know if there was a pattern. Whether what I had been doing had a logic. Whether the mechanism had been recording something that, when I finally looked at it directly, would tell me something I didn't already know.

**CAMERA OP:**
Did it?

**TYLER:**
*(pause)*
Yes.

**CAMERA OP:**
What?

**TYLER:**
The quality distinction.

*He pauses.*

**TYLER:**
I have spent a very long time — I don't know how long — moving. Arriving, staying, leaving. I have been good at moving. I have been very poor at choosing. Most of what I have done has been STAYING — being somewhere because it was where I was. Choosing freely was rare. Prague was rare. And Prague generated 144 feet.

**CAMERA OP:**
So the mechanism has been trying to tell you —

**TYLER:**
That the choosing matters. Yes. That most of what I was doing was not DWELLING. That I have been treating presence as a default rather than as a decision. And the mechanism has been recording the difference for — for as long as it has been recording.

**CAMERA OP:**
The constructions are not a record of where you were. They're a record of what you chose.

**TYLER:**
And what I couldn't choose — what happened to me, what I witnessed, what I carried, what I left without understanding the cost. All of that is in the record too. The mechanism doesn't privilege the chosen. It records everything. But the chosen generates the most.

**CAMERA OP (V.O.):**
Tyler told me once that FAREWELL-A — conscious permanent departure — extracts 36% and leaves 64% behind. The departure costs. You take 36 cents on every dollar of what you were somewhere, and the rest stays.

And he told me later that DWELLING — fully chosen presence — generates four times more per month than STAYING. That choosing something generates more than defaulting to it.

I've been thinking about what this means for anyone. Not just Tyler. For anyone.

We all leave things behind when we leave somewhere. Some of it by choice, some of it by circumstance, some of it because we couldn't carry it. The 64% stays. The next visitor to wherever you were finds you there, residual, at whatever fraction the mechanism extracts.

We all default to STAYING most of the time. And DWELLING — choosing fully, voluntarily, completely — is rarer than we think and generates more than we expect.

I don't have a mechanism. I don't know if I generate constructions. But I have eleven months of footage of a man who discovered that the difference between choosing and defaulting is the difference between 144 feet and 47 feet in the same duration, and that the mechanism has been trying to tell him this since before the archive started.

The choosing matters.

I don't know what I do with that either. But I know that I know it now. And the knowing is from the work.

*— Camera Op*

**Entry 70 — The mechanism question**

The Season 8 retrograde has not resolved the mechanism's origin. What we know:

1. The mechanism is active since at least 1127 CE.
2. The mechanism operates consistently across centuries (1127 ≈ 1351 ratios).
3. The mechanism generates constructions correlated with the quality of Tyler's presence.
4. The mechanism extracts departure measurements at type-consistent ratios.
5. The mechanism has been recording passively since before Tyler began intentional archiving (Avignon 1348, pre-archive).
6. The mechanism understood Tyler's departures as significant before Tyler did (Marrakech 1131).
7. Tyler was alive before 1127. The mechanism may have been active before 1127.

What we do not know:
- Whether Tyler is the mechanism or the mechanism operates on Tyler
- What the mechanism is recording into (what are the constructions made of?)
- Whether the mechanism is unique to Tyler or potentially present in other subjects
- What triggers the mechanism to begin or whether it has always been present
- What the mechanism would record after Tyler's death, if he can die

The CARDINAL-3 project began as documentation. It has produced a taxonomy. The taxonomy implies a theory. The theory requires a mechanism. The mechanism is unknown.

*Cataloguer Footnote #91: Cataloguer note for the record. The retrograde found that the mechanism generates the most when Tyler chooses. The mechanism seems to reward choosing. Whether this is a property of the mechanism or a property of Tyler — whether the mechanism measures choosing or choosing generates more for the mechanism to measure — is the central unanswered question of Season 8. Season 9 will not answer it. It will provide more data. The question may not have an answer within the scope of a documentary. It may require something other than a documentary. This note is for the record, not the archive.*

### SEASON 8 FINDINGS SUMMARY

The CARDINAL-3 retrograde, Season 8, has produced the following confirmed findings:

**1. The archive is not the network.** Extra-archive sites (Lyon, Avignon, Burgundy, Prague, Marrakech) were not filed by Tyler despite generating major constructions. The archive contains events. The extra-archive contains life.

**2. The mechanism was active before the archive.** Avignon 1348 (274 years pre-archive) confirmed passive recording. The mechanism records without Tyler's conscious archival intent.

**3. The network is older than 899 years.** Tyler was alive before 1127. The mechanism may have been active before 1127. The network's true age is unknown.

**4. The quality of presence determines construction size, not duration.** Prague (2 years DWELLING) = 144ft. Amsterdam (111 years STAYING) = ~100ft total. DWELLING generates 4.4ft/month. STAYING generates <0.1ft/month.

**5. Departure type determines how much is extracted.** FAREWELL-A: 36% extracted, 64% stays. FAREWELL-C: 21% extracted, 79% stays. Type-weighted global remainder: >30,000ft across 900 sites.

**6. The construction constants are consistent across centuries.** DWELLING work-to-residence ratio, departure ratios — confirmed identical at Marrakech (1127) and Prague (1351).

**7. The mechanism knew before Tyler knew.** Marrakech 1131: Tyler departed without understanding permanence; mechanism recorded significant FAREWELL-C. The mechanism has been tracking what Tyler was doing before Tyler understood what he was doing.
