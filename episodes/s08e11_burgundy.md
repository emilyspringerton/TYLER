## COLD OPEN

*Contemporary. A road in Burgundy, France. Morning light through old oak trees. Tyler is walking ahead of the Camera Op. Not quickly. Deliberately slowly.*

*The Camera Op holds back, filming him from behind. He is not looking at a map or a phone. He is looking at the ground ahead of him, then periodically to one side, then to the other. Adjusting course.*

**CAMERA OP (V.O.):**
He told me he doesn't remember the monastery's name. He told me he doesn't remember what order it belonged to. He told me he's been back twice in the last four hundred years, and both times he found it by walking toward where it felt like something had been left.

*Beat.*

**CAMERA OP (V.O.):**
I had been filming Tyler for eleven months at this point. I had watched him give a monastery's address as "a road in Burgundy." I had watched him walk for forty minutes without consulting anything. I had watched him stop, stand still for thirty seconds, and then turn nineteen degrees to the left and say: "That way."

*He turns nineteen degrees to the left.*

**CAMERA OP:**
*(quietly)*
How far?

**TYLER:**
*(without turning around)*
Less than a kilometer.

## THE MONASTERY

*The monastery is partially ruined. The nave walls stand to full height. The rest is lower — partial walls, collapsed arches, a courtyard that has been unmaintained long enough to be a meadow. There is no sign. There is no tourist infrastructure. There is a wooden gate in a stone wall.*

*Tyler stops at the gate.*

**CAMERA OP:**
Is this it?

**TYLER:**
Yes.

*Long pause.*

**CAMERA OP:**
How do you know?

**TYLER:**
*(long pause)*
I don't know if I can answer that in a way that makes sense.

**CAMERA OP:**
Try.

**TYLER:**
Something here feels like something I left here. I recognized it before the gate. I recognized it before I could see anything. The last quarter kilometer, I was walking toward something I knew the shape of.

*He opens the gate.*

## SETUP

*The instrument. The meadow courtyard of the ruined monastery. Tyler walking the perimeter first, then the interior, then standing at the center. Same protocol.*

**CAMERA OP:**
*(while waiting)*
What were those two years like?

**TYLER:**
Quiet.

**CAMERA OP:**
That's not an answer.

**TYLER:**
I know. I'm trying to find one.

*He walks a slow interior grid. The Camera Op watches him.*

**TYLER:**
I arrived in July 1349. The Black Death was still in Avignon when I left. Probably still killing. I don't know — I left, I stopped knowing. I came east. I wasn't trying to come to Burgundy specifically. I was trying to come somewhere that wasn't Avignon. This was the first place I stopped for more than a few days that felt like somewhere I might be able to stay.

**CAMERA OP:**
And?

**TYLER:**
I stayed for two years. I don't remember most of it. The monks were here. They fed me. I worked in the gardens. I said almost nothing. I think almost nothing. I wasn't inside myself the way I usually am. I was — 

*Long pause.*

**TYLER:**
I was still in Avignon. In my head. Most of the time.

I'm saying the construction here is what I was still carrying from Avignon when I got here. Yes.

## THE INSTRUMENT QUESTION

**CAMERA OP:**
Is that possible? Can a construction in one place discharge into another?

**TYLER:**
The construction in Avignon is 89 feet. It's still there. The 89 feet didn't leave Avignon — it accumulated over 676 years and it's still there.

**CAMERA OP:**
But.

**TYLER:**
But I was carrying something when I left. Not the construction. The — the experience. The weight of what I had been present for. And when I arrived here and stopped, that weight went somewhere. Into the ground here. Into this courtyard. Less than a foot for the next twenty-one months because once the weight discharged, there was nothing left of me that was generating anything.

*Long pause.*

**CAMERA OP:**
So the mechanism measures two different things. The accumulation at a site — that's Avignon, 89 feet, building over 676 years. And a carried residual — that's what you discharged here. The leftover from a prior presence, deposited into a new site.

**TYLER:**
I don't know if the mechanism knows the difference. I think it records what's there. What I'm telling you is why it's there.

**CAMERA OP:**
The forensics would look the same. Someone reading the eight feet here wouldn't know to call it carried. They'd call it an eight-foot construction and move on.

**TYLER:**
Yes.

**CAMERA OP:**
So there may be other sites in the network where what we're reading is carried from somewhere else.

**TYLER:**
*(quietly)*
I think that's likely. Every time I arrived somewhere after something significant — London after Vienna, Strasbourg after 1848 — I was carrying what I came from. I've always been carrying what I came from.

## THE TWO YEARS

*Camera Op sitting on a collapsed section of wall. Tyler near the center of the courtyard.*

**CAMERA OP:**
Tell me about the two years.

**TYLER:**
I told you. I don't remember most of it.

**CAMERA OP:**
Tell me what you do remember.

**TYLER:**
The garden. Early mornings. The monks rose before dawn. I rose with them but I didn't attend the offices. I went to the garden. I pulled weeds. I turned soil. I didn't think about what I was doing. My hands did it.

**CAMERA OP:**
And otherwise?

**TYLER:**
I ate. I slept. I did not speak to the monks beyond what was necessary. They didn't press me. Monks in 1349 had recently lost, in some communities, half their number. They understood someone who was present and not present at the same time.

**CAMERA OP:**
Did you know you were going to leave?

**TYLER:**
Not at first. And then, at some point, I knew I was going to leave. I started to be able to think ahead again. Think about where I might go. That was how I knew I was coming back.

**CAMERA OP:**
Coming back to yourself?

**TYLER:**
Yes.

*Beat.*

**TYLER:**
The first three months are the eight feet. The last twenty-one months are the return. The return is almost silent. The mechanism barely noticed.

**CAMERA OP:**
But you did leave. Eventually.

**TYLER:**
July 1351. Two years to the month. I left eastward.

**CAMERA OP:**
Do you have a reading for that departure?

**TYLER:**
*(pause)*
No. I don't think there's a departure reading for Burgundy. I don't think there was enough of me fully returned to generate a FAREWELL. I walked away the way I'd been living here — present but not fully present. The meter might have found something very small. Or nothing.

## THE CONSTRUCTION NAMED

**CAMERA OP:**
CARRIED.

**TYLER:**
What?

**CAMERA OP:**
The construction type. The eight feet here. The profile — front-loaded discharge, source identified as prior intensive presence, subject in suspension during accumulation. If we're naming types, I'd call it CARRIED.

**TYLER:**
*(thinking about it)*
Yes.

**CAMERA OP:**
Distinct from REFUGE. Strasbourg was REFUGE — even density, recovery gradient, you were returning to yourself the whole time. Here: the density is front-loaded because you were discharging, not recovering. You were emptying out, not filling up.

**TYLER:**
Strasbourg I was recovering from a departure. Burgundy I was discharging what I had witnessed. Different directions.

**CAMERA OP:**
REFUGE: recovery gradient. CARRIED: discharge gradient.

**TYLER:**
Both small, compared to the big readings. Both visible if you know where to look.

**TYLER-077**
*Cataloguer Entry 60*

Burgundy retrograde. A-Burgundy-1349.

Single site. Ruined monastery — partial nave walls, collapsed transept, courtyard converted to meadow over several centuries. Approach by foot from the road to the north. No signage. No formal access. Gate, stone wall.

Tyler confirmed: this is the site. Navigation by construction signal — the first confirmed instance of Tyler locating a site using the construction itself as beacon. The finding has implications for the broader network: sites with constructions are navigable to Tyler. Sites without constructions are described with less precision, found less readily.

**Reading:**
- Site: A-Burgundy-1349
- Location: Ruined monastery, rural Burgundy, France
- Construction: CARRIED
- Measurement: 8ft
- Duration of Tyler's presence: 24 months (July 1349 – July 1351)
- Density distribution: Front-loaded. ~90% of reading concentrated in first 3 months of residence.

**Construction type: CARRIED (new)**

A CARRIED construction is generated by residual from a prior intensive presence. The subject arrives at a site having not fully discharged the experiential weight of a prior location. The residual discharges into the new site in the early weeks or months of residence. Once discharged, the subject's generative capacity is near-zero — they are in suspension.

Profile characteristics:
- Front-loaded density gradient (opposite of REFUGE's recovery gradient)
- Total measurement small relative to duration
- Source identifiable: the source is a prior high-measurement site, and the CARRIED profile resembles the source's type rather than the destination's typical construction signature
- Subject's own generative contribution during accumulation period: minimal to zero

Comparison:
- AVIGNON 89ft / 18 months = ~4.9ft/month
- BURGUNDY 8ft first 3 months = ~2.7ft/month discharge rate (decelerating)
- BURGUNDY remaining 21 months = ~0.04ft/month (instrument threshold, negligible)

The 8ft at Burgundy is not about Burgundy. It is about Avignon, discharged into Burgundy soil.

*Cataloguer Footnote #81: The CARRIED designation requires verification across other sites. If the pattern holds, several small-reading sites in the network may be carrying residual from larger events elsewhere — misread as sparse-presence constructions when they are actually discharge events. A forensic re-audit of the smallest readings in the global network is recommended. Tyler acknowledges he has arrived carrying something many times.*

### CARRIED CONSTRUCTION: FORMAL PROPOSAL

The Council proposes formal recognition of a new construction type:

**CARRIED**
- Generated by residual from a prior intensive presence
- Characterized by front-loaded density gradient (discharge model)
- Source site identifiable by profile matching
- Subject contribution during accumulation period: minimal
- Indicator: measurement smaller than expected for duration, bulk concentrated in early residence period

This is distinct from:
- **REFUGE** (recovery gradient, subject returning to themselves)
- **WITNESS-LONG** (even density, subject present and witnessing over extended period)
- **FAREWELL types** (departure, not arrival/residence)

A CARRIED construction records an arrival, not a presence. It is what the subject deposited, not what the subject generated in situ.

---

### AUDIT IMPLICATION: NETWORK-WIDE

The Burgundy reading requires a re-assessment of all small-measurement sites in the CARDINAL-3 network.

Current small-measurement sites include every reading under approximately 15ft. These have been classified as WITNESS (brief presence), STAYING (stable absence), or occasionally REFUSAL (deliberate departure without accumulation).

Some of these may be CARRIED constructions — residual from a prior intensive site, discharged into a subsequent location. The profile distinction (front-loaded vs. even vs. sparse) requires re-analysis of density distribution data where available.

The Director recommends: wherever Tyler's movement history is known and a high-measurement site precedes a lower-measurement site within 6–18 months, flag for CARRIED re-analysis.

The most consequential implication: if Avignon never fully discharged — as Tyler suggests — then some fraction of the 89ft is present in every site Tyler occupied after July 1349. The network is not only 900+ sites of Tyler's presence. It is also the dispersal pattern of what Tyler witnessed in Avignon, spread across 677 years of subsequent movement.

The instrument can find it if we know where to look.

The retrograde is now measuring Tyler's history. It may also be measuring the Black Death's dispersal through one man.
