### COLD OPEN

**[INTERIOR — BLOOMSBURY FLAT. DAY 283. 5:47 AM.]**

*[The hum, low under the early-morning sound of London. The table is covered: the CARDINAL-3 London file opened and sorted into stacks, index cards arranged in a row from left to right. The Exchange Student has been here since before four AM. The Camera Op is at the doorway, camera running.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(without looking up)* "You don't have to stand in the doorway."

**CAMERA OP:** "I'm documenting."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You're looking at the index cards."

**CAMERA OP:** "That too."

*[Tyler is at the window. He has been there since five o'clock. He is watching the street. He knew the Exchange Student would do this analysis. He left her alone with it. He waited.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "I sorted the London file chronologically. Every site in the CARDINAL-3 notebook where Tyler has a notation with a date. The notation might be short — just a street and a year, sometimes just a month and a year — but where there's a date, I sorted it." She picks up the leftmost card in the row. "The earliest date in the London file."

*[She places it separate from the others.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "November 1553."

*[Silence. Tyler does not turn from the window.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The next earliest: April 1592. July 1602. November 14, 1623. 1648. 1652." She sets them down in sequence, left to right. "Eight sites in the London file have notation dates that predate 1620. Before the Cairngorms archive began its London documentation."

*[The Camera Op moves closer to the table, filming the row of cards.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Before anyone was watching."

*[Tyler turns from the window. He looks at the row. He does not move toward the table yet.]*

**TYLER:** "How many of those eight are already on the measurement list?"

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Four confirmed — A-295 Bankside, A-297 Borough Market, A-301 London Bridge, and A-292 Wood Street. Four more, unvisited." She touches the November 1553 card. "The oldest is unvisited."

*[Tyler crosses to the table. He picks up the card. He reads the address. He is careful.]*

**TYLER:** *(quietly)* "Watling Street."

*[He sets the card back down. He takes the coin from his right-hand pocket — not flipping it, just holding it in his palm with the 1901 face up. The Camera Op catches this. He times it to the camera, as he always does. The camera catches the motion; it is, as always, slightly late for the face.]*

**TYLER:** "We don't have a word for this."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Not yet."

**TYLER:** "But we should."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Yes."

*[A pause. The Exchange Student picks up her pen. She pulls the blank card from the front of the row. She writes a word.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The constructions that started before the archive was keeping the record. Before you knew what you were building. Before anyone knew to watch." She turns the card so Tyler can read it.

*[One word: PRIOR.]*

**TYLER:** "That's accurate."

*[The Camera Op reads it over the Exchange Student's shoulder. She writes the word in her notebook without speaking.]*

### ACT ONE — THE SCOPE

**[INTERIOR — BLOOMSBURY FLAT. MORNING. DAY 283.]**

*[The flat, morning light now. Tyler is at the table. The Exchange Student pours tea. The Camera Op is filming from the chair near the door.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Eight PRIOR sites. The range is 1553 to 1618. The earliest by sixty-one years is Watling Street." She sits. "The other three unvisited are: Moorgate; Aldersgate Street; and a section of Cheapside west of A-292, near Bread Street. Different address, different period."

**TYLER:** "I know those streets."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "I know you do."

*[Tyler picks up the November 1553 card.]*

**TYLER:** *(reading what he wrote)* "November 1553. The crowd stood in Watling Street to hear the bells. I stood with them. I did not know what I was doing."

*[He sets the card down.]*

**TYLER:** "I wrote that sentence often in those years. I didn't know what I was doing, but I knew something had happened. The notation was the only vocabulary I had."

**CAMERA OP:** *(from behind the lens)* "And the PRIOR construction built anyway."

**TYLER:** "And the construction built anyway."

*[He pockets the coin.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The Cairngorms archive's first London documentation begins approximately 1620. TYLER-001 — their first London entry — is a Cheapside sighting, around that year. Before that: nothing in the archive. Not because you weren't in London. Because no one had opened the London file yet."

**TYLER:** "The record starts when someone decides to keep one."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Yes."

**TYLER:** "The constructions start earlier."

*[He picks up his jacket from the back of the chair.]*

**TYLER:** "Watling Street is what, twenty minutes?"

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Fifteen, walking through the City."

**TYLER:** "Then we're walking through the City."

---

**[EXTERIOR — LONDON STREETS. MORNING. DAY 283.]**

*[The three of them walk. The Camera Op is slightly behind, filming the morning light as it opens across the City. The hum follows, continuous, under the street noise. Nobody mentions it.]*

*[Tyler carries the leather journal.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(walking)* "Eight PRIOR sites changes the shape of what we're auditing. We've been treating 1620 as the London start date. The actual start date for the London constructions is at minimum 1553. Probably earlier."

**TYLER:** "Probably."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "There could be London constructions that predate even the leather journal — sites that started before you wrote anything down."

**TYLER:** *(after a moment)* "There could be."

*[Carefully. Not evasively. Carefully.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "How do we find those?"

**TYLER:** "I don't know yet." He pauses. "We work the list we have. Then we see what the list reveals."

### ACT TWO — A-304

**[EXTERIOR — WATLING STREET, CITY OF LONDON. MID-MORNING. DAY 283.]**

*[Watling Street is narrow, following the line of the ancient Roman road through the City. Modern glass buildings rise on either side; the street is old. St. Paul's Cathedral is visible at the western end. The Camera Op stops at the corner and holds the frame.]*

*[The hum here is heavier. The Exchange Student feels it and stops walking. Tyler does not stop. He walks to the center of the street and stands, facing east, and is still.]*

**TYLER:** "Here."

*[The Camera Op has the frame on him. The Exchange Student opens the measuring equipment.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "I'll start from where you're standing and measure outward."

**TYLER:** "You know how."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "I've been watching you do it for two seasons."

*[She begins walking outward from him, measuring rod in hand, moving carefully. The Camera Op keeps the frame on Tyler.]*

*[Tyler opens the leather journal to the November 1553 entry. He reads it to himself first. The Camera Op can see him reading but cannot hear the words yet.]*

*[The Exchange Student reaches the edge and marks it. She calls back: "107 feet."]*

*[Tyler looks up from the journal. He looks at the mark, then at the journal page, then at the street around him.]*

**TYLER:** "107 feet. From November 1553."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "473 years."

*[A pause. He closes the journal. He opens it again to the same page, finds the entry, and holds the journal out toward the Camera Op.]*

**TYLER:** *(reading aloud, for the camera, for the record)* "November 1553. Watling Street. The crowd stood in Watling Street to hear the bells of St. Paul's after the announcement. I stood with them. I did not know what I was doing. I knew that the crowd was holding something in common and that the moment would not repeat. I stood in it with them. That is what I did. I did not know what I was doing."

*[He closes the journal.]*

*[The Camera Op holds the frame. She is at the edge of the construction — the threshold. She finds thresholds. She does not move from this one.]*

**CAMERA OP:** *(very quietly)* "You stood with them."

**TYLER:** "I stood with them."

*[The hum. The street. The geometry of 107 feet, running since November 1553.]*

*[The Exchange Student marks A-304 on the map: PRIOR. UNINTENTIONAL. 107FT. NOVEMBER 1553. She draws the PRIOR symbol — a circle with its center point slightly off-center, like a notation begun just before the line was drawn.]*

### ACT THREE — THE PRIOR SCOPE

**[EXTERIOR — ST. PAUL'S CATHEDRAL FORECOURT. AFTERNOON. DAY 283.]**

*[They have been in the City for several hours. Tyler and the Camera Op are on a bench near the Cathedral's west face. The Exchange Student is photographing the new map symbol.]*

*[Tyler is looking at the Cathedral. The hum, lower here, under the tourist traffic.]*

**CAMERA OP:** "What was the announcement?"

**TYLER:** *(a pause)* "It doesn't matter for the construction. The construction doesn't know the announcement. It knows that a crowd stood in a narrow street and held the same thing in common, and I was one of them, paying attention in my specific way."

**CAMERA OP:** "But you know what it was."

**TYLER:** *(looking at the Cathedral, carefully)* "I know what it was."

*[He does not say it. She does not ask again. Both of these are correct.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(returning)* "The PRIOR category adds a layer we hadn't anticipated. We've been measuring what you built. PRIOR is what you built before anyone had the framework to see it — including you."

**TYLER:** "Before I was keeping the measurement."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You wrote: I did not know what I was doing."

**TYLER:** "I wrote that sentence four times in the first hundred pages of the leather journal. Then fewer. Then I stopped, because I'd started to know."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "When did you start to know?"

*[A long pause. Tyler looks at the Cathedral. He is thinking about an honest answer.]*

**TYLER:** "In layers. The first layer was noticing that something had happened at a location after I'd left. The second layer was noticing that I was doing something while I was still there. The third layer was understanding the word for it." He pauses. "The constructions didn't wait for the third layer. They started at the first layer. Maybe before the first layer."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The Borough Market construction. April 1592. You wrote: *I knew something had happened. That was all I had.*"

**TYLER:** "The first layer."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Watling Street. November 1553. *I did not know what I was doing.*"

**TYLER:** "Before the first layer."

*[He looks at the Cathedral. The bells, on the hour, quietly.]*

**TYLER:** "PRIOR is the right word. Before the record. Before the vocabulary. Before any of it." He pauses. "The constructions were running anyway."

---

**[EXTERIOR — WATLING STREET, AT DUSK. DAY 283.]**

*[Tyler returns to A-304 alone. The Camera Op is there, at the threshold, filming. She has stayed the full day.]*

*[He walks up to the center of the street, to the same point. He takes the coin out — reads it — puts it back. The camera catches the motion and is slightly late for the face.]*

**TYLER:** "You stayed."

**CAMERA OP:** "Someone should be here for the full day."

**TYLER:** "It's been running for 473 years. You can leave."

**CAMERA OP:** "I know I can leave."

*[He looks at her. The smile: slightly too knowing, not specifying what it knows. Two seconds maximum toward the camera. Then he looks at the street.]*

**TYLER:** "The PRIOR sites are the oldest part of the record. The record didn't know they were there. I mostly didn't know they were there." He pauses. "You knew before I said it that Borough Market was PRIOR. You wrote *I was here for fish.* You understood that the ledger needed to be complete."

**CAMERA OP:** *(quietly)* "I'm a witness. Witnesses complete the record."

*[The hum. The City at dusk. The light on Watling Street.]*

**TYLER:** "Yes. That's what witnesses do."

*[He turns and walks toward the Cathedral. The Camera Op holds the frame on the empty center of the street where he was standing: 107 feet of construction, running since November 1553. The bells of St. Paul's, on the half hour, reaching them both.]*
