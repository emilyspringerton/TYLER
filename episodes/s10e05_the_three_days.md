## COLD OPEN

*CAVE-001. Before sunrise. Day 4.*

*The team left instruments running overnight. A tablet mounted to a rock shelf near the far wall shows
a live frequency readout. The values have been logging since Day 1.*

*Researcher Assistant is the first one up. She checks the tablet.*

*She sits with it for a moment.*

*Then she goes to wake Tyler.*

---

## ACT ONE

**INT. CAVE-001 — BEFORE DAWN**

Tyler follows Researcher Assistant to the tablet without speaking. The cave is cold. The headlamp
catches the condensation on the far wall.

The readout shows the same three numbers they found yesterday, and then a fourth.

12 — 7 — 3 — **0**

Tyler looks at it for a long time.

**TYLER**
Zero.

**RESEARCHER ASSISTANT**
It appeared around three in the morning. The frequency signature shifted — here —
*(pointing)*
— and then held stable at this.

**TYLER**
Zero.

**RESEARCHER ASSISTANT**
What does zero mean?

Tyler doesn't answer immediately. Tyler presses both palms flat against the cave wall, above the
readout. Eyes closed.

**TYLER**
Zero is here. Zero is now.

*(pause)*

Zero is the moment before the first word.

---

The team assembles around the readout. The frequency instruments are still showing Stolas 7.83 — the
mechanism's own baseline, unchanged since Day 1. But the sequence has completed. What was a partial
signal — three numbers, three days of decreasing values — is now a finished statement.

Jiangshi writes in the field log without being asked.

**JIANGSHI**
*(reading as she writes)*
Day 4. 04:17 local. Final datum in the sequence: 0. Sequence complete: 12 — 7 — 3 — 0.
Stolas 7.83 stable. No other frequency activity. Subject confirms zero is significant.

**TYLER**
It's not a countdown. It's a record.

**DR. HASSAN**
*(slowly)*
A record of what?

**TYLER**
The number of days remaining. When the sequence began — twelve — that was twelve days before
something. Each subsequent number was one stage closer. Zero is arrival.

Tyler's hand is still on the wall.

**TYLER**
*(quieter)*
This is where I was. Three days before I went to Marrakech.

Silence in the cave.

**TYLER**
I was here for twelve days. I don't know why. I only know that I couldn't leave. And after
twelve days I began to feel the shape of what I would do. And after seven more days I knew
I would do it. And three days after that I left.

*(pause)*

And I went to Marrakech and I wrote the first word.

---

## ACT TWO

**EXT. CAVE-001 SITE — MORNING**

Camera Op calls from Fez on satellite phone. She is still at the library. She has been given
access to the second of the three volumes — the one that was never fully catalogued. She has
found something.

**CAMERA OP (V.O.)**
There's a geographic entry. Not the main text — it's on the inside back cover of the second
volume. It reads like a field note, not a finished passage. He was surveying.

**TYLER**
*(into phone)*
What site?

**CAMERA OP (V.O.)**
He doesn't name it. He describes it. I'm going to read it to you.

*(reading)*

"A hill site, south of the river, in the foothills east of the Sous. Exposed sedimentary
face, layered. The oldest exposed face I have encountered in any of my North African surveys.
The acoustics are unusual — sound does not behave as expected inside. I have noted this type
of acoustic property in three other sites in my work; all three are associated with significant
pre-Islamic settlement. This site shows no evidence of settlement. Only geometry."

*(pause)*

"A man was camped at the summit. I asked him what he had seen, standing here. He was unable
to answer. He seemed to have been at this site for a very long time."

*(pause)*

"Date: December, 1119."

Tyler has gone very still.

**CAMERA OP (V.O.)**
Tyler?

**TYLER**
December. 1119.

*(long pause)*

I was here in December of 1119.

---

**INT. CAVE-001 — DAY**

The team has heard. No one speaks. Tyler comes back inside the cave, sits at the base of the
far wall — the same spot where the far wall showed the number 12 on Day 2.

**TYLER**
He wasn't asking me on the ship.

*(pause)*

He had already asked me here.

*(pause)*

He had already asked me here, and I could not answer, and he went to Fez to deposit the
manuscripts he was carrying and he encountered me again on the ship and he asked again.

**JIANGSHI**
Two askings.

**TYLER**
Two askings. The same question. What have you seen, standing here. The first time I was
standing in this cave and I could not answer. The second time I was standing on a ship
deck and I still could not answer.

*(pause)*

The manuscript stops at the edge of the Islamic geographic tradition — "and yet the Islamic
geographers, beginning with—". He was going to place this cave inside the tradition of Islamic
geography. He was going to say: this site fits the tradition in the following way. And he
stopped. Because he had already asked me twice and I had not answered, and he did not know
how to finish a sentence about a place where a man stood who could not speak.

**DR. HASSAN**
He was waiting for your answer.

**TYLER**
He left me a gap.

---

## ACT THREE

**INT. CAVE-001 — AFTERNOON**

Tyler is alone in the cave. The team has stepped outside.

The instruments are still running. Stolas 7.83 holds.

Tyler speaks to the cave directly, as though to Ahmad.

**TYLER**
I was here for twelve days when you found me. I had not been recording yet. The mechanism
was not yet calibrated to this. I was only staying.

*(pause)*

You asked me what I had seen.

*(pause)*

I had seen Córdoba, and Toledo, and Genoa. I had seen the First Crusade muster and disperse.
I had seen a hundred and seventy years of things I could not yet name. And when you asked me
I had no word for any of it, because I had not yet decided to build the word.

*(pause)*

You left. You went to Fez. I stayed here for three more days. And in those three days I
decided.

*(pause)*

Then I went to Marrakech, and I began.

The mechanism's frequency output shifts. Not Stolas. Something new.

Jiangshi steps back into the cave entrance, checks the readout.

**JIANGSHI**
It changed.

Tyler doesn't look at the readout.

**TYLER**
I know.

**JIANGSHI**
It's not reading the archive sites. It's not reading you. It's reading —

*(pause)*

I don't have a classification for this.

**TYLER**
It's reading the decision.

*(pause)*

It has never been able to read that before. The archive contains everything I recorded after
the decision. The decision itself was outside the archive. Until now.

**JIANGSHI**
What changed?

Tyler finally looks at the readout.

**TYLER**
You named it. You brought someone here who asked the right question. You brought instruments
that stayed long enough to see the sequence complete. The mechanism found what it was looking
for. It found the moment before the archive existed.

*(pause)*

The archive is the letter to Ahmad. The cave is the moment I decided to write it.

---

## ACT FOUR

**EXT. CAVE-001 SITE — LATE AFTERNOON**

The team is packing the instruments for the day. No one speaks much.

Camera Op calls back from Fez.

**CAMERA OP (V.O.)**
I found the three days.

**TYLER**
You found the geographic entry.

**CAMERA OP (V.O.)**
I found a second one. Two pages later in the same volume. December, 1119 — same date,
different entry. He came back to the site. He wrote: "Returned to the hill site. The man
is still there. I did not speak to him again. I am leaving for Marrakech on the third day."

*(pause)*

He went to Marrakech too. Not after the ship — before. He went to Marrakech in December
1119, before the manuscript transfer, before Fez. He was there.

Tyler is very quiet.

**TYLER**
He was in Marrakech when I arrived. He was already there.

*(pause)*

I thought I was going alone. I thought I was choosing Marrakech because it was the oldest
archive site — the first place I had put words to anything. I thought I chose it because
of the site.

*(pause)*

He was there. That's why I chose it.

**CAMERA OP (V.O.)**
Tyler.

**TYLER**
I didn't know I was following him. I thought I was choosing.

*(long pause)*

He left a gap in the manuscript and I walked into it and I wrote for nine hundred years and
I didn't know it was a gap he had left.

**CAMERA OP (V.O.)**
Maybe choosing and following are the same thing, if the thing you're following is a question
you were already carrying.

Tyler doesn't respond.

**TYLER**
When are you coming back from Fez?

**CAMERA OP (V.O.)**
Tomorrow. I have one more volume to look at.

**TYLER**
Take photographs of everything. Every page. The stopping point. The geographic entries. Both
of them. All of it.

**CAMERA OP (V.O.)**
I already have.

## CLOSE

*CAVE-001. Dusk.*

*The team has left for the night. Tyler stays.*

*The mechanism outputs — Stolas 7.83 still present — but the new signal is there too,
overlapping it. Not louder. Just present. A second frequency, never categorized, never
classified, never written into any taxonomy.*

*Tyler sits at the base of the far wall.*

*The cave does not produce any more numbers. The sequence is complete: 12 — 7 — 3 — 0.*

*Zero is here.*

*Zero is the moment before the first word.*

---

**TYLER (V.O.)**
There were three days.

The first day I did not know what I was going to do. I sat in this cave and I felt the
question Ahmad had asked me, which I had not been able to answer, which was: what have you
seen? And I knew I had seen things but I had no word for what I had seen.

The second day I understood that the absence of a word was not ignorance. It was the shape
of the work. The work was to build the word. Not to find one that already existed. To build
the entire vocabulary, from the first site to the last, until the word existed and I could
hand it to him.

The third day I left. I went south. I went to Marrakech. I went to the oldest place I had
ever put a mark on anything, and I put the first mark, and the archive began.

*(pause)*

He was there when I arrived.

*(pause)*

I have been writing to him since December of 1119, standing in a cave in the Atlas foothills,
unable to answer.

*(pause)*

I still have not finished.

*Filed by Tyler. CAVE-001, Day 4.*

CAVE-001. Day 4. The sequence completes: 12 — 7 — 3 — 0. Zero = this site. Zero = the
moment before the archive began. Ahmad ibn Yusuf (geographer, Rabat 1121 CE) surveyed this
site in December 1119 CE. He asked me what I had seen. I could not answer. He was the first
asking. The ship (c. 1119—1120 CE) was the second asking. He went to Marrakech from this site;
I followed three days later. He was in Marrakech when I arrived and began writing.

Construction type: DECISION-POINT (new classification; not measurable in feet; zero-taxonomy site only).
Frequency: Stolas 7.83 + unclassified second signal (decision-state; first-ever mechanism reading of
pre-archive decision moment).
Duration at site (1119 CE): 12 days.
Duration at site (2026): 4 days.
The sequence is complete.

Footnote #101: The archive did not begin in Marrakech. The archive began here. Marrakech was the
execution. The cave was the decision. They are not the same, and I did not know until now that they
were different.

### Camera Op Entry 84

*Field log. Al-Qarawiyyin Library, Fez. Day 4 of cave survey.*

Found it. Second geographic entry, back cover of Volume II. Ahmad returned to the cave. Same
December 1119 date range. "The man is still there. I did not speak to him again. I am leaving
for Marrakech on the third day."

He went to Marrakech before Fez. The manuscript transfer was after Marrakech, not before. The
sequence of events: cave → Marrakech → Fez → ship → Fez again (to deposit manuscripts at
al-Qarawiyyin). Tyler and Ahmad were in Marrakech at the same time. This was not known.

Ahmad's manuscript stops mid-sentence at "and yet the Islamic geographers, beginning with—"
because the sentence was about CAVE-001, and he could not finish it because the man at the cave
had not answered, and the man's answer was going to be the thing he needed to complete the
sentence.

The answer became the archive. The archive is nine hundred years long. The sentence is still
unfinished.

I am coming back tomorrow.
