## COLD OPEN

The hallway smells like a building that has stopped pretending to be used. Not abandonment — not yet — but the particular quality of a space that knows its current function is temporary.

Detroit Receiving Hospital, 2026. Most of the building is closed. The east wing is accessible through a permission Tyler got through Bacon, who knows someone on the conversion committee, who owed Bacon a long-standing debt from 1987 that neither of them ever discussed directly.

The Camera Op is filming the hallway. She's been in hospitals before. She's filming differently here — slower pans, more negative space. The camera knows this is a different kind of site.

## EPISODE STRUCTURE

**Day 94, morning — Ward C**

The doors open easily. The ward is large: twelve beds, all removed, the frames still showing their shadows on the floor where the linoleum was protected from decades of light. The windows face east. At 9 AM, the light comes in at a low angle.

Tyler walks to the center of the room. He stands there.

The Camera Op stays near the door. She knows when to film from a distance.

After two minutes:

**TYLER:** "Yes. It's here."

He doesn't say what. He doesn't need to.

**EMILY:** *(from the doorway, notebook open)* "How strong?"

**TYLER:** "Strong. Stronger than the room at Bacon's before I started working on it. The 1918 event was large. The ward held a lot of it."

He walks the perimeter of the room slowly, the way he walks Bacon's building — reading what the space holds, checking corners, pausing at the east windows.

**TYLER:** "The difference here is the scale. The room at Bacon's was small — one event, well-contained, the container had 400 years to settle. This ward held hundreds of people. The event isn't one thing. It's layered."

**EMILY:** "Does that change the construction?"

**TYLER:** "It changes the time required."

---

**Day 94, late morning — the Exchange Student maps under pressure**

The Exchange Student has her laptop open on a folding table she brought. She's been mapping since they arrived.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "I have a preliminary dependency scan. The Stolas frequency reads consistently throughout the ward — the building is old enough and the events were significant enough that it's been running continuously since 1918. That's the good news."

She turns the laptop.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The constraint is the conversion timeline. The construction at Bacon's took six days at a site Tyler had been working in for months, with unlimited access and four resonant witnesses. Here we have 82 days, partial access, and three confirmed resonant witnesses. The fourth—" she pauses "—I'm still calibrating to this space."

**EMILY:** "How long does calibration take?"

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "At Bacon's it happened through the dependency mapping. I was calibrated by the time I entered the room. Here I'm mapping actively, so—" she looks at the ward "—I think I'm calibrating now. I can feel what this room is holding."

She writes a line in her notebook: *calibrating — Day 94.*

**EMILY:** "That's four."

---

**Day 94, midday — what the construction needs to be different**

Tyler comes back to the folding table at noon. He looks at the Exchange Student's dependency map.

**TYLER:** "Two things are different here. First, the scale — the ward is larger, the event is layered, the construction requires more surface coverage. Not just a central point. The whole room needs to be addressed."

**EMILY:** "The Subscriber's notation covered one room at Bacon's. Do we need more notation?"

**TYLER:** "The Subscriber's notation is the instruction set. What I need to do here is apply it to a larger container. The notation doesn't change — the application does."

He looks at the east windows.

**TYLER:** "Second: we don't own this building. When the conversion starts, this room becomes something else. The construction needs to complete before the container changes. And it needs to be robust enough that the conversion doesn't disrupt it."

**EMILY:** "Is that possible?"

**TYLER:** "I think so. Bacon's building has been renovated four times since 1623. The room behind the table was rerouted once, replastered twice, the door was moved in 1887. The Witness Room stayed intact through all of it. The event doesn't care about the wallpaper."

He pauses.

**TYLER:** "But the frequencies need to be set deeply enough that the construction work doesn't knock them loose. That's what the time is for."

---

**Day 94, afternoon — what the Camera Op films**

The Camera Op has been moving through the ward all afternoon. At 3 PM she stops at a specific spot near the east windows — one of the original bed positions, marked by the shadow on the linoleum floor.

She stands there for a moment. She doesn't lower the camera.

**CAMERA OP:** *(to Tyler, across the room)* "There's something specific here. This position."

Tyler walks over. He looks at the floor shadow. At the window angle. At the Camera Op.

**TYLER:** "Yes."

**CAMERA OP:** "What happened here?"

**TYLER:** "Someone stayed. Through the end of 1918, into 1919. Everyone else in this ward died or left. One person stayed for a different reason — they were a nurse, not a patient. They witnessed the whole thing from approximately this position. They were the witness for this ward."

The Camera Op is very still.

**CAMERA OP:** "Like me."

**TYLER:** "Yes."

She films the shadow on the floor for a long time. This is a different kind of footage from anything she's shot before: she's filming her own professional ancestor, invisible, standing in the same position for the same function in a different century.

---

**Day 94, late afternoon — the construction begins**

Tyler goes to the center of the ward. He takes out the coin. Not to look at it — he knows it by touch. He stands in the middle of the layered event and begins to work.

What this looks like from outside: a man standing quietly in a large empty room. What the Camera Op knows how to capture: the way the light sits differently near him when he's working, the way the room sounds different, the particular quality of the silence that is not emptiness.

She films from the doorway.

She films from the window.

She films from the nurse's position by the east wall.

At 5 PM Tyler stops. He hasn't moved more than a few feet in an hour.

**TYLER:** "Day one. The room knows what's being built now."

**EMILY:** "How many days?"

**TYLER:** "More than six. This one will take longer." He looks at the ward. "Forty days. Maybe forty-five."

Emily writes it in the Witness Room Network map: *Detroit Receiving — construction active, Day 94. Estimated completion: Day 134–139. Conversion deadline: Day 176.*

Thirty-seven day buffer. Not comfortable, but workable.
