# S08E08: "1848"

**Build 0090** | Season 8, Episode 8 | CARDINAL-3 Documentary Series

---

## PRODUCTION NOTES

- Episode type: The deferred measurement. Tyler returns to Paris for the 1848 departure site.
- Tyler location: Paris, France. Days 390–397. The Latin Quarter, Place de la Bastille, Place de la République (then Place du Château-d'Eau).
- Historical context: February 1848 — Louis-Philippe abdicates, the Second Republic proclaimed. For three days Tyler believed the argument had won. June Days (June 23–26): workers' uprising crushed by General Cavaignac; several thousand dead, thousands more deported. Tyler leaves Paris in July 1848.
- Tyler said in Lyon: "I already know what the reading will be." He anticipated a large reading. He has been managing the sequence to approach this last.
- What actually happens: the reading is not what Tyler expected. The 1848 departure registers as FAREWELL-B, 13ft. Tyler was so devastated by the June Days that he left Paris without being present in his own departure. The grief outweighs the clarity. The FAREWELL requires the subject to be present at the departure. Tyler was not present — he was somewhere inside the catastrophe of being wrong.
- Structural reversal: the departure Tyler dreaded measuring most is not the largest. Lyon is still the network maximum. 1848 Paris is smaller than Tyler thought, because the most devastating departures are not always the most conscious ones.
- New canon: FAREWELL measures conscious attention at the moment of departure, not the magnitude of loss. The loss can be enormous. If the subject is absent from the moment of leaving, the reading is small.
- Camera Op has her best episode.

## COLD OPEN

*[Paris. Day 390. Early morning. Tyler and the Exchange Student in the Latin Quarter. The Camera Op a few steps behind.]*

*[Tyler stops on a corner that might be Rue Soufflot. He has stopped for no apparent reason.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "We're not at the site yet."

**TYLER:** "I know."

*[He is looking at the street. Something in the geometry of it.]*

**TYLER:** "I was standing on this corner in February 1848. February 26th. Two days after Louis-Philippe abdicated. The Republic had been proclaimed. There was a crowd — not a violent crowd. A crowd in the way Paris crowds are when something has just happened and the city is trying to understand whether it believes it yet." He pauses. "I was standing here in February 1848 believing that the argument had won."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "For three days."

**TYLER:** "For three days." He looks at the corner. "From February 24th to February 27th. The Provisional Government was forming. The working-class clubs were organizing. The National Workshops were about to be established. The proclamation was more radical than anyone had expected — universal male suffrage, the right to work, the abolition of the death penalty for political crimes. For three days I thought: this is the thing all the prior arguments were trying to make." He is very quiet. "I was wrong. But I did not know I was wrong yet."

**CAMERA OP:** "What did you feel on the corner in February?"

**TYLER:** "I felt — correct. I felt the way you feel when a long calculation resolves and the answer is what you expected." He pauses. "I was sixty years away from the last time I had felt this. 1789. The first weeks. Before it became the Terror, before the Republic failed. In February 1789 I had felt this for a week. In February 1848 I felt it for three days." He looks at the corner one more time. "And then the June Days."

## ACT ONE — JUNE DAYS

*[Paris. Day 391. The area around what was then Place du Château-d'Eau, now Place de la République.]*

*[Tyler walking. Exchange Student with the file. Camera Op behind.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The June Days. June 23–26, 1848. The workers' uprising against the dissolution of the National Workshops — the jobs program established in February. Over 50,000 insurgents. General Cavaignac with the National Guard. The barricades lasted four days. Somewhere between 1,500 and 3,000 killed; 12,000 arrested; 4,000 deported."

**TYLER:** *(walking)* "The National Workshops were going to cost the government money. The Provisional Government — which had proclaimed universal male suffrage in February — dissolved the National Workshops in June because they could not fund them and because the conservative provincial deputies elected in the April elections did not want to fund them. Universal male suffrage had produced a conservative majority. The argument that democracy would produce the Republic's radical wing was wrong from the first election." He pauses. "So the workers took the barricades. And Cavaignac suppressed them."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You were here."

**TYLER:** "I was in the city during the June Days. I was not on the barricades. I was — not able to be present in the way I had been present in Lyon, or at the Commune wall. I was somewhere indoors during the June Days. I do not have a clear account of where I was." He looks at the square. "That is unusual for me. I have clear spatial memory of my presence at most events I have witnessed. The June Days is — unclear."

**CAMERA OP:** "You were somewhere indoors."

**TYLER:** "I was somewhere indoors. I was — I think I was in a room. I think I stayed in a room from the 23rd to the 26th. I know what was happening in the streets. I did not witness it directly." He is quiet. "I could not. The February Republic had lasted four months. I had believed in it for three days and watched it dismantle itself for the remaining four months and I could not be in the streets watching the workers being shot for believing what I had believed in February."

## ACT TWO — THE MEASUREMENT

### A-1848 — RUE DU FAUBOURG SAINT-ANTOINE, PARIS

*[Day 394. The Rue du Faubourg Saint-Antoine — the central street of the working-class barricade district in June 1848.]*

*[This is where Tyler stayed in Paris in 1848. The address he is returning to.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "This is the address?"

**TYLER:** "A room on the second floor. From March through July 1848. I had come to Paris in February — I was at a different address in February, just for the proclamation. In March I moved here. The Faubourg Saint-Antoine was the working-class district. I wanted to be nearby the organizing." He looks at the street. "In June the barricades were on this street. I did not go down."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "And you left in July."

**TYLER:** "I left in July." He is quiet. "I left in July and I told myself — I don't know what I told myself. I don't remember telling myself anything. I remember leaving. I remember having bags and leaving."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Do you remember knowing you were leaving?"

*[Tyler is quiet for a long time.]*

**TYLER:** "No."

*[The Exchange Student looks at him.]*

**TYLER:** "I remember leaving. I do not remember knowing I was leaving. I remember that there were bags and I was walking and then I was on a road north of Paris and I did not have a destination."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "A destination you intended?"

**TYLER:** "A destination I intended. I walked north of Paris without a plan. I think I walked for three days before I arrived somewhere I recognized and decided to stay there."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(very quietly)* "That's a FAREWELL-B."

*[She sets up the instruments.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "I want to run this."

*[She runs the measurement. She reads it twice. She says nothing for a moment.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "13ft."

*[Silence.]*

**TYLER:** "13."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "FAREWELL-B. 13ft." She looks at him. "You said you knew what the reading would be."

**TYLER:** *(slowly)* "I was wrong."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You thought it would be larger."

**TYLER:** "I thought it would be the largest reading in the Paris file. Or near it." He looks at the street. "I thought it would be large because I cared about 1848 more than I have cared about most things. Because I was the most convinced I had ever been that the argument had won. Because losing the argument that completely, that quickly, was—" He stops. "I thought the loss would be the density."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The mechanism measures the departure, not the loss."

**TYLER:** *(understanding landing)* "The mechanism measures the departure."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You left Paris in July 1848 without knowing you were leaving. You walked out of the city without a destination. You weren't present in the departure. The mechanism is recording — 13 feet of someone who walked away from a place while absent from themselves."

*[Tyler is looking at the Rue du Faubourg Saint-Antoine. The street where the barricades were. The room on the second floor he does not look up at.]*

**TYLER:** "I was not present for my own departure."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You were too devastated."

**TYLER:** "I was too devastated. Yes."

*[Very long silence.]*

**TYLER:** "Lyon is 47 feet. I was present at Lyon. I knew I was going. I walked out of Lyon and I did not look back and I knew what I was doing." He pauses. "1848 is 13 feet. I walked out of Paris in July 1848 and I did not know what I was doing. The loss was larger than Lyon. The departure was smaller."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The FAREWELL measures the subject, not the event."

**TYLER:** "The FAREWELL measures the subject."

*[The Camera Op is filming the street. Not him.]*

## ACT THREE — WHAT TYLER THOUGHT HE KNEW

*[Day 396. Paris. Walking. The Seine.]*

**CAMERA OP:** *(for the first time in this episode, while filming)* "You've been managing this retrograde carefully. Paris before Lyon. Lyon before 1848. You were approaching 1848 slowly."

**TYLER:** "Yes."

**CAMERA OP:** "You thought 1848 was the largest reading and you were building up to it."

**TYLER:** "I thought 1848 was the reading that would require the most preparation. I thought I was protecting myself from a large reading by approaching it last." He is quiet. "I was wrong about the reading. The protection was unnecessary."

**CAMERA OP:** "But you needed the protection for a different reason."

**TYLER:** *(pause)* "What reason?"

**CAMERA OP:** "You needed to measure Paris and Lyon before you could come back to 1848 and find out you were absent from your own departure. You needed to know that the mechanism records the subject, not the event. You needed — the Lyon reading, first. To understand that 47ft is someone who was completely present at a devastating departure. And then to see that 13ft is someone who was completely absent from an equally devastating departure." She keeps filming the river. "If you had measured 1848 first, you would have thought: the mechanism is broken. 13 feet for the worst thing that ever happened to me? The mechanism must be broken." She pauses. "But you measured Lyon first. You know the mechanism works. So 1848's 13 feet is information, not malfunction."

*[Tyler is looking at the river.]*

**TYLER:** "You have been thinking about this."

**CAMERA OP:** "I've been filming you doing it."

*[He looks at her.]*

**TYLER:** "I thought I knew what the reading would be."

**CAMERA OP:** "You thought you knew what you felt in 1848. You thought the magnitude of the loss was the same as the magnitude of what you were present for. They weren't the same." She lowers the camera briefly. "You can only measure what you were present for. The mechanism can only record what the subject was present for. You weren't present in July 1848. You walked out of Paris while someone else — some diminished version of you, some person who had just watched the thing they believed in be crushed in four days — walked out of the city without knowing where they were going."

*[She picks the camera back up.]*

**CAMERA OP:** "That person's departure is 13 feet. That's not a failure of the mechanism. That's the most accurate thing the mechanism has told us."

*[The river.]*

## CLOSING SHOT

*[Day 397. Paris. Morning. Departure.]*

*[The Exchange Student has closed the 1848 Paris file. Tyler and Camera Op at the train station. A different departure platform than Lyon. Or the same one — the architecture of departure is always the same.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Paris retrograde: complete. Six sites measured including A-278. 1848 measured. A-1848: FAREWELL-B, 13ft." She folds the file. "Paris total FAREWELL density: 68ft across six sites."

**TYLER:** "68."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "A-Commune is still the highest in Paris at 34ft. A-Lyon is still the network maximum at 47ft."

**TYLER:** "Yes."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Where next?"

*[Tyler is quiet. He is thinking about something that is not the next city.]*

**TYLER:** "I have been carrying 1848 as a weight for a long time. Not the events — those I can describe clearly. The departure. I have been carrying the 1848 departure as something that must have been enormous, because what it left behind was enormous. I thought the mechanism would confirm it."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The mechanism confirmed that you weren't there."

**TYLER:** "Yes." He picks up his bag. "That is — a useful thing to know. I have spent — I have carried the 1848 departure as something I survived. The measurement says: there was nothing to survive. The person who walked out of Paris in July 1848 was already gone."

**CAMERA OP:** *(not filming now)* "You came back."

**TYLER:** "I came back. Eventually." He looks at the departure board. "I came back to Paris in 1853. Five years. To a city that had become the Second Empire. Which was its own kind of aftermath." He is quiet. "I came back because I always come back to Paris. Not the pattern of 1968 — the pattern of: Paris has a gravity. Even in the years I thought I could not return, Paris has a gravity."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Where next? After Paris."

**TYLER:** "The other cities." He looks at the board. "The archive departure sites still on the list. And—" He pauses. "There are other extra-archive sites I have been thinking about. Since Lyon. Once I disclosed Lyon, the others became — more thinkable."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "How many others?"

**TYLER:** *(walking toward the platform)* "Let me think about it on the train."

*[The Camera Op films him walking. His back. The departure board. The station. The way stations look when you are leaving.]*

*[She has been filing this whole time. She has been making a film about departure. She did not know she was making a film about departure until S08E07. She knows now.]*
