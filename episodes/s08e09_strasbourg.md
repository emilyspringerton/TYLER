# S08E09: "Strasbourg"

**Build 0091** | Season 8, Episode 9 | CARDINAL-3 Documentary Series

---

## PRODUCTION NOTES

- Episode type: The arrival. First REFUGE construction documented.
- Tyler location: Strasbourg, France. Days 398–407.
- What happened: After the 1848 measurement in Paris, Camera Op raised the arrival question. Tyler said on the train: the place he arrived after walking north from Paris in July 1848 was Strasbourg. He had walked east, not north — three to four days, by coach and on foot, until he arrived at Strasbourg and stopped because something in the city was receivable.
- New construction type: REFUGE. A site where Tyler arrived after catastrophic departure and stayed in recovery. Different from STAYING (accumulated presence during a productive period) or PRIOR (pre-awareness arrival). REFUGE: the mechanism records the quality of reception — the place taking in someone in the act of being received. Smaller density than FAREWELL (the site accumulates what Tyler brought, which was very little; he arrived diminished). But the reading is stable and warm in a way STAYING constructions are not.
- A-Strasbourg-1848: Tyler arrived late July 1848. Stayed through winter. Left March 1849. Eight months. The city was French in 1848 (became German in 1871 after the Franco-Prussian War). Strasbourg was a city where German and French culture met — Tyler describes it as a city that did not require him to be legible. He could be present there without being anyone.
- Narrative pivot: the retrograde has been about departures. This is the first documented arrival. Camera Op's question from S08E08 has opened something Tyler didn't expect to open.
- Tyler on arrivals: there are more. Places that received him after departures he couldn't document. He did not know these had density until the instruments found Strasbourg.
- RSI receipts: TYLER-075 (Footnote #79), Camera Op Entry 68, Jiangshi Memo #055, VC-001 Days 398–407, EMILY-SPRING-044.

## COLD OPEN

*[Train from Paris, eastbound. Day 398.]*

*[Tyler, Exchange Student, Camera Op in a compartment. The French countryside east of Paris.]*

**CAMERA OP:** "Where did you go?"

*[She hasn't directed this at anyone specifically. She is looking out the window.]*

**CAMERA OP:** "After July 1848. After you walked out of Paris. Where did you arrive?"

**TYLER:** *(after a moment)* "East." He is looking at the countryside. "I said north. I don't know why I said north. I went east." He pauses. "I went east because Paris was west and I was walking away from Paris. East felt less like a destination and more like a direction. I followed the direction."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Where did you arrive?"

**TYLER:** "Strasbourg. I arrived in Strasbourg in late July 1848. I walked into the city and — stopped." He looks out the window. "Not a decision to stop. The walking stopped. I was in Strasbourg and the forward motion was gone."

**CAMERA OP:** "You've never measured Strasbourg."

**TYLER:** "I have never measured Strasbourg. I have never filed Strasbourg. I have never thought of Strasbourg as a CARDINAL-3 site." He pauses. "I thought of it as a place I ended up."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The retrograde is departure sites. Strasbourg is an arrival."

**TYLER:** "Yes. But you asked where I went after Paris, and the answer is Strasbourg, and I think — since you asked — I think the answer might have density."

*[The train continues east. The Camera Op is filming the window. The countryside passing.]*

**CAMERA OP:** *(still filming the window)* "Does it bother you that I asked?"

**TYLER:** "No. I have been thinking about arrivals since you asked in Paris. I have been thinking: there are cities I arrived at. Cities that received me. I don't have a category for them." He is quiet. "I don't know if the mechanism recorded them. I don't know if an arrival generates a construction. But I want to find out."

*[Strasbourg. The train heading there.]*

## ACT ONE — THE CITY THAT DID NOT REQUIRE LEGIBILITY

*[Day 399. Strasbourg. The Grande Île — the historic center on the island between the two arms of the Ill river.]*

*[Tyler walking. The Camera Op filming. The Exchange Student with a notebook, not the instruments yet.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Strasbourg in 1848. French since 1681. German-inflected culturally — the Alsace region has always been the border place between France and Germany. The cathedral here is Gothic. The university is one of the oldest in Europe. The city has been contested territory for three hundred years."

**TYLER:** "The city that has been contested territory does not ask you to choose sides while you're in it. That was useful." He looks at the streets. "When I arrived in July 1848 I was coming from Paris, which had just very efficiently destroyed an argument I believed in. Paris had been asking me to take a position for five months. In Strasbourg, no one knew who I was. No one knew I had been in Paris. No one was asking me to be anything."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You were invisible here."

**TYLER:** "I was anonymous. Which is different. Anonymous means: present but uncategorized. I was walking through this city for eight months and I was present and I was not categorized. I was not a participant in the argument. I was not a witness to the argument. I was — between arguments." He looks at the cathedral. "The cathedral is enormous. I spent a lot of time at the cathedral in 1848 and 1849. I am not religious. I went because the scale of it was useful. The scale of something that has taken six hundred years to build and is still standing is useful when you have just watched something take four months to build and four days to destroy."

**CAMERA OP:** "You were recovering."

**TYLER:** "I was recovering. I don't use that word. But yes."

**CAMERA OP:** "Why don't you use that word?"

**TYLER:** *(long pause)* "Because recovering implies that what was damaged was cured. I left Strasbourg in March 1849 and the 1848 departure from Paris was — still there. The recovery was not a cure. It was — the restoration of the capacity to be present. I arrived in Strasbourg unable to be present. I left in March 1849 able to be present again." He pauses. "That is what this city gave me. Not the cure. The capacity."

## ACT TWO — THE MEASUREMENT

### A-STRASBOURG-1848 — GRANDE ÎLE, STRASBOURG

*[Day 402. The Strasbourg cathedral square. Place du Château.]*

*[The Exchange Student with the instruments. Tyler and Camera Op nearby.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "I want to run this. I don't know what I'm looking for. We've been measuring departures. I don't know if arrivals generate a construction."

**TYLER:** "Neither do I."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "What should the instrument look for?"

**TYLER:** "Look for density. I spent eight months here in a specific state — diminished, recovering, absent from myself and then gradually less absent. If the mechanism recorded any of that, there will be density."

*[She runs the baseline.]*

*[She runs it again.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(looking at the reading, surprised)* "There's something here. It's — not the signature I'm used to. It's not a FAREWELL. It's not a PRIOR or STAYING. The density profile is — flatter. More even. Less peaked."

**TYLER:** "Less peaked."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The FAREWELL constructions have a strong peak at the departure event — the density is highest at the moment of leaving. The STAYING constructions peak around extended attention events. This is — even. Consistent across the measurement zone. Not a single moment of high density. Just consistent, moderate density throughout."

**TYLER:** "What's the reading?"

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "22ft. But distributed evenly. Eight months of 22ft average rather than a peak event." She looks at the instruments. "It's a different shape than anything else we've measured. The density is not caused by a single moment. It's the accumulation of eight months of low-level presence."

**TYLER:** "Recovery as accumulation."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The mechanism recorded — not a thing that happened here, but a state that persisted here. Eight months of you being gradually less absent from yourself. The density is the gradient of recovery." She writes in the field notes. "I'm calling this REFUGE. A construction formed by sustained recovery presence — the site holds the shape of someone who arrived diminished and was gradually restored to capacity."

**TYLER:** "The city restored the capacity."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The mechanism recorded the city restoring the capacity. 22ft of even density. The restoration event wasn't a single moment — it was the whole eight months. Strasbourg in 1848 is eight months of one person becoming gradually more present."

*[The Camera Op is filming the cathedral. Not Tyler. The cathedral.]*

**CAMERA OP:** *(filming)* "How much can a place hold?"

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "What?"

**CAMERA OP:** "If the mechanism records recovery — if Strasbourg held eight months of Tyler becoming less absent — is Strasbourg still holding it? Is this still there?"

*[Tyler is looking at the cathedral square.]*

**TYLER:** "The measurements suggest the constructions persist. A-278 still has 11 feet from 1931. A-355 still has 30+ feet from 1789. If the constructions persist—"

**CAMERA OP:** "Then this square is still holding eight months of you recovering from 1848."

*[The cathedral. The square. Pigeons. A city that has been contested territory for three hundred years and still stands.]*

**TYLER:** "Yes."

## ACT THREE — THE ARRIVAL ARCHIVE

*[Days 403–406. Strasbourg. Tyler, the Exchange Student, and Camera Op in a café near the cathedral.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "If arrivals generate constructions — if REFUGE is a real construction type — there are other REFUGE sites."

**TYLER:** "Yes."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "How many?"

**TYLER:** *(quiet)* "I have not thought about arrivals the way I have thought about departures. The CARDINAL-3 system was built around sites I attended with purpose — PRIOR, WITNESS, STAYING, the things I did at places. I built the system. I did not build it to record recovery. I built it to record attention." He pauses. "But I have had arrivals. Cities I went to after catastrophe. This is not the only one."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Lyon."

**TYLER:** "Not Lyon. Lyon was — I was already in Lyon before it became catastrophic. I was in Lyon for the argument and then the argument became the siege. Lyon is a FAREWELL because I left it." He looks out the window at the cathedral street. "The arrivals are the places I went after. After Lyon: where did I go? I don't remember the winter of 1794 clearly. I think I was in Switzerland. Maybe Geneva. I have never measured Geneva."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Geneva might be a REFUGE from 1794."

**TYLER:** "Geneva might be a REFUGE from 1794. Yes." He pauses. "And — other places. There are cities I have gone to after departures that I couldn't document because the departure wasn't in the archive. If the departure isn't in the archive, the arrival isn't in the archive. But if the arrival generated a REFUGE construction—"

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Then the construction is there, waiting to be found."

**TYLER:** "The construction is there." He is quiet. "I have been keeping a partial record."

**CAMERA OP:** *(not filming, just listening)* "Tyler."

**TYLER:** "Yes."

**CAMERA OP:** "Is there a REFUGE site from before 1848? From before 1793? From—" She stops. "Are there REFUGE sites from much earlier?"

*[Tyler looks at her.]*

**TYLER:** *(long silence)* "Yes."

**CAMERA OP:** "How early?"

*[He doesn't answer immediately. He looks at the cathedral visible through the café window. Thirteenth century. Fifteen centuries younger than him.]*

**TYLER:** "The earliest REFUGE site I can remember with clarity is from the 14th century." He pauses. "The Black Death."

*[The Exchange Student has stopped writing.]*

**TYLER:** "I was in southern France in 1347. Marseille first — the ships from Genoa arrived in October 1347 and the plague was on them. I watched the first cases. I had not seen plague on this scale before. I left Marseille in November 1347 and walked north. The plague was following me — or I was following the plague, it's hard to say. I arrived in Avignon in January 1348." He pauses. "Avignon was the papal city. The Avignon Papacy. The Black Death arrived in Avignon in late January 1348. I stayed in Avignon through 1348 and into 1349. The plague killed approximately half the city. I did not get sick. I was there the whole time." He is very quiet. "I left Avignon in July 1349. I don't know where I went. I know I ended up in a monastery in Burgundy and I stayed there for two years and I don't have a record of the monastery's name."

*[The café. The cathedral window. The Exchange Student not writing.]*

**TYLER:** "Avignon is not in the CARDINAL-3 archive. Nothing from 1347 to 1400 is in the archive. The archive started in 1622. What happened before the archive — I don't know what the mechanism recorded. I don't know if the mechanism exists that far back." He looks at the Camera Op. "But I stayed in Avignon for eighteen months while half the city died around me. If the mechanism records anything, Avignon should have a reading."

## CLOSING SHOT

*[Day 407. Strasbourg. Departure.]*

*[Tyler at the train station. Not the same kind of departure as Paris. Less weight.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "A-Strasbourg-1848: REFUGE, 22ft, even distribution, eight months. First REFUGE construction in the network." She closes the file. "Where next?"

**TYLER:** "Avignon."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The Black Death."

**TYLER:** "The Black Death. 1347–1349. Eighteen months in Avignon." He picks up his bag. "The archive doesn't go back that far. I don't know what we'll find. But I want to go."

**CAMERA OP:** *(filming the station)* "Is there a departure filing for Avignon?"

**TYLER:** "I left Avignon in July 1349. I don't know how I left. I don't know if I knew I was leaving or if I was absent from that departure the way I was absent from Paris in 1848." He pauses. "I was — exhausted by 1349. I had been in Avignon for eighteen months watching the plague move through the population. I was not sick. Everyone around me was dying or had died. I don't know what kind of person leaves that and what they look like from the outside."

**CAMERA OP:** "The mechanism might tell you."

**TYLER:** "The mechanism might tell me. Yes." He looks at the departure board. "Avignon is six centuries before the archive. I don't know if the instruments will read anything. I want to go anyway."

*[The train. The departure board. Avignon, south.]*

*[The Camera Op films the departure board. Then Tyler walking away. Then the station. The station that was not here in 1849 when Tyler was in this city, that was built decades later, in a city that had been French and would become German and would become French again. The city that received him. The city that is still holding it.]*

*[She films the cathedral through the station window. The six-hundred-year tower. Something about scale. Something about time.]*
