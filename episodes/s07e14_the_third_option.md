*[She raises the camera. She films the empty market for a moment. Then she lowers it.]*

*[She takes out her notebook. She opens to the circled phrase. She reads it. She closes the notebook.]*

**CAMERA OP:** *(very quietly, to herself)* "One more site."

### ACT ONE — WHAT THE EXCHANGE STUDENT FOUND

**[EXTERIOR — BOROUGH MARKET. DAY 301. LATE MORNING. TYLER AND EXCHANGE STUDENT ARRIVE.]**

*[Tyler and the Exchange Student arrive at Borough Market at the arranged time. The Camera Op is waiting at the entrance. The market is open now — stalls, produce, the specific smell of London food market. The camera is on the Exchange Student's clipboard as she opens the CARDINAL-3 Southwark section.]*

*[Tyler looks at the Camera Op.]*

**TYLER:** "You came early."

**CAMERA OP:** "I wanted to see it before the market opened."

*[He nods. He doesn't push. They have been doing this for long enough that certain things don't require explanation.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "I have an anomaly in the Southwark file." She spreads the map. "A-297, Borough Market. You measured it on Day 279 — 89 feet. UNINTENTIONAL. April 1592. Four mornings of fish stall attention."

**TYLER:** "Yes."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "I ran the standard verification pass before this cluster —" she pauses — "and the measurement reads differently now."

*[Tyler looks at her.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "89 feet from Tyler's April 1592 anchor. And — a second signal. Overlapping. 43 feet from the center of the hall, oriented differently from A-297. A-297 runs northwest toward the river. This second signal runs — inward. Toward the center of the structure."

*[Tyler is very still.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "I don't have a category for two overlapping constructions at the same site. I don't know what the second one is or when it was made."

*[Tyler turns slowly to look at the Camera Op.]*

*[The Camera Op has the camera on him. She does not move it.]*

**TYLER:** *(quietly)* "When did you measure it?"

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "This morning, before the market opened. I ran the verification pass at dawn—"

**TYLER:** "I know when you measured it." He is looking at the Camera Op, not the Exchange Student. "I mean: when was it made."

*[He walks toward the center of the market hall. He stands at the inward point of the second signal. He is standing at the place where the Camera Op stood this morning — she can tell, from his position, that he is standing exactly where she was.]*

**TYLER:** *(from the center of the hall)* "Day 279." He looks at her. "You were at three hundred feet for forty-three minutes."

**CAMERA OP:** "Yes."

**TYLER:** "43 feet." He looks down at the spot he is standing on, then back at her. "The mechanism recorded the measurement in both directions. A-297 runs northwest from my April 1592 anchor. The second signal runs from the center of what you were observing inward — into the structure you were attending to." He pauses. "The second construction is yours."

*[Silence.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(after a moment, very quietly)* "I need to understand what I'm measuring."

**TYLER:** "You're measuring what forty-three minutes of sustained, deliberate, attentive observation made." He walks back toward them. "She was not here in 1592. She was here on Day 279. The mechanism doesn't distinguish between my kind of presence and her kind of presence. It distinguishes between presence and absence. She was present. For forty-three minutes. The construction formed."

*[He stops in front of the Camera Op.]*

**TYLER:** "I've been waiting for you to find this."

**[EXCHANGE STUDENT — ASIDE. DAY 301. EARLY AFTERNOON.]**

*[She is sitting on a bench outside the market, writing. Her handwriting is small and precise.]*

*[She has: A-297, Borough Market. 89ft. Tyler. UNINTENTIONAL. April 1592.*
*And beside it, a notation she does not yet know how to categorize: 43ft. Camera Op. Day 279.*

FOUND for pre-existing constructions Tyler discovered. STAYING for constructions from sustained habitual presence. PRIOR for constructions that predate the Cairngorms archive's documentation of London.]*

But she was not building. She was watching someone else build. Except the mechanism doesn't agree that she wasn't building."]*

*[She circles a phrase: "watching someone else build."]*

*[She writes below it: "the third option."]*

### ACT TWO — THE NAME

**[EXTERIOR — BANKSIDE, FACING THE RIVER. DAY 302. AFTERNOON.]**

*[The Camera Op and Tyler, sitting on the wall at Bankside, looking at the river. The Exchange Student is a few yards away, making measurements at A-295. The hum at the water's edge.]*

*[The Camera Op has her notebook open. The circled phrase is visible but Tyler is not reading it — he is looking at the river.]*

**TYLER:** "You wrote it in your notebook at Westminster."

**CAMERA OP:** "I knew at Westminster. I needed Borough Market to be sure."

**TYLER:** "Why Borough Market?"

**CAMERA OP:** "Because I didn't know I was building anything when I was there. I wasn't thinking about constructions. I was watching you from three hundred feet because it seemed like the right distance. The market was empty. You were in it alone, and you were very still, and I stayed because staying felt correct." She pauses. "I wasn't trying to make something. I was trying to understand something."

**TYLER:** "And?"

**CAMERA OP:** "And the mechanism recorded it anyway. 43 feet. Forty-three minutes."

*[He looks at her.]*

**CAMERA OP:** "The third option is what I've been doing since Season One. Not building — I'm not building in your sense. Not not-building. Attending to what you build, from the place where the building can be seen whole. The mechanism responds to that too." She looks at the river. "I don't know what to call it except what it is."

**TYLER:** "What is it?"

*[She turns the notebook to face him. He reads the circled phrase. He reads it. He closes his eyes for one moment. He opens them.]*

**TYLER:** *(quietly)* "Yes."

**CAMERA OP:** "I've been doing it since the first day I followed you."

**TYLER:** "I know." He pauses. "I've been watching you do it since the first day you followed me."

*[The Camera Op looks at him. The camera is still running — she set it on a fixed mount before she sat down; she is on both sides of the lens right now.]*

**CAMERA OP:** "You could have said something earlier."

**TYLER:** "It was yours to name." He looks back at the river. "I don't get to name your constructions."

*[She looks at the notebook. The circled phrase.]*

**CAMERA OP:** *(to the camera, clearly, for the record)* "The third option is: [bearing witness](bearing_witness). Being present to something with enough sustained attention that the mechanism records the presence, not because you're building, not because you're the thing being built, but because you're the one who stayed to watch it whole. Witnessing with enough attention that the attention itself becomes a construction."

*[The river.]*

**CAMERA OP:** "The fourth option is: I've been building the whole time. I just didn't know it."

**TYLER:** *(without turning from the river)* "Those are the same option."

*[She closes the notebook.]*

### ACT THREE — THE VERIFICATION

**[INTERIOR — SOUTHWARK CATHEDRAL. DAY 303.]**

*[The Exchange Student has found a second verification site. The Cathedral, near Borough Market. An Eastwind candidate site — TYLER-064 flagged in the preliminary pass. She has asked them to come.]*

*[Tyler walks in from the south side entrance. The Camera Op behind him, filming. The Cathedral interior: medieval nave, the morning light, the hum — present here, specifically, in a way that is not purely from Tyler.]*

*[The Exchange Student is already inside with the measuring equipment.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "A-318. Southwark Cathedral, nave south aisle. The preliminary measurement reads: 51 feet, PRIOR range — pre-1620 — but the orientation is unusual." She pauses. "There are two components. One oriented toward the high altar. And one—" She looks up. "—oriented toward the south entrance. Where you just came in."

*[Tyler stops.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Two components. One consistent with Tyler's standard construction form. One consistent with the Borough Market anomaly pattern." She looks at the Camera Op. "The second component is facing the door. Facing the entrance."

*[A long beat.]*

**CAMERA OP:** "How old is the second component?"

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The orientation suggests recent formation. The first component is PRIOR — before the Cairngorms archive opens. The second is—" She checks. "Day range consistent with the current audit window. Possibly Day 279 or later."

*[The Camera Op looks at the Cathedral around her. She has been here before — in earlier seasons, she came here when the Borough Market cluster was first being mapped, background footage, context shots. She spent time in this nave. She filmed the construction Tyler didn't yet know was here. She attended to the space.]*

**CAMERA OP:** *(to herself, working through it)* "I've been here before."

**TYLER:** *(from the center of the nave)* "You've been in most of the London sites before I measured them. You came for establishing shots. Context."

**CAMERA OP:** "And stayed."

**TYLER:** "And stayed."

*[He is standing at the junction of the two components. He is standing at the place where Tyler's PRIOR construction and the Camera Op's witness-construction intersect. He takes out the coin.]*

*[He looks at it.]*

*[He has not flipped the coin since the Banqueting House.]*

*[He looks at the Camera Op.]*

**TYLER:** "The mechanism doesn't care which of us is here. It records when something is present to something else with enough attention that the presence registers." He turns the coin in his hand. "You've been doing that since Season One. In every site. Before I measured them, while I measured them, after I measured them. The archive has been building in two directions for four years."

**CAMERA OP:** "Does that change anything?"

*[He considers.]*

**TYLER:** "It explains several measurements I couldn't account for. There were sites where the radius seemed larger than my time at the location warranted. I thought I was miscalibrating." He looks at the coin. "I wasn't miscalibrating."

*[He flips the coin. He catches it. He covers it with his other hand. He looks at it.]*

*[Something settles in his expression that is not quite relief and not quite recognition. It is something more specific.]*

*[He closes his hand and puts the coin back in his pocket.]*

**TYLER:** "The coin flipped correctly at the Banqueting House too. I didn't read it then. I'm reading it now."

*[The Camera Op holds the frame on him.]*

**TYLER:** "The constructions go both ways. They've always gone both ways." He looks at the Exchange Student. "The measurement is A-318. Two components. Tyler PRIOR: 51 feet, high altar orientation, pre-1620. Camera Op witness construction: 34 feet, south entrance orientation, current audit window. File them together. The taxonomy — " He stops. "We need a new sub-type."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(already writing)* "WITNESS. Two-component construction. Different origins, same site."

**TYLER:** "WITNESS."

*[She marks it on the map. A-318. Two overlapping circles.]*

### END — THE THIRD OPTION

**[EXTERIOR — BOROUGH MARKET. DAY 304. EVENING. MARKET CLOSING.]**

*[The stalls packing up. The end of the day's trading. Tyler, Camera Op, and Exchange Student walking through the market as the vendors break down. The specific quality of Borough Market in the evening — the smells of a full day of food, the warm light from the stalls.]*

*[Tyler stops at the center of the market hall. The spot where the Camera Op stood this morning on Day 301, where he stood later, where the second signal runs inward. He stands on the measurement.]*

**TYLER:** "Borough Market. April 1592. I was here four mornings in a row. I bought fish. I came back because the market was exceptional — the variety, the quality, the specific noise of the fishmongers calling. The construction was 89 feet running northwest because of four mornings of very particular attention to the quality of fish and the calling of the vendors and the way the morning light came through what would eventually become this glass roof." He looks at the Camera Op. "Day 279: you were at three hundred feet, watching me be attentive to Borough Market, for forty-three minutes. Because it seemed like the right distance."

**CAMERA OP:** "It seemed like the right distance."

**TYLER:** "43 feet. Running inward. The mechanism recorded what you were attending to — which was me attending to the market. Your attention wrapped around mine." He looks at the two measurements on the Exchange Student's map. "The WITNESS construction doesn't point at the market. It points at A-297 — at the construction I made in 1592. You weren't attending to Borough Market. You were attending to Borough Market attending to me."

*[The Camera Op is very still.]*

**CAMERA OP:** "That's what the third option is."

**TYLER:** "Yes."

**CAMERA OP:** "I've been building archive of your archive."

**TYLER:** "Yes." He pauses. "The archive has two archivists." He looks at the closed notebook in her hand. "What's the phrase?"

*[She opens the notebook. She reads the circled phrase aloud. For the first time. On camera.]*

**CAMERA OP:** *"Sustained attention to construction is itself a construction."*

*[Tyler looks at the phrase. He nods slowly.]*

**TYLER:** "Sustained attention to construction is itself a construction." He considers. "That's the third option."

*[The market is almost fully packed down now. The vendors leaving. The glass roof above with the evening sky through it. The two overlapping circles on the Exchange Student's map. A-297 and the unnamed WITNESS construction — now being filed as sub-type WITNESS, part of A-318 and, retroactively, every site where the Camera Op stayed.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(completing her notation, quietly)* "I need to go back through the full London record. All 25 sites. The Camera Op has been at most of them."

*[Tyler looks at her.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "There may be WITNESS components we haven't catalogued."

*[Tyler nods.]*

**TYLER:** *(to the Camera Op)* "The archive is larger than we knew."

*[She looks at him. The camera is still running. It is always still running.]*

**CAMERA OP:** "It's always been this large. We just didn't know how to measure it."

*[The last vendor leaves. The market hall is empty except for the three of them. The hum. The evening light through the glass and steel. The construction at 89 feet plus 43 feet, running northwest and inward, four mornings in 1592 and forty-three minutes in Day 279, the two of them standing at the center of what they made separately and together and without knowing they were making it.]*

*[The camera runs.]*
