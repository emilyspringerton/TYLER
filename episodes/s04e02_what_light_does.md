## EPISODE STRUCTURE

**Day 78, morning — Bacon's building, the table**

Emily Springerton arrives at 9 AM with a revised notebook. She sits across from the Camera Op. The sealed log is open between them — not literally, but the dynamic is there: two people who have been keeping their own records of the same events, comparing notes.

**EMILY:** "He said 'light' and I went home and tried to figure out what that meant technically. Not metaphorically — technically."

**CAMERA OP:** "What did you come up with?"

Emily opens the notebook. On the page: the words STOLAS #36 — 7.83 Hz — persistence / Tyler's hum with three arrows pointing to a question mark.

**EMILY:** "When all five Goetia frequencies run simultaneously, we've documented it twice. Day 44, when Stage 5 commenced. Day 49, when the sentence completed. Both times the coincidence was sequential — one frequency announcing, the next confirming."

She taps the question mark.

**EMILY:** "Tyler said the construction uses light to make a place navigable in the dark. I think he means the frequencies. I think the construction is a space where the Day 49 event is permanently present — not recorded, present. You can stand in it and be in the moment of completion. The light isn't metaphorical. It's Stolas running at full amplitude, continuously, in an enclosed space."

The Camera Op is quiet.

**CAMERA OP:** "He said the shape was the room behind the table."

**EMILY:** "Yes."

**CAMERA OP:** "And he's been in that room since six this morning."

**EMILY:** "Yes."

**Day 78, late morning — the room behind the table**

The door opens. Tyler comes out. He has a piece of paper in his hand — not a notebook page, a separate sheet, written on in a hand that is not his.

He stops when he sees Emily and the Camera Op at the table.

**TYLER:** "You worked it out."

**EMILY:** "The frequencies."

**TYLER:** "Yes." He sits down. He puts the piece of paper on the table, face-up. It is covered in notation — not musical notation, something older. "The Subscriber left instructions. I found them this morning in the room. They were always there. I didn't know what I was looking for until I knew what I was building."

The Camera Op zooms in slightly on the paper. The notation is in a hand that matches the handwriting in the Subscriber's second message to the camera in S03E05.

**EMILY:** *(quietly)* "SUBSCRIBER-TRANSMISSION-001."

**TYLER:** "It's a frequency diagram. How to run all five simultaneously without the sequential announcement-confirmation pattern. How to make them continuous instead of event-triggered." He looks at Emily's notebook. "How does your map account for this?"

Emily opens to the 43-node map.

**EMILY:** "There's a node called primary architecture. It's been blank."

She writes in it: *Subscriber's frequency diagram — continuous simultaneous activation*.

**EMILY:** "Now it's not blank."

**Day 78, midday — the Exchange Student, the supply chain**

The Exchange Student has been at a corner table since the morning, her laptop open, the CEDAR-4 file next to it. She has been mapping quietly.

Emily notices and comes over.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "I've been tracing what you'd need to run five frequencies simultaneously in an enclosed space on a continuous basis. Not physically — I don't know the physics. Materially. What would you need to have, what would you need to source, what's the supply chain."

She turns the laptop to show Emily. A spreadsheet: categories, sources, availability.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The frequencies are running at Bacon's already. I checked the documentation — Stolas 7.83 has been present here consistently since 1623. The question is what keeps it running at full amplitude rather than background level. And the answer is—" she points to a row "—people who resonate with it. Witnesses."

Emily reads the row. Witness threshold: minimum 3, documented resonance required. Current confirmed resonators at this location: Tyler (continuous), Camera Op (calibrated Day 23), Emily Springerton (SPRING-4 registered).

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You need a minimum of three people who resonate with the Stolas frequency to maintain full amplitude in an enclosed space. You have three. You might need more, depending on the size of the construction."

**EMILY:** "What would more look like?"

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "More people who've been documented at the right frequency." She pauses. "I don't know where you find those. I'm just tracking what you'd need."

Emily writes in the notebook: *resonant witnesses — supply chain. Exchange Student tracking.*

The map gets a new node.

**Day 78, afternoon — the construction named**

Tyler is back at the table with the Subscriber's notation. Emily is updating the map. The Camera Op is filming. Bacon brings more coffee. The Exchange Student has closed her laptop and is reading the CEDAR-4 file.

**TYLER:** "I've been trying to figure out what to call it."

No one asks what he means. They've been in this room long enough to know when he's talking to the work and when he's talking to them. This is both.

**TYLER:** "The room behind the table is a space that tells you something has already happened. You walk into it and the weight of completion is there — it's in the air, in the way the light sits. You don't need to be told. You just know." He looks at the Subscriber's notation. "I want to build more of those. Not the same room — rooms. Places where something that happened is permanently in the air."

**CAMERA OP:** *(from behind the camera)* "A witness room."

He looks at her.

**TYLER:** "Yes."

**CAMERA OP:** "That's what it is. A room that has witnessed something, permanently."

**TYLER:** "The construction is called a witness room."

Emily writes it at the top of the dependency map page: *WITNESS ROOM — Tyler's first original construction.*
