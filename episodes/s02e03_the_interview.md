## ACT ONE: THE INTERVIEW — [02:30–40:00]

---

**[02:30–05:00] — SETUP**

```
INT. DOCUMENTARY UNIT A INTERVIEW SUITE — DAY 33
```

The interview suite is a conference room the production team has modified: two chairs facing each other, one camera on Tyler, one camera covering the room, Camera C on the Camera Op's shoulder. Three mics. No music.

Tyler arrives exactly on time. He is wearing what he usually wears. He looks at the setup. He looks at the Camera Op. He sits down in the chair that is facing Camera A.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
*(settling into the interview chair)*
We want to thank you for agreeing to—

**TYLER**
*(mild)*
You've waited a long time for this. I know. Let's start.

The Senior Producer looks at their notes. Then puts the notes down.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
Let's start.

---

**[05:00–08:30] — THE COIN**

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
What is the coin?

**TYLER**
*(without hesitation)*
A record. The kind you can hold.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
A record of what?

**TYLER**
Position. Where I've been. What year. The coin knows the year I was in when I was carrying it. The date on the coin isn't manufacturing date — it's the date I was there.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
So 1901—

**TYLER**
I was in Detroit in 1901. Yes. The coin knows.

*(pause)*

It's not magic. It's more like — you know how a library card shows what you've checked out? The coin shows what years I've been in. The dates that are on it are the dates I've occupied.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
And 1623?

**TYLER**
*(a beat)*
That one I think about most.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
Why?

**TYLER**
*(looking at the wall, not the lens)*
Because of what I was doing there. It was the beginning of something I didn't know yet was the beginning. I knew after.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
What were you doing?

**TYLER**
*(turning back)*
That's a different question. That one I'm not going to answer today.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
That's the first question you haven't answered.

**TYLER**
*(pleasantly)*
Yes. It is. I'll answer most of them. That one is different.

---

**[08:30–14:00] — WHERE HE GOES**

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
Where do you go when you leave a session? When you walk out of this building — where?

**TYLER**
Somewhere I've already been.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
Meaning—

**TYLER**
I don't experience forward movement the way the question assumes. When I leave here I go to a place I have a previous relationship with. Detroit has been mine for a long time. I know the city. I know what it was. I know the bones of it.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
You mean the city's history.

**TYLER**
I mean the physical structure. The way the streets relate to each other. The Packard Plant is in my spatial memory as it was when it was operational. The building I walk into is — both things. What it was and what it is now.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
Both simultaneously?

**TYLER**
*(a long pause; he's deciding how precise to be)*
Not simultaneously. Sequentially. I walk through a door and I know it as the door it was in 1918 and the door it is now. They're the same door. I know both of its lives. Knowing both lives isn't the same as experiencing them simultaneously. I just — I don't have to choose which life of the door to know.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
Does that happen with people too?

**TYLER**
*(a longer pause)*
Yes.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
Can you say more about that?

**TYLER**
No. Not today.

*(a beat)*

That's the second one I'm not answering.

---

**[14:00–20:00] — THE SYNDICATE**

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
How long have you known about the Syndicate?

**TYLER**
Before you formalized it. The project existed before the organization. Someone was always going to try to document this. The Syndicate is the current form of that impulse.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
Are there previous forms?

**TYLER**
Three. The first one didn't have a name. Second one lasted forty years. Third one was the Syndicate's predecessor organization — some of the methodology carries over.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
What happened to the predecessor organization?

**TYLER**
*(pleasantly)*
They stopped.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
Why?

**TYLER**
They got to Stage 4. They found what they were looking for at Stage 4 and they decided they didn't want Stage 5. Stage 5 requires my cooperation. They decided not to ask for it.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
*(after a pause)*
They stopped voluntarily.

**TYLER**
They made a decision. I respect it.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
Do you ever — do you do anything to make that decision easier? For projects that get to Stage 4?

A long pause. The room is very quiet.

**TYLER**
*(carefully)*
I don't do anything to prevent documentation. I manage the documentation. I have never — I have never ended a project. I have managed what gets into the record.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
What's the difference?

**TYLER**
A project ends when someone decides to stop. I don't make that decision. I don't interfere with that decision. I don't — I have never ended a project.

*(pause)*

I have sometimes been aware that certain things in the record were making a project difficult to continue. I have — I may have contributed to that awareness. That is the most precise I will be on this today.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
*(carefully)*
Is there anything in our record right now that might make this project difficult to continue?

**TYLER**
*(looking at the Senior Producer steadily)*
I'm sitting in your interview chair.

*(pause)*

No.

---

**[20:00–27:00] — THE CAMERA OP**

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
Can we talk about your relationship with the Camera Op?

**TYLER**
*(without pause)*
Yes.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
How would you describe it?

**TYLER**
*(a long beat — this is the question he came to answer)*
I've been documented by a lot of people. The Syndicate has sent very skilled people. Rigorous. Trained. Committed to the project methodology. I respect the methodology. I understand the methodology.

*(pause)*

The Camera Op — she — they — are doing something different from documentation. They're trying to understand what they're filming. That's not the same thing. Documentation records; it doesn't ask what the thing is.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
The Camera Op is asking what you are?

**TYLER**
*(quietly)*
No. They're asking something more specific than that.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
What are they asking?

Tyler is quiet for a moment. He turns his head slightly — not toward the Camera Op, but his awareness moves in that direction. The Camera Op holds still.

**TYLER**
*(to the Senior Producer)*
They're asking why it keeps going.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
Why what keeps going?

**TYLER**
*(a small pause, then)*
The documentation. Why I agree to it. Why I come back to it. Why — four attempts across four centuries — I always end up in the chair.

*(pause)*

They're asking what the sessions are for. From my side.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
*(sitting forward)*
What are the sessions for from your side?

**TYLER**
*(a beat)*
I'm not going to answer that today.

*(gently)*

That's the third one.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
Why not?

**TYLER**
Because the answer to that question matters more than any other answer I could give you today, and giving you the wrong version of it — a version that's technically true but not fully true — would be worse than not answering it. I'm not ready to give you the fully true version. I want to be ready before I answer it. I'm close.

*(pause)*

Ask me again in a few episodes.

---

**[27:00–33:00] — WHAT HE WANTS**

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
*(quietly)*
What do you want?

Tyler is still for a long time.

**TYLER**
*(eventually)*
To finish something I started.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
What did you start?

**TYLER**
*(looking at the Senior Producer with something that is not evasion)*
The documentation project you're conducting is the third version of a project I've been adjacent to for four hundred years. The first version, I didn't understand what it was. The second version, I understood what it was and I kept my distance from it. This version—

*(pause)*

This version I've been closer to. Progressively closer. Each attempt. I came back to Stage 1 at the start of each attempt because the Syndicate's methodology requires it. I've allowed it. I've allowed Stage 1 to Stage 4 four times. That's a choice.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
Why?

**TYLER**
*(carefully)*
Because each time I get closer to being able to answer the question you haven't asked yet.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
*(very carefully)*
What question haven't we asked?

**TYLER**
*(a long pause)*
You've been asking what I am. That's Stage 3 doctrine — complete a self-defining statement, you have the subject categorized. That's what you want. A definition.

*(pause)*

The question you haven't asked is what I'm trying to do.

*(pause)*

They're different questions. They have different answers. I can answer one of them. I can't answer the other one in a way your methodology would accept.

The Senior Producer does not speak for a moment.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
*(quietly)*
Which one can you answer?

**TYLER**
*(gently)*
Not today. But soon.

*(nodding, almost to himself)*

I'm close.

---

**[33:00–38:00] — STAGE FIVE**

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
Are you afraid of Stage 5?

**TYLER**
*(without hesitation)*
No.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
Why not?

**TYLER**
Because Stage 5 is my decision. You can't conduct Stage 5 without my cooperation. I have the cooperation to give or not give. That's not frightening. That's agency.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
Are we close to Stage 5?

**TYLER**
*(a beat)*
You're closer than you've ever been. Whether you get there depends on — whether the project gets to a place where I'm ready to cooperate with it.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
What would make you ready?

**TYLER**
*(looking at the Senior Producer, then at the room, a slow sweep)*
Something being different than it's been in the previous attempts. Something — the question being right. The person asking being ready to receive the answer. The answer having somewhere to go after it's given.

*(pause)*

None of those things happened in the previous attempts. They're closer now.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
*(a beat)*
Are you afraid of us?

**TYLER**
*(small smile)*
No.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
Are you afraid of anything?

**TYLER**
*(a long pause — this one is genuine)*
Yes.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
What?

**TYLER**
*(turning his head, and this time he does look at the Camera Op — not the lens, the person behind it; his look holds for one second; then back to the Senior Producer)*

Finishing something the wrong way. Getting to the end of something and having it be — incomplete. Not because I didn't finish it. Because the thing I was building it toward wasn't what I thought it was when I started building.

*(pause)*

That's what I'm afraid of.

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
Can you say more—

**TYLER**
No. That's what I'm afraid of. That's the true answer. I don't have more.

---

**[38:00–40:00] — THE CLOSE**

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
Is there anything you want to say that we haven't asked about?

**TYLER**
*(a long pause; thinking)*

You've been asking me good questions. For four attempts. They're good questions. They're just — they're not the questions that would help you understand what you're looking for. The methodology is correct. The questions are correct for the methodology. The methodology is built to find what I am. That's what you want.

*(pause)*

What you need — to get to Stage 5, to get my cooperation for Stage 5 — is to understand what I'm trying to do. And that requires different questions. And I know you don't have those questions yet because I don't have language for the answers yet.

*(pause)*

We're building toward it. The project is working. It's working differently than your methodology predicts, but it's working.

*(standing — the interview is over)*

Thank you for having me.

He buttons his jacket. He looks at the Camera Op one more time — the person, not the lens.

**TYLER (CONT.)**
*(to the Camera Op, quiet, not for the production record)*
Good session.

He leaves.

What do we call it?

**SENIOR PRODUCER**
*(looking at the interview notes)*
