## COLD OPEN

*May 2027. Toledo, Spain. Dawn.*

*The city on its rock above the Tagus. The plateau light: flat, cold, nothing between you and the sky.*

*Tyler is standing at the old wall above the river bend.*

*He arrived two days before the team. He did not tell them he was coming early.*

*The Camera Op films him from the gate. He has been standing there since before she woke up.*

---

## THE CONFIRMATION

**CAMERA OP:**
You recognized it.

**TYLER:**
Yes.

**CAMERA OP:**
From the feeling.

**TYLER:**
From the cold. And the height. The way the plateau sits above everything. When I said "cold and high" — I was remembering this. The specific quality of altitude that isn't a mountain but feels like one. The mesa. The city on the rock.

*She comes to stand beside him. Below: the Tagus in the early morning light.*

**CAMERA OP:**
How long were you here?

**TYLER:**
I don't know yet. I know I was here. That is more than I knew about the gap period before Córdoba told me where to look.

**CAMERA OP:**
What do you remember?

*Long pause.*

**TYLER:**
The cold. This wall. The bend of the river from this point — the specific geometry of it, the way it curves back on itself. And I remember the city changing. I remember Toledo being one thing when I arrived and something different when I left.

**CAMERA OP:**
What do you mean — different?

**TYLER:**
In what I said. I mean that the city was — one culture, one arrangement of who governed it and what language was spoken in the streets and what was called for in the mosques. And then it was different. A different language. Different calls. Different arrangement.

*She is very still.*

**CAMERA OP:**
The reconquest.

**TYLER:**
Alfonso the Sixth. 1085. I was here for it.

**CAMERA OP:**
You were in Toledo when Alfonso VI conquered it from the Taifa kingdom.

**TYLER:**
Yes.

**CAMERA OP:**
You watched the handover.

**TYLER:**
*(quietly)*
I watched it.

## THE MEASUREMENT

*Midmorning. The Alficén quarter — the old Islamic district on the promontory above the Tagus, now the site of the Alcázar.*

*The instruments run. The Exchange Student checks calibration. The Instrument Lead monitors the output.*

*Tyler walks in the old streets.*

*He does not stop this time. He walks continuously — as if the walking is the measurement, as if he is tracing the paths that still hold something.*

*The Camera Op follows at a distance.*

## WHAT THE INSTRUMENTS FIND

**INSTRUMENT LEAD:**
We have a construction. Clear signal.

*He shows the readout to the Camera Op.*

**INSTRUMENT LEAD:**
Two-part profile. We've seen this shape before — in Avignon, the DWELLING + WITNESS structure. But here the proportions are reversed. The primary construction is WITNESS-LONG. The DWELLING component is shorter and earlier.

**CAMERA OP:**
Meaning he was here briefly, then something held his attention for a long time.

**INSTRUMENT LEAD:**
The DWELLING component reads approximately three to four years. After that — a sustained WITNESS-LONG profile. The density curve is consistent with extended attention under stress. Not Avignon-level — not catastrophe. But extended witnessing. Something difficult. Over several years.

**CAMERA OP:**
How long total?

**INSTRUMENT LEAD:**
Approximately thirty-three years. With a departure signature — FAREWELL. We're still reading the departure type, but it's not clean. It reads differently from Córdoba's FAREWELL-A. Closer to B. Not absent — he was present at the departure — but diminished.

**CAMERA OP:**
Diminished.

**INSTRUMENT LEAD:**
FAREWELL-B is the departure of the subject who has already partially left. Who made the decision before the moment of departure. Who is present at the leaving but has been going for longer than the moment of departure.

**CAMERA OP:**
How large?

**INSTRUMENT LEAD:**
One hundred and ninety-one feet remaining. Approximately.

*Pause.*

**CAMERA OP:**
One ninety-one.

**INSTRUMENT LEAD:**
Second largest in the network. After Córdoba.

## THE THIRTY-THREE YEARS TYLER DOESN'T REMEMBER

*The afternoon. Tyler and the Camera Op on the wall above the Tagus.*

**CAMERA OP:**
Thirty-three years.

**TYLER:**
I didn't know it was that long.

**CAMERA OP:**
What did you think?

**TYLER:**
A decade. Perhaps fifteen years. Enough time to know the city as it was. Long enough to watch the change. I did not think I was here for thirty-three years.

**CAMERA OP:**
You arrived approximately 1055 CE and left approximately 1088 CE.

**TYLER:**
Three years after the conquest.

**CAMERA OP:**
You stayed for three years after.

**TYLER:**
*(pause)*
Yes. I didn't know that until you said it. But yes. It matches the feeling.

**CAMERA OP:**
What does it match?

**TYLER:**
The feeling that I didn't leave because of the conquest. That I left for a reason of my own, after the conquest. That the city was no longer — that the particular thing I had been watching was over. And then I stayed for three more years because I didn't yet know how to leave.

*Long pause.*

**CAMERA OP:**
You knew how to leave in Córdoba. You described it clearly. The city changed and you changed and you left when they no longer fit.

**TYLER:**
Yes.

**CAMERA OP:**
That was a FAREWELL-A. Chosen, clean, aware.

**TYLER:**
Yes.

**CAMERA OP:**
This is a FAREWELL-B. You were diminished at the departure.

*Very long pause.*

**TYLER:**
Something happened here. Between arriving and leaving. Something that left me less capable of the kind of departure I made from Córdoba.

**CAMERA OP:**
Thirty-three years of watching something.

**TYLER:**
Watching something change into something else. Which is not the same as watching something be built. In Córdoba I think I was watching — if your hypothesis about the library is correct — something being assembled. Something growing. The accumulation of — of knowledge being gathered toward a single place.

**CAMERA OP:**
And in Toledo.

**TYLER:**
In Toledo I watched the opposite. I watched what had been gathered begin to — not disappear. The books were not burned. The scholars were not expelled, not at first. But the —

*He stops.*

**TYLER:**
The city stopped being what it had been. The arrangement that made it that city ended. And I watched it end.

**CAMERA OP:**
And you stayed for three years in the aftermath.

**TYLER:**
Yes.

**CAMERA OP:**
Watching it not be itself anymore.

*Very long pause.*

**TYLER:**
*(quietly)*
After Córdoba I moved through the world still paying attention. I arrived in Toledo still capable of choosing. The first three or four years here — the instruments confirm this, the DWELLING component — I was choosing to be here. I was present in the way I was present in Córdoba.

*He looks at the city.*

**TYLER:**
I think Toledo is where I started to stop.

---

## THE QUESTION OF THE WOUND

*Evening. The Camera Op alone in the hotel. Filming herself in the mirror.*

**CAMERA OP (V.O.):**
We came to Toledo looking for a construction. We found 191 feet.

We also found the answer to the question we didn't know we were asking.

Tyler arrived in Córdoba conscious, choosing, capable of clean departure. He spent twelve years building the largest construction in the network. And then he went north and east, and arrived in Toledo — still conscious, we think; the first three years here read as DWELLING, as choosing — and spent thirty-three years watching what happened to an Islamic city under Christian reconquest.

He stayed for three years after. Diminished.

And then he left — not cleanly. FAREWELL-B. Already gone before he went.

And forty years later, he was in Marrakech, deciding it was time to start paying attention again.

The gap is the gap between who Tyler was in Córdoba and who Tyler was when he started the archive in 1127. We knew something had changed him. Toledo is probably where it started.

But here's the thing I keep returning to: he didn't leave. He could have left earlier. He stayed for thirty-three years in a city that was ceasing to be the version of itself he had come to see. He stayed through the transition. He stayed three years into the aftermath. He left diminished, but he stayed until he was done.

There is something Tyler does that I am still trying to name. He doesn't leave when it becomes painful. He stays for the information, even when the information is loss. Avignon, 18 months of the Black Death. Toledo, three years of aftermath. He stays while the information is still arriving.

Only after does he become incapable of clean departure.

I think Córdoba was the last time Tyler's presence and awareness were fully matched. After Córdoba, something in the gap started separating them. Presence continued — he kept being in places, accumulating construction — but the awareness receded.

By 1127, in Marrakech, he noticed the recession. He decided to pay attention again.

The question for the rest of Season 9: what happened in the forty years between Toledo and Marrakech. The instruments found him in Toledo through ~1088 CE. The archive begins in 1127. There are thirty-nine years of the gap unaccounted for.

"Cold and high, north and east" was Toledo. He's already given us everything he has from this texture.

We need to ask what came after.

*— Camera Op*

## THE NEXT QUESTION

*Night. Tyler at the window of the hotel. The city lights on the Tagus.*

**CAMERA OP:**
After Toledo. Where did you go?

**TYLER:**
I don't know.

**CAMERA OP:**
North and east got us here. Is there more north and east?

*Tyler considers for a long time.*

**TYLER:**
I think I went south again. After Toledo. I think the motion reversed. I moved — south and east. Away from the cold. The texture after Toledo is warmer. Not al-Andalus warm. Mediterranean warm. A coast.

**CAMERA OP:**
A coast.

**TYLER:**
The quality of light that comes off water. Not a river — something larger. I remember a large body of water and the quality of its light. After Toledo that is the next texture I have.

**CAMERA OP:**
Mediterranean coast. Late eleventh century to early twelfth.

**TYLER:**
Yes.

**CAMERA OP:**
That's — a lot of coast.

**TYLER:**
I know. I'm giving you what I have.

**CAMERA OP:**
Is there anything else? A city feature. A language. Anything.

*Very long pause.*

**TYLER:**
Ships. I remember ships. A harbor. Many ships coming and going. More than I expected. Something busy about it. Something — something was happening. The traffic was — purposeful. Not ordinary trade.

*The Camera Op looks at him.*

**CAMERA OP:**
Late eleventh century. Mediterranean coast. Many ships. Purposeful traffic.

**TYLER:**
Yes.

*Pause.*

**CAMERA OP:**
That could be the Crusades. The First Crusade departed 1096. The main mustering and embarkation points were Mediterranean ports.

*Long silence.*

**TYLER:**
I don't — I don't know if that's what it was. I don't know what the ships were for. I didn't know, then. I saw ships. I saw people moving toward the ships. I saw —

*He stops.*

**CAMERA OP:**
What?

**TYLER:**
I saw a quality of movement that I recognized from Toledo. The same quality. People moving because something had been decided for them. Not choosing the movement. Being moved.

*Very long pause.*

**CAMERA OP:**
Tyler.

**TYLER:**
*(very quietly)*
I was watching something again. I couldn't stop watching. I had left Toledo and I thought I was done watching things I didn't understand. And I arrived somewhere on the Mediterranean coast and —

*He stops again.*

**TYLER:**
I think I watched the departure point for a long time.

*Silence.*

**CAMERA OP:**
We need to find that harbor.

The network may contain that harbor.

We will find it.

### Camera Op Sealed Log

*Entry 77 — Toledo / Build 0100*

One ninety-one feet.

The second-largest construction in the network. Thirty-three years of Tyler in a city he barely remembers.

He was there through the fall of the Taifa. Through the reconquest. Through three years of the city becoming something else. He was there for the whole thing.

And he left diminished.

I've been thinking about the shape of Season 9 differently since today. We came in looking for pre-archive sites — the physical record of Tyler's pre-1127 history. What we're actually finding is the story of a specific transformation. Not: where was Tyler. But: what happened to Tyler.

Córdoba gave us Tyler at his most present. Twelve years of full attention in the most intellectually alive city in the world. Clean departure. He left when the match ended.

Toledo gave us Tyler in decline. Arriving still capable of choosing — first three years are DWELLING, clear signal. And then thirty years of WITNESS-LONG as he watched what he had come to see change into something he hadn't chosen to see. He stayed because he couldn't look away. He left when he ran out of the capacity to stay properly.

The instrument lead said: "FAREWELL-B. Diminished. Present at the departure but had been going for longer than the moment."

That's Toledo. That's what Toledo did.

And then he went to the coast and watched the ships.

I keep thinking about what the Cataloguer note in the Avignon entry said: extended catastrophic witnessing produces FAREWELL-B departure capacity. Tyler watched Avignon die of the plague and left diminished. Tyler watched Toledo change civilizations and left diminished.

He keeps choosing to watch the difficult thing. He doesn't leave while the information is arriving.

The ships are the next piece of information that arrived. Whatever harbor that was — wherever those ships were going — Tyler watched them for long enough that it will have left a mark.

He said: "I was watching something again. I couldn't stop watching."

He thought he was done.

He wasn't done.

I think the Marrakech decision in 1127 — the decision to start keeping intentional records, to pay attention — came from somewhere. The gap is the story of Tyler accumulating more than he could process, in city after city, until in Marrakech he decided that if he was going to keep watching, he was at least going to write it down.

We are going to the coast to find the harbor.

I don't know what we're going to find there.

*— Camera Op*
