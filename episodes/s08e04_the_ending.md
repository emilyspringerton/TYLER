## ACT ONE — WHAT HAPPENED

*[They walk. Tyler leads them through the route, explaining as they go.]*

**TYLER:** "The Dutch Republic in the 1780s was — fractured. There was a reform movement. The Patriots wanted to shift power from the Orange Stadtholders to a more democratic arrangement. It was a genuine popular movement. The Amsterdam city council had Patriot sympathies. Half the country was Patriot, half was Orangist. By 1785, the conflict was open."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You were sympathetic to the Patriots?"

**TYLER:** "I was sympathetic to the argument. The argument was good. The Dutch Republic had pioneered a certain kind of pluralism — tolerance as practical policy, commercial rather than religious grounds for civil society. The Patriot movement wanted to extend that logic. It seemed — " He pauses. "It seemed like the next correct step for what the Republic had been building for 150 years."

**CAMERA OP:** "And the Prussians came."

**TYLER:** "The Prussians came because Orange was a Hohenzollern connection — the Stadtholder's wife was a Prussian princess. Frederick William II sent in the army. The Patriots were suppressed in three weeks." He looks at the street. "The Amsterdam city council — which had been Patriot — surrendered without a fight. The Patriots who could flee, fled. Most went to France. The argument was over."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "And you?"

**TYLER:** "I stayed through 1787. I stayed through the suppression. I stayed through the Orange restoration. And then — " He is quiet. "The Republic continued, technically. William V was restored as Stadtholder. But the thing it had been — the thing that had made me come back every few years for a hundred and sixty years — was gone."

*[He stops at the Herengracht bridge — the same bridge where he stood on the first day they arrived.]*

**TYLER:** "I came back in 1789. To say goodbye, I think. I didn't articulate it to myself as that. I told myself I was coming to see if anything had changed. Nothing had changed. The Republic was still intact and the thing that had made it worth returning to was absent. The change was internal. The city was the same. The city I had been coming to was gone."

## ACT TWO — THE LAST THREE SITES

### A-353 — PRINSENGRACHT, 1785

*[The Prinsengracht — not far from A-352, the 1672 site. Day 353.]*

**TYLER:** "I came in late 1785 when the Patriot-Orangist split was escalating. Twelve days. I wanted to understand what the city was deciding."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "FOUND construction — information-seeking."

**TYLER:** "The city was deciding who it was. I wanted to be present for the deciding. I thought — " He pauses. "I thought it was going to go the right way. The argument was good. The number of people who believed in it was substantial. I thought the Republic was going to take the next correct step."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "And the 1787 site?"

### A-354 — KEIZERSGRACHT, 1787

*[The Keizersgracht. September 1787. The Prussians had crossed the border two weeks before.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(running the measurement)* "A-354. 1787. Eleven days. Forty-four feet."

*[She looks up. Forty-four feet for eleven days is the crisis-amplification range — above baseline.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Elevated yield. Crisis-amplification range."

**TYLER:** "I was afraid."

**CAMERA OP:** "Again."

**TYLER:** "Again." He looks at the canal. "In 1672 I was afraid of the Republic ending and it didn't end. In 1787 I was afraid of the argument ending and it did end." He pauses. "The fear may have been the same. The outcomes were different."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The mechanism ran the same — fear as density. The crisis-amplification coefficient doesn't know whether the crisis is survived."

**TYLER:** "No. It doesn't." He looks at the water. "I stayed eleven days in September 1787 while the Prussians suppressed the Patriot movement. I was present for the ending. I stayed because — " He stops.

**CAMERA OP:** "Because you thought maybe it wasn't the ending?"

**TYLER:** *(quiet pause)* "Because I thought maybe it wasn't the ending." He looks at her. "It was the ending."

### A-355 — HERENGRACHT, 1789

*[The Herengracht. The Golden Bend. A-340, where the 161-year STAYING construction is running — fourteen returns, most recent exactly this location, 1789.]*

*[Tyler stands at the A-340 site. The Exchange Student checks the instruments.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "This is A-340 also. The same location."

**TYLER:** "The last visit is at the same location as the first. Yes."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(running the measurement)* "A-355. 1789. Four days." She looks at the readings. "This is — unusual." She adjusts the instruments. Runs again. "A-355 and A-340 are overlapping. The 1789 visit is the fourteenth return — it's been counted in A-340 as the final data point in the STAYING construction. But there's also something else here." She looks at Tyler. "The 1789 reading has a distinct signature that doesn't fit the STAYING model."

**TYLER:** "What does it fit?"

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "It reads like A-286. The Westbahnhof departure-trace. The platform where F. left Vienna." She looks at the instruments carefully. "It reads like a departure."

*[Tyler is quiet.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You came back to the first place. In 1789. And you left."

**TYLER:** "I came back to the first place. I sat on the bank for four days. I looked at the canal. I looked at the light on the water. And I left."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "What were you thinking?"

**TYLER:** "I was thinking: this is still here. The canal is still here. The light is still the same. The city is still here." He pauses. "And then I was thinking: I am leaving this place. I do not know when I will be back. I may not come back." He looks at the canal. "And then I left."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The mechanism recorded the leaving."

**TYLER:** "Apparently."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** *(measuring the distinct signature)* "It's not a REFUSAL. You didn't refuse the place. It's not a DEPARTURE-TRACE in the A-286 sense — that was someone else leaving and you holding their going. This is — " She pauses. "This is you leaving a place you loved because it had become something you couldn't stay for."

*[The Camera Op holds the shot on Tyler at the canal.]*

**CAMERA OP:** *(quietly)* "The account wasn't ongoing anymore."

**TYLER:** "The account was ongoing. The city was still there. The mechanism was still running. I was — I was stopping my participation in the account. Temporarily." He pauses. "I thought temporarily."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Two hundred and thirty-seven years."

**TYLER:** "I know."

## ACT THREE — THE NEW TYPE

*[The Keizersgracht room. Evening.]*

*[The Exchange Student has the three measurements in front of her. A-353, A-354, A-355. She is trying to classify A-355 within the taxonomy.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The categories we have: UNINTENTIONAL, AMPLIFICATION, FOUND, STAYING, PRIOR, WITNESS. Sub-types: REFUSAL (pre-awareness and purpose-based), DEPARTURE-TRACE, CRISIS-AMPLIFICATION, gap-accumulation STAYING-B. None of these fit A-355 exactly."

**TYLER:** "What does it fit?"

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "It fits — grief, I think. Measured grief."

*[She writes it down:]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Provisional designation: FAREWELL. A construction formed by sustained presence at a site while in the act of voluntary departure. Different from STAYING, which accumulates across returns. Different from DEPARTURE-TRACE, which records someone else's leaving. FAREWELL is: you, at the site, while leaving. The mechanism records the love of the place in the act of deciding to go."

*[Tyler looks at what she's written.]*

**TYLER:** "FAREWELL."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The A-286 Westbahnhof construction — that was F.'s departure, which Tyler witnessed. DEPARTURE-TRACE. A-355 is Tyler's departure from a city he loved, witnessed by himself." She pauses. "The mechanism may have been running FAREWELLs throughout the archive at moments we classified differently. Anywhere Tyler left and didn't come back — the leaving may have a distinct signature."

**CAMERA OP:** "How many FAREWELLs are in the archive?"

*[Tyler is quiet for a long time.]*

**TYLER:** "More than I want to count."

---

*[They sit with that for a moment.]*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "We should rerun the measurement at every site where Tyler left and didn't return within a normal interval. We might find FAREWELLs in the archive we filed as something else."

**TYLER:** "Yes."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "Including the Paris site from A-278 — the Île Saint-Louis, where you applied the 1931 rule and left. Pre-awareness refusal. But you also never came back to that specific address."

**TYLER:** "I was aware when I left. It wasn't a 1931-rule refusal — I left because the room asked and I didn't know why. I wasn't refusing to amplify. I was following a rule I made to protect myself from situations I didn't understand." He pauses. "But I never went back."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "The departure might be a FAREWELL."

**TYLER:** *(quietly)* "It might be."
