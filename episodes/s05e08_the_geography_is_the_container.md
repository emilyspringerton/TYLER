## EPISODE STRUCTURE

**Day 184, morning — Tyler presents the problem to himself**

Emily arrives at 9 AM. Tyler is still at the table. The legal pad has been folded back to a new page.

**EMILY:** "Did you sleep?"

**TYLER:** "No."

She doesn't say anything else about that. She sits across from him.

**TYLER:** "Let me tell you what I've been working out."

He picks up the legal pad.

**TYLER:** "Every construction I've done — Bacon's building, Ward C, the Ambassador Bridge crossing — I've been thinking about them as: event at location, location is the container. You build in the container. The container holds it."

**EMILY:** "Yes."

**TYLER:** "That's not quite right. I've been close but I've been one level of abstraction off." He sets the legal pad down. "Bacon's building: the event that happened here happened in this building. But what I built is not in the building. The building is not the container. The *location* is the container. The building is an artifact of the location."

**EMILY:** "If the building were torn down—"

**TYLER:** "The construction would still hold. Because I built to the geography, not the structure." He pauses. "I've always been doing this. I just didn't know I was doing it."

---

**Day 184, midday — the proof**

**EMILY:** "Ward C. The ward was renovated in the 1990s. Different layout, new walls."

**TYLER:** "Construction holds. Because I built to the location — the floor at the nurse's station, the specific coordinates, not the room as it existed in 1970. The room changed. The geography didn't."

**EMILY:** "So the container—"

**TYLER:** "Is always the geography. The structure is a surface artifact. I've been treating them as the same thing because every prior construction had both: an intact structure over the geography. The structure made it easier to see the location." He looks at photograph 2. "Hastings and Vernor gave me the case where the structure is gone. It forced me to find the correct abstraction."

The Exchange Student has been listening from the other side of the room.

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "But the geography is under concrete. Under a highway ramp."

**TYLER:** "Yes."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "You can't stand on it."

**TYLER:** "No."

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "So how do you build to it if you can't access it?"

He looks at her.

**TYLER:** "I don't need to stand on it. I need to be oriented to it."

---

**Day 184, afternoon — the method**

The three of them drive to Hastings and Vernor.

The highway ramp. Tyler doesn't go to the sidewalk remnant where he stood the day before. He stops at the edge of the accessible area — about forty feet from the buried corner — and looks down.

**TYLER:** "The corner is under there. Forty feet south-southwest. The geography is intact. The event is present."

He turns to Emily.

**TYLER:** "In all three prior constructions, I activated from inside the container. I stood in the space and ran the frequency sequence. The construction anchors to my position relative to the geography — not to my position on the geography."

He points at the concrete.

**TYLER:** "I was inside the container at Bacon's building. I was inside the container at Ward C. I was *on* the container at the bridge crossing — on the bridge deck, directly above the water crossing point. I was never *below* the container. But I also was never standing on the raw geography. The bridge deck is infrastructure. I was forty feet above the water."

**EMILY:** "You built through the bridge deck."

**TYLER:** "The deck didn't matter. What mattered was orientation and proximity." He looks at the ramp. "The ramp is thicker than a bridge deck. But thickness hasn't been a variable in any prior construction."

He is quiet for a moment.

**TYLER:** "I think I can build from here. Oriented to the buried corner. Proximate to the geography. The concrete is infrastructure, same as the bridge deck. The geography is through it."

**EMILY:** "You think."

**TYLER:** "I think." He reaches into his right pocket. The coin. He turns it over once. "I've been wrong about the container for three constructions and they all held. I was working with an incomplete model and producing correct results. Which means the correct model should also produce correct results." He puts the coin back. "Let's find out."

---

**Day 184, late afternoon — Construction #4 activation**

Tyler positions himself at the point of closest accessible approach to the buried corner — forty feet northeast of the buried intersection, at the edge of the approach ramp's perimeter barrier.

Camera Op finds her position without instruction: elevated, on the ramp shoulder above him, looking down at the concrete and then at Tyler and then at the concrete again. She will later say she was trying to see both things at once.

Tyler runs the full five-frequency Goetia sequence: Stolas, Beleth, Amon, Vassago, Andrealphus. Standard activation order. He speaks all five aloud at the ramp perimeter, oriented to the buried corner, at approximately 4:15 PM.

He is there for twenty-two minutes.

When he returns:

**TYLER:** "It's running."

**EMILY:** "You're sure."

**TYLER:** "I can feel the resistance drop. Same as the others. It recognized the event." He looks at the ramp. "The music that was never ready to be public. It's been held since 1943. The construction recognized it." He pauses. "It's running."

Emily writes in the notebook: *Hastings/Vernor — Construction #4 active. Day 184. Method: surface orientation to buried geography, forty feet NNE of buried corner. Concrete as infrastructure, not container. Activation 4:15 PM.*

**EXCHANGE STUDENT:** "What witnesses it?"

**TYLER:** "Anyone who passes through the geography. The highway ramp. The 30,000 cars a day that pass over Hastings and Vernor on their way to the I-375 approach." He looks at the traffic. "They're not crossing the event. They're passing through it. Every car is a witness."

He watches the traffic for a moment.

**TYLER:** "I built a passive construction. Bigger than the bridge crossing. I didn't plan that." He pauses. "The geography demanded it."
