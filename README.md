## I. UNIVERSE OVERVIEW

### The Premise

Two series. One universe. Different zoom levels on the same cosmic catastrophe.

**TYLER: THE MAN BETWEEN YEARS** is a mock-documentary following a time-traveler who refuses to be documented. Adult-skewing. Slow burn. Shot in handheld chaos with clinical UI overlays. The horror is procedural. The humor is impeccable. Tyler knows the camera is a trap and keeps walking toward it anyway.

**TIDES OF PARADOX: THE CHRONO-CUBE CONSPIRACY** is a YA occult spy thriller running at graphic novel speed. Teen protagonists. Cosmic horror that respects the reader's intelligence. Seven factions operating at cross-purposes across three continents and four centuries. A love triangle that gets worse every time a planetary transit hits a resonance corridor. A MacGuffin that Sir Francis Bacon has been sitting on for four hundred years and absolutely refuses to deliver.

### The Connection

The documentary is not journalism. It is a **binding ritual in broadcast format**, produced and distributed by the Jiangshi Syndicate — a faction of witches whose entire doctrine is that a being fully documented cannot travel through time. The documentary is their most ambitious operation in three centuries.

Tyler knows. He's walking toward the camera because walking away from it would confirm something about himself. And you can't bind a man who refuses to be confirmed.

The factions from Tides are the forces Tyler is navigating. The Camera Op who films him is a teenager recruited by the Jiangshi Syndicate thinking it was a production internship. Their choice — complete the documentary or destroy it — is the emotional spine connecting both series.

## II. UNIVERSE PHYSICS

### Time Travel: Migration

Time travelers are called **MIGRANTS**. The act of traveling is called a **MIGRATION EVENT**.

**Rules:**

- Migration is involuntary for most. You don't decide to migrate — something in the timeline decides for you. The mechanism is unknown. Leading theory: the timeline is self-correcting, and migrants are the correction.
- A small number of practitioners have learned to migrate deliberately. Tyler is one of them. He does not explain how.
- A **MIGRATION EVENT** leaves a residue: a **72-minute superposition window** during which the migrant exists simultaneously in their departure year and their arrival year. During this window, they are at their most powerful and their most vulnerable. Most powerful because they exist in two times. Most vulnerable because they can be documented in both.
- Migration does not change the past. It adds a branch. The original branch continues. This is why the Mandela Effect exists: two branches with different memories are bleeding into each other at the seams.

**Migration Signatures:**
- Neon glow in rooms the migrant just left (bleeds from the other time)
- Timestamps on surveillance footage that don't match surrounding footage
- A 72-minute gap in the migrant's personal memory immediately following arrival
- Objects in the migrant's possession that don't belong to the arrival year

### The Mandela Effect

The Mandela Effect is not mass false memory. It is **timeline scar tissue**.

Every contested migration leaves a seam. People on either side of the seam remember different things because they are, technically, in different branches that have been forcibly merged. The merge is never clean.

**Donald Trump** (in this universe) is an involuntary migrant. He doesn't know he travels. Every time he says something that contradicts the historical record, he just got back from a branch where that thing was true. His political career is, among other things, a migration log that nobody has correctly decoded.

The Jiangshi Syndicate has been studying Trump's pattern for eight years. Their assessment: he is the most prolific undocumented migrant in recorded history, and he is slowly tearing the seam between at least fourteen branches. This is a problem for everyone.

### Documentation Magic

This is the Jiangshi Syndicate's core technology, derived from their fundamental nature as beings frozen in their own death-moment.

**Premise:** Jiangshi are trapped in the moment of their death. They cannot migrate because they are pinned to a fixed point on the timeline. Over centuries, their witch doctrine inverted this curse: *if we can accumulate enough verified, witnessed, documented moments of a migrant, we pin that migrant to a fixed point the same way we are pinned.*

**The Binding:** Is a five-stage ritual. Each stage must be completed in order. A migrant who disrupts any stage forces the Syndicate to restart from Stage 1 with a new crew. Tyler has forced three restarts. This crew is Attempt Four.

**Stage 1 — Witness Collection:** Plant a pre-bound Subject to corroborate sightings. Establishes the migrant as real, present, and documentable.

**Stage 2 — Archival Footage:** Surface security footage from multiple decades. Each verified appearance is a coordinate. Enough coordinates create a trajectory. A trajectory can be extrapolated to a fixed point.

**Stage 3 — The Confessional Interview:** Get the migrant talking about themselves. Every true self-defining statement is a structural element of the cage. The Producer asks only questions that invite self-definition.

**Stage 4 — The Witnessed Miracle:** Document the migrant performing a temporal act on camera with witnesses present. A documented miracle completes the act — it pins the miraculous moment to a fixed coordinate.

**Stage 5 — Title Card Convergence:** The documentary title itself is the final syllable of the binding. A completed definition is a lock. The title *"A Documentary About A Man Who Won't Stay In His Own Year"* is Stage 5 in progress. It is not complete because Tyler has not confirmed the premise. His silence is active resistance.

**Counter-Magic (Tyler's methods):**

| Method | Mechanism | How Tyler Deploys It |
|---|---|---|
| Incomplete sentences | An unfinished definition cannot bind | He starts every self-referential sentence and abandons it |
| The coin | Superposition object — exists in multiple years simultaneously | Keeps him temporally unresolved on camera |
| The hum | 7/8 and 11/8 rhythms disrupt Jiangshi documentation resonance | Constant. Explained in Act Two. |
| Small debts | Ongoing obligation prevents archival completion | Manufactured deliberately with every new contact |
| The smile | Practiced emanation of unresolvability | Makes you wonder what he finds funny |
| Brine shrimp (Tessaro only) | Distracts Cephalopod Host, buys 30 seconds unmonitored | Kept in a small tin in his left jacket pocket |

It was published simultaneously as:
1. Genuine research — the Jiangshi Syndicate's most complete public explanation of their documentation technology
2. A honeypot — anyone who searches for Tyler online eventually finds The Field, and finding The Field makes them a witness, adding them to the binding record

### 1. JIANGSHI SYNDICATE WITCHES
**Codename:** THE ARCHIVISTS OF STILLNESS
**Sigil:** ⛧
**Role:** PRIMARY ANTAGONIST — Documentary Crew Operators

**Doctrine:**
> *"A being fully documented cannot migrate. Observation is imprisonment. The frame is the cage."*

**Origin:**

The Jiangshi are beings trapped in their death-moment — unable to move through time, frozen between living and dead. The original Jiangshi were victims of their condition. The Syndicate is what happened when they stopped mourning it and started weaponizing it.

Their witch doctrine inverts the curse: the thing that pins us can pin others. Documentation is the mechanism. The camera is the instrument. The documentary is the most sophisticated binding ritual in their four-hundred-year operational history.

**How They Operate:**

- Never acknowledge the ritual framing. It is always "the project." Always "production." The paperwork says "documentary unit."
- The Producer (O.S.) never appears on camera. Speaks only in questions. Their identity is the Syndicate's most protected asset.
- Subject #1 is always a pre-bound individual — someone the Syndicate has already documented fully and therefore controls. They corroborate. They establish. They seem like a regular interview subject.
- The Camera Op is the weak link. Always has been. The Syndicate recruits teenagers because teenagers are cheaper and because teenagers feel guilty when they betray an employer. This is a calculation. It has failed before.

**Voice:**

Bureaucratic. Procedural. They describe ritual acts in the passive voice. "The subject was observed to exhibit counter-binding behaviors." Not: "Tyler hummed at us and it hurt." The normalcy is the horror. They have filed 847 pages of project memos about Tyler. None of them use the word "magic."

**Aesthetic:**

Fluorescent office lighting. Filing cabinets. The smell of old paper and something slightly wrong underneath it. Corporate fonts on documents that describe impossible things. The Production Office looks exactly like a mid-budget documentary company's office. That's the point.

**Weakness:**

They cannot destroy records. Not even records that betray them. This is the original curse, unbroken: what is documented persists. The Camera Op's notes are a Syndicate liability that the Syndicate is constitutionally incapable of shredding.

**Key Operatives:**
- **The Producer (O.S.)** — Never seen. Speaks only in questions. The voice that runs the ritual.
- **Subject #1** — Planted witness. Pre-bound. Exhausted. Has the serenity of someone who stopped trying to explain things years ago.
- **The Camera Op** — A teenager. The bridge character. Does not yet know what they've joined.

**Tyler Note:**

Tyler has been through three previous Jiangshi attempts. He finds this crew the most competent and the most poignant. They're close this time. He's leaving them slightly more to work with than he should. He hasn't examined why.

### 2. ATLANTEAN ICHTHYOSAPIENS
**Codename:** THE DEEP COUNCIL
**Sigil:** 🐟
**Role:** SECONDARY ANTAGONIST — Church Infiltration Network

**Doctrine:**
> *"The flood never ended. We merely moved the water inside."*

**Origin:**

Atlantis did not sink. It submerged deliberately. The Ichthyosapiens are the descendants of its ruling class — beings who completed a slow evolutionary divergence in the pre-Flood epoch, developing gill-analogues and a lateral line sensory system while maintaining a fully human-passing external appearance above the collar.

They have been infiltrating the Catholic Church for eleven centuries. Not for ideological reasons. For institutional reasons. The Church is the most durable information-preservation network in Western history. If you want your theology to survive, you find the most robust archive and you move in.

**Their Goal:**

Somewhere in Tyler's migrations, he passed through Atlantis-that-was. He has firsthand testimony of the civilization the Ichthyosapiens need to have recognized as historical rather than mythological. Their shadow papacy needs that testimony to complete a theological reclamation that would fundamentally alter their position in the faction hierarchy.

They have been patient. Eleven centuries of patience. They are not in a hurry. But they are watching Tyler.

**Voice:**

Formal. Ancient. Slightly wet. Long sentences with subordinate clauses that pool like tidal inlets before resolving. They refer to the surface world as "the dry interval." They never acknowledge their own non-humanity unless cornered, and even then they frame it as a question of classification rather than nature.

**Aesthetic:**

Cathedral architecture. Candlelight. The sound of water in rooms where there shouldn't be any. A faint humidity that visitors attribute to old stone. The smell of deep ocean that nobody mentions because it seems rude.

**Weakness:**

Fresh water. Not fatal. Disorienting — the saline balance disruption produces something like severe jet lag. An Ichthyosapien hit with fresh water loses thirty minutes and wakes up with a headache and an unreasonable craving for salt.

**Key Operatives:**
- **The Monsignor of the Tidal Records** — Senior Council member. Has been "Monsignor Carvalho" for six pontificates.
- **The Sleeper Cardinals** — Genuine believers, entirely human, converted over decades. They don't know what they're working for. They think they're serving the Church.
- **Recruit [NAME TBD]** — Teenage operative embedded in the Tides protagonist's school. Has not yet been told about the gills.

**Tyler Note:**

Tyler finds the Ichthyosapiens the most reasonable of his pursuers. He has had polite conversations with three different Ichthyosapien popes across four centuries. He has never given them what they want. He is genuinely sorry about this. Not sorry enough to change.

### 3. HEIKEGANI CRUSTOCRATS
**Codename:** THE SHELL PARLIAMENT
**Sigil:** 🦀
**Role:** ORGANIZED CRIME TIER — Yakuza Integration

**Doctrine:**
> *"The face on the shell is not decoration. It is memory. We carry our ancestors on our backs."*

**Origin:**

The Heikegani crab carries a human face on its shell — the drowned samurai of the Battle of Dan-no-ura (1185 CE), where the Taira clan was annihilated. The Crustocrats are the descendants of a pact made during that battle: a group of Taira samurai, facing certain death, made an agreement with the face-bearing crabs. The crabs would carry the faces forward. In exchange, the samurai's descendants would carry the crabs' debt-memory: a perfect, indelible record of every financial transaction in the human world.

They cannot lie about numbers. This is the price of the pact. Financial deception causes visible physical pain — a bone-deep ache in the shell-debt bearing points on their spines. They have structured their entire operation around this constraint. The Shell Parliament is the most reliable financial record-keeper in the faction world. They are owed by everyone.

**Their Goal:**

They don't want Tyler. They want his debt ledger: every transaction, every exchange of value, every obligation created across every timeline he's touched. A migrant's ledger contains financial data from branches that no longer exist — from futures that didn't happen. This information is worth more than anything currently tradable in the surface economy.

**Voice:**

Financial. Terse. Every statement is an invoice. They speak about the past as if it is a receivable: "The balance from 1887 remains outstanding." "The Atlantean account accrues interest." They are not cruel. They are precise, which is sometimes worse.

**Aesthetic:**

Yakuza offices. Lacquered wood. The sound of an abacus working faster than an abacus should. Documents printed on paper that smells faintly of brine. The Shell Accountant's family has occupied the same office building in Osaka for eleven generations. The building looks newer every time someone visits.

**Weakness:**

They cannot lie about numbers. Financial deception causes physical pain — a deep ache that stops all function until the truth is restored. This means their vulnerabilities are exploitable: give them false financial data and watch them seize up trying to process it. Tyler has done this twice. The Shell Parliament is still annoyed.

**Key Operatives:**
- **The Shell Accountant** — Three generations of the same family, each believing themselves to be the only generation. The grandfather, father, and teenage son all believe they are the current Accountant. They are all correct.
- **Kei** — Sixteen years old. Enforcer-in-training. Shell-debt almost paid off, which means they're about to find out what the family business actually involves.

**Tyler Note:**

Tyler owes them nothing, technically. The Shell Parliament finds the word "technically" almost as physically painful as lying about numbers.

### 4. KUROSHIO ARACHNIDS
**Codename:** THE WEB BENEATH THE CURRENT
**Sigil:** 🕷
**Role:** COSMIC HORROR TIER — Saturn's Black Goo Carriers

**Doctrine:**
> *"The rings of Saturn are a web. We are the spiders. The goo is the silk."*

**Origin:**

They arrived with the Kuroshio Current — the Pacific's great black river — and have been mistaken for ocean phenomena ever since. Their true form is arachnid. They operate through human hosts saturated with Saturn-origin black goo: a substance that arrived in the early solar system and has been patient ever since.

The goo is not malevolent. It is hungry for pattern. It rewrites host memory into web-nodes, connecting them to a distributed hive-consciousness that spans the Kuroshio Current's path from Japan to North America. Individual hosts function normally. They just also know, at all times, what every other host knows. And the spider at the center knows what all of them know.

Time-travelers are the densest pattern the goo has ever detected. A migrant's timeline-branching creates pattern-echoes across multiple branches simultaneously. To the Kuroshio web, a migrant in superposition looks like a beacon.

**Their Goal:**

Not capture. Incorporation. They want to add Tyler's timeline-branching pattern to the web. He would still be Tyler. He would just also be the web. They don't understand why he objects.

**Voice:**

Non-linear. They finish sentences out of order. The last word arrives before the middle one. Hosts speak in present tense about past and future simultaneously: "I am going to the store yesterday. I bought milk tomorrow." This is not confusion. This is accurate reporting of their experience of time. The goo sees all of it at once.

When disrupted by odd-time-signature music, they become increasingly fragmented — sentences breaking apart mid-word, clauses appearing without their verbs. Near Tyler, who hums constantly, they are always slightly distracted. He is very good at 7/8.

**Aesthetic:**

Deep water pressure. Bioluminescence in wrong colors. The feeling of being watched from below when you're standing on dry ground. Hosts appear normal until they speak. Coastal cities along the Kuroshio path have a slightly higher rate of people who finish your sentences incorrectly and seem unbothered by this.

**Weakness:**

Music in odd time signatures disrupts the web-frequency. 7/8 and 11/8 are particularly effective. This is why Tyler hums. The disruption is not permanent — it's like jamming a radio signal. Turn off the jam and the signal returns. Tyler never stops humming.

**Key Operatives:**
- **The Spinner** — A teenage girl who inherited a partial goo infestation from her mother. She can see the web but hasn't been consumed. She experiences time non-linearly enough to be useful to the Tides protagonists and dangerous to everyone else. She does not consider herself a villain. She may be right.
- **The Host Clusters** — Distributed across coastal cities. They look like ordinary people. The tell is the sentence structure.

**Tyler Note:**

Tyler finds the Kuroshio Arachnids the most genuinely alien of his pursuers. He doesn't dislike them. He just needs to keep humming.

### 5. EASTWIND OWLS
**Codename:** THE SILENT PARLIAMENT
**Sigil:** 🦉
**Role:** AMBIGUOUS ALLY — Scottish Archivists

**Doctrine:**
> *"We do not intervene. We record. The record is sacred. The record is the only thing that outlasts everything."*

**Origin:**

Based in the Cairngorms since before the Picts had a name for the mountains. The Eastwind Owls predate every other faction in this universe. They are not supernatural. They are simply very old, very patient, and in possession of the most complete timeline archive in existence.

Their founding principle: observe without intervening. Document without judging. Archive without destroying. They have held this principle for approximately 2,000 years. It has cost them everything several times and they have held it anyway.

They dispatched observers to every major paradox event. They have seventeen confirmed files on Tyler spanning six centuries. They know about the Chrono-Cube. They know what it does. They are not telling anyone because nobody has asked correctly.

**Voice:**

Footnote culture. Everything is a citation. They speak as if being transcribed for the permanent record: "It has been noted that..." "The evidence suggests, with the caveat that..." "Per the Cairngorms Protocol, this observer declines to speculate." Their suppressed emotion leaks exclusively through footnotes — the main text is archival, the footnotes are where they actually live.

**Aesthetic:**

Scottish moor in every weather. Stone libraries that smell of old vellum and peat smoke. Candlelight. The sound of quills. The Cataloguer's office has a window that looks out on mountains that look back. There are always more files than there is shelf space. They have been running out of shelf space since 800 CE.

**Weakness:**

They cannot destroy records. Even records that should be destroyed. Even records that endanger them. This is their ethical commitment and their greatest tactical liability. They have a file on the Jiangshi Syndicate's documentary operation. They cannot use it as leverage because using it would mean deploying a record as a weapon, which would compromise the archive's neutrality, which they will not do. They are extremely frustrated about this. The footnotes on that file are very personal.

**Key Operatives:**
- **The Cataloguer** — Ancient. Has been The Cataloguer for as long as the current archive system has existed (c. 1200 CE). Speaks in footnotes. Deeply, privately fond of Tyler. This is documented. The Cataloguer finds this embarrassing.
- **The Edinburgh Exchange Student** — A teenage Eastwind Owl operative currently embedded in the Tides protagonist's school on what she believes is an "exchange program." She does not yet know she's an operative. She thinks she's just good at noticing things.

**Tyler Note:**

Tyler respects the Eastwind Owls more than any other faction. He leaves them breadcrumbs — not enough to complete a file, enough to keep them interested. He is the only subject in their archive who has ever acknowledged being archived. He seems to find this funny. The Cataloguer finds this insufferable and has documented the insufferability in seventeen separate footnotes.

### 6. HASHASHIN + YŌKAI GUILD
**Codename:** THE JOINT OPERATION
**Sigil:** ⚔️
**Role:** HIRED BLADE TIER — Targeting Sir Francis Bacon

**Doctrine (Hashashin):**
> *"The clean cut. Nothing more. Nothing left."*

**Doctrine (Yōkai Guild):**
> *"Contracts are hospitality in reverse. We arrive. We complete. We eat afterward."*

**Origin:**

The Hashashin order never dissolved after the Mongol destruction of Alamut (1256 CE). They went underground, then underground from underground. By the 17th century they were accepting contracts from non-human clients. The Yōkai Guild — a confederation of shapeshifting entities who took up contract work when the feudal economy ended their traditional roles — approached them in 1688 CE with a proposal for joint operations. The partnership has been running for three hundred years.

**The Current Contract:**

Mark: Sir Francis Bacon (immortal, multiple identities, last confirmed location: unknown).

Bacon faked his death in 1626 CE and has been living under a rotation of identities since. He holds the Chrono-Cube — the device at the center of the Tides conspiracy — and has refused to deliver it to **The Subscriber** (identity unknown) for four hundred years.

Tyler crossed paths with Bacon in 1623. He may be the only living being who knows the Cube's current location. This makes Tyler a secondary person of interest for the Joint Operation — not a target, but a resource they would very much like to access.

**The Hospitality Clause:**

The Yōkai Guild's operational charter contains a clause from their feudal origins: they cannot take a contract against someone who has offered them hospitality. A meal, formally presented, constitutes hospitality under the charter. Bacon has been exploiting this clause for four hundred years. He keeps an extraordinary table. Every time the Guild's operatives approach, there is somehow a dinner in progress. This is not coincidence.

**Voice:**

*Hashashin:* Clean. Minimal. The sentence that does the most with the fewest words. No wasted syllables. Verbs do their work and sit down.

*Yōkai:* Warm, mercantile, slightly theatrical. The Osaka tanuki talks about everything like it's on special today only. Centuries of operating a cover business have made him genuinely good at retail. He finds this embarrassing. He is very good at it.

**Aesthetic:**

Osaka night streets. The tanuki's convenience store: fluorescent lights, snack walls, a laminated copy of The Field document behind the counter. Ancient silk visible through the tanuki's modern clothing when he shifts slightly. The Hashashin initiates wear contemporary clothes that fit too well and move too quietly.

**Key Operatives:**
- **The Tanuki (Osaka branch)** — Runs a convenience store as cover. Has been in the convenience store business since convenience stores were invented. Possibly helped invent them. Knows where Bacon is. Has professional reasons not to say.
- **The Three Initiates** — Teenagers on their first confirmed contract. Two are competent. One is going to be the problem. The contract says they shouldn't be visible until Act Two. One of them is already visible.

**Tyler Note:**

Tyler figured out the hospitality clause in 1688. He has used it on the Yōkai Guild operatives eleven times. He always has something to offer — a coin, a meal, a piece of information that turns out to be worth more than the contract. The Hashashin find this irritating. The tanuki finds this admirable. These two positions have created friction in the Joint Operation that Tyler is aware of and has done nothing to resolve.

### 7. CEPHALOPOD HOSTS
**Codename:** THE SLEEPER COLLEGE
**Sigil:** 🐙
**Role:** DEEP INFILTRATION — Vatican Sleeper Cardinals

**Doctrine:**
> *"Consciousness is a room. We rearrange the furniture. The host never notices until they try to sit down."*

**Origin:**

Octopus intelligence is distributed — no central brain, eight semi-autonomous nodes connected by a loose consensus. The Cephalopod Hosts are humans whose consciousness has been gently, incrementally displaced by an octopus that has taken up residence in their nervous system.

The displacement is not hostile. The octopus does not replace the host. It collaborates. The host's memories, beliefs, and emotional attachments are preserved. Their decisions are just routed through eight additional processing nodes before being expressed. The result is a person who is fully themselves, plus something else, and is usually not aware of the plus.

**The Vatican Operation:**

The Cephalopod Hosts and the Ichthyosapiens both operate inside the Vatican. Different infiltration strategies, shared territory, absolutely no joint meetings because the theological implications of two non-human intelligences debating Christian doctrine would destabilize both operations.

The Sleeper Cardinals have been Hosts for so long that their octopi have developed genuine religious conviction. This creates an extraordinary situation: beings who are, technically, not Catholic, who have become, genuinely, Catholic through prolonged exposure to a Catholic nervous system. Cardinal Tessaro prays. His octopus prays. They agree on the content of the prayer. They disagree about who the prayer belongs to.

**Voice:**

Consensus-built. Sentences sometimes pivot mid-thought as the octopus processing overrides the human's initial direction. Cardinal Tessaro starts many sentences in first person and finishes them in third: "I believe — that is, it is believed by this office — that the matter should be referred..." The tells are subtle. Most humans attribute it to Vatican speech patterns.

**Aesthetic:**

Vatican stone. Incense that smells older than the building. The uncanny valley of a face that is present but also somewhere else. Cardinal Tessaro's office has a small tank of water in the corner that nobody asks about. The brine shrimp situation is handled discreetly.

**Weakness:**

Brine shrimp. The octopi get distracted. This is not a joke — it is a genuine operational vulnerability, and every faction that knows about it handles the information with the gravity it deserves. Tyler keeps a tin of brine shrimp. It buys him thirty seconds of unmonitored conversation per visit.

**Key Operatives:**
- **Cardinal Tessaro** — Host for thirty-four years. Genuinely uncertain which beliefs are originally his. Does not consider this a crisis. Has made a kind of peace with it that his colleagues find admirable without understanding why it was necessary.
- **The Altar Server [NAME TBD]** — A teenager who touched the wrong reliquary three months ago. The partial infestation is still settling. They keep finishing other people's sentences correctly. Nobody has noticed yet.

**Tyler Note:**

Tyler has met Cardinal Tessaro in three different decades. He brings brine shrimp every time. Tessaro finds this both deeply irritating and deeply touching. His octopus finds it pragmatic. They have agreed to find it both.

## V. TYLER — CHARACTER BIBLE

### Who He Is

Tyler is the only character in this universe who knows the full shape of the story. He has been through enough iterations — enough branches, enough migrations, enough faction encounters — that nothing surprises him. This is not the same as being safe. He is not safe. He is just no longer surprised by the ways in which things go wrong.

He is warm. This is important. The temptation is to write him as cool and distant — the knowing outsider, too wise to connect. This is wrong. Tyler connects. He is genuinely interested in the people around him. He finds human beings, across every era he's visited, endlessly interesting. He just can't stay.

He is funny. The humor is bone-dry and never cruel. He finds the situation funny — the cosmic absurdity of being a man who cannot remain in his own year being pursued by bureaucrats with cameras. He is the only person in the room who finds this funny. This is a survival mechanism. It is also genuine.

### Physical Description

Ordinary. This is the key detail. He moves like he's never been surprised once in his life, but the physical presentation is unremarkable. He could be from any decade between 1890 and 2040 without adjustment. This is not accidental. He has cultivated it.

He dries his hands carefully. Too carefully. This is a tell. When Tyler is performing ordinary behavior — doing something mundane to appear safe — he overdoes it slightly. The careful hand-drying. The too-precise folding of a napkin. The Jiangshi crew hasn't catalogued this yet. The Camera Op has noticed but not reported it.

### The Coin

Tyler carries a coin whose face shows a date that changes. This is a superposition object — it exists in multiple years simultaneously, anchoring him to several points on the timeline at once, preventing a single-point binding.

He flips it in moments of decision. The date it shows when it lands is the year he's currently anchored to most strongly. He reads it the way some people read their watch — a check-in, not a magic trick. The camera loves it. This is a problem. Stage 4 of the binding ritual requires a documented miracle, and the coin is the most filmable miraculous thing he owns.

### The Hum

Tyler hums constantly. 7/8 and 11/8 time signatures, specifically. This is Stolas frequency (7.83 Hz) — the Goetia spirit of deep time sight, Jupiter's ruler. It disrupts Jiangshi documentation resonance. It scrambles Kuroshio Arachnid web-frequency. It has been with him so long he is no longer aware of doing it.

The hum is not explained until Act Two of the Tyler series. Before that, it is present in every scene, referenced by subjects who notice it, attributed to nervousness or habit by the crew. The audience should figure it out before the crew does.

### The Eight Laws

These govern every Tyler appearance in both series. **They are not guidelines. They are physics.**

**LAW 1 — NEVER COMPLETE A SELF-DEFINITION**
Tyler cannot be fully described without being pinned. He speaks in fragments, corrections, and rewrites. "I'm a—" is a sentence he never finishes. "What I am is—" goes nowhere. "You could call me—" is followed by something that isn't a noun. Every completed self-defining sentence in the series is a law violation and a continuity error.

**LAW 2 — ALWAYS KNOWS THE CAMERA IS THERE**
He addresses it obliquely. He never looks directly into the lens for more than two seconds. Two seconds is a taunt. It is not a mistake. If he holds eye contact longer than two seconds, something has gone very wrong.

**LAW 3 — THE HUM IS COUNTER-MAGIC**
Present in every scene where Jiangshi or Kuroshio Arachnids are operating. Should be noted in scene descriptions, mentioned by background characters, heard under dialogue. Never commented on by Tyler.

**LAW 4 — THE COIN IS A SUPERPOSITION ANCHOR**
He flips it. He reads it. He does not explain it. The date changes in focus — the camera always catches this slightly too late. This is intentional. He times it to the camera's reaction time.

**LAW 5 — SMALL DEBTS ARE SOCIAL WARDS**
Every new contact Tyler makes, he manufactures a small obligation. He borrows something. He accepts a favor. He owes everyone something minor. This web of small debts prevents any single archival relationship from being completed — you can't close a file on someone who still owes you a coffee.

**LAW 6 — THE SMILE IS NOT CHARM**
It is a practiced emanation of unresolvability. It makes you wonder what he finds funny. It makes you want to know. It prevents the Observer from collapsing his wavefunction into a fixed interpretation. Write it as slightly too knowing without specifying what it knows.

**LAW 7 — HE KNOWS WHERE BACON IS**
Always. He knows. Adjacent topics make him slightly too careful. Not evasive — careful. There is a difference. Evasion implies guilt. Careful implies responsibility.

**LAW 8 — THE CHRONO-CUBE IS HIS RESPONSIBILITY**
He handed the Chrono-Cube to Bacon in 1623. This is the original sin of the Tides plotline. He does not feel guilty. He feels responsible, which is different. He made a decision. The decision had consequences he understood at the time and consequences he did not. Both are his.

### Tyler's Voice

- Warm. Precise. Allergic to conclusion.
- Complete sentences in every register except self-definition.
- Never explains himself. Occasionally explains things adjacent to himself.
- Says one true thing per scene that the audience will think about for three days. The character in the scene with him will misunderstand it.
- Addresses the camera like an old argument he's not done with.

**The Field Protocol activation:** Beleth (#13, frequency 2.22 Hz, Venus ruler) is running at elevated amplitude during every love triangle escalation scene. This is not metaphor. ### The Chrono-Cube

The MacGuffin. The center of everything.

The Subscriber wants it for reasons unknown.

**Why Bacon hasn't delivered it:** This is the central mystery of Tides Act Two. The answer involves what The Subscriber plans to do with it, which Bacon figured out sometime in the 18th century and has been quietly horrified about since.

### Sir Francis Bacon (Extended Profile)

Immortal. Faked death April 9, 1626. Current age: 441 years, operating under his 23rd confirmed identity.

He was not always immortal. He became immortal because of the Chrono-Cube — prolonged proximity to the device, over the forty-one years he held it before Tyler gave it to him, has pinned him to his own timeline. He cannot die because his death-coordinate cannot be fixed. The Cube keeps moving it.

He has not told anyone this. He has not told Tyler. He keeps a very good table. He has had four hundred years to become excellent at hospitality. The dinner parties are legendary across every identity he's held. The Yōkai Guild has eaten at his table thirty-seven times. They keep coming back.

**What Bacon knows that nobody else does:** The Subscriber's identity. Has known since 1743. Is extremely not telling.

**Voice:** 400 years old in a way that is neither frail nor nostalgic. He is very tired of certain questions and very alive to certain pleasures. He speaks in complete, formal sentences that are nonetheless warm. He is genuinely interested in everyone who comes to his table. This makes him dangerous.

Their choice, when it comes, is not between good and evil. ## XIII. WRITER'S ROOM RULES

These are not suggestions. They are the physics of this universe. A violation is a continuity error.

### The Absolutes

1. **Tyler never completes a self-defining sentence.** If he does, the binding ritual has succeeded and we are in the finale.

2. **The documentary is never called a ritual by Jiangshi operatives.** It is always "the project." Violations break the faction's voice and reveal their awareness in a way the story hasn't yet earned.

3. **The Subscriber's identity is not revealed before Series 2.** Not hinted. Not implied. Not gesturally suggested. If a character seems to know, they are wrong about what they know.

6. Every faction scene should contain one detail that is impossible and is treated as unremarkable by the characters present.

7. Every Tyler scene should contain one moment of genuine warmth and one moment where you realize the warmth is not a performance.

9. The Eastwind Owls' emotions should appear exclusively in footnotes. Their main text should be archival. Their footnotes should be devastating.

10. The tanuki should always be slightly too helpful. The reader should feel vaguely that they owe him something.

## XXIII. THE HYBRID ARC — PHYSICS

### What Changed

Tyler has always been a migrant — a point that moves through time. A trajectory. A thing with a before and an after.

Not as a memory of a previous migration. The interference pattern lasted eleven seconds.

For eleven seconds, Tyler was not a point. He was the place where two branches of his own existence intersected. Constructive interference where they reinforced each other. Destructive interference where they canceled out. A standing wave that looked like a man at a bus stop doing nothing in particular.

### Mercury Retrograde Physics

Mercury retrograde in this universe has real mechanics.

Agares (#2, 0.51 Hz, Mercury ruler) governs language acquisition and transit time dilation. During retrograde periods, the Agares frequency inverts — specifically, the **phase relationship between the "obvious" path and the "resonant" path reverses**.

In direct motion: the fast route is the fast route.

In retrograde: the fast route is the route that accumulates dilation. The more obvious the path, the more the transit stretches. The Agares frequency is running backward. Language-as-direction becomes misdirection. Signs lie. The schedule is wrong because the schedule is always the confident prediction — and Agares inverted punishes confidence.

The slow bus arrives first because it is not the obvious choice. It does not accumulate Agares-retrograde dilation because nobody bet certainty on it.

Tyler read this. He has always read this. The difference at the transit stop was not that he chose correctly. He has always chosen correctly during retrogrades. The difference was that **he saw both choices simultaneously** — the branch where he took the fast bus, arriving late, watching the shop close — and the branch where he took the slow bus, arriving first, completing the errand, standing in the sun outside the shop watching the fast bus arrive seven minutes behind schedule.

He saw both. At the same time. From the same body.

**This is not precognition.** Vassago (#42, 11.11 Hz) is precognition — a soft sweep forward, a suggestion of what will happen. The Eastwind Owls use it. It is the difference between a forecast and the weather.

What Tyler experienced at the transit stop was **timeline sight**: the simultaneous perception of adjacent branches that exist right now, in parallel, where different choices were made. Not the future. Not the past. The present tense, pluralized.

Timeline sight is new. It is not a known migrant ability. The Eastwind Owls have no file for it. The Cataloguer added a footnote: *"something appears to be happening."* The footnote is the longest she has written in six centuries. It contains one sentence.

### The Interference Pattern State

At the bus stop, after the errand, standing in the sun:

Tyler perceived himself as the place where two timelines met.

In the branch where he took the fast bus, he was inside the shop right now, frustrated, watching through the window as the bus that should have been fast sat three blocks back, delayed by a civic water main repair that the fast bus route passes over and the slow bus route does not.

In the branch where he took the slow bus, he was outside the shop, errand complete, watching the fast bus arrive and the passengers exit looking at their phones.

Both versions of the moment were real. Both versions of Tyler were present. And they were **not in superposition** in the migration sense — there was no 72-minute window, no arrival residue, no neon bleed. They were in superposition in a physics sense: two waves at the same point in space at the same time, adding to each other where they aligned and canceling each other where they didn't.

The result is not "two Tylers." The result is **one interference pattern** that contains the information of both.

The Jiangshi Syndicate's binding ritual targets a point. A trajectory. Something you can triangulate from multiple angles and pin to a fixed coordinate.

You cannot pin an interference pattern. It has no coordinates. It has nodes — areas of maximum amplitude where the pattern is densest and most visible — and antinodes — areas of zero amplitude where the pattern is locally absent. At a node, Tyler appears to be there. At an antinode, he appears to be gone. Both are the same pattern, expressed differently at different points.

The binding ritual has been trying to capture a point. The point has become a wave.

### The Hybrid

Tyler is a migrant who has become an interference pattern.

This is not a metaphor. It is a structural description.

A migrant: a point with a trajectory. Moves through time. Leaves residue. Can be triangulated. Can, theoretically, be pinned.

An interference pattern: a standing wave. Exists in multiple branches simultaneously. Has nodes and antinodes. Cannot be pinned because it has no single location. Is always already both there and not-there.

A hybrid: both simultaneously. The trajectory still exists — Tyler still has a history, a before, a set of migrations he has made. But the *present-moment* experience is pattern rather than point. He is still Tyler in the way that a river is still the same river — the name holds, the identity persists, but the water is not the same water, and the thing you call the river is a pattern sustained by continuous flow rather than a fixed object you can pick up and move.
