## III. THE FILE FOLDER

**What it contains:**

CEDAR-4 was a Shell Parliament-adjacent research function operating from approximately 1990–1997. Its primary documentation concerned the institutional dependencies of the SPRING line's archive work — specifically SPRING-2 (Margaret Springerton), who maintained a shadow archive of custody chain documentation through her professional roles.

The 1993 research file is CEDAR-4's terminal document. It contains:
- SPRING-2's professional identity (Margaret Springerton, research librarian)
- A partial map of her archive methodology
- A note, in CEDAR-4's handwriting, that reads: *"The work continues after the worker. Someone will know where to look."*
- A blank page at the end, date-stamped 1993-07-22, with nothing written on it.

The blank page is the dependency. CEDAR-4 knew there would be a successor. CEDAR-4 left the page for them.

**What the Exchange Student knows about the file:**

She found it in a storage unit she was clearing. She doesn't know whose unit it was. She knows the folder is old. She knows Margaret Springerton was real because she checked — she found a library science credential in the county records that matched. She knows the research is about something she doesn't have a frame for yet. She kept it because it felt like the beginning of something.

She is correct. It is the beginning of something.

**What she does not know yet:**

That SPRING-2 is Emily Springerton's mother. That the SPRING line is a custody chain. That CEDAR was monitoring it. That she has been selected — not by any person but by the architecture's own search function — as the person who will run what CEDAR tracked, now that the chain has closed and the use phase has begun.

The architecture needed someone who would find the file, recognize it as the beginning of something, and not put it down. It found her.

## IV. THE CEDAR SUCCESSOR FUNCTION

**What CEDAR tracked:**

The SPRING line's witnesses needed material support to do the work. Not dramatic support — archival supply orders, research access, institutional affiliations that gave their work credibility without explaining what the work was for. Someone had to track the supply chain of the custody chain. CEDAR did this.

CEDAR's tracking was passive: document what the witnesses needed, confirm it was available, update the record when the configuration changed. No intervention. No contact. The witnesses never knew CEDAR existed. CEDAR's function was to make the witnesses' work possible by monitoring its material dependencies.

**What CEDAR successor tracks:**

The custody chain is closed. SPRING-4 is active. The use phase has begun. SPRING-4's work now is construction on the architecture — building, not witnessing. The material dependencies for building are different from the material dependencies for witnessing. CEDAR successor tracks what SPRING-4 needs to build on the infrastructure.

Practically: what does Emily Springerton need to build EINHORN_INDUSTRIAL as a use-phase organization? What institutional dependencies does she have? What supply chains run under her work? What is the state of those supply chains? Are they intact, interrupted, approaching capacity?

CEDAR successor answers these questions. It doesn't ask them directly. It observes. It documents. It maintains the record so that if something breaks in the supply chain, someone will know when it broke and what depended on it.

**The Exchange Student's version of this function:**

She doesn't run it as a formal function. She runs it as curiosity. She pays attention to how things arrive — who calls whom, what gets delivered when, which institutional relationships open which doors. She has excellent instincts for supply chains without knowing she has them. She will discover that her instincts are not incidental. They are the function running before she named it.

Her mapping moment (Series 2 arc): she maps what she has been doing and recognizes the pattern. She finds CEDAR's 1993 methodology in the file and reads it against her own last year of attention. The match is exact. She was already doing it. She was CEDAR successor before she accepted the function. That's when she accepts it.
