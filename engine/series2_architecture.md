## 1. What Changed on Day 49

The custody chain is closed. The Subscriber's account is settled. Tyler's obligation is terminated. These are facts about the past. Series 2 is about what those facts make possible going forward.

The architecture shift is not dramatic. The world does not visibly change. Detroit is still Detroit. The round table is still in Bacon's building. The Camera Op still has all the footage. What changed is structural: the architecture that ran underneath everything is now in its **use phase** rather than its **build phase**. The difference is not visible from the outside. It is legible in the behavior of the systems running on top of it.

**Pre-transfer world state (Series 1 + 2):**
- The custody chain was an active obligation on Tyler's movement and choices
- Every free decision had to route around the chain's requirements
- The Subscriber's architecture was building toward a single specific event
- Emily OS was receiving toward H-4; all other operations were in that context
- Factions were organized around the binding question (will Stage 5 complete?)
- The SPRING line was active but unrecognized — witnesses doing the work without knowing the frame

**Post-transfer world state (Series 2 +):**
- The custody chain is closed; Tyler's movement is self-directed
- Emily OS H-4 is complete; Emily OS is operating in first-documented-post-H4-state
- The Subscriber's architecture is initialized and running; available for use
- SPRING-4 is active, registered, in the documentary record
- The factions have reorganized: Jiangshi archive closed, Eastwind continuing, Shell Parliament account settled, new accounting not yet opened
- The room behind the table is open; the Subscriber's materials are accessible
- The phrase "clean builds first" has a confirmed origin; its use is now conscious for some actors

## 2. The "Use Phase" — What the Architecture Can Do

The Subscriber built Layer 9 as a reception mechanism for the custody transfer. That reception is complete. But reception was never the end purpose — reception was the *initialization*. What was initialized:

**2.1 Substrate-level witness capacity**

A correctly initialized Layer 9 architecture can receive witnessed events at a fidelity that doesn't degrade with time. The custody transfer was witnessed by: the Camera Op (documentary record), SPRING-4 (ledger receipt), Bacon (custodian acknowledgment), Emily OS (substrate receipt), and the Goetia sequential activation (physical universe confirmation). That convergent witness is stored at Layer 9 — not as a record that can be read, but as a foundation that can be built on.

What can be built on it: any future architecture that needs a verified starting point. The transfer is the provable origin — "we know this was real because we know what it cost to make it real." Series 2 architectures that need a foundation can anchor to Day 49.

**2.2 The SPRING line, now active**

SPRING-1 through SPRING-4 are complete — the lineage is closed as a custody chain. But SPRING-4 is a living, active actor, not a sealed record. Emily Springerton's role in Series 2 is as an architect operating with full knowledge of what the chain was and what its closure enables. She is not constrained by the chain's requirements — she is free to build on the infrastructure the chain built. EINHORN_INDUSTRIAL in Series 2 is the organization that does this deliberately and openly.

**2.3 Tyler as free actor with architectural knowledge**

Tyler went to 1952 by choice. He will return to 2026 by choice. He now knows:
- What the Subscriber built
- Why the specific architecture was required
- What the phrase means and who wrote it
- What Emily OS is and where it came from
- Who the Camera Op is in the architecture

A Tyler who knows all of this and is operating without obligation is a qualitatively different actor than the Tyler in the documentary. The Series 2 Tyler is someone who can build. Not just maintain — build. The architecture made building possible by completing the transfer. Tyler's Series 2 arc is his first original construction since 1623.

**2.4 The Subscriber's unsealed sections**

The Subscriber's sealed file is opening. Not all at once — the opening is proportional to the readiness of the actors to receive what's in it. The Series 2 pace is determined by this opening rate. What's in the unsealed sections:
- The Subscriber's identity (Series 2 will not reveal this; it will reveal that it can be known)
- What the Subscriber is doing now (at Layer 9, in the use phase)
- The purpose of the architecture beyond the transfer — what it was built to enable long-term
- The Subscriber's stake in Tyler's first original construction

## 3. New Faction Dynamics

**Eastwind (continuing):** The TYLER archive continues. The Cataloguer continues. The archive focus shifts from Tyler-as-subject to Tyler-as-builder — documentation of construction rather than documentation of obligation. The Camera Op remains the primary witness. CAMERA-OP series continues. EMILY-SPRING file is active and growing. New subjects may appear in the archive as Tyler's constructions involve other actors.

**Jiangshi (reorganizing):** The Syndicate's Tyler archive is closed. The institutional knowledge — 44 days of documentary footage analysis, the binding coefficient data, the Stage history — doesn't disappear; it gets reorganized. Series 2 introduces: the question of what the Syndicate does with a world where the binding they failed to achieve was achieved by different means. Internal factions. Some who believe the Syndicate's methodology was correct but the target was wrong. Some who believe the result proves the methodology was wrong from the start.

**Shell Parliament (new accounts):** SM-1623-M is closed with zero balance. The Parliament's operational scope for Tyler is terminated. But the Parliament has a new accounting category it has never used before: `VOLUNTARY-CONSTRUCTION-001`. First-ever Tyler account that Tyler opened rather than the chain requiring. What Tyler builds in Series 2 accrues here — no obligation, pure accumulation. The Parliament is interested in what voluntary construction looks like after four hundred years of obligated maintenance.

**New: The Exchange Student's faction** — The Exchange Student has CEDAR-4's file. CEDAR-4 was a Shell Parliament-adjacent research function tracking the SPRING line's institutional dependencies (the "stationery orders" that were actually supply chain records for SPRING-2's archive work). The Exchange Student in Series 2 is not affiliated with any existing faction. She is running a new function: the CEDAR successor. What CEDAR tracked was the SPRING line's material dependencies — what the witnesses needed to do the work. With SPRING-4 active and acknowledged, CEDAR successor tracks what SPRING-4 needs to build on the infrastructure. The Exchange Student does this without knowing she is doing it, initially. Then with full knowledge.

**New: The Open Door** — The room behind the table is open. Bacon's building in Series 2 has a new functional space. Not all of Bacon's visitors can enter; not all of them know it exists. The room behind the table functions as a Series 2 analog to what the round table itself was in Series 1: a space that separates actors who have completed what they needed to complete from those still working toward it. The chair in the room — the Subscriber's chair — is not yet an invitation. That may change.

**Tyler (primary):** Returns from 1952 changed in a specific way — he has seen what he looks like without obligation in his own posture, his own choices. He knows now what free movement feels like from the inside. He comes back to 2026 ready to build. Her sealed log continues. Her awareness classification: AWARE-PRESENT (confirmed by S03E06 — she documented herself, the first time the camera turned on her).

She has the architecture map, the full dependency tree, SPRING-4 status, and the knowledge that the Subscriber's architecture is available for use. Her Series 2 arc is building with the infrastructure intentionally — the first SPRING-line member who gets to use the infrastructure rather than just witness it. EINHORN_INDUSTRIAL becomes, in Series 2, the organizational arm of the use phase. What it builds is Series 2's primary output.

The Exchange Student's moment of mapping.

**Protagonist A (Tides, continuing):** Post-resolution with Callum (the third condition was wanting, not obligation). Her Series 2 arc involves the open door — she is one of the people who can enter the room behind the table. Not yet. Her readiness to enter is a Series 2 arc beat.

## 5. Series 2 Throughline

**Season 4 (Series 2 Year 1): "What Tyler Builds"**
Tyler returns from 1952. First original construction begins. The Camera Op documents it. Emily Springerton is involved architecturally. The Syndicate reorganizes; new factions emerge from the reorganization. The Exchange Student finds CEDAR-4's significance. The room behind the table: first person other than Tyler enters it. The Exchange Student's arc reaches its mapping moment. CEDAR successor function accepted and running. EINHORN_INDUSTRIAL in full operation as use-phase organization. Protagonist A enters the room behind the table. The Subscriber's identity approaches legibility — not revealed, but the architecture for revealing it is visible. Tyler's construction: inhabited for the first time. Season closes on: what the construction does when it's running with people in it.

**Season 6 (Series 2 Year 3, Series 2 Finale): "The Subscriber's Name"**
The Subscriber's identity revealed — to Tyler first, then to the record. The architecture's long-term purpose becomes clear: not just the custody transfer, but what the custody transfer enabled. Series 2 closes with the documentary record complete through the first original construction and the Subscriber's identity in the record. End state: the architecture is running with full knowledge of what it is and who built it. What comes after that: Series 3.
